The Rough Collie is a graceful, long-coated herding breed famous for its flowing mane, wedge-shaped head, and gentle, devoted nature. Popularized worldwide by the fictional dog Lassie, this Scottish farm dog combines striking beauty with keen intelligence and an affectionate, sensitive temperament.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Size | Large (roughly 22–26 in tall, 50–75 lb) |
| Lifespan | About 12–14 years |
| Coat | Long, dense double coat (rough variety) |
| Energy | Moderate to high |
| Good with kids | Yes, gentle and patient |

## Appearance & temperament

The Rough Collie is a medium-to-large dog with a lean, athletic frame and an abundant double coat that forms a dramatic mane around the neck and chest. Coat colors include sable and white, tricolor, blue merle, and white. The breed's long, smooth muzzle and almond-shaped eyes give it a soft, alert expression.

Temperamentally, Rough Collies are friendly, loyal, and highly sensitive to their family's moods. They are gentle with children and rarely aggressive, though they can be reserved with strangers and may bark to alert. Their herding heritage gives them a watchful, responsive nature.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced diet formulated for the dog's life stage and activity level. Most adult Rough Collies do well on two measured meals per day. Because the breed can be prone to weight gain when under-exercised, monitor body condition and avoid overfeeding treats.

Provide constant access to fresh water, especially given the heavy coat. Some Collie lines carry the MDR1 gene mutation affecting drug sensitivity, so always consult your veterinarian before giving any medications or supplements.

## Exercise & training

Rough Collies are active dogs that need daily exercise such as brisk walks, play sessions, or securely fenced running. They thrive with about 60 minutes of activity per day and enjoy having a job to do. Mental stimulation through puzzle toys, obedience, herding trials, or agility helps prevent boredom.

This breed is exceptionally intelligent and eager to please, making training relatively easy. They respond best to positive, reward-based methods and gentle handling, as harsh corrections can damage their sensitive disposition. Early socialization helps build confidence.

## Grooming & care

The rough coat requires regular maintenance. Brush at least two to three times per week to prevent mats and tangles, focusing on the dense undercoat behind the ears, under the legs, and around the mane. During seasonal shedding periods (typically spring and fall), daily brushing is helpful as the undercoat blows.

Bathe occasionally when needed, and keep nails trimmed, ears clean, and teeth brushed regularly. Despite the long coat, routine brushing keeps grooming manageable without frequent professional clipping.

## Health & lifespan

Rough Collies typically live around 12 to 14 years. Responsible breeders screen for conditions known in the breed, including Collie eye anomaly, progressive retinal atrophy, hip dysplasia, and the MDR1 drug-sensitivity mutation. Bloat (gastric torsion) is a risk in deep-chested dogs, so avoid heavy exercise right after meals.

Routine veterinary checkups, parasite prevention, dental care, and weight management support a long, healthy life. Ask breeders for documented health testing on the parents.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Rough Collies shed a lot?**
Yes. Their dense double coat sheds year-round and heavily during seasonal coat blows. Regular brushing several times a week reduces loose hair and prevents matting.

**Are Rough Collies good family dogs?**
Very much so. They are gentle, patient, and devoted, getting along well with children and other pets. Their loyal, sensitive nature makes them excellent companions for active households.

## Conclusion

The Rough Collie blends elegance, intelligence, and a kind, loyal temperament into a devoted family companion. With consistent grooming, daily exercise, positive training, and attentive health care, this beautiful herding breed rewards its owners with years of gentle, affectionate partnership.
