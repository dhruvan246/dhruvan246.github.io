The Rottweiler is a robust, confident working breed descended from Roman drover dogs that herded and guarded livestock. Today they are devoted family companions and capable working dogs, prized for their calm steadiness, loyalty, and natural protective instincts.

## Overview

| Trait | Details |
|-------|---------|
| Size | Large (85-135 lb / 39-61 kg) |
| Lifespan | 8-10 years |
| Coat | Short, dense double coat |
| Energy | Moderate to high |
| Good with kids | Yes, with supervision and training |

## Appearance & temperament

The Rottweiler is a powerfully built, muscular dog with a broad head, strong jaws, and a deep chest. Males typically stand 24-27 inches at the shoulder and females 22-25 inches. The breed wears a distinctive black coat with clearly defined rust to mahogany markings on the cheeks, muzzle, chest, legs, and over the eyes.

In temperament, well-bred Rottweilers are calm, confident, and courageous rather than nervous or aggressive. They are deeply loyal to their families, often forming strong bonds and showing a watchful, reserved attitude toward strangers. Early socialization is essential to channel their guarding instincts into stable, good-natured behavior.

## Diet & nutrition

Rottweilers thrive on a high-quality diet formulated for large breeds, with adequate protein to support their substantial muscle mass. Because they grow quickly, puppies should be fed a large-breed puppy food that promotes slow, steady bone development and helps reduce joint stress.

Portion control matters: this breed gains weight easily, and excess pounds strain the hips, knees, and heart. Divide daily food into two meals and avoid heavy exercise immediately before or after eating, as deep-chested breeds carry a higher risk of bloat. Always provide fresh water, and consult your veterinarian about appropriate amounts.

## Exercise & training

Rottweilers need regular daily exercise, including brisk walks, play sessions, and opportunities to use their minds. Around an hour or more of activity per day keeps them physically fit and mentally satisfied; a bored Rottweiler may become destructive or develop unwanted habits.

This is a highly trainable, intelligent breed that responds best to firm, consistent, positive reinforcement. Begin obedience training and socialization in puppyhood, exposing the dog to many people, animals, and environments. Their strength and protective nature make early structure non-negotiable. Many excel at obedience, tracking, carting, and other dog sports.

## Grooming & care

The short double coat is low maintenance and easy to keep clean. Weekly brushing removes loose hair and distributes natural oils; expect heavier shedding during seasonal coat changes, when more frequent brushing helps. Bathe only as needed, typically every few months or when dirty.

Round out grooming with routine care: trim nails regularly, brush teeth several times a week, and check the ears for debris or signs of infection. Because Rottweilers are deep-chested and muscular, monitor weight and skin folds for irritation.

## Health & lifespan

Rottweilers typically live 8-10 years. The breed is generally hardy but predisposed to certain conditions, including hip and elbow dysplasia, osteosarcoma (bone cancer), heart conditions such as aortic stenosis, and gastric bloat (GDV). Eye disorders and obesity-related joint problems can also occur.

Choosing a breeder who screens for hip, elbow, heart, and eye issues lowers risk. Keep your Rottweiler at a healthy weight, maintain regular veterinary checkups, and stay current on vaccinations and parasite prevention to support a long, comfortable life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Rottweilers good family dogs?**
Yes. With proper socialization and training, Rottweilers are affectionate, loyal companions that bond closely with their families. Supervise interactions with young children given the breed's size and strength.

**Do Rottweilers require a lot of grooming?**
No. Their short double coat needs only weekly brushing most of the year, with more frequent brushing during seasonal shedding. Regular nail, teeth, and ear care complete their routine.

## Conclusion

The Rottweiler is a strong, intelligent, and devoted companion that rewards committed owners with loyalty and steady protection. With early socialization, consistent training, daily exercise, and attentive health care, this noble breed makes a confident and loving addition to an active household.
