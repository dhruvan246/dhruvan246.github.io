The King Shepherd is a large American breed developed in the 1990s to resemble an oversized, sturdier German Shepherd. Bred from German Shepherd, Shiloh Shepherd, and other large herding lines, it is prized as a gentle family guardian with an imposing presence and a calm, loyal disposition.

## Overview

| Trait | Details |

| Lifespan | 10-11 years |

| Energy | Moderate to high |
| Good with kids | Yes, gentle and protective |

## Appearance & temperament

The King Shepherd is a powerfully built dog standing roughly 25-29 inches at the shoulder, with males noticeably larger than females. It carries a broad head, erect ears, and a bushy tail. The breed comes in two coat varieties, coarse-haired and long-haired, in colors such as black and tan, sable, and solid black.

Temperamentally, the King Shepherd is known for being even-tempered, confident, and devoted. It tends to be calmer and less sharp than many working-line German Shepherds, making it a steady companion. Naturally protective, it bonds closely with its family and is typically gentle with children, though early socialization helps it remain relaxed around strangers and other animals.

## Diet & nutrition

As a large, deep-chested breed, the King Shepherd does best on a high-quality diet formulated for large breeds. Choose a food with quality protein to support muscle and joints. Puppies should eat a large-breed puppy formula that promotes slow, steady growth, which reduces stress on developing bones.

Feed measured meals two or three times daily rather than free-feeding, and avoid heavy exercise right after eating, since deep-chested dogs can be prone to bloat. Monitor body condition closely, as excess weight strains the hips and joints. Always provide fresh water and consult your veterinarian about portion sizes.

## Exercise & training

The King Shepherd has moderate to high energy and needs daily physical and mental activity. Aim for at least an hour of exercise a day through brisk walks, hikes, fetch, or off-leash play in a secure area. Mental challenges such as scent games, obedience drills, and puzzle toys are just as important for this intelligent breed.

Training is usually rewarding, as the King Shepherd is smart and eager to please. Use consistent, positive reinforcement methods and start early. Socialization with people, places, and other dogs during puppyhood builds a confident, well-mannered adult. These dogs excel in obedience, tracking, and as therapy or service companions.

## Grooming & care

The thick double coat sheds year-round, with heavier seasonal sheds in spring and fall. Brush at least two or three times a week, and daily during heavy shedding periods, to remove loose hair and prevent matting in longer-coated dogs. Bathe only as needed, every couple of months or when dirty.

Routine care includes trimming nails every few weeks, checking and cleaning the ears, and brushing the teeth several times a week to support dental health.

## Health & lifespan

King Shepherds typically live around 10 to 11 years. Because of their size and German Shepherd ancestry, they can be prone to hip and elbow dysplasia, so buying from breeders who screen breeding stock is important. Other concerns include bloat (gastric torsion), degenerative myelopathy, and allergies.

Keep your dog at a healthy weight, provide regular exercise, and schedule routine veterinary checkups to catch issues early. Responsible breeding and good preventive care give this breed the best chance at a long, comfortable life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the King Shepherd the same as a German Shepherd?**
No. The King Shepherd is a separate, larger American breed developed using German Shepherd and other large herding lines. It is generally bigger, heavier, and often calmer in temperament.

**Are King Shepherds good family dogs?**
Yes. They are loyal, gentle, and protective, and typically do well with children when properly socialized. Their large size means supervision around very young kids is wise.

## Conclusion

The King Shepherd is a devoted, intelligent giant that combines the working heritage of the German Shepherd with a calmer, family-friendly nature. With daily exercise, consistent training, regular grooming, and attentive health care, this loyal guardian makes a rewarding companion for homes that can meet its size and activity needs.
