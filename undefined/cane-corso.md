Muscular, confident and deeply loyal, the Cane Corso is an Italian mastiff descended from Roman war and farm dogs. Once used to guard property and drive livestock, the breed today is a devoted family guardian that bonds closely with its people and is happiest when given a job and clear leadership.

## Appearance & temperament

The Cane Corso is a large, athletic dog standing roughly 23-28 inches at the shoulder, with a broad head, square muzzle and powerful, well-muscled body. The short coat comes in black, gray, fawn, red and brindle variations, sometimes with a black or gray mask. Ears may be left natural or cropped, though many regions now discourage cropping.

Temperamentally, the Corso is intelligent, alert and intensely loyal to its family. It is naturally reserved with strangers and protective of its home, which makes early, consistent socialization essential. Well-raised Corsos are calm and steady indoors, affectionate with their household, and confident rather than nervous or aggressive.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced diet formulated for large-breed dogs, with portions matched to age, weight and activity level. Because the breed grows quickly, large-breed puppy food with controlled calcium and calories supports steady skeletal development and helps avoid joint problems linked to rapid growth.

Adults typically eat measured meals split into two servings a day rather than free-feeding. As a deep-chested breed, the Corso has an elevated bloat risk, so avoid heavy exercise right before and after meals. Keep fresh water available and monitor body condition, since excess weight strains joints. Consult your veterinarian for guidance on amounts and any supplements.

## Exercise & training

Despite its imposing size, the Corso has moderate to high energy and needs daily activity to stay balanced. Aim for at least an hour of exercise through brisk walks, secure off-lead play, structured games or activities such as obedience, tracking or weight pulling that engage both body and mind.

Training is non-negotiable for this powerful breed. Begin obedience and socialization in puppyhood, exposing the dog to varied people, animals and settings. Corsos respond best to firm, fair, reward-based methods from an owner who is a calm, consistent leader. This is not a beginner's dog and benefits from an experienced handler.

## Grooming & care

The short double coat is low-maintenance. Weekly brushing removes loose hair and keeps the coat healthy, increasing during seasonal sheds. Bathe only as needed when the dog gets dirty.

Routine care matters: trim nails regularly, brush teeth several times a week, and check the ears for dirt or moisture. Because Corsos can have loose lips, wiping the face helps manage drool and prevents skin irritation around the mouth.

## Health & lifespan

The Cane Corso typically lives 9-12 years. As a large, deep-chested breed, it can be prone to hip and elbow dysplasia, gastric dilatation-volvulus (bloat), certain heart conditions, and eye issues such as entropion or cherry eye. Buying from a breeder who screens for hip, heart and eye problems reduces risk.

Maintain a healthy weight, provide regular veterinary checkups, and learn the warning signs of bloat. Good preventive care, exercise and nutrition all support a longer, healthier life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Cane Corso a good family dog?** Yes, when properly raised. Corsos are devoted and protective of their families and can do well with children, but their size, strength and guarding instincts require early socialization, training and adult supervision around young kids.

**Do Cane Corsos need a lot of space?** They do best with room to move and a secure, fenced yard, though they can adapt to smaller homes if given sufficient daily exercise and mental stimulation. They are indoor dogs that want to be near their people, not kennel dogs.

## Conclusion

The Cane Corso is a magnificent, loyal guardian that rewards committed owners with steadfast companionship. With early socialization, consistent training, proper nutrition and attentive health care, this Italian mastiff becomes a calm, confident and devoted member of the family.
