The Kooikerhondje (pronounced roughly "koy-ker-hond-yuh") is a small, spaniel-type sporting dog from the Netherlands, where it was historically used to lure ducks into traps called *eendenkooien*. Striking in red-and-white markings and gentle by nature, it has grown into a much-loved companion and versatile family dog.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Small (roughly 9–11 kg) |

| Energy | Moderate to high |
| Good with kids | Good, especially with calm, respectful children |

## Appearance & temperament

The Kooikerhondje is best known for its distinctive coat: clear orange-red patches on a white background, with feathering on the ears, legs and tail. Many also have black-tipped ear hair known as "earrings." Lightly built and athletic, they have an alert, friendly expression.

In temperament, Kooikers are loyal, affectionate and attentive to their owners. They tend to be lively and playful at home yet reserved with strangers, so early, positive socialization is important. Sensitive by nature, they respond poorly to harsh handling and thrive on gentle guidance.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a good-quality, complete dog food appropriate for a small-to-medium active breed, divided into measured daily portions. Because Kooikers are energetic, match the amount to their activity level and adjust as they age. Watch treats and table scraps to prevent weight gain, which adds strain to joints, and keep fresh water available. Ask your veterinarian for guidance suited to your individual dog.

## Exercise & training

Despite their modest size, Kooikerhondjes are true sporting dogs with real stamina. Plan on daily walks plus active play, and they especially enjoy fetch, swimming and brain-engaging games that nod to their working past. Many excel at dog sports such as agility, obedience and scent work.

They are intelligent and eager to please, but their sensitivity means training should be reward-based, patient and consistent. Short, upbeat sessions and plenty of socialization build a confident, well-mannered companion.

## Grooming & care

The medium-length coat is fairly low-maintenance. Brush once or twice a week to remove loose hair and prevent tangles, with extra attention to the feathered ears, legs and tail. They shed seasonally, when more frequent brushing helps. Bathe only as needed, check and clean the ears regularly, trim nails, and keep up routine dental care.

## Health & lifespan

Kooikerhondjes typically live around 12–14 years. As a breed with a limited founding population, they can be predisposed to certain inherited conditions, including hereditary necrotising myelopathy (a nervous-system disorder), von Willebrand disease (a bleeding disorder), patellar luxation and certain eye issues. Responsible breeders screen their dogs to reduce these risks. Routine veterinary checkups, parasite prevention and weight management all support a long, healthy life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Kooikerhondjes good for first-time owners?**
They can be, for owners willing to provide daily exercise, gentle training and early socialization. Their sensitivity means a calm, consistent home suits them best.

**Do Kooikerhondjes shed a lot?**
They shed moderately, with heavier seasonal shedding. Weekly brushing keeps the coat tidy and manages loose hair well.

## Conclusion

The Kooikerhondje is a charming, agile and devoted companion that pairs an eye-catching red-and-white coat with a sweet, sensitive personality. Give it regular exercise, kind training and routine health care, and this Dutch original will be a happy, loyal member of the family for years.
