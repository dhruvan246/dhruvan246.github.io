Fancy goldfish are selectively bred varieties of the common goldfish, prized for their rounded, egg-shaped bodies and ornamental fins. Popular types include the Fantail, Oranda, Ryukin, Black Moor, Telescope and Ranchu. Though they have a reputation as easy "starter" fish, they live longest and look their best when given a properly sized, well-filtered cold-water aquarium.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Adult size | Typically 5-8 inches (sometimes larger) |
| Lifespan | Around 10-15 years with good care |
| Minimum tank size | About 20-30 gallons for one, +10 gallons per extra fish |
| Water type | Freshwater, cool/cold water |
| Care level | Beginner to intermediate |

## Appearance & behaviour

Fancy goldfish are distinguished from single-tailed goldfish by their compact, round bodies and usually double (paired) tail and anal fins. Colours range from metallic orange and red through calico, white, black and blue. Many varieties have special features such as the Oranda's fleshy head "wen," the Black Moor's protruding eyes, or the Ranchu's arched back and lack of dorsal fin.

They are peaceful, sociable and curious, often learning to recognise the person who feeds them and begging at the front of the tank. Their rounded bodies make them slower and less agile swimmers than slim-bodied goldfish, which affects both tank design and tank-mate choices.

## Tank setup & water parameters

Provide a spacious aquarium rather than a bowl; goldfish are messy and produce a lot of waste. Strong but gentle filtration is essential, ideally with the flow softened so weak swimmers are not pushed around. Use a smooth gravel or sand substrate and avoid sharp décor that can injure delicate fins or eyes.

Aim for water temperatures of roughly 65-75°F (18-24°C); fancy goldfish prefer cooler water and do not need a heater in most homes. Keep pH around neutral to slightly alkaline (about 7.0-8.0). Ammonia and nitrite must read zero, with nitrate kept low through weekly partial water changes and never overstocking.

## Diet & feeding

Goldfish are omnivores. Feed a quality goldfish pellet or gel food as the staple, supplemented with blanched vegetables such as peas, spinach or zucchini, and occasional protein like brine shrimp or daphnia. Sinking foods are gentler on the digestive tract than floating flakes, which can cause fancy varieties to gulp air.

Feed small amounts once or twice daily, only as much as they finish in a couple of minutes. Overfeeding is a leading cause of poor water quality and digestive trouble.

## Tank mates & compatibility

Fancy goldfish do best with their own kind or with similarly slow, round-bodied varieties. Avoid housing them with fast single-tailed or common goldfish, which outcompete them for food. Keep them away from fin-nipping species and from fish that need tropical temperatures, since goldfish thrive in cooler water. Small snails are generally compatible, but tiny fish or shrimp may be eaten.

## Health & common issues

Common problems include swim bladder disorder (causing floating or sinking), often linked to overfeeding or constipation, and fin rot or fungal infections tied to poor water quality. Telescope-eyed and wen-headed varieties are more prone to eye and skin injuries, so décor should be smooth.

Most illnesses trace back to water quality, so consistent maintenance is the best prevention. Quarantine new fish before adding them, and consult an aquatic-experienced veterinarian for persistent or serious symptoms rather than self-medicating.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can fancy goldfish live in a bowl?** No. Bowls are too small, lack filtration and quickly accumulate toxic waste. A filtered aquarium of at least 20-30 gallons gives them room to grow and stay healthy.

**Do fancy goldfish need a heater?** Usually not. They prefer cooler water around 65-75°F and most indoor rooms stay within this range, though a heater can help avoid rapid temperature swings.

## Conclusion

Fancy goldfish are rewarding, long-lived pets when their cold-water, high-waste nature is respected. Give them a generously sized, well-filtered tank, stable cool water, a varied diet and gentle tank mates, and these charming fish can thrive for well over a decade.
