The platy is one of the most popular freshwater aquarium fish, prized for its bright colours, peaceful temperament and easygoing care. A livebearer related to the molly and the swordtail, it suits beginners while still appealing to experienced hobbyists who enjoy its many colour varieties.

## Overview

| Attribute | Typical detail |
|---|---|
| Adult size | Around 4-6 cm (1.5-2.5 in) |
| Lifespan | Roughly 2-4 years |
| Minimum tank size | About 40 litres (10 gallons) |
| Water type | Freshwater (slightly hard, alkaline) |
| Care level | Easy, beginner-friendly |

## Appearance & behaviour

Platies have a stout, rounded body and come in an enormous range of colours and patterns, including red, orange, blue, sunset and the spotted "wagtail" and "mickey mouse" forms. Females are usually a little larger and fuller-bodied than males, while males have a modified anal fin called a gonopodium used in breeding.

They are active, sociable and almost always visible, swimming in the middle and upper levels of the tank. Platies are peaceful and do best in small groups, ideally with more females than males to reduce males constantly pursuing one female.

## Tank setup & water parameters

Provide a planted aquarium with open swimming space, a gentle to moderate filter and a secure lid, as platies can jump. Live or silk plants and some hiding spots help fry and shy individuals feel safe.

Aim for these parameters:

- Temperature: roughly 22-26 C (72-79 F)
- pH: about 7.0-8.0
- Hardness: moderately hard to hard water suits them well

Keep ammonia and nitrite at zero in a fully cycled tank and perform regular partial water changes to keep nitrate low.

## Diet & feeding

Platies are omnivores with a healthy appetite. A good-quality flake or micro-pellet forms the staple diet, supplemented with vegetable matter such as blanched spinach, spirulina or algae-based foods, since they graze on plant material in nature. Offer occasional treats of frozen or live foods like daphnia, brine shrimp or bloodworms for variety and colour.

Feed small amounts once or twice a day, only what the fish finish in a couple of minutes, to avoid overfeeding and water-quality problems.

## Tank mates & compatibility

Platies are excellent community fish. Suitable companions include other peaceful species of similar size, such as mollies, guppies, swordtails, neon and harlequin tetras, corydoras catfish and small rasboras. Avoid fin-nippers and aggressive or much larger fish that may bully or eat them.

Because platies breed readily and are prolific livebearers, a mixed-sex group will produce fry regularly. If you do not want a population boom, keep a single sex or provide plenty of dense plants so a natural balance is maintained.

## Health & common issues

Hardy as they are, platies can suffer from common aquarium ailments such as ich (white spot), fin rot and fungal infections, usually triggered by poor water quality, stress or sudden temperature swings. Stable, clean water and a varied diet are the best prevention.

Quarantine new fish before adding them, watch for clamped fins, loss of appetite or unusual spots, and act early. For persistent or severe illness, seek advice from an aquatic vet or an experienced fishkeeper rather than guessing at treatments.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do platies need to be kept in groups?** Yes. They are social fish and feel most secure in a small group, ideally with more females than males to spread out breeding attention.

**Are platies easy for beginners?** Very. Their tolerance of a range of conditions, peaceful nature and simple diet make them one of the best starter fish, provided the tank is properly cycled and maintained.

## Conclusion

The platy is a colourful, peaceful and forgiving fish that rewards even first-time aquarists with constant activity and a kaleidoscope of varieties. Give it a cycled, well-maintained tank, stable warm water, a varied omnivorous diet and compatible tank mates, and your platies will thrive and likely raise families of their own.
