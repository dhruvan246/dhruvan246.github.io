The Basset Bleu de Gascogne is a French scent hound prized for its striking mottled coat and its calm, friendly temperament. Developed in the Gascony region of southwestern France for hunting small game on foot, it is a short-legged, low-set member of the basset family, valued as much for its melodious voice and keen nose as for its good looks.

## Appearance & temperament

The breed's most distinctive feature is its coat: a base of black-mottled white hair that creates an overall slate or "blue" appearance, marked with black patches and usually tan points above the eyes, on the cheeks and on the legs. The body is long and low, with a domed head, long drooping ears and a noble, slightly mournful expression typical of French hounds. Temperamentally the Basset Bleu is affectionate, sociable and even-tempered, getting on well with people and other dogs. Like most scent hounds it can be independent and single-minded once it picks up a trail, and it is known for a deep, far-carrying bark.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food suited to a medium-sized breed and your dog's life stage. Because the long-backed, short-legged build means excess weight places extra strain on the spine and joints, portion control matters: you should be able to feel the ribs without a thick fat layer. Divide the daily ration into two meals, keep fresh water available at all times, and introduce any dietary changes gradually. Ask your veterinarian for advice tailored to your individual dog.

## Exercise & training

The Basset Bleu de Gascogne has moderate but genuine exercise needs and real stamina in the field. Daily walks, along with chances to follow scents and explore, help keep it physically and mentally satisfied. Because its nose can override its recall, keep it on a lead or in a securely fenced area when outdoors. Training should be patient, consistent and reward-based; this is an intelligent but independent breed that responds far better to food and praise than to pressure. Early socialization helps it grow into a relaxed, well-mannered companion.

## Grooming & care

The short, dense coat is low-maintenance and needs only regular brushing to remove loose hair and keep it healthy. The long, pendulous ears deserve particular attention: their shape reduces airflow, so check and gently clean them regularly to help prevent infections. Routine care also includes nail trimming, dental hygiene and keeping any facial folds clean and dry.

## Health & lifespan

The Basset Bleu de Gascogne is generally a hardy breed, typically living around 12 to 14 years. As with other long-backed, short-legged dogs, the back and joints can be vulnerable, so discourage excessive jumping and keep your dog at a healthy weight. The long ears can make ear infections more likely, and eye care is also worth monitoring. Responsible breeders aim to screen their stock, and regular veterinary checkups support early detection of any problems. Always seek professional veterinary advice for any health concern.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Basset Bleu de Gascogne noisy?**
It can be. As a scent hound bred to bay on the trail, it has a loud, deep voice and may use it readily, so early training and enrichment help manage excessive barking.

**Does this breed need a lot of grooming?**
No. The short coat needs only routine brushing, though the long ears should be checked and cleaned regularly to help prevent infection.

## Conclusion

The Basset Bleu de Gascogne is a gentle, sociable and handsome French hound that suits owners who appreciate its scent-driven nature and distinctive voice. With secure exercise, simple coat care, attentive ear checks and patient, reward-based training, this even-tempered breed makes a devoted and characterful companion.
