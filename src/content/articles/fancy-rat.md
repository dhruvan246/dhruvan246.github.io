The Fancy Rat is the domesticated form of the brown rat (*Rattus norvegicus*), bred over generations for friendly temperament and varied colours. Intelligent, social, and surprisingly affectionate, they make rewarding companions for owners willing to meet their need for company and stimulation.

## Overview

| Trait | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Size | 9–11 in (23–28 cm) including tail; 250–500 g |
| Lifespan | 2–3 years |
| Housing | Tall multi-level wire cage, kept in same-sex pairs or groups |
| Temperament | Sociable, curious, intelligent, affectionate |
| Care level | Beginner-friendly with daily interaction |

## Appearance & temperament

Fancy Rats have sleek bodies, rounded ears, bright eyes, and a long, mostly hairless tail used for balance and temperature control. Coat varieties include standard, rex (curly), hairless, and dumbo (low-set ears), in colours from agouti and black to white, blue, and hooded patterns. Bucks (males) tend to be larger and more laid-back, while does (females) are often smaller and more active. Rats are among the most trainable rodents, capable of learning their names, simple tricks, and litter habits. They are crepuscular, most lively around dawn and dusk.

## Housing & environment

Choose a tall wire cage with horizontal bars for climbing and solid or mesh-covered shelves, allowing roughly 2 cubic feet of space per rat as a minimum. Use absorbent paper-based or aspen bedding; avoid pine and cedar shavings, whose aromatic oils can irritate the respiratory tract. Provide hammocks, ropes, tunnels, hideouts, and chew-safe toys, plus a sturdy water bottle. Keep the cage out of direct sun and draughts at a comfortable room temperature of about 18–26 C (65–79 F). Rats are highly social and should never be kept alone; same-sex pairs or small groups prevent loneliness and depression.

## Diet & nutrition

A quality commercial rat block or pellet forms a balanced staple, ensuring correct protein and fat levels. Supplement with small daily portions of fresh vegetables and occasional fruit, plus modest amounts of cooked grains, egg, or lean protein. Suitable treats include peas, carrot, broccoli, banana, and apple (no seeds). Avoid sugary, salty, or fatty human snacks, and never feed chocolate, citrus in excess (for bucks), raw beans, or mouldy food. Provide clean, fresh water at all times. Scatter-feeding or food puzzles encourage natural foraging and mental exercise.

## Handling & enrichment

Fancy Rats thrive on interaction and should have daily time out of the cage in a safe, supervised area. Scoop them up gently, supporting the body rather than grabbing the tail. Most rats enjoy riding on shoulders, exploring play areas, and gentle wrestling games. Rotate toys, offer cardboard boxes, digging boxes, and foraging challenges, and teach simple tricks with food rewards to keep their sharp minds engaged. Consistent, calm handling from a young age builds trusting, well-socialised pets.

## Health & lifespan

With good care, Fancy Rats typically live 2–3 years. Common health concerns include respiratory infections (often signalled by sneezing, wheezing, or a red porphyrin discharge around the eyes and nose) and mammary or other tumours, which are frequent in older rats, especially does. Overgrown teeth, mites, and obesity can also occur. Watch for changes in appetite, weight, breathing, or activity, and consult an exotics-savvy vet promptly. Spaying does can reduce tumour risk. Routine cage cleaning and a dust-free environment greatly support respiratory health.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can I keep just one rat?** It is strongly discouraged. Rats are deeply social and become stressed or withdrawn when alone, so keep at least two compatible same-sex companions.

**Do Fancy Rats bite?** Well-socialised pet rats rarely bite and are known for gentleness. Nips usually stem from fear, pain, or mistaking fingers for food, and fade with patient handling.

## Conclusion

Fancy Rats reward attentive owners with intelligence, affection, and genuine companionship. Give them roomy housing, a balanced diet, fellow rats, and daily one-on-one time, and these charming small pets will flourish throughout their short but lively lives.
