The Indian Runner is one of the most recognizable domestic duck breeds, famous for standing nearly upright and running rather than waddling. Originally raised across Southeast Asia as prolific egg layers and pest controllers, Runners are lightweight, energetic, and a favourite on small farms and homesteads.

## Overview

| Trait | Details |
|-------|---------|
| Size | 3.5–5 lb (1.6–2.3 kg); tall, slim build |
| Lifespan | 8–12 years with good care |
| Space needs | Moderate; needs daily outdoor foraging room |
| Temperament | Active, alert, flighty but non-aggressive |
| Care level | Beginner-friendly |

## Appearance & temperament

Indian Runners have a distinctive bottle-shaped, vertical posture, long necks, and upright carriage that makes them look like bowling pins on legs. They come in many colours including fawn-and-white, black, chocolate, blue, and pure white. Unlike most ducks, they rarely fly and instead sprint across the ground.

Temperamentally they are curious, busy foragers that stay constantly on the move. They tend to be nervous and easily startled, often moving as a tight flock. They are not aggressive but are less cuddly than some heavier breeds; calm, consistent handling from a young age produces the friendliest birds.

## Housing & space

Provide a predator-proof, well-ventilated house with clean, dry bedding such as straw or wood shavings. Allow at least 4 square feet of indoor floor space per duck, plus a secure outdoor run. Runners are excellent free-rangers and thrive when given a yard or pasture to patrol for slugs, snails, and insects.

A source of water deep enough to dunk their heads and clean their nostrils is essential. While they do not strictly require a pond, a shallow pool or kiddie pool for bathing keeps them healthy and content. Always lock them up at dusk to protect against foxes, raccoons, and birds of prey.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a commercial waterfowl or poultry pellet appropriate to their life stage. Laying females benefit from a layer ration with added calcium. Runners are enthusiastic foragers and will supplement their diet with bugs, slugs, worms, and greens, which makes them valued natural pest control.

Offer occasional vegetables, leafy greens, and small amounts of grain as treats. Provide insoluble grit to aid digestion if they do not free-range on soil. Avoid bread, salty foods, and anything mouldy. Fresh, clean water must be available at all times, and feed should never be given without water nearby.

## Care & handling

Runners are hardy and low-maintenance. Keep bedding dry, refresh water daily, and check feet regularly for cuts or bumblefoot. When handling, move slowly and herd them gently rather than chasing, since stress causes panic. Support the body fully when lifting, holding the wings against the sides.

They are sociable and should always be kept in groups of at least three; a lone duck becomes stressed and lonely. A duck-to-drake ratio of several females per male prevents over-mating.

## Health & lifespan

With proper care Indian Runners commonly live 8–12 years. Watch for common waterfowl issues such as bumblefoot, mites, and niacin deficiency in growing ducklings, which can cause leg weakness. Wet, dirty housing invites respiratory and foot problems, so cleanliness is key.

Provide a balanced diet, dry shelter, and clean water to prevent most issues. Consult an avian or poultry veterinarian if a bird shows lethargy, lameness, nasal discharge, or sudden drops in laying.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Indian Runner Ducks need a pond?** No. They enjoy water for bathing but do well with just a shallow pool or tub deep enough to submerge their heads and keep their eyes and nostrils clean.

**How many eggs do they lay?** A healthy female can lay 150–300 eggs per year, making Runners one of the most productive duck breeds for backyard egg keepers.

## Conclusion

Indian Runner Ducks are charming, comical, and practical birds that suit beginners and experienced keepers alike. Give them companionship, a secure house, room to forage, and clean water, and they will reward you with abundant eggs, natural pest control, and years of entertaining, upright antics.
