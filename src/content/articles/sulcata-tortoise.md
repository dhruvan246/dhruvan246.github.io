The Sulcata Tortoise (also called the African spurred tortoise) is the largest mainland tortoise and one of the biggest of all tortoise species. Native to the arid Sahel region of Africa, it is hardy and personable but grows enormous, making it a serious long-term commitment rather than a casual starter pet.

## Overview

| Attribute | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Adult size | Typically 18-30 inches long; 70-150+ lbs |
| Lifespan | Often 50-80+ years with good care |

| Temperament | Calm, curious, strong, and persistent |
| Care level | Advanced (size and longevity) |

## Appearance & temperament

Sulcatas have a broad, oval, tan to brown shell with prominent growth rings and the distinctive spur-like scales on the back of each thigh that give them their name. Their thick, scaly limbs and blunt head suit a digging, grazing lifestyle. They are generally docile and can become quite tame, often recognizing keepers and approaching at feeding time. Despite their slow image, they are surprisingly strong and active, capable of pushing through fences and digging deep burrows.

## Habitat & enclosure

Because adults are so large, Sulcatas are best kept outdoors in warm climates with secure, sturdy fencing sunk below ground level to stop both escaping and burrowing out. Provide a spacious, well-drained area with grass to graze, plus a dry shelter or insulated shed for shade and overnight protection. Hatchlings and juveniles can be raised indoors temporarily but quickly outgrow tabletop enclosures. Avoid damp, cold, or soggy conditions, which this desert species tolerates poorly.

## Heating, lighting & humidity

Sulcatas need warmth and sunshine. Outdoors, natural sunlight provides UVB and heat; indoors or in heated shelters they require a basking spot in the upper 90s F, cooler ambient zones, and full-spectrum UVB lighting for healthy bone and shell development. Nighttime temperatures should stay mild, and supplemental heat is essential in cooler regions. Overall humidity should be low to moderate, matching their dry origins, though hatchlings benefit from slightly higher humidity and a humid hide to prevent shell pyramiding.

## Diet & feeding

These tortoises are grazing herbivores that thrive on a high-fiber, low-protein, low-fruit diet. Grasses and hay should form the bulk of their food, supplemented with edible weeds, dark leafy greens, and broad-leaf plants. Avoid sugary fruits, animal protein, and rich commercial foods, which can cause rapid, unhealthy growth and shell deformities. Offer occasional calcium supplementation, and always provide fresh water for drinking and soaking.

## Handling & health

Handle Sulcatas calmly and support their weight, but keep handling to a minimum as they prefer ground-level activity. Common health concerns include shell pyramiding from poor diet or low humidity, respiratory infections from cold damp conditions, and bladder stones. Wash your hands after contact, as reptiles can carry Salmonella. An exotics veterinarian familiar with tortoises should provide routine checkups and guide any treatment.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Sulcata Tortoises good pets for beginners?** They are friendly but not beginner-friendly because of their massive adult size, decades-long lifespan, and need for large outdoor space and specialist care.

**Are Sulcata Tortoises legal to keep?** Legality varies by region and some areas restrict ownership, so always check local laws before acquiring one.

## Conclusion

A Sulcata Tortoise can be a rewarding, long-lived companion for owners with the climate, space, and dedication to meet its needs. With a grass-based diet, secure outdoor housing, proper heat and UVB, and routine veterinary care, this gentle giant can thrive for many decades.
