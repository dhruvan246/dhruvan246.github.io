Don't let the size fool you — the Chihuahua is one of the boldest, most loyal dogs you can own. The world's smallest breed packs an enormous personality into a tiny frame. Here's how to care for one.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Tiny (1.5–3 kg) |
| Lifespan | 14–18 years |
| Coat | Smooth or long-haired |
| Energy | Moderate |
| Good with kids | Better with gentle, older children |

## Appearance & temperament

Chihuahuas have an apple- or deer-shaped head, big expressive eyes and large erect ears. They are fiercely devoted to their chosen person, alert, and surprisingly brave. They can be wary of strangers, so early socialisation is key.

## Diet & nutrition

- Feed a small-breed formula in tiny, measured portions.
- Their fast metabolism means small, frequent meals suit puppies.
- Watch for low blood sugar (hypoglycemia) in young pups.
- Avoid overfeeding — even a little excess weight is a lot on this frame.

## Training & socialisation

Chihuahuas are smart but can be stubborn and prone to "small dog syndrome" if spoiled. Use positive reinforcement, set gentle boundaries, and socialise them with people and other dogs early.

## Grooming & care

- **Smooth coat:** weekly brushing. **Long coat:** brush 2–3 times a week.
- They feel the cold — a sweater helps in winter.
- Brush teeth regularly; small breeds are prone to dental disease.

## Health & lifespan

Chihuahuas are long-lived but watch for dental problems, luxating patella (slipping kneecap), heart issues and a soft spot (molera) on the skull. Regular vet checks keep them thriving well into their teens.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Chihuahuas good apartment dogs?**
Excellent — their size and modest exercise needs suit small spaces.

**Do they bark a lot?**
They can be vocal watchdogs; training helps manage it.

## Conclusion

A Chihuahua is a pocket-sized companion with a giant heart. Give them warmth, gentle training and regular dental care, and you'll have a devoted friend for well over a decade.
