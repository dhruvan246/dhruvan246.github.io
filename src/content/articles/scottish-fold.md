Instantly recognisable by their folded ears and round, owl-like faces, Scottish Folds are sweet, placid cats that bond closely with their families. They're charming companions — though their signature ears come with important health considerations.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (3–6 kg) |
| Lifespan | 11–15 years |
| Coat | Short or long, dense |
| Energy | Moderate |
| Good with kids | Yes — gentle and affectionate |

## Appearance & temperament

The folded ears come from a natural cartilage gene. Folds are affectionate, calm and playful, often following their owners around and enjoying gentle company. (Some kittens are born "straight-eared.")

## Diet & nutrition

- Quality cat food in measured portions; watch weight to reduce joint strain.

## Grooming & care

- Brush weekly (more for longhaired Folds).
- **Check the ears regularly** — folded ears can need gentle, careful cleaning.

## Health & lifespan — important

The same gene that folds the ears affects cartilage throughout the body and can cause **osteochondrodysplasia**, a painful joint condition. Watch for stiffness or reluctance to jump, keep your cat lean, and work with a vet experienced in the breed. Responsible breeding matters greatly here.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Scottish Folds in pain?**
The cartilage gene can cause joint problems; keeping them lean and monitoring mobility is essential, with vet guidance.

**Do all Scottish Folds have folded ears?**
No — some are born straight-eared, and they make equally lovely pets.

## Conclusion

The Scottish Fold is a sweet, affectionate and distinctive companion. Stay attentive to joint health, keep them lean, and partner with a knowledgeable vet to give this gentle breed the best quality of life.
