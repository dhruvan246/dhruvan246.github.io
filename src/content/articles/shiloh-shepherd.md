The Shiloh Shepherd is a large, intelligent herding-type dog developed in the United States from German Shepherd lines, with the goal of producing a bigger, calmer, more sound-natured companion. Created by breeder Tina Barber in New York beginning in the 1970s and 1980s, the breed emphasizes good hips, an even temperament and an imposing yet gentle presence. Though not recognized by the major all-breed registries, it has a devoted following among families seeking a steady, devoted guardian.

## Appearance & temperament

The Shiloh Shepherd resembles an oversized, more substantial German Shepherd. Males generally stand around 28-30 inches at the shoulder and females slightly less, with a strong, well-proportioned frame and a level or only slightly sloping back. Two coat varieties exist: a plush coat with longer, fuller hair, and a smooth coat that lies closer to the body. Colors range through various shades of sable, black-and-tan, silver, gold and solid black.

Temperamentally, the breed is bred to be calm, confident and affectionate rather than sharp or high-strung. Shiloh Shepherds are typically devoted to their families, gentle with children and tolerant of other pets when socialized. They are watchful and make good companions, but the emphasis is on stability and companionship rather than intense protection drive.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced diet formulated for large-breed dogs, which helps support steady growth in puppies and joint health in adults. Because of the breed's size, controlled feeding and avoiding excess weight are especially important to reduce stress on hips and elbows. Divide daily food into two meals rather than one large meal, which may help lower the risk of bloat in deep-chested dogs. Always provide fresh water, and adjust portions to activity level and body condition. Consult your veterinarian on amounts and any supplements.

## Exercise & training

Shiloh Shepherds have moderate energy and benefit from daily walks plus play, training games or moderate hikes totaling roughly an hour or more. They are intelligent and eager to please, responding very well to positive, reward-based training. Early socialization and consistent, gentle leadership produce a confident, well-mannered adult. Mental work such as obedience, scent games or simple tasks keeps this thinking breed satisfied. Avoid hard, repetitive jumping in puppies whose joints are still developing.

## Grooming & care

The double coat sheds year-round and more heavily during seasonal coat changes. Brush at least once or twice weekly, and more often during shedding periods, to manage loose hair and keep the coat healthy. Plush coats need more frequent attention to prevent matting behind the ears and on the legs. Bathe only as needed. Routine care includes nail trimming, ear checks and regular tooth brushing to support overall health.

## Health & lifespan

With a typical lifespan of about 9-14 years, the Shiloh Shepherd was specifically bred to improve hip soundness, and responsible breeders screen for hip and elbow dysplasia. Other concerns common to large shepherd-type dogs include bloat (gastric dilatation-volvulus), degenerative myelopathy and certain digestive sensitivities. Buying from breeders who perform health testing, maintaining a healthy weight and scheduling regular veterinary checkups all support a long, comfortable life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Shiloh Shepherd the same as a German Shepherd?**
No. The Shiloh Shepherd was developed from German Shepherd stock but selectively bred to be larger, calmer and with improved hip health. It is treated as a separate breed by its own clubs, though it is not recognized by major all-breed registries.

**Are Shiloh Shepherds good family dogs?**
Yes. They are bred for a gentle, stable temperament and are typically patient with children and devoted to their families, making them well suited to homes that can provide space, exercise and companionship.

## Conclusion

The Shiloh Shepherd offers the loyalty and intelligence of a shepherd in a larger, calmer package. With consistent training, moderate daily exercise, routine grooming and attention to large-breed health, this devoted dog can be a steady and loving family companion for many years.
