Known as the "Grey Ghost" for its sleek silver coat and striking eyes, the Weimaraner is a powerful, energetic hunting dog. Devoted and athletic, this breed needs a very active owner who can match its drive.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Large (25–40 kg) |
| Lifespan | 10–13 years |
| Coat | Short, smooth, silver-grey |
| Energy | Very high |
| Good with kids | Yes, with supervision and exercise |

## Appearance & temperament

Weimaraners are elegant and muscular with a distinctive grey coat and amber or blue-grey eyes. They are intelligent, fearless and deeply attached to their family — and famously prone to separation anxiety if left alone too much.

## Exercise & training

- Need **extensive daily exercise** plus mental work — they're tireless athletes.
- Smart but strong-willed; early socialisation and consistent, positive training are essential.

## Grooming & care

- Very easy — short coat needs only occasional brushing.
- They feel the cold and appreciate a coat in winter.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for bloat (GDV) — feed smaller meals and avoid exercise right after eating — plus hip dysplasia and certain hereditary conditions. Choose health-tested lines.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Weimaraners good for first-time owners?**
Challenging — their energy, intelligence and need for company suit experienced, active owners.

**Do they like being alone?**
No — they're prone to separation anxiety and need lots of company and activity.

## Conclusion

The Weimaraner is a striking, athletic and devoted companion for a very active household. Provide ample exercise, training and togetherness, and the Grey Ghost will be a loyal, energetic friend.
