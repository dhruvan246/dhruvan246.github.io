Far more than a show dog, the Poodle is one of the most intelligent and capable breeds in the world. Athletic, elegant and eager to please, Standard Poodles excel at almost everything — and make devoted family companions.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium–large (20–32 kg, Standard) |
| Lifespan | 12–15 years |
| Coat | Curly, dense, low-shedding |
| Energy | High |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Poodles have a distinctive curly coat and a proud, athletic build. They are exceptionally smart, trainable and sensitive — affectionate with family and quick to learn almost any task or trick.

## Exercise & training

- Need plenty of daily exercise plus mental challenges — they're athletes and thinkers.
- Among the easiest breeds to train; they thrive on obedience, agility and games.

## Grooming — the main commitment

- The curly coat doesn't shed much but **mats easily** — brush several times a week and schedule professional grooming every 4–6 weeks.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for hip dysplasia, eye conditions (PRA), Addison's disease, bloat and epilepsy. Choose health-tested lines and keep regular vet care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Poodles hypoallergenic?**
No dog truly is, but their low-shedding coat is often tolerated better by allergy sufferers.

**Are Poodles smart?**
Exceptionally — frequently ranked among the most intelligent of all breeds.

## Conclusion

The Poodle is a brilliant, elegant and versatile companion for an active owner. Commit to regular grooming, give them exercise and mental work, and you'll have one of the most capable dogs around.
