The Cesky Fousek (also called the Bohemian Wirehaired Pointing Griffon) is a versatile hunting dog from the Czech Republic, prized for its ability to point, retrieve and track on land and in water. With its harsh, bearded coat and devoted nature, it is a hardworking, affectionate companion for active, outdoor-minded households.

## Appearance & temperament

The Cesky Fousek is a medium-to-large, athletic dog standing roughly 58–66 cm at the shoulder, with males generally larger than females. Its hallmark is a distinctive three-part coat: a soft, dense undercoat for insulation, a harsh outer coat that repels water and brush, and longer, coarse hair forming the breed's characteristic beard, eyebrows and moustache. Coats are typically brown and white, brown roan or solid brown, often ticked.

Bred as an all-purpose gundog, the Cesky Fousek is intelligent, trainable and deeply loyal to its family. The breed is known for a calm, biddable disposition combined with strong hunting drive. It tends to be affectionate at home and can be reserved with strangers, but it is rarely aggressive when well socialized.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced diet suited to an active large-breed dog, split into two meals a day rather than one large portion. Adjust the amount to your dog's age, weight and activity level, as a working Cesky Fousek burns far more calories than a less active one. As a deep-chested breed, it may be at higher risk of bloat, so avoid vigorous activity immediately before and after eating. Provide constant access to fresh water and consult your veterinarian before making dietary changes.

## Exercise & training

This is a high-energy working breed that needs substantial daily exercise, well over an hour of running, swimming, retrieving or field activity. Casual neighborhood walks alone will not satisfy it. The Cesky Fousek thrives on having a job and excels at hunting, tracking, agility and other dog sports that engage both body and mind.

Highly intelligent and eager to please, the breed responds best to consistent, patient, reward-based training started in puppyhood. Early socialization helps temper natural wariness of strangers. Without enough mental and physical outlets, a bored Cesky Fousek may become restless or develop unwanted habits.

## Grooming & care

The wiry coat is relatively low-maintenance but benefits from weekly brushing to remove loose hair and debris. Periodic hand-stripping helps preserve the harsh texture rather than clipping, which can soften the coat. Wipe and dry the beard after meals and water work, and check the drop ears regularly to help prevent infections. Routine nail trimming and regular dental care complete a straightforward grooming routine.

## Health & lifespan

The Cesky Fousek is generally a hardy, robust breed with a typical lifespan of around 12–14 years. Like many large, deep-chested dogs, it can be susceptible to hip dysplasia, gastric bloat (gastric dilatation-volvulus), and ear infections owing to its drop ears. Choosing a responsible breeder who health-tests their breeding stock, keeping your dog at a healthy weight and maintaining regular veterinary checkups all support a long, active life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Cesky Fousek a good family dog?**
Yes, for active families able to meet its high exercise needs. It is loyal, gentle and affectionate at home, though it may be reserved with strangers.

**Does the Cesky Fousek shed a lot?**
It sheds moderately. The wiry coat needs weekly brushing and occasional hand-stripping rather than intensive grooming.

## Conclusion

The Cesky Fousek is a versatile, intelligent and devoted gundog built for work in the field and water. Give this breed plenty of exercise, early training and close companionship, and it will reward you with a capable, loyal partner for many years.
