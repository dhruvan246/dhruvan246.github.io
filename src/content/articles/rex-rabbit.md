The Rex Rabbit is prized for its uniquely soft, velvety coat and calm, friendly nature, making it one of the most popular pet rabbit breeds for families and first-time owners alike.

## Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|---|---|
| Size | Standard Rex 3.5-4.5 kg; Mini Rex about 1.4-2 kg |
| Lifespan | Around 5-8 years with good care |
| Housing | Large pen or enclosure with secure run space |
| Temperament | Docile, calm, sociable, often gentle |
| Care level | Beginner-friendly with daily commitment |

## Appearance & temperament

The Rex's signature feature is its dense, plush coat. A genetic trait makes the guard hairs the same length as the undercoat, producing a smooth, velvet-like feel unlike any other breed. They come in many colors, including black, blue, chinchilla, lilac, and broken (spotted) patterns. The Mini Rex is a smaller, equally popular version.

Rex Rabbits are known for being calm, intelligent, and people-oriented. Many tolerate handling well and can form strong bonds with their owners. They are generally placid, though, like all rabbits, individual personalities vary, and they appreciate gentle, patient interaction.

## Housing & environment

Provide the largest enclosure you can. Rabbits need room to hop, stretch up, and lie out fully, plus daily access to a safe exercise area. A solid floor is important, as Rex Rabbits have thinner fur on their feet and are prone to sore hocks on wire or hard surfaces; offer soft bedding and resting mats.

Keep rabbits indoors or in a sheltered, predator-proof space away from extreme heat and cold. Line the enclosure with paper-based or hay bedding, provide a litter tray, and clean regularly. Rabbits are social and often happiest kept in compatible, neutered pairs.

## Diet & nutrition

The foundation of a healthy diet is unlimited fresh grass hay, such as timothy or meadow hay, which supports digestion and keeps their continuously growing teeth worn down. Hay should make up the bulk of what they eat every day.

Supplement with a daily portion of fresh leafy greens and herbs, and a small measured amount of quality pellets. Avoid sugary treats, and never feed iceberg lettuce or large quantities of fruit. Always provide clean, fresh water. Sudden diet changes can cause digestive upset, so introduce new foods gradually.

## Handling & enrichment

Support a rabbit's body fully when lifting, with one hand under the chest and the other supporting the hindquarters; never pick them up by the ears or scruff. Many Rex Rabbits enjoy being stroked once they trust you, but most prefer interaction at ground level.

Enrichment keeps them mentally and physically healthy. Offer tunnels, cardboard boxes, chew toys, and safe items to dig and forage with. Daily out-of-enclosure time for exercise is essential.

## Health & lifespan

With proper care, Rex Rabbits typically live around 5-8 years. Watch for common rabbit health issues, including overgrown teeth, sore hocks, digestive slowdown (GI stasis), and parasites. Their fine fur means they need monitoring during molts. Spaying or neutering improves behavior and reduces certain health risks.

Find a vet experienced with rabbits, and arrange vaccinations where recommended. Loss of appetite or no droppings is an emergency requiring prompt veterinary care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Rex Rabbits good for beginners?** Yes. Their calm, friendly temperament and straightforward care make them a popular choice, though they still need daily attention and a long-term commitment.

**Does the Rex coat need special grooming?** Their short, dense fur requires only light, occasional grooming. Avoid over-brushing, which can damage the delicate velvet coat, and brush more during molts.

## Conclusion

The Rex Rabbit combines a remarkably soft coat with a gentle, affectionate personality. Give it spacious housing, a hay-based diet, soft flooring, daily enrichment, and rabbit-savvy veterinary care, and you will have a rewarding companion for years.
