The Ringneck Dove (often called the ring-necked or barbary dove) is one of the most popular and easygoing pet doves. Domesticated for centuries, it is calm, quiet, and forgiving, making it an excellent choice for beginners and families who want a gentle feathered companion.

## Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|---|---|
| Size | Roughly 10-12 inches (25-30 cm) long |
| Lifespan | Commonly 10-15 years, sometimes longer with good care |
| Noise level | Low to moderate; soft cooing |
| Talking ability | None; does not mimic speech |
| Care level | Easy; great for beginners |

## Appearance & temperament

Ringneck Doves are slender, medium-sized birds with smooth plumage and a distinctive thin black band, or "ring," across the back of the neck. The wild type is a soft fawn or tan color, but selective breeding has produced many color varieties, including white, pied, and orange shades. Both sexes look very similar.

Temperamentally, these doves are famously gentle and rarely bite. They tend to be placid rather than playful, content to perch, coo, and observe. With patient, regular contact they often become tame and will sit calmly on a hand or shoulder. They are social and usually do best with a companion dove, though a single bird kept with daily human attention can also thrive.

## Housing & cage setup

Provide the largest cage you reasonably can, with horizontal space prioritized over height since doves fly side to side rather than climb. A cage at least 24-30 inches wide is a sensible minimum for one bird, and bar spacing should be narrow enough to prevent escape or injury.

Include sturdy perches of varying diameters, placed so the bird can move easily without tail feathers dragging. Doves appreciate a flat platform or low perch for resting. Line the tray with paper or another safe substrate, and clean it regularly. Place the cage in a draft-free spot away from kitchen fumes, and allow supervised out-of-cage flight time in a bird-safe room.

## Diet & nutrition

A quality dove or small-bird seed mix forms the base of the diet, ideally supplemented with formulated pellets to improve balance. Offer small amounts of fresh greens and vegetables such as leafy greens, chopped carrot, and peas, introducing new foods gradually.

Doves need grit far less than once believed, but a cuttlebone or other calcium source supports healthy eggshells and bones, especially in breeding females. Always provide clean, fresh water for both drinking and the occasional bath. Avoid avocado, chocolate, caffeine, and salty or processed foods, which are toxic or harmful to birds.

## Enrichment & handling

Though calm, Ringneck Doves still benefit from enrichment. Offer safe foraging opportunities, a shallow dish for bathing, and gentle daily interaction. Talk softly and move slowly; sudden movements can startle them. Tame doves enjoy being near their owners and may coo in response to your voice. Handle them by allowing the bird to step up rather than grabbing, and support the body gently.

## Health & lifespan

Ringneck Doves are hardy when well cared for. Watch for signs of illness such as fluffed feathers, lethargy, labored breathing, discharge, or changes in droppings or appetite, and consult an avian veterinarian promptly if these appear. Common concerns include respiratory issues, parasites, and egg-binding in females that lay frequently. A balanced diet, clean housing, and stable temperatures prevent most problems. Legal ownership and availability vary by region, so check local rules and seek specialist avian care when needed.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Ringneck Doves need a companion?** They are social and often happier in pairs, but a single dove given regular daily attention can be content and bond closely with its owner.

**Are Ringneck Doves loud?** No. Their soft, rhythmic cooing is one of their main appeals and is far quieter than parrots, making them suitable for apartments.

## Conclusion

The Ringneck Dove is a peaceful, low-maintenance bird whose gentle nature and soothing calls suit first-time keepers and busy households alike. With a spacious cage, a balanced diet, fresh water, and patient daily interaction, this hardy dove can be a calm and affectionate companion for many years.
