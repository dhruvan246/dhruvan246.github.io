Bright, athletic and eager to please, the Brittany is a versatile gundog that also makes a wonderful active family companion. Compact, leggy and seemingly tireless, this French-bred pointing dog loves nothing more than vigorous exercise and close time with its people.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (around 13–18 kg) |
| Lifespan | About 12–14 years |
| Coat | Dense, flat or slightly wavy |
| Energy | Very high |
| Good with kids | Yes, with enough exercise |

## Appearance & temperament

The Brittany is an agile, square-built dog standing roughly knee-high, with long legs that give it a light, springy gait. The coat is most often orange-and-white or liver-and-white, sometimes ticked or roan. Temperamentally, Brittanys are happy, affectionate and notably sensitive. They bond closely with their families, tend to be friendly with strangers and other dogs, and thrive on activity and companionship rather than being left alone for long stretches.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a good-quality complete dog food appropriate to the Brittany's life stage and high activity level. Portion meals to keep the dog lean and fit; a working or very active Brittany may need more calories than a less busy companion. Divide food into two meals a day, provide constant access to fresh water, and keep treats modest so they do not unbalance the diet. Monitor body condition over time and adjust amounts as activity changes.

## Exercise & training

Brittanys need plenty of vigorous daily exercise — long walks, running, fetch, hiking and dog sports such as agility or scent work all suit them well. Without enough physical and mental outlet, they can become restless, vocal or anxious. Happily, they are highly trainable and eager to please, with a strong instinct to work alongside people. Because they are sensitive, they respond best to consistent, positive, reward-based methods; harsh corrections tend to backfire. Early socialisation helps them grow into confident, well-mannered adults.

## Grooming & care

Grooming is fairly low-maintenance. A weekly brush keeps the coat tidy and removes loose hair, with a little extra attention during seasonal shedding. Check and clean the ears regularly, as drop ears can trap moisture and lead to infections. Trim nails as needed, brush teeth often for good dental health, and bathe only when the dog is genuinely dirty.

## Health & lifespan

The Brittany is generally a healthy, athletic breed with a typical lifespan of around 12 to 14 years. As with many active dogs, hip dysplasia can occur, and the breed's pendant ears make ear infections a common concern. Epilepsy is seen in some lines, and eye conditions are worth watching for. Choosing a responsible breeder who health-tests their stock, keeping up routine veterinary checks, and maintaining a healthy weight all support a long, sound life. Always consult your own vet for any specific health concerns.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Brittanys good family dogs?**
Yes — for active families who can meet their considerable exercise needs. They are affectionate, gentle and generally good with children when properly socialised and well exercised.

**Are they easy to train?**
Very. Brittanys are intelligent, eager and willing workers, but they are sensitive, so kind, consistent and positive training gets the best results.

## Conclusion

The Brittany is a joyful, energetic and affectionate companion best suited to an active household with time to spare. Give this capable gundog ample daily exercise, gentle and consistent training, and plenty of togetherness, and you will have a devoted, good-natured friend for years to come.
