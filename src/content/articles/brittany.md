Bright, athletic and eager to please, the Brittany is a versatile gundog that doubles as a wonderful active family companion. Compact and tireless, they love nothing more than exercise and time with their people.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (13–18 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–14 years |
| Coat | Dense, flat or wavy |
| Energy | Very high |
| Good with kids | Yes, with enough exercise |

## Appearance & temperament

Brittanys are agile, leggy dogs with an orange-and-white or liver-and-white coat. They are happy, affectionate and sensitive, bonding closely with their family and thriving on activity and companionship.

## Exercise & training

- Need **plenty of vigorous daily exercise** — running, fetch, hiking, dog sports.
- Highly trainable and eager to please; respond best to gentle, positive methods (they're sensitive to harshness).
- Without enough activity, they become restless and anxious.

## Diet & nutrition

- A quality, active-dog diet portioned to their energy.
- Keep them lean and fit.

## Grooming

- Brush weekly; the coat is fairly low-maintenance.
- Check ears (they can trap moisture), trim nails, maintain dental care.

## Health & lifespan

Generally healthy and athletic. Watch for hip dysplasia, ear infections, and epilepsy in some lines. Regular vet checks and an active lifestyle keep them in top form.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Brittanys good family dogs?**
Yes — for active families who can meet their exercise needs.

**Are they easy to train?**
Very — eager, smart and willing, but sensitive, so keep training kind.

## Conclusion

The Brittany is a joyful, energetic and affectionate companion for an active household. Give them ample exercise and gentle training, and they'll be a devoted and capable friend.
