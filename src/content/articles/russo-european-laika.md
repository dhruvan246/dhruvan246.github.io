The Russo-European Laika is a medium-sized spitz-type hunting dog developed in northern Russia and Scandinavia. Bred to track and bay game in cold forests, it is energetic, courageous, and devoted to its family while remaining wary of strangers.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Size | Medium (roughly 21-23 in / 17-23 kg) |
| Lifespan | About 10-14 years |
| Coat | Dense double coat, typically black and white |
| Energy | High |
| Good with kids | Yes, with supervision and early socialization |

## Appearance & temperament

The Russo-European Laika is a compact, square-built dog with the classic spitz silhouette: pricked triangular ears, a curled tail carried over the back, and an alert, fox-like expression. The coat is almost always black and white, with a harsh outer layer and a soft insulating undercoat suited to harsh winters.

Temperamentally, this is a bold, independent, and highly driven dog. It bonds strongly with its owner and can be affectionate at home, but it retains a strong prey drive and a vocal, watchful nature. The breed is intelligent and quick to learn, yet may be reserved or aloof toward unfamiliar people and animals.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, high-quality dog food appropriate for an active medium breed. Because the Russo-European Laika is a working dog with a fast metabolism, calorie needs rise during heavy exercise or cold weather and should be adjusted accordingly. Divide the daily amount into two meals to support steady energy.

Monitor body condition closely, as a lean, muscular build is healthiest for this athletic breed. Provide constant access to fresh water, and consult your veterinarian before changing diets or adding supplements.

## Exercise & training

This is a high-energy breed that needs substantial daily activity. Long walks, runs, hikes, scent games, and off-leash exploration in secure areas help burn physical and mental energy. Without enough outlet, the breed can become restless, vocal, or destructive.

Training should be consistent, patient, and reward-based. The Russo-European Laika is intelligent but independent-minded, so early socialization and firm, fair guidance are essential. A reliable recall is important, since the strong prey drive can pull the dog toward wildlife.

## Grooming & care

The dense double coat is relatively low-maintenance day to day. Weekly brushing keeps it clean and reduces loose hair, but expect heavy seasonal shedding in spring and autumn, when more frequent brushing helps manage the blowing undercoat. Bathe only as needed to preserve natural coat oils.

Check and trim nails regularly, brush the teeth several times a week, and inspect the ears for debris or moisture, especially after time outdoors.

## Health & lifespan

The Russo-European Laika is generally a hardy, robust breed with few widespread inherited problems, owing to its working background. As with many active dogs, owners should watch for joint issues such as hip dysplasia and keep an eye on ear and dental health.

Routine veterinary care, vaccinations, parasite prevention, and maintaining a healthy weight support a long life. Typical lifespan ranges from about 10 to 14 years.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Russo-European Laika a good apartment dog?**
Generally no. This breed's high energy, working drive, and tendency to bark make it better suited to active homes with space and regular access to the outdoors.

**Does the Russo-European Laika get along with other pets?**
It can, but the strong prey drive means small animals may trigger chasing. Early socialization and careful introductions improve compatibility with other dogs.

## Conclusion

The Russo-European Laika is a tough, loyal, and energetic hunting companion best matched with active owners who can meet its exercise and training needs. With proper socialization and care, it makes a devoted and capable partner for an outdoor lifestyle.
