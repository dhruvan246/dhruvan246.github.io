The Brazilian Terrier, known in its homeland as the Fox Paulistinha, is one of the few breeds native to Brazil. Compact, alert and bursting with energy, this tricolor terrier was developed as a farm and household ratter and remains a spirited, affectionate companion that thrives on activity and family life.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |

## Appearance & temperament

The Brazilian Terrier is a small, square-built dog with a lean, athletic frame, standing around 14–16 inches at the shoulder. Its short, sleek coat is always tricolor, featuring a base of white marked with black, brown, or blue patches and tan points over the eyes, muzzle, and legs. The breed has a wedge-shaped head, semi-erect "button" ears that fold forward, and an attentive, intelligent expression.

In temperament, this is a classic terrier: bold, curious, and tireless. Brazilian Terriers are deeply attached to their families and tend to be playful and affectionate at home. They are naturally alert and make keen watchdogs, often quick to bark at anything unusual. Like most terriers, they have a strong prey drive and an independent streak.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food appropriate for a small to medium, high-energy breed. Because this is an active dog, portion size should match its daily exercise level to avoid excess weight, which strains the joints. Most adults do well on two measured meals per day, with puppies fed more frequently. Use treats sparingly, especially during training, and always provide fresh water. If you have questions about your individual dog's needs, consult your veterinarian.

## Exercise & training

Brazilian Terriers are energetic and need substantial daily activity, including brisk walks, play sessions, and chances to run in a secure area. Their agility and drive make them well-suited to dog sports such as agility, flyball, and earthdog trials. Without enough physical and mental stimulation, they can become bored and develop nuisance behaviors like digging or excessive barking.

Training should be consistent, positive, and engaging. These dogs are intelligent and learn quickly but can be willful, so reward-based methods work far better than harsh corrections. Early, ongoing socialization helps temper their suspicion of strangers and their chase instinct toward small animals.

## Grooming & care

Grooming needs are minimal thanks to the short, single coat. A weekly brushing removes loose hair and keeps the coat shiny, with only occasional baths needed. Check and clean the ears regularly, trim the nails as they grow, and maintain a routine of dental care. Because the coat is thin, these dogs feel the cold and may need a sweater in chilly weather.

## Health & lifespan

The Brazilian Terrier is generally a hardy, robust breed with a typical lifespan of about 12 to 14 years. As with many breeds, potential concerns can include patellar (kneecap) luxation, eye conditions, and skin sensitivities. Choosing a responsible breeder, keeping your dog at a healthy weight, and scheduling routine veterinary check-ups all support a long, healthy life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Brazilian Terriers good apartment dogs?**
They can adapt to apartments if given plenty of daily exercise and mental stimulation, but their energy and tendency to bark mean they do best with active owners and consistent training.

**Do Brazilian Terriers get along with other pets?**
They usually enjoy other dogs, but their strong prey drive means small pets like cats or rodents require careful introductions and supervision, ideally with early socialization.

## Conclusion

The Brazilian Terrier is a lively, loyal, and intelligent companion that rewards an active household with affection and fun. With regular exercise, positive training, and basic care, this hardy Brazilian native makes a devoted and entertaining family dog.
