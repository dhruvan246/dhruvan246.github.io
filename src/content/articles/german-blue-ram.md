The German Blue Ram (*Mikrogeophagus ramirezi*) is a small, brilliantly coloured dwarf cichlid prized by aquarists for its bold personality and stunning patterns. Native to the warm, slow-moving waters of the Orinoco basin in Venezuela and Colombia, it is a peaceful fish that rewards attentive care with vivid colour and interesting behaviour.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Adult size | Around 5-7 cm (2-3 inches) |
| Lifespan | Typically 2-4 years |
| Minimum tank size | About 75-80 litres (20 gallons) |
| Water type | Warm, soft, slightly acidic freshwater |
| Care level | Intermediate |

## Appearance & behaviour

German Blue Rams are compact, deep-bodied cichlids with a base of iridescent blue and yellow, often marked by a dark vertical band through the eye and a black blotch on the flank. Many show red bellies and electric-blue speckling that intensifies in healthy, settled fish. Males tend to be larger with more elongated dorsal fin rays, while females often show a pinkish belly.

Despite being cichlids, rams are generally peaceful and rather shy. They spend much of their time near the lower and middle levels, picking at substrate and exploring territory. Pairs may form strong bonds and display courtship behaviour, defending a small patch when breeding.

## Tank setup & water parameters

Rams thrive in mature, stable aquariums with soft substrate, plenty of live plants, driftwood, and rocks to create hiding spots and gentle territory boundaries. Subdued lighting and a calm environment help them feel secure.

Aim for warm water of roughly 27-30 C (80-86 F), a pH around 5.5-7.0, and soft to moderately soft water. These fish are sensitive to poor water quality and sudden changes, so gentle filtration, low flow, and regular partial water changes are essential. They should only be added to a fully cycled, established tank.

## Diet & feeding

German Blue Rams are omnivores that benefit from a varied diet. Offer high-quality sinking pellets or flakes designed for cichlids, supplemented regularly with frozen or live foods such as bloodworms, daphnia, brine shrimp, and cyclops. Feed small portions once or twice a day, only what they finish in a couple of minutes, to keep water clean and fish healthy.

## Tank mates & compatibility

Their peaceful nature makes rams compatible with many calm community fish. Good companions include small, gentle species such as tetras, rasboras, corydoras catfish, and peaceful dwarf shrimp in well-planted tanks. Avoid aggressive, fin-nipping, or boisterous tank mates, and keep water-temperature needs in mind, since rams require warmer water than many community fish tolerate.

## Health & common issues

Rams are sensitive fish, and most health problems trace back to water quality, stress, or temperatures that are too low. Common issues include bacterial infections, fin problems, and parasites, often appearing as faded colour, clamped fins, or loss of appetite. Stable warm water, a cycled tank, and consistent maintenance are the best prevention. If a fish appears ill, quarantine when possible and consult an aquatic veterinarian or knowledgeable specialist rather than guessing at treatments.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are German Blue Rams good for beginners?** They are best suited to aquarists with some experience, because they need stable warm water and a mature, well-maintained tank to stay healthy.

**Do German Blue Rams need to be kept in pairs?** They can be kept singly or as a bonded pair. A compatible pair often shows the most natural behaviour, but a single ram also does well in a peaceful community tank.

## Conclusion

The German Blue Ram is a rewarding centrepiece for a calm, well-planted community aquarium. With warm, soft, clean water, a varied diet, and gentle tank mates, this colourful dwarf cichlid can display its full beauty and charm. Availability and any regional rules can vary, so source fish responsibly and provide the specialist care they deserve.
