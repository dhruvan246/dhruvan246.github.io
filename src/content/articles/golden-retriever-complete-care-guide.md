The Golden Retriever is one of the most beloved dog breeds in the world — and for good reason. Friendly, intelligent and endlessly patient, Goldens make wonderful family companions. In this guide we'll cover everything you need to raise a happy, healthy Golden Retriever.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium–large (25–34 kg) |
| Lifespan | 10–12 years |
| Coat | Dense, water-repellent double coat |
| Energy | High — needs daily exercise |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Golden Retrievers are sturdy, well-proportioned dogs with a signature golden coat that ranges from light cream to rich gold. They're famous for their gentle mouths (originally bred to retrieve game without damaging it) and their eager-to-please nature.

Temperament-wise, they are affectionate, social and highly trainable. They thrive on human company and can become anxious if left alone for long periods.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a high-quality diet appropriate for their age and activity level:

- **Puppies:** 3–4 small meals a day of large-breed puppy food.
- **Adults:** 2 measured meals a day.
- Watch portions carefully — Goldens love food and are prone to obesity.
- Avoid toxic foods: chocolate, grapes, onions, xylitol.

## Care & grooming

That beautiful coat needs work:

- Brush 2–3 times per week (daily during shedding season).
- Bathe every 6–8 weeks or when dirty.
- Check and clean ears regularly — floppy ears trap moisture.
- Trim nails every few weeks.

## Exercise

Goldens are active dogs that need **at least 1–2 hours** of exercise daily — walks, fetch, swimming and training games all work well. A bored Golden is a destructive Golden.

## Health & lifespan

Common health concerns include hip and elbow dysplasia, certain cancers, and heart conditions. Buy from reputable breeders who screen for these, schedule regular vet check-ups, and keep your dog at a healthy weight.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Golden Retrievers good for first-time owners?**
Yes — their trainability and gentle nature make them one of the best beginner breeds.

**Do they shed a lot?**
Yes, significantly. Regular brushing is essential.

## Conclusion

With proper exercise, nutrition and lots of love, a Golden Retriever will reward you with years of loyal, joyful companionship. They truly are one of the best family dogs you can welcome into your home.
