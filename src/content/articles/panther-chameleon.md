The Panther Chameleon (*Furcifer pardalis*) is a striking, color-changing lizard native to the warm, humid forests of northern and eastern Madagascar. Famous for the brilliant blues, reds, greens, and oranges of mature males, it is one of the most popular pet chameleons. It is also a hands-off, intermediate-level reptile that rewards careful husbandry rather than frequent interaction.

## Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|---|---|
| Adult size | Males 14-20 in (35-50 cm); females smaller, 9-14 in (23-35 cm) |
| Lifespan | Males 5-7 years; females typically 3-5 years |
| Enclosure size | 24 x 24 x 48 in (60 x 60 x 120 cm) tall screen, minimum for one adult |
| Temperament | Solitary, territorial, easily stressed; best kept singly |
| Care level | Intermediate |

## Appearance & temperament

Panther Chameleons have laterally flattened bodies, a prehensile tail, fused "mitten" feet for gripping branches, and independently rotating turret eyes. Color varies by locale (often named for regions like Ambilobe, Nosy Be, or Sambava). Males are larger and far more vibrant; females stay green, peach, or tan and flash darker patterns when gravid or unreceptive. Color change reflects mood, temperature, and health more than camouflage. These are solitary animals that should never be housed together, as cohabitation causes chronic stress and aggression.

## Habitat & enclosure

Use a tall, well-ventilated screen enclosure rather than glass, which traps stagnant air. Furnish it densely with horizontal branches and vines at varying heights, plus live or sturdy artificial plants (pothos, ficus, and schefflera are popular) for cover and climbing. Vertical space matters more than floor space because chameleons are arboreal. Skip loose substrate, which can cause impaction if swallowed; bare bottoms or paper towel are easiest to keep clean and hygienic.

## Heating, lighting & humidity

Provide a basking spot of 82-90 F (28-32 C) at the top, with ambient temperatures in the high 70s F (24-27 C) and a several-degree night drop. A linear UVB tube (often labeled 5.0/T5) mounted over the screen is essential for vitamin D3 synthesis and calcium use; replace bulbs every 6-12 months as output fades. Aim for 50-70% humidity using regular misting sessions and/or an automated dripper or fogger. A live-planted setup helps hold humidity naturally. Ensure good airflow to prevent respiratory infection.

## Diet & feeding

Panther Chameleons are insectivores. Offer a varied diet of crickets, dubia roaches, black soldier fly larvae, silkworms, and the occasional hornworm or waxworm as a treat. Gut-load feeder insects with nutritious vegetables 24 hours before feeding, then lightly dust them with calcium. Use a plain calcium (no D3) at most feedings, a calcium-with-D3 supplement sparingly, and a reptile multivitamin every couple of weeks. Feed juveniles daily and adults every other day. Chameleons often will not drink from a still bowl, so they rely on droplets from misting or a dripper for hydration.

## Handling & health

This is a display animal, not a cuddly one. Frequent handling stresses chameleons, so keep it brief and let the animal walk onto your hand rather than grabbing it. Watch for warning signs: sunken eyes (dehydration), persistent dark coloration, closed or swollen eyes, swollen limbs or a rubbery jaw (metabolic bone disease from poor calcium or UVB), and labored breathing. Most health problems trace back to husbandry errors in lighting, humidity, or supplementation. See a reptile-experienced veterinarian for any persistent issue.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can two Panther Chameleons live together?** No. They are solitary and territorial. Housing them together, even a male and female long-term, leads to stress, fighting, and suppressed feeding. Keep one chameleon per enclosure.

**Why is my chameleon turning dark?** Brief darkening can mean it is warming up, but persistent dark or dull coloring often signals stress, cold temperatures, illness, or dehydration. Review temperatures, lighting, and hydration if it lasts.

## Conclusion

A Panther Chameleon is a rewarding pet for keepers willing to dial in temperature, UVB, humidity, and a varied insect diet. Give it a tall, planted screen enclosure, respect its solitary nature, and minimize handling. Because exotic reptile ownership rules differ by country, state, and city, confirm that keeping one is legal in your area before bringing one home.
