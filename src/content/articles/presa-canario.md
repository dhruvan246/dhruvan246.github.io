The Presa Canario, also known as the Dogo Canario or Canary Mastiff, is a large, powerful working breed originally developed in Spain's Canary Islands for guarding livestock and farm property. Calm yet imposing, it is a serious guardian best suited to experienced owners.

## Overview

| Trait | Details |
|-------|---------|
| Size | Large (88-130+ lbs) |
| Lifespan | 9-11 years |
| Coat | Short, coarse, single |
| Energy | Moderate |
| Good with kids | With supervision and early socialization |

## Appearance & temperament

The Presa Canario is a thickly built, muscular dog with a broad, square head, strong jaws and a deep chest. Males typically stand about 24-26 inches at the shoulder and females slightly less. The short coat comes in shades of fawn (often called verdino or brindle) and black, frequently with a black mask.

Temperamentally, the breed is calm, confident and deeply devoted to its family. It is naturally suspicious of strangers and territorial, traits that make it an excellent guardian but also demand responsible ownership. A well-raised Presa is steady and reserved rather than nervous or hyperactive. Without early, consistent socialization, the breed's protective instincts can become problematic, so exposure to people, places and other animals from puppyhood is essential.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a high-quality diet formulated for large or giant breeds, with controlled calcium and balanced protein to support steady growth in puppies and lean muscle in adults. Because the Presa Canario is prone to bloat, divide daily food into two or three meals rather than one large serving, and avoid heavy exercise immediately before or after eating.

Monitor body condition closely, as excess weight stresses joints and the heart. You should be able to feel the ribs without a thick fat layer. Always provide fresh water, and consult your veterinarian about portion sizes appropriate to your dog's age, weight and activity level.

## Exercise & training

Despite its size, the Presa Canario has moderate exercise needs. Two daily walks plus opportunities for play or structured activity in a secure yard usually suffice. Avoid over-exercising puppies, whose joints are still developing.

Training is where this breed truly demands commitment. The Presa is intelligent and willing but strong-willed, and its power means obedience is not optional. Use firm, fair, reward-based methods and establish clear leadership from day one. Early socialization and puppy classes are strongly recommended. This is not a breed for first-time dog owners, and secure fencing is a must.

## Grooming & care

Grooming requirements are minimal. The short, coarse coat needs only weekly brushing to remove loose hair and distribute skin oils, with more frequent brushing during seasonal shedding. Bathe occasionally as needed.

Check and clean the ears regularly, trim nails every few weeks, and brush the teeth several times a week. Because of the breed's loose lips, wiping the muzzle helps keep the face clean. Routine attention to these basics keeps a Presa Canario comfortable and healthy.

## Health & lifespan

The Presa Canario typically lives 9 to 11 years. As a large breed, it is predisposed to hip and elbow dysplasia, so buying from a breeder who screens breeding stock is wise. Other concerns include bloat (gastric dilatation-volvulus), certain heart conditions and, in some lines, epilepsy.

Keeping your dog at a healthy weight, providing regular veterinary checkups and staying current on vaccinations and parasite prevention all support a long, comfortable life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Presa Canario a good family dog?**
It can be, in the right home. With early socialization and consistent training, a Presa is loyal and protective of its family, but its size and guarding instincts make experienced, attentive ownership essential.

**How much exercise does a Presa Canario need?**
Moderate amounts. Two daily walks plus play in a secure area generally meet its needs, while avoiding strenuous activity in young puppies.

## Conclusion

The Presa Canario is a magnificent, loyal guardian that rewards dedicated owners with steadfast companionship. With proper socialization, firm training, good nutrition and routine care, this powerful breed can be a calm and trustworthy member of an experienced household.
