The Smooth Collie is the short-coated variety of the Collie, sharing the same lean, graceful build and keen herding heritage as its more famous Rough-coated cousin. Intelligent, sensitive, and devoted to its family, this Scottish farm dog combines a gentle temperament with an alert, watchful nature that makes it both a loving companion and a capable working partner.

## Appearance & temperament

The Smooth Collie is identical to the Rough Collie in every way except its coat, which is short, flat, and dense rather than long and flowing. It has the breed's distinctive wedge-shaped head, expressive almond eyes, and semi-erect ears that tip forward at the tips. Common colors include sable and white, tricolor, blue merle, and white. The body is athletic and slightly longer than tall, conveying speed and stamina.

Temperamentally, Smooth Collies are friendly, loyal, and remarkably sensitive to their owners' moods. They bond closely with family and are typically gentle and patient with children. Many retain a natural herding instinct and a tendency to bark, especially when alerting to visitors or unusual activity. They are generally sociable with other dogs and pets when well socialized.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a high-quality complete dog food appropriate for the Smooth Collie's age, size, and activity level. Most adults do well on two measured meals per day. Because the breed is athletic but can gain weight if underexercised, monitor body condition and avoid overfeeding treats. Always provide fresh water. Some Collies carry the MDR1 gene mutation, which affects sensitivity to certain medications, so discuss any drug or supplement choices with your veterinarian.

## Exercise & training

This is an active, intelligent breed that needs daily physical exercise and mental stimulation. Aim for at least an hour of activity through walks, play, and off-lead running in a safe area. Smooth Collies excel at canine sports such as herding, agility, obedience, and rally, which channel their energy and intellect productively.

Training is usually straightforward, as the breed is quick to learn and eager to please. They respond best to positive, reward-based methods and can become anxious with harsh handling. Early socialization helps curb excessive barking and shyness.

## Grooming & care

A major advantage of the Smooth Collie is its low-maintenance coat. Weekly brushing keeps the short double coat healthy and removes loose hair, with heavier shedding during seasonal coat changes when more frequent brushing helps. Bathe only as needed. Routine care includes regular nail trimming, ear checks, and dental hygiene. Despite the easier coat, this breed still sheds noticeably.

## Health & lifespan

Smooth Collies are generally healthy and typically live 12–14 years. Responsible breeders screen for conditions known in the breed, including Collie eye anomaly, progressive retinal atrophy, and hip dysplasia. The MDR1 (multidrug resistance) gene mutation is also relevant, as it can cause adverse reactions to some common medications. Routine veterinary checkups, eye testing, and genetic screening from breeding stock support a long, healthy life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Smooth Collie a different breed from the Rough Collie?**
No. They are two coat varieties of the same Collie breed and are identical apart from coat length. In some registries they are shown separately, while others treat them as one breed.

**Do Smooth Collies shed a lot?**
Yes. Although the short coat is easy to brush, Smooth Collies are double-coated and shed steadily year-round, with heavier seasonal shedding when weekly brushing should increase.

## Conclusion

The Smooth Collie offers all the intelligence, devotion, and gentle charm of the classic Collie in an easier-care, short-coated package. With daily exercise, consistent positive training, and routine health care, this versatile herding dog makes a loyal and affectionate family companion well suited to active homes.
