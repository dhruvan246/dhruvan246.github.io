The national dog of Cuba, the Havanese is a charming, affectionate companion with a silky coat and an irrepressibly happy nature. Sociable and adaptable, they thrive as devoted family lap dogs.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Small (3–6 kg) |
| Lifespan | 14–16 years |
| Coat | Long, silky, low-shedding |
| Energy | Moderate |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Havanese have a long, silky coat and an expressive, cheerful face. They are affectionate, playful and people-loving — sociable little dogs that bond closely and dislike being left alone for long.

## Diet & nutrition

- Small portions of quality small-breed food; watch treats.

## Grooming

- Brush several times a week to prevent matting; many owners keep a shorter "puppy cut."
- Clean around the eyes and keep up dental care.

## Exercise & training

- Daily walks and play are plenty.
- Very trainable and eager to please — they excel at tricks and gentle training; they're sensitive, so keep it positive.

## Health & lifespan

Generally long-lived. Watch for luxating patella, eye conditions, hip issues, and dental disease. Regular grooming and vet care keep them thriving.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Havanese shed?**
Very little — often tolerated by allergy sufferers, but they need regular grooming.

**Can they be left alone?**
Not happily — they're companion dogs prone to separation anxiety.

## Conclusion

The Havanese is a cheerful, affectionate and adaptable companion ideal for families and apartment life. Keep up grooming and give them plenty of company, and you'll have a devoted little friend for years.
