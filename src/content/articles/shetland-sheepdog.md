Resembling a miniature Rough Collie, the Shetland Sheepdog — the "Sheltie" — is a bright, sensitive and devoted herding breed. Highly trainable and affectionate, the Sheltie is a wonderful companion for an active family.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Small–medium (6–12 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–14 years |
| Coat | Long, dense double coat |
| Energy | Moderate to high |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Shelties have a beautiful long coat, often sable-and-white, and an elegant, alert expression. They are intelligent, sensitive and loyal — eager to please and deeply bonded to their family, though sometimes reserved with strangers. They can be vocal.

## Exercise & training

- Need regular daily exercise plus mental stimulation; they're herding dogs that love activity.
- Among the most trainable breeds — they excel at obedience and agility. Use gentle, positive methods (they're sensitive).

## Grooming

- **Brush 2–3 times a week** to prevent mats; heavier shedding seasonally.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for eye conditions (Collie Eye Anomaly), hip dysplasia, the **MDR1 gene** drug sensitivity (tell your vet), and thyroid issues. Choose health-tested lines.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Shelties easy to train?**
Exceptionally — they're highly intelligent and eager to please.

**Do they bark a lot?**
They can be vocal; training and exercise help manage it.

## Conclusion

The Shetland Sheepdog is a bright, sensitive and devoted companion that excels at training and family life. Provide exercise, gentle training and regular grooming, and you'll have a loyal, intelligent friend.
