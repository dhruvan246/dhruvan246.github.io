The White's Tree Frog (*Litoria caerulea*), also called the dumpy tree frog or Australian green tree frog, is one of the most popular pet amphibians. Native to Australia and New Guinea, it is prized for its calm nature, sturdy build, and surprisingly long life in captivity.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Size | 3.5-4.5 inches (females larger than males) |
| Lifespan | 10-16 years in captivity |
| Enclosure | 18 x 18 x 24 inch vertical terrarium for 1-2 frogs |
| Temperament | Docile, slow-moving, tolerant of gentle handling |
| Care level | Beginner-friendly |

## Appearance & behaviour

White's Tree Frogs are stocky, with smooth skin ranging from bright green to bluish or brownish, often shifting shade with temperature and mood. Older frogs develop characteristic fatty ridges above the eyes, giving them a sleepy, "dumpy" look. They have large toe pads for climbing and are mainly nocturnal, becoming active and vocal at night. Males produce a loud, repetitive bark, especially before rain or during breeding season. By day they tuck into plants or perch quietly, and they are generally unbothered by activity around the enclosure.

## Habitat & water

Provide a tall, well-ventilated terrarium with sturdy branches, cork bark, and broad-leaved plants (live or artificial) for climbing. Use a substrate that holds moisture, such as coconut fibre, and avoid loose materials small enough to be swallowed. Maintain daytime temperatures of 75-82°F (24-28°C) with a slight night drop, using a low-wattage heat source on one side to create a gradient. Humidity should sit around 50-70%, raised by misting once or twice daily. Always supply a shallow dish of dechlorinated water for soaking, since frogs absorb water through their skin and never drink. Refresh the water daily to prevent bacterial build-up. A low-output UVB light is beneficial, though not strictly required.

## Diet & feeding

These frogs are enthusiastic insectivores. Offer a varied diet of appropriately sized crickets, dubia roaches, and the occasional waxworm or earthworm as a treat. Feed juveniles daily, and adults two to three times per week, since White's Tree Frogs are prone to obesity and will overeat if allowed. A frog should look plump but still show some definition; heavy fatty ridges and a bloated belly signal overfeeding. Dust insects with a calcium supplement at most feedings and a vitamin/mineral supplement once or twice weekly to prevent metabolic bone disease. Remove uneaten insects so they do not stress or nibble the frog.

## Handling & health

White's Tree Frogs tolerate handling better than most amphibians, but it should be brief and infrequent. Always wet your hands first with dechlorinated water, never use lotions or soaps, and wash thoroughly before and after, as their permeable skin absorbs chemicals easily. Common health issues include obesity, metabolic bone disease from poor supplementation, and bacterial skin infections in dirty enclosures. Warning signs include lethargy, weight loss, cloudy eyes, sores, or refusal to eat. Keep the habitat clean and stable, and consult a veterinarian experienced with amphibians if problems appear.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q: Are White's Tree Frogs good for beginners?**
A: Yes. Their hardiness, long lifespan, and tolerance of minor husbandry mistakes make them one of the best starter amphibians, provided you maintain proper temperature, humidity, and a measured feeding schedule.

**Q: Can I keep more than one together?**
A: They can be housed in same-sex or mixed groups of similar size, but never mix sizes, as larger frogs may try to eat smaller cage mates. Scale enclosure size up with each additional frog.

## Conclusion

With a roomy vertical terrarium, steady warmth and humidity, clean soaking water, and a disciplined feeding routine, a White's Tree Frog can thrive for well over a decade. Their easygoing temperament and charming appearance make them a rewarding choice for first-time amphibian keepers. Note that legality and collection rules vary by region, so check local regulations before acquiring one.
