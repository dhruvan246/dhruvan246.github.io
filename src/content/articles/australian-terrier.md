The Australian Terrier is a small, hardy working terrier developed in Australia to control rodents and snakes and to serve as a loyal companion on remote homesteads. Confident, alert, and surprisingly tough for its size, it remains an affectionate and adaptable family dog today.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Size | Small (approx. 10–11 in / 25–28 cm; 12–18 lb / 5–8 kg) |
| Lifespan | 11–15 years |
| Coat | Harsh, weather-resistant double coat |
| Energy | Moderate to high |
| Good with kids | Yes, especially with respectful older children |

## Appearance & temperament

The Australian Terrier is a sturdy, low-set dog with a long body, short legs, and an alert, keen expression. Its harsh outer coat comes in blue and tan, solid sandy, or red, with a softer topknot of finer hair on the head and a distinctive ruff around the neck. The ears are small, pricked, and set high.

Despite its modest size, this breed has the bold, spirited character typical of terriers. Australian Terriers are devoted to their families, courageous, and quick to alert owners to anything unusual, making them effective little watchdogs. They are generally friendly but can be assertive with other dogs and will instinctively chase small animals.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a high-quality complete dog food formulated for small breeds and appropriate to your dog's age and activity level. Because Australian Terriers are small and can gain weight easily, measure portions carefully and divide the daily amount into two meals rather than free-feeding.

Use treats sparingly, especially during training, and keep them within roughly ten percent of daily calories. Always provide fresh water. Monitor body condition regularly: you should be able to feel the ribs without pressing hard. Consult your veterinarian about portion sizes and any specific dietary needs.

## Exercise & training

Australian Terriers are energetic and enjoy daily activity, but their exercise needs are manageable. A couple of brisk walks plus play and mental stimulation each day will keep them content. They excel at terrier-friendly activities such as earthdog trials, agility, and scent games, which channel their natural drive.

Intelligent and eager to please yet independent-minded, these terriers respond best to consistent, reward-based training. Start socialization early to encourage calm behavior around other dogs and strangers. A securely fenced yard is recommended, as their hunting instinct may prompt them to dig or chase.

## Grooming & care

The harsh double coat is relatively low-maintenance and naturally sheds dirt. Brush weekly to remove loose hair and prevent mats, and bathe only when necessary so the coat retains its protective texture. Many owners hand-strip the coat a couple of times a year to maintain its characteristic harshness, though pet dogs can simply be tidied.

Trim nails regularly, check ears for debris, and brush the teeth several times a week to support dental health. Routine grooming sessions are also a good opportunity to check the skin for any issues.

## Health & lifespan

The Australian Terrier is generally a robust, long-lived breed, typically enjoying 11 to 15 years. Like many breeds, it can be predisposed to certain conditions, including patellar luxation, allergies and skin sensitivities, diabetes mellitus, and Legg-Calvé-Perthes disease affecting the hip.

Responsible breeders screen breeding stock to reduce inherited problems. Regular veterinary checkups, dental care, weight management, and parasite prevention all help keep an Australian Terrier healthy throughout its life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Australian Terriers good apartment dogs?**

Yes. Their small size and adaptable nature make them suitable for apartments, provided they receive daily exercise and mental stimulation. Be mindful that they may bark to alert you to noises.

**Do Australian Terriers shed a lot?**

No. They are low-shedding dogs with a coat that sheds minimally. Regular brushing keeps loose hair under control, though they still require routine grooming.

## Conclusion

The Australian Terrier packs the courage, loyalty, and lively spirit of a working terrier into a compact, low-shedding package. With consistent training, daily activity, and routine care, this devoted little dog makes a spirited and rewarding companion for many types of households.
