Russia's native forest cat, the Siberian is a sturdy, affectionate breed with a luxurious triple coat built for cold winters. Playful and devoted, they're also famous for being more tolerable to many allergy sufferers.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium–large (4.5–9 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–18 years |
| Coat | Long, dense, triple-layered |
| Energy | Moderate to high |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Siberians are powerful and agile with a thick, water-resistant coat and full ruff. They are friendly, playful and affectionate — sociable "people cats" that bond closely with the family and often enjoy water.

## Diet & nutrition

- A quality diet portioned for their size; they mature slowly over several years.

## Grooming & care

- Brush 2–3 times a week, more during heavy seasonal sheds.
- Provide climbing spaces and interactive play to satisfy their active streak.

## Allergies note

Siberians reportedly produce lower levels of the Fel d 1 allergen, so some allergy sufferers tolerate them better — but this varies by individual cat, so always spend time with one before committing.

## Health & lifespan

Generally hardy and long-lived. Watch for **HCM (heart disease)**. Choose a reputable breeder and keep up regular vet care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Siberian cats hypoallergenic?**
No cat is truly hypoallergenic, but many produce less allergen — reactions vary by person and cat.

**Are they affectionate?**
Very — playful, sociable and devoted to their families.

## Conclusion

The Siberian is a hardy, playful and deeply affectionate companion with a stunning coat. Keep up regular grooming, provide play and climbing, and you'll have a wonderful, devoted family cat.
