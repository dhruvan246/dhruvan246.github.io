Friendly, clever and famously sociable, the Goldendoodle is a popular crossbreed created by mating a Golden Retriever with a Poodle. Bred largely from the 1990s onward as a companion and assistance dog, it combines the easy-going warmth of the Golden with the smarts and low-shedding coat of the Poodle.

## Overview

| Trait | Typical range |
|-------|---------------|
| Size | Small to large (varies by Poodle parent) |
| Lifespan | 10-15 years |
| Coat | Wavy to curly, often low-shedding |
| Energy | Moderate to high |
| Good with kids | Yes, generally excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Because the Goldendoodle is a cross rather than a standardized breed, size and looks vary widely depending on whether a Standard, Miniature or Toy Poodle was used. Medium Goldendoodles often stand around 17-20 inches at the shoulder and weigh roughly 30-50 pounds, though smaller and larger dogs are common.

Coats range from straight or wavy to tight curls, in shades of gold, cream, apricot, red, chocolate and black. Temperamentally, Goldendoodles are typically affectionate, eager to please and highly people-oriented. They tend to be gentle with children and friendly toward strangers and other pets, which makes them poor guard dogs but wonderful family companions and therapy animals.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, high-quality dog food appropriate to your Goldendoodle's size, age and activity level. Because the Golden Retriever side can be prone to weight gain, measure portions carefully and limit treats to avoid obesity, which strains joints. Split the daily ration into two meals rather than free-feeding.

Puppies of medium and large size benefit from a large-breed puppy formula to support steady growth. Always provide fresh water, and consult your veterinarian before changing diets or adding supplements.

## Exercise & training

Goldendoodles are active, intelligent dogs that need daily exercise, usually 45 to 60 minutes of walking, fetch, swimming or play, plus mental stimulation. Bored or under-exercised dogs may bark, chew or become anxious.

Their intelligence and willingness to please make them highly trainable. They respond best to positive, reward-based methods. Early socialization and basic obedience set the foundation, and many excel at agility, scent work and assistance tasks. Because they bond closely with people, they dislike being left alone for long stretches.

## Grooming & care

Goldendoodles are often marketed as low-shedding, but their coats are high-maintenance. Curly and wavy coats mat easily and usually need brushing several times a week, plus professional grooming or clipping every 6-10 weeks. Check and clean the floppy ears regularly, as trapped moisture can lead to infections. Trim nails, brush teeth and bathe as needed.

## Health & lifespan

Most Goldendoodles live 10-15 years. As a cross, they can inherit conditions from either parent, including hip and elbow dysplasia, progressive retinal atrophy and ear infections. Responsible breeders screen parent dogs for hip, eye and heart issues. Routine veterinary care, parasite prevention, dental hygiene and a healthy weight all support a long, comfortable life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Goldendoodles hypoallergenic?**
No dog is truly hypoallergenic. Many Goldendoodles shed little and may suit some allergy sufferers, but coat type varies, so spend time with a dog before committing.

**Are Goldendoodles good for first-time owners?**
Generally yes. Their friendly, trainable nature suits novices, provided owners commit to daily exercise, training and the considerable grooming the coat requires.

## Conclusion

The Goldendoodle blends the affectionate temperament of the Golden Retriever with the intelligence and lower-shedding coat of the Poodle, making a versatile family companion. With consistent training, regular grooming and plenty of exercise and company, a well-bred Goldendoodle can be a devoted, joyful friend for many years.
