Tough, tireless and brilliant, the Australian Cattle Dog (often called the "Blue Heeler") was bred to drive cattle across vast distances. Intensely energetic and loyal, this is a working dog that demands an active, engaged owner.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (15–22 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–16 years |
| Coat | Short, dense double coat |
| Energy | Very high |
| Good with kids | Yes, with exercise and supervision |

## Appearance & temperament

Cattle Dogs are compact and muscular with a speckled blue or red coat. They are highly intelligent, energetic and devoted — focused working dogs with strong herding instincts (including nipping at heels) and a one-person loyalty streak.

## Exercise & training — essential

- Need **a great deal of daily exercise plus mental work** — herding, agility, running, training games.
- Without enough activity they become destructive and may nip/herd people. Not a dog for a sedentary home.
- Highly trainable; channel their drive with jobs and early socialisation.

## Grooming

- Easy — weekly brushing, more during seasonal sheds.

## Health & lifespan

Long-lived and hardy. Watch for deafness (BAER testing), eye conditions (PRA), and hip dysplasia. Choose health-tested lines.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Cattle Dogs good for first-time owners?**
Challenging — their intense energy and drive suit experienced, active owners.

**Do they nip?**
They have a herding instinct to nip heels; training and exercise help manage it.

## Conclusion

The Australian Cattle Dog is a tireless, brilliant and loyal working dog for a very active owner. Give them serious exercise, mental challenge and a job to do, and you'll have an unbeatable, devoted partner.
