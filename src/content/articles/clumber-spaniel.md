The Clumber Spaniel is the largest and heaviest of the spaniels — a calm, dignified gundog built low to the ground. Known for its mellow disposition and soulful expression, this English breed is a devoted, easygoing companion that prefers a steady walk to a frantic sprint.

## Overview

| Trait | Details |
|---|---|
| Size | Large (heavy-boned, low-set) |
| Lifespan | Around 10-12 years |
| Coat | Dense, straight white coat, often with lemon or orange markings |
| Energy | Low to moderate |
| Good with kids | Yes, gentle and patient |

## Appearance & temperament

The Clumber Spaniel is unmistakable: a long, low, substantial body, a massive head with a deep muzzle, and a thoughtful, somewhat droopy expression. Adults are noticeably heavier than other spaniels, with mature dogs often weighing well into the 50-85 pound range. The coat is predominantly white with lemon or orange markings, particularly around the ears and face.

In temperament, the Clumber is famously laid-back, affectionate, and loyal. It tends to bond closely with its family and can be reserved or quiet around strangers rather than boisterous. While intelligent, it has an independent, deliberate streak and is generally less excitable than a Cocker or Springer Spaniel.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food appropriate to the Clumber's age and activity level. Because the breed is prone to weight gain and carries a heavy frame, portion control is important — excess weight adds strain to joints. Measure meals rather than free-feeding, and account for treats used in training. Always provide fresh water, and ask your veterinarian for guidance on the right amount and any weight-management needs.

## Exercise & training

Despite their hunting heritage, Clumbers have modest energy needs. A daily walk plus some play or low-impact activity usually keeps them content; they are happy to be calm housemates. Avoid over-exercising puppies while joints are developing, and be cautious with heat, as the breed can tire and overheat.

Training benefits from patience and positive, reward-based methods. Clumbers are food-motivated and willing but can be stubborn, so short, consistent sessions work best. Early socialization helps the naturally reserved Clumber feel comfortable with new people and situations.

## Grooming & care

The dense double coat sheds steadily and needs brushing several times a week to manage loose hair and prevent matting, especially around the feathering on the legs, chest, and ears. Routine ear checks are important, as the long, low-set ears can trap moisture. Keep nails trimmed and tend to the facial folds and flews; Clumbers are known to drool, so regular wiping helps keep skin clean and comfortable.

## Health & lifespan

Clumber Spaniels typically live around 10-12 years. As a large, heavy breed they can be predisposed to hip and elbow joint issues, and eye conditions are also seen in the breed. Their deep build means weight control matters for joint and overall health. Buying from a responsible breeder who screens breeding stock, plus routine veterinary checkups, supports a healthy life. Discuss any concerns with your veterinarian.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Clumber Spaniels good family dogs?** Yes. They are gentle, patient, and affectionate, generally getting along well with children and other pets when properly socialized.

**Do Clumber Spaniels shed and drool a lot?** Yes to both. They shed steadily year-round and tend to drool, so regular brushing and occasional face-wiping are part of routine care.

## Conclusion

The Clumber Spaniel is a calm, loving companion that rewards owners willing to manage its grooming, drooling, and weight. With moderate exercise, consistent training, and attentive care, this dignified gundog makes a loyal and gentle addition to a relaxed household.
