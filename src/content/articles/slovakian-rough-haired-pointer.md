The Slovakian Rough-Haired Pointer (also called the Slovakian Wirehaired Pointing Dog) is a versatile hunting breed developed in Slovakia after the Second World War. Bred to point, track and retrieve on land and in water, it is a hardy, even-tempered working dog that has remained relatively rare outside its homeland.

## Appearance & temperament

This is a lean, athletic gundog standing roughly 57–68 cm at the shoulder, with a rectangular, well-muscled frame built for endurance rather than bulk. Its defining feature is the harsh, close-lying wirehaired coat, usually a grey or "grey roan" shade often with darker patches, accompanied by a distinct beard, moustache and bushy eyebrows that give the breed a serious expression.

Temperamentally, the Slovakian Rough-Haired Pointer is calm and steady in the home yet driven and focused in the field. These dogs are loyal, biddable and form close bonds with their families. They are typically good with children and tolerant of other dogs when properly socialised, though their strong hunting instinct means small pets may trigger a chase.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced diet formulated for a large, active dog, with protein and fat levels that match the dog's considerable energy output. Working or heavily exercised individuals may need more calories than companion dogs, so adjust portions to maintain a trim, athletic body condition where the ribs are easily felt.

Divide the daily ration into two meals to support steady energy and reduce the risk of bloat, a concern in deep-chested breeds. Provide constant access to fresh water, especially after exercise, and avoid heavy feeding immediately before or after strenuous activity.

## Exercise & training

As a true working gundog, this breed has high exercise needs and is not suited to a sedentary household. Aim for substantial daily activity, ideally a mix of long walks, free running, scent work and retrieving games that engage both body and mind. Without enough outlet, these intelligent dogs can become bored and restless.

Training should be consistent, reward-based and started early. The breed is eager to please and quick to learn, responding well to positive methods. Early socialisation with people, other animals and varied environments helps produce a confident, well-mannered adult. A reliable recall is especially valuable given the strong prey drive.

## Grooming & care

The wiry coat is relatively low-maintenance but benefits from weekly brushing to remove loose hair and debris. Periodic hand-stripping or tidying helps preserve the harsh texture, though many companion owners simply keep the coat neat. Pay attention to the beard and facial furnishings, which can trap food and moisture.

Check and clean the ears regularly, as drop ears can retain moisture after swimming. Trim nails as needed and maintain routine dental care.

## Health & lifespan

The Slovakian Rough-Haired Pointer is generally a robust, healthy breed with a typical lifespan of around 12 to 14 years. As a large, deep-chested dog, owners should be aware of the risk of bloat (gastric dilatation-volvulus) and learn to recognise its warning signs. Like many large breeds, it can also be predisposed to hip and elbow joint issues, so choosing puppies from health-screened parents is wise.

Routine veterinary check-ups, sensible exercise on developing joints, parasite control and maintaining a healthy weight all support long-term wellbeing. Always consult your veterinarian for individual health advice.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Slovakian Rough-Haired Pointer a good family dog?**
Yes. It is typically calm and affectionate at home and good with children when well socialised, provided it receives plenty of daily exercise and mental stimulation.

**Does this breed need a lot of exercise?**
Absolutely. As an active hunting breed it requires substantial daily activity and is best suited to energetic owners or working homes rather than apartment living.

## Conclusion

The Slovakian Rough-Haired Pointer is a capable, even-tempered gundog that rewards active, committed owners with loyalty and versatility. Given enough exercise, early training and basic coat care, this rare wirehaired pointer makes a devoted companion both in the field and at home.
