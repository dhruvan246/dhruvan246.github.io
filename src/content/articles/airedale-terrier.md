The largest of the terriers, the Airedale earns its title "King of Terriers." Versatile, intelligent and confident, this athletic breed has served as a hunter, guardian and even a wartime messenger — and makes a spirited family companion.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (19–29 kg) |
| Lifespan | 11–14 years |
| Coat | Harsh, wiry double coat |
| Energy | High |
| Good with kids | Yes, with exercise and supervision |

## Appearance & temperament

Airedales are tall and sturdy with a tan-and-black wiry coat and a signature beard. They are confident, intelligent and energetic — bold and playful, with the independent streak typical of terriers.

## Exercise & training

- Need plenty of daily exercise and mental stimulation; they're athletic and clever.
- Trainable but independent and strong-willed; consistent, positive training and early socialisation help. They have a strong prey drive.

## Grooming

- Brush a couple of times a week; the wiry coat is traditionally hand-stripped, though many pets are clipped every couple of months.

## Health & lifespan

Generally healthy. Watch for hip dysplasia, hypothyroidism, skin conditions, and bloat. Choose health-tested lines and keep up regular vet care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Airedales good family dogs?**
Yes for active families — they're playful and devoted, but need exercise and training.

**Do they shed?**
Low shedding with the wiry coat, but they need regular grooming/stripping.

## Conclusion

The Airedale Terrier is a versatile, confident and energetic companion worthy of its royal title. Provide exercise, training and grooming, and the King of Terriers will be a spirited, devoted friend.
