Famous as Toto from *The Wizard of Oz*, the Cairn Terrier is a small, hardy and spirited working terrier from Scotland. Bold, busy and full of character, the Cairn is a fun-loving companion with a big personality.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Small (6–7.5 kg) |
| Lifespan | 13–15 years |
| Coat | Harsh, weather-resistant double coat |
| Energy | Moderate to high |
| Good with kids | Yes |

## Appearance & temperament

Cairns have a shaggy, weatherproof coat and an alert, foxy expression. They are confident, curious and lively — independent little terriers that love to explore, dig and stay busy.

## Diet & nutrition

- Quality small-breed food in measured portions; watch for weight gain.

## Grooming

- Brush a couple of times a week; the harsh coat is traditionally hand-stripped, though many pets are clipped.

## Exercise & training

- Daily walks and play; they're energetic and enjoy activity.
- Smart but independent with a strong prey drive; consistent, positive training and a secure yard (they dig!) help.

## Health & lifespan

Generally hardy and long-lived. Watch for luxating patella, eye conditions, and some hereditary issues. Regular vet care keeps them in good shape.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Cairn Terriers like to dig?**
Yes — they're natural diggers; a secure, dig-friendly space helps.

**Are they good family dogs?**
Yes — sturdy, lively and good with respectful children.

## Conclusion

The Cairn Terrier is a hardy, spirited and characterful little companion full of terrier charm. Provide exercise, training and a secure space, and you'll enjoy this busy little adventurer for years.
