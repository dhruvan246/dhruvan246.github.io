The Hermann's Tortoise (*Testudo hermanni*) is one of the most popular pet tortoises, prized for its manageable size, hardy nature and engaging, active personality. Native to southern Europe, it is a long-term commitment that can easily outlive its keeper.

## Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|---|---|
| Adult size | Around 13-20 cm (5-8 in) shell length; some western individuals stay smaller |
| Lifespan | Commonly 50+ years with good care, sometimes much longer |
| Enclosure size | Adults need a large tortoise table or outdoor pen; bigger is always better |
| Temperament | Active, curious, generally tame and not aggressive |
| Care level | Intermediate; long-lived with specific climate needs |

## Appearance & temperament

Hermann's Tortoises have a domed, yellow-and-brown patterned carapace, sturdy scaly forelimbs and a distinctive horny tip on the tail. Many have a divided plate above the tail. They are diurnal and surprisingly busy, spending the day grazing, basking and exploring. With routine they become confident and may approach their keeper at feeding time, though they are not "cuddly" and prefer to keep their feet on the ground.

## Habitat & enclosure

These tortoises do best in a large open-topped tortoise table or, ideally, a secure outdoor enclosure during warm weather. Glass tanks are generally unsuitable because of poor ventilation. Provide a substrate such as a topsoil-and-sand mix that allows light digging, plus hides, rocks and edible plants for cover. Outdoor pens must be escape-proof, predator-proof and offer both sun and shade. Hermann's Tortoises are strong diggers and climbers, so walls should be solid and sunk into the ground.

## Heating, lighting & humidity

Provide a thermal gradient with a basking spot around 32-35°C (90-95°F) at one end and a cooler area in the low 20s°C. A nighttime drop to room temperature is fine. UVB lighting is essential for vitamin D3 synthesis and healthy bone and shell growth; use a quality reptile UVB tube and replace it as the manufacturer recommends. Humidity should be moderate and not damp, though hatchlings benefit from slightly higher humidity and access to a humid hide. Many keepers hibernate healthy adults in cool conditions over winter, but this should only be attempted after research and a vet health check.

## Diet & feeding

Hermann's Tortoises are herbivores that thrive on a high-fibre, low-protein, calcium-rich diet of weeds and leafy greens. Offer dandelion, plantain, clover, sow thistle, mallow and other safe wild plants, supplemented with greens like rocket and endive. Avoid fruit, which causes digestive upset, and never feed dog or cat food, peas, beans or sugary items. Dust food with a calcium supplement, and provide a cuttlebone and constant access to a shallow water dish for drinking and soaking.

## Handling & health

Handle gently and infrequently, supporting the body and avoiding drops. Wash hands before and after because reptiles can carry *Salmonella*. Common health issues include shell pyramiding from poor diet or humidity, metabolic bone disease from inadequate UVB or calcium, respiratory infections and parasites. Watch for a runny nose, swollen eyes, lethargy or loss of appetite, and consult a reptile-experienced vet. Legality and protected status vary by region, so buy only captive-bred animals with the correct paperwork.

## Frequently asked questions

**How big does a Hermann's Tortoise get?** Most reach roughly 13-20 cm (5-8 inches) in shell length, with females typically larger than males. Western Hermann's tend to stay smaller than the eastern subspecies.

**Do Hermann's Tortoises need to hibernate?** Healthy adults can be hibernated in cool, controlled conditions, which mirrors their natural cycle, but it is optional. Always do thorough research and get a pre-hibernation health check first.

## Conclusion

A Hermann's Tortoise is a rewarding, long-lived companion for keepers prepared to meet its needs for space, UVB lighting, warmth and a proper weed-based diet. With responsible, captive-bred sourcing and good husbandry, this charming reptile can stay healthy for many decades.
