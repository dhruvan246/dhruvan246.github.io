The Polish Lowland Sheepdog, known in its homeland as the Polski Owczarek Nizinny (often shortened to PON), is a compact, shaggy herding dog with a long history of working flocks across the plains of Poland. Hardy, clever, and devoted, the breed combines a watchful guarding instinct with an affectionate family temperament.

## Overview

| Trait | Details |
|---|---|
| Size | Medium (roughly 30-50 lb / 14-23 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12-15 years |
| Coat | Long, dense, shaggy double coat |
| Energy | Moderate to high |
| Good with kids | Yes, with supervision |

## Appearance & temperament

The PON is a muscular, slightly rectangular dog standing about 17-20 inches at the shoulder. Its most famous feature is the profuse, shaggy double coat that often hangs over the eyes. The coat comes in many colors and patterns, including white, gray, black, and combinations with markings. Despite its small-to-medium frame, the breed is sturdy and surprisingly agile.

Temperamentally, the Polish Lowland Sheepdog is intelligent, self-confident, and famous for an excellent memory. It bonds closely with its family and tends to be reserved or watchful around strangers, making it a natural alert dog. The breed is lively and can be strong-willed, so it thrives with owners who provide structure and companionship.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced diet formulated for the dog's life stage and activity level. Most adult PONs do well on two measured meals a day. Because the breed has a moderate build hidden under a heavy coat, it can be hard to judge weight by sight alone, so check body condition by feel along the ribs and adjust portions accordingly. Provide constant access to fresh water and keep treats modest to prevent excess weight, which strains the joints. Consult your veterinarian about specific portions or any dietary concerns.

## Exercise & training

This is a working breed that needs daily physical and mental activity. Aim for at least an hour of exercise per day through brisk walks, play, and off-leash romps in safe areas. PONs excel at dog sports such as agility, obedience, herding trials, and rally, which channel their energy and intelligence.

Training should start early and rely on consistent, positive, reward-based methods. The breed is smart and learns quickly but can also be independent and a little stubborn, so patient repetition and firm, fair leadership work best. Early socialization helps temper natural wariness toward strangers and other animals.

## Grooming & care

The dense double coat requires real commitment. Brush thoroughly several times a week to prevent the long hair from matting, paying special attention to the legs, behind the ears, and the hindquarters. Many owners trim the hair around the eyes so the dog can see clearly. Bathe as needed, and check ears, teeth, and nails on a regular schedule. The PON sheds relatively little for such a hairy dog, but neglected grooming quickly leads to tangles.

## Health & lifespan

The Polish Lowland Sheepdog is generally a robust, healthy breed with a typical lifespan of 12 to 15 years. Like many breeds, it can be prone to hip dysplasia and certain inherited eye conditions such as progressive retinal atrophy. Responsible breeders screen their stock for these issues. Routine veterinary care, parasite prevention, dental hygiene, and maintaining a healthy weight all support a long, comfortable life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Polish Lowland Sheepdogs good apartment dogs?**
They can adapt to apartment living if given enough daily exercise and mental stimulation, but they are active herders that do best with regular outdoor activity and engaged owners.

**Do Polish Lowland Sheepdogs shed a lot?**
For such a hairy dog, the PON sheds relatively little. However, the long double coat needs frequent brushing to prevent mats, so grooming time is significant even if loose hair is minimal.

## Conclusion

The Polish Lowland Sheepdog is a loyal, intelligent, and hardworking companion that rewards owners willing to meet its grooming and exercise needs. With early training, consistent socialization, and regular coat care, the PON makes a devoted family dog and a vigilant, charming partner for active households.
