Nicknamed the "little captain," the Schipperke is a small black Belgian breed that once guarded barges and shops along the canals of Brussels. Curious, fearless and full of energy, this fox-faced dog packs a big personality into a compact, tailless frame and thrives with active owners who appreciate a watchful, mischievous companion.

## Overview

| Trait | Typical Range |
|---|---|
| Size | Small (10-16 lb / 4.5-7 kg, 10-13 in tall) |
| Lifespan | 12-15 years |
| Coat | Dense, harsh black double coat with a distinctive ruff |
| Energy | High |
| Good with kids | Yes, with supervision and early socialization |

## Appearance & temperament

The Schipperke has a sturdy, square build with a fox-like head, small triangular ears that stand erect, and dark, mischief-filled eyes. Its hallmark is the abundant black coat, which forms a pronounced mane or "ruff" around the neck, a cape over the shoulders and feathered "culottes" on the hind legs. Many Schipperkes are born with a naturally short or absent tail.

Temperamentally, this is a confident, intensely curious and devoted little dog. Schipperkes are alert watchdogs that bark to announce anything unusual, and they form strong bonds with their families. They can be independent and stubborn, with a strong prey drive that makes them prone to chasing small animals. Early socialization helps temper their natural wariness toward strangers.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a high-quality complete dog food formulated for small, active breeds, divided into two measured meals a day. Because Schipperkes are small and prone to weight gain when underexercised, portion control is important; obesity stresses their joints and shortens lifespan. Use their food allowance for training rewards rather than adding extra treats, and keep fresh water available at all times. Adjust amounts to your dog's age, weight and activity level, and consult your veterinarian about the best diet for each life stage.

## Exercise & training

Do not be fooled by the Schipperke's size; this is an energetic, athletic breed that needs daily exercise and mental stimulation. Aim for brisk walks plus active play, and channel their drive into games, fetch and canine sports such as agility, obedience and rally, where they often excel. A securely fenced yard is ideal, as their curiosity and prey drive make them skilled escape artists who should be kept on a lead in open areas.

Training works best with positive, reward-based methods and consistency. Schipperkes are smart and quick to learn but can be willful, so keep sessions short, varied and engaging. Early socialization curbs excessive barking and suspicion of strangers.

## Grooming & care

The double coat is relatively low-maintenance for most of the year and needs only weekly brushing to manage loose hair and keep the ruff looking tidy. Schipperkes "blow" their undercoat once or twice a year, during which daily brushing helps control heavy shedding. Bathe only as needed. Routine care includes regular nail trims, tooth brushing to support dental health, and periodic ear checks to keep them clean and dry.

## Health & lifespan

The Schipperke is generally a hardy, long-lived breed with a typical lifespan of 12 to 15 years. Like all breeds, it can be affected by certain inherited conditions, including a rare storage disease known as MPS IIIB, as well as hip and joint issues, luxating patellas, eye disorders and hypothyroidism. Responsible breeders screen their stock for known concerns. Keeping your Schipperke at a healthy weight, maintaining dental care and scheduling regular veterinary checkups give it the best chance at a long, active life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Schipperkes bark a lot?**
Yes, they are naturally alert and vocal watchdogs that bark to signal anything new. Early training and socialization help keep barking to a reasonable level.

**Are Schipperkes good apartment dogs?**
They can adapt to apartment living if given enough daily exercise and mental enrichment, though their tendency to bark means consistent training is important in close quarters.

## Conclusion

The Schipperke is a spirited, intelligent companion that rewards active, engaged owners with loyalty and endless entertainment. With proper exercise, consistent training and routine care, this hardy "little captain" makes a devoted family member and a vigilant little watchdog for many years.
