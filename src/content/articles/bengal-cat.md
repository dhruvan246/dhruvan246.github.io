Looking like a miniature leopard, the Bengal is a stunning, athletic breed created by crossing domestic cats with the Asian leopard cat. Behind the wild coat is an energetic, intelligent and highly active companion.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium–large (4–7 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–16 years |
| Coat | Short, spotted or marbled, glossy |
| Energy | Very high |
| Good with kids | Yes — playful and active |

## Appearance & temperament

Bengals have a muscular build and a striking spotted or marbled coat that often glitters. They are highly intelligent, athletic and playful — curious explorers who love climbing, water and games.

## Enrichment & exercise — essential

- Bengals **need a lot of stimulation.** Provide tall cat trees, puzzle feeders, interactive toys and play sessions.
- Many enjoy leash walks and even water play.
- A bored Bengal can become destructive — they're not a "leave alone all day" cat.

## Diet & nutrition

- A quality, high-protein diet suits their active metabolism.

## Grooming & care

- Easy — the short, glossy coat needs only occasional brushing.

## Health & lifespan

Generally healthy and athletic. Watch for **HCM (heart disease)**, certain eye conditions (PRA), and patellar luxation. Buy from reputable breeders who health-test.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Bengals high maintenance?**
Low grooming, but high energy and enrichment needs — they demand interaction and play.

**Do Bengals like water?**
Many do — don't be surprised if yours joins you at the tap or tub.

## Conclusion

The Bengal is a gorgeous, intelligent and energetic companion for an owner ready to provide plenty of play and stimulation. Meet their active needs and you'll have a spectacular, engaging feline friend.
