The Common Goldfish (*Carassius auratus*) is one of the oldest and most popular ornamental fish, descended from wild carp domesticated in China over a thousand years ago. Though often seen as a "starter" pet, this slender, single-tailed fish is large, messy and long-lived, and it thrives only with proper space and filtration.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Adult size | Commonly 15-25 cm; can exceed 30 cm |
| Lifespan | Typically 10-15 years; sometimes longer |
| Minimum tank size | Around 75-110 litres for one fish, more per extra fish |
| Water type | Freshwater, coldwater |
| Care level | Beginner-friendly, but needs real commitment |

## Appearance & behaviour

The Common Goldfish has a streamlined body and a single tail fin, unlike fancy varieties with twin tails. Colours range from the classic orange-red through yellow, white, and mottled patterns. It is an active, fast swimmer that spends much of the day foraging along the substrate. Goldfish are social, curious, and surprisingly intelligent, often learning to recognise the person who feeds them and gathering at the front of the tank at feeding time.

## Tank setup & water parameters

Because of their size, waste output, and swimming activity, Common Goldfish need a large tank or pond rather than a bowl. Provide a generous footprint, smooth gravel or sand, and hardy plants or decorations with no sharp edges. Strong, well-cycled filtration is essential, as goldfish produce a heavy bioload.

Aim for the following general ranges:

- Temperature: roughly 18-22°C; they tolerate cooler water well and need no heater in most homes.
- pH: around 7.0-7.5.
- Ammonia and nitrite: 0; nitrate kept low through regular water changes.

Perform partial water changes weekly to maintain quality.

## Diet & feeding

Goldfish are omnivores with a tendency to overeat. Feed a quality goldfish pellet or flake as the staple, supplemented with blanched vegetables such as peas, spinach, or zucchini, plus occasional treats like brine shrimp or bloodworms. Feed small amounts once or twice a day, offering only what they finish in a couple of minutes. Overfeeding fouls the water and contributes to digestive problems, so err on the side of less food.

## Tank mates & compatibility

Common Goldfish do best with their own kind or other coldwater, single-tailed goldfish of similar size and speed. They generally should not be housed with slow fancy goldfish, which they outcompete for food, nor with tropical species that require warmer water. Very small fish may be eaten, since goldfish will swallow anything that fits in their mouths. A small group of compatible goldfish in a suitably large tank or pond is ideal.

## Health & common issues

With good care, goldfish are hardy. Most health problems stem from poor water quality, overcrowding, or overfeeding. Common issues include swim bladder problems (often linked to diet), ich (white spot), fin rot, and fungal infections. Watch for clamped fins, loss of appetite, gasping at the surface, or unusual swimming. Stable, clean water and a varied diet prevent most illness. If a fish appears sick, isolate it if possible and consult a vet experienced with fish.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can a goldfish live in a bowl?** No. Bowls are far too small, lack filtration, and lead to stunted growth and a short, stressful life. A properly sized, filtered tank or pond is essential.

**How long do Common Goldfish live?** With good care they commonly live 10-15 years, and well-kept pond fish can live longer still, so adopting one is a long-term commitment.

## Conclusion

The Common Goldfish is a rewarding, personable pet that can live well over a decade when given space, clean coldwater, and a balanced diet. Treat it as the large, long-lived fish it truly is rather than a disposable bowl pet, and it will thrive for many years.
