With a fox-like face and a cloud of fluff, the Pomeranian is a tiny dog with a huge, confident personality. Descended from large sled dogs, "Poms" are lively, smart and endlessly charming.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Tiny (1.5–3.5 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–16 years |
| Coat | Thick, fluffy double coat |
| Energy | Moderate to high |
| Good with kids | Best with older, gentle children |

## Appearance & temperament

Poms are famous for their dense double coat and plumed tail. They're alert, curious and bold — often unaware of how small they are. They bond closely with their owners and make excellent little watchdogs.

## Diet & nutrition

- A high-quality small-breed diet in small portions.
- Watch the calories — their fluff hides weight gain.
- Provide fresh water and consider dental-friendly food.

## Grooming — the main job

That coat needs regular attention:

- Brush 2–3 times a week to prevent mats.
- Expect seasonal shedding ("blowing coat").
- Avoid shaving the double coat — it can damage regrowth.
- Regular nail trims and dental care.

## Training & exercise

Poms are intelligent and trainable but can be vocal. Short daily walks plus play keep them happy. Early socialisation curbs excessive barking.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for luxating patella, dental disease, collapsing trachea (use a harness, not a collar) and a condition called "black skin disease" (alopecia X). Routine vet care keeps them healthy.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Pomeranians bark a lot?**
They can be chatty; training and exercise help.

**Are they good for first-time owners?**
Yes, with commitment to grooming and gentle training.

## Conclusion

The Pomeranian is a spirited, affectionate companion that thrives on attention and grooming. Keep that coat brushed and use a harness on walks, and your Pom will be a joyful little presence for years.
