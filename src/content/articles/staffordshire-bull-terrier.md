Affectionately known as the "Staffy," the Staffordshire Bull Terrier is a muscular, courageous dog with a famously loving nature — especially towards children, earning the old nickname "nanny dog." Behind the strong build is a soft, devoted companion.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (11–17 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–14 years |
| Coat | Short, smooth |
| Energy | Moderate to high |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Staffies are compact and powerfully built with a broad head and a short, glossy coat. They are affectionate, brave and people-loving — devoted to their family and famously fond of children. Proper socialisation and training bring out their best.

## Diet & nutrition

- Quality food in measured portions to maintain their lean, muscular build.

## Exercise & training

- Need a good hour or more of daily exercise plus play.
- Intelligent and eager to please; use consistent, reward-based training and socialise early.
- Many Staffies are best as the only dog or need careful introductions — socialisation with other dogs matters.

## Grooming & care

- Very easy — a weekly brush keeps the short coat healthy.
- Routine nail trims, ear checks and dental care.

## Health & lifespan

Generally robust. Watch for hip dysplasia, certain hereditary eye conditions, and skin allergies. Responsible breeding (with health testing) and regular vet care help. Keep them cool in hot weather.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Staffies good with children?**
Famously so — gentle, patient and affectionate, though all dog–child interactions should be supervised.

**Do they need much grooming?**
No — one of the easiest coats to care for.

## Conclusion

The Staffordshire Bull Terrier is a loving, loyal and people-focused companion that thrives on family life. Give them exercise, early socialisation and consistent training, and you'll have a devoted, affectionate friend.
