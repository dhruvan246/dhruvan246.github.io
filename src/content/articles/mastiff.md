One of the heaviest dog breeds in the world, the Mastiff is a calm, dignified and devoted guardian. Despite their imposing size, well-raised Mastiffs are gentle, affectionate and remarkably easy-going family companions.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Giant (54–100+ kg) |
| Lifespan | 7–10 years |
| Coat | Short, dense |
| Energy | Low |
| Good with kids | Yes, with supervision |

## Appearance & temperament

Mastiffs are enormous and powerfully built, typically fawn or apricot with a dark mask. They are calm, courageous and devoted — naturally protective yet gentle and affectionate with their family. They're laid-back indoors and rarely hyperactive.

## Diet & nutrition

- A quality large/giant-breed diet; puppies need controlled, slow growth (giant-breed puppy food).
- Feed measured meals and **take bloat precautions** (raised feeding, rest after meals).

## Exercise & training

- Modest daily walks suit their low energy; avoid high-impact exercise, especially in puppies.
- **Early socialisation and training are vital** — a dog this size and strength must be well-mannered and confident. Use calm, consistent, positive methods.

## Grooming & care

- Easy — weekly brushing.
- Expect some drooling, and provide large, supportive bedding for their joints.
- Keep them cool — their size makes them heat-sensitive.

## Health & lifespan

Giant-breed risks apply: hip and elbow dysplasia, bloat (GDV), heart conditions, and a shorter lifespan. Keep them lean (excess weight is hard on joints), choose health-tested lines, and budget for vet care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Mastiffs aggressive?**
No — well-bred, socialised Mastiffs are calm and gentle, though naturally protective.

**Do they need a lot of exercise?**
No — they're low-energy, but they do need space and good early training.

## Conclusion

The Mastiff is a gentle, loyal and dignified guardian for an owner prepared for a giant breed. Socialise and train them early, feed carefully, keep them lean and cool, and you'll have a devoted, calm companion.
