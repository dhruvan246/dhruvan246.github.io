The Landseer is a large, powerful working dog instantly recognized by its striking black-and-white coat. Closely related to the Newfoundland, it shares that breed's gentle nature, love of water and devotion to family. The breed takes its name from the English painter Sir Edwin Landseer, who famously depicted these dogs in his artwork. In Europe, the Landseer is recognized as a distinct breed, while in some registries it is classified as a color variety of the Newfoundland.

## Appearance & temperament

The Landseer is a tall, athletic dog standing roughly 26-31 inches (67-80 cm) at the shoulder, with males larger than females. The defining feature is the coat: a predominantly white body with black markings, ideally including a black head. The build is more leggy and athletic than the heavier Newfoundland, reflecting its working water-dog heritage.

Temperamentally, the Landseer is calm, patient and affectionate. It is known for being exceptionally good with children, earning a reputation as a true family companion. These dogs are intelligent and eager to please, though they can have an independent streak. They tend to be friendly toward strangers and other animals, making them poor guard dogs but wonderful household members.

## Diet & nutrition

As a giant breed, the Landseer needs a complete, balanced diet formulated for large or giant dogs. Feeding amounts should match the dog's age, weight and activity level, and meals are best split into two servings a day to support digestion. Puppies should eat a large-breed puppy formula that controls growth rate, since overly rapid growth can stress developing joints.

Because giant breeds can be prone to bloat (gastric torsion), avoid heavy exercise right before and after meals. Keep treats moderate and monitor body condition closely, as excess weight places extra strain on the joints. Always provide fresh water, and consult your veterinarian about portion sizes.

## Exercise & training

Despite its size, the Landseer has only moderate energy needs. Daily walks plus some free play or swimming usually keep it content. The breed has a natural affinity for water and excels at swimming, a nod to its history assisting fishermen and performing water rescues. Avoid strenuous activity in young dogs until their joints have matured.

Training should begin early and rely on positive, reward-based methods. Landseers are sensitive and respond poorly to harsh corrections. Their size makes early socialization and basic obedience essential, so that an adult dog is well-mannered and easy to handle. Consistency and gentle patience produce the best results.

## Grooming & care

The thick double coat sheds steadily and more heavily during seasonal changes. Brush several times a week, increasing to daily during heavy shedding, to remove loose hair and prevent mats, especially behind the ears and on the legs. Bathe occasionally as needed.

Routine care includes regular nail trimming, ear checks to catch moisture-related infections, and dental hygiene. Landseers can drool, so wiping the mouth area helps keep them tidy. They tolerate cold well but can overheat, so provide shade and water in warm weather.

## Health & lifespan

Landseers typically live about 9-11 years, which is common for giant breeds. Like other large dogs, they can be predisposed to hip and elbow dysplasia, heart conditions, and bloat. Responsible breeders screen breeding stock for joint and heart issues.

Maintaining a healthy weight, providing appropriate exercise and scheduling regular veterinary checkups all help support a long, comfortable life. Discuss any breed-specific concerns with your vet.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is a Landseer the same as a Newfoundland?**
They are closely related. Some registries treat the Landseer as a separate breed with a more athletic build, while others consider it a black-and-white color variety of the Newfoundland.

**Are Landseers good family dogs?**
Yes. They are calm, gentle and especially patient with children, making them excellent family companions when given proper training and socialization.

## Conclusion

The Landseer is a gentle giant that combines an eye-catching black-and-white coat with a kind, devoted temperament. With moderate exercise, consistent grooming and attentive health care, this water-loving dog makes a loyal and loving member of an active family home.
