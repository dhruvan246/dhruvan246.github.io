The Firefish Goby (*Nemateleotris magnifica*), also called the fire dartfish, is one of the most popular small saltwater fish for reef and nano aquariums. Hardy, peaceful, and strikingly coloured, it suits both beginner and experienced marine keepers, provided its tank is securely covered.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Adult size | 2.5-3 inches (6-8 cm) |
| Lifespan | 3-4 years (sometimes longer) |
| Minimum tank size | 10-20 gallons |
| Water type | Saltwater (marine) |
| Care level | Easy / beginner-friendly |

## Appearance & behaviour

The Firefish Goby has a slender, elongated body that fades from a pale cream or white head into a fiery orange-red rear half. Its most distinctive feature is a tall, elongated first dorsal fin that flicks up and down constantly as the fish hovers in the water column. This twitching display is normal and often signals alertness.

Firefish are shy but curious. They typically hover just above the substrate near a rock crevice or burrow, darting back to cover at the first sign of danger. They are not aggressive and spend much of the day picking food from the water flow.

## Tank setup & water parameters

Provide a mature, well-established marine tank with plenty of live rock offering caves and overhangs. Firefish need hiding spots they can retreat into and even sleep in. A sand bed suits their natural behaviour.

Aim for these stable parameters:

- Temperature: 72-78 F (22-26 C)
- Salinity: 1.020-1.025 specific gravity
- pH: 8.1-8.4
- Ammonia and nitrite: 0 ppm; nitrate kept low

A tight-fitting lid is essential. Firefish are notorious jumpers and will leap through small gaps when startled, so cover all openings.

## Diet & feeding

Firefish are carnivorous micro-predators. In the wild they feed on zooplankton and small crustaceans drifting in the current. In the aquarium, offer a varied diet of frozen mysis shrimp, brine shrimp, finely chopped seafood, and quality marine pellets or flakes. Feed small amounts once or twice daily. A well-fed Firefish stays out in the open more and shows brighter colour.

## Tank mates & compatibility

Choose calm, non-aggressive companions such as clownfish, small wrasses, gobies, and peaceful blennies. Avoid large or boisterous fish that will bully or eat them. Firefish are reef-safe and will not harm corals or invertebrates.

A single Firefish is usually best for smaller tanks. They can be territorial toward their own kind, so keep only one per tank unless you have a large system and introduce a bonded pair simultaneously.

## Health & common issues

Firefish are generally hardy when water quality is stable. The most common cause of loss is jumping out of an uncovered tank rather than disease. They can be susceptible to marine ich (white spots) and other parasites if stressed or kept in fluctuating conditions, so quarantine new arrivals and maintain steady parameters.

Watch for faded colour, hiding excessively, clamped fins, or refusal to eat, which can indicate stress, poor water, or illness. Slow, careful acclimation and a peaceful environment go a long way toward keeping them healthy.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q: Do I really need a lid for a Firefish Goby?**
A: Yes. Firefish are exceptional jumpers and will leap through even small gaps when frightened. A secure, tight-fitting lid is the single most important step to keeping one alive.

**Q: Can I keep more than one Firefish together?**
A: It is risky in small tanks, as they can be aggressive toward their own species. Keep a single fish per tank, or only a confirmed bonded pair in a larger aquarium introduced at the same time.

## Conclusion

The Firefish Goby is a colourful, peaceful, and beginner-friendly marine fish that brings constant movement to reef and nano tanks. With stable water, a secure lid, plenty of rock hideaways, and a varied carnivorous diet, this hardy little dartfish will reward you with vivid colour and charming behaviour for years.
