The Dogo Argentino is a large, muscular working breed developed in Argentina during the 1920s by Dr. Antonio Nores Martínez, who set out to create a brave, athletic dog for big-game hunting and companionship. Recognizable by its all-white coat and powerful build, the Dogo is loyal and protective, but its strength and drive make it best suited to experienced, committed owners.

## Appearance & temperament

The Dogo Argentino is unmistakable for its solid white, short coat, a muscular frame, broad head and strong jaws. Males typically stand 60–69 cm tall and weigh 35–45 kg, with females slightly smaller. A single small dark patch on the head is permitted in the breed standard, but the body remains white.

In temperament, the Dogo is courageous, affectionate with its family and deeply loyal. It forms strong bonds and can be gentle and patient with children it has grown up with. However, the breed has a strong protective instinct and prey drive, so early, consistent socialization is essential. The Dogo is intelligent and eager to please but needs a calm, confident handler.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a high-quality, protein-rich diet formulated for large, active breeds. As a powerful working dog, the Dogo benefits from balanced nutrition that supports lean muscle and joint health. Divide daily food into two meals rather than one large serving to reduce the risk of bloat, a serious concern in deep-chested breeds. Avoid heavy exercise immediately before or after eating. Monitor body condition closely, since excess weight stresses the joints. Always provide fresh water, and consult your veterinarian about appropriate portions for your dog's age and activity level.

## Exercise & training

The Dogo Argentino is an athletic, high-energy breed that needs substantial daily exercise: long walks, jogging, structured play and mental challenges. A bored or under-exercised Dogo can become destructive. Because of its size and strength, leash training and reliable recall are essential from puppyhood.

Begin obedience training and socialization early, exposing the dog to many people, animals and environments. Use firm, positive, reward-based methods; harsh handling can damage trust. A securely fenced yard is important, as is supervision around unfamiliar dogs given the breed's protective nature.

## Grooming & care

Grooming the Dogo is straightforward thanks to its short, smooth coat. Weekly brushing removes loose hair and keeps the coat healthy; the breed sheds moderately year-round. Bathe only as needed. The white coat and skin can be prone to sunburn, so limit prolonged sun exposure and provide shade. Routine care includes regular nail trimming, dental brushing and weekly ear checks to prevent infection.

## Health & lifespan

With good care, the Dogo Argentino typically lives 9–15 years. The breed is generally robust but predisposed to certain conditions. Pigment-related deafness is the most notable, affecting some white-coated dogs; responsible breeders test puppies' hearing. Other concerns include hip dysplasia, hypothyroidism and, as in many deep-chested breeds, gastric bloat. Choosing a reputable breeder who screens breeding stock, plus routine veterinary checkups, supports a long, healthy life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Dogo Argentinos good family dogs?**
Yes, with the right owner. They are loyal and affectionate with their families and can be excellent companions, but they need experienced handling, early socialization and consistent training to thrive in a home.

**Why are some Dogo Argentinos deaf?**
Deafness in the breed is linked to the genes that produce the white coat. It can affect one or both ears. Reputable breeders test puppies so owners know in advance and can plan training accordingly.

## Conclusion

The Dogo Argentino is a striking, powerful and devoted breed that rewards dedicated owners with loyalty and companionship. It needs plenty of exercise, early socialization, firm positive training and attentive health care. For an experienced owner ready to meet those needs, the Dogo can be a remarkable lifelong partner.
