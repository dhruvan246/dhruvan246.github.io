The Spinone Italiano is one of Italy's oldest pointing breeds, a versatile gundog prized for its methodical hunting style and famously affectionate, easygoing nature. With its dense wiry coat, soulful expression and signature bushy eyebrows and beard, the Spinone combines rugged working ability with the temperament of a devoted family companion.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
|---|---|
| Size | Large; typically about 23–28 inches at the shoulder |
| Lifespan | Around 10–12 years |
| Coat | Dense, wiry single coat with eyebrows, beard and moustache |
| Energy | Moderate; steady stamina rather than frantic bursts |
| Good with kids | Yes; patient and gentle with children |

## Appearance & temperament

The Spinone is a solidly built, squarely proportioned dog with a relaxed, almost soft expression. Its harsh, close-lying coat may appear in white, white with orange or brown markings, and a roan patterning, and the breed's distinctive facial furnishings give it a wise, gentle look. Loose skin and a deliberate, trotting gait add to its rustic appeal.

Temperamentally, the Spinone is known for being mellow, sociable and deeply attached to its people. It tends to be friendly toward strangers and other dogs, patient with children, and sensitive to harsh handling. While calm indoors, it remains an alert and capable hunting partner outdoors.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food appropriate to the Spinone's age, size and activity level. As a large breed, puppies benefit from a large-breed growth formula to support steady, controlled development. Divide daily food into two meals and measure portions to maintain a lean body condition, since excess weight strains joints.

Because deep-chested breeds can be at risk of bloat, avoid heavy exercise immediately before and after meals. Always provide fresh water, and consult your veterinarian about the right diet and feeding amounts for your individual dog.

## Exercise & training

The Spinone has moderate but genuine exercise needs. Daily walks, free running in a secure area, and opportunities to use its nose keep it physically and mentally satisfied. As a water-loving gundog, it often enjoys swimming and retrieving games. Without enough activity and companionship it can become bored or restless.

Training should be patient, consistent and reward-based. The Spinone is intelligent but can be independent and sensitive, so gentle, positive methods work far better than pressure. Early socialization and basic obedience help this people-oriented breed become a well-mannered companion.

## Grooming & care

The wiry coat is relatively low-maintenance but not wash-and-wear. Weekly brushing removes loose hair and debris, and periodic hand-stripping or tidying helps preserve the harsh texture. The beard and facial furnishings can trap food, water and dirt, so wiping the face regularly keeps the dog cleaner and more comfortable.

Routine care also includes checking and cleaning the ears, since drop ears can retain moisture, along with regular nail trims and dental care.

## Health & lifespan

The Spinone Italiano is generally a hardy breed with a typical lifespan of around 10 to 12 years. As with many large dogs, responsible breeders screen for hip and elbow dysplasia. The breed's deep chest means owners should be aware of the signs of bloat (gastric dilatation-volvulus), a sudden emergency. Eye conditions and ear infections are also worth monitoring.

Regular veterinary checkups, weight management and routine preventive care support a long, comfortable life. Choosing a breeder who health-tests their stock reduces the risk of inherited problems.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Spinone Italiano a good family dog?**
Yes. The breed is well known for being gentle, patient and affectionate, generally getting along well with children and other pets when properly socialized.

**Does the Spinone need a lot of grooming?**
Its wiry coat is fairly easy to maintain with weekly brushing and occasional hand-stripping, but the beard and face need regular wiping to stay clean.

## Conclusion

The Spinone Italiano offers a rare blend of capable gundog and calm, loving housemate. Given moderate daily exercise, patient training, basic coat care and attentive health monitoring, this gentle Italian breed rewards its family with steady devotion and an easygoing presence for years to come.
