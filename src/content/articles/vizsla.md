Sleek, golden-rust and full of energy, the Vizsla is a Hungarian gundog famous for being a "velcro dog" — it wants to be with you constantly. Affectionate and athletic, the Vizsla thrives with an active, present owner.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (18–30 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–15 years |
| Coat | Short, smooth, rust-coloured |
| Energy | Very high |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Vizslas are lean and graceful with a short golden-rust coat. They are loving, sensitive and intensely bonded to their people — they don't cope well with being left alone and can develop separation anxiety.

## Exercise & training

- Need **a great deal of daily exercise** — running, hiking, fetch, dog sports.
- Highly trainable and eager to please, but sensitive — use gentle, positive methods.

## Grooming & care

- Very easy — the short coat needs only occasional brushing.
- They feel the cold (low body fat, thin coat) — a coat helps in winter.

## Health & lifespan

Generally healthy and athletic. Watch for hip dysplasia, epilepsy, eye conditions and certain cancers. Choose health-tested lines and keep up regular vet care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can Vizslas be left alone?**
Not happily — they're prone to separation anxiety and need company and activity.

**Are they good for first-time owners?**
Only if you can meet their high exercise and companionship needs.

## Conclusion

The Vizsla is an affectionate, athletic and devoted companion for an active owner who's home a lot. Meet their exercise and togetherness needs, and you'll have a loving, loyal friend.
