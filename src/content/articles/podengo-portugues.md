The Podengo Portugues is an ancient Portuguese hunting hound prized for its agility, alert ears and lively, independent character. It comes in three sizes — small, medium and large — with the medium variety being a versatile, athletic companion that thrives with an active family.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (roughly 16–22 kg, 40–54 cm tall) |
| Lifespan | 12–15 years |
| Coat | Smooth or wire, both short to medium |
| Energy | High — built for endurance and the chase |
| Good with kids | Yes, with supervision and early socialisation |

## Appearance & temperament

The medium Podengo is a lean, square-built hound with a wedge-shaped head and large, erect, triangular ears that give it a constantly alert expression. The coat appears in two types: a short, dense smooth coat and a harsher, slightly longer wire coat, often with a beard on the wire variety. Common colours include shades of yellow and fawn, sometimes with white markings.

Bred to hunt rabbits in packs, the Podengo is intelligent, curious and spirited. It tends to be loyal and affectionate with its family yet reserved toward strangers, making it a watchful companion. Its strong prey drive and independent streak mean it likes to investigate everything around it.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced diet appropriate to the dog's age, size and activity level. As an athletic breed, the medium Podengo does well on quality food that supports lean muscle, divided into two meals a day rather than one large portion. Because they are agile and food-motivated, watch portion sizes and limit treats to prevent unwanted weight gain. Always provide fresh water, and consult your veterinarian before changing diets or adding supplements.

## Exercise & training

This is an energetic working breed that needs substantial daily activity — brisk walks, running, and games that engage both body and mind. A securely fenced area is important, as the Podengo's hunting instinct can send it chasing small animals, and many are skilled jumpers and climbers. They enjoy canine sports such as lure coursing and agility.

Training works best with patience, consistency and positive, reward-based methods. The breed is clever but independent, so keep sessions short and engaging. Early socialisation helps them grow into confident, well-mannered adults.

## Grooming & care

Grooming needs are modest for both coat types. The smooth coat requires only occasional brushing, while the wire coat benefits from a weekly brush and the occasional hand-tidying to remove dead hair. Bathe only as needed. Routine care includes regular nail trimming, tooth brushing, and checking those large ears for dirt or signs of irritation.

## Health & lifespan

The Podengo Portugues is generally a hardy, robust breed with a typical lifespan of around 12 to 15 years. There are no widespread breed-defining hereditary diseases, but like all dogs they benefit from routine veterinary check-ups, vaccinations and parasite prevention. Maintaining a healthy weight and good dental care supports long-term wellbeing. Always seek professional veterinary advice for any health concerns.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Podengo Portugues a good family dog?**
Yes. With early socialisation it is affectionate and loyal with its family and can do well with children, though its energy and prey drive mean supervision and secure spaces are wise.

**Does the Podengo Portugues need a lot of exercise?**
Yes. As an active hunting hound it needs plenty of daily physical and mental exercise; without enough activity it can become bored and restless.

## Conclusion

The medium Podengo Portugues is a spirited, intelligent and athletic hound that rewards active owners with loyalty and companionship. With consistent training, secure outdoor space, regular exercise and routine veterinary care, this hardy Portuguese breed makes a charming and long-lived family member.
