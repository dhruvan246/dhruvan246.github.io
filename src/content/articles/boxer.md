Playful, energetic and devoted, the Boxer is a fun-loving family dog that stays youthful well into adulthood. Behind the muscular, athletic body is a goofy, affectionate companion and a natural protector.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Large (25–32 kg) |
| Lifespan | 10–12 years |
| Coat | Short, smooth |
| Energy | High |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Boxers are strong and square-built with a short muzzle and an expressive face. They are exuberant, loyal and patient with children, with a protective streak that makes them good guardians. They mature slowly and stay playful for years.

## Exercise & training

- Need **plenty of daily exercise** — walks, play and games to burn their abundant energy.
- Intelligent but spirited; consistent, positive training and early socialisation are key.
- They crave companionship and don't do well left alone for long.

## Diet & nutrition

- A quality, active-dog diet portioned to keep them lean and muscular.

## Grooming & care

- Easy — weekly brushing keeps the short coat healthy.
- Their short muzzle means they can be heat-sensitive — avoid overexertion in hot weather.

## Health & lifespan

Boxers are prone to certain cancers, heart conditions (including boxer cardiomyopathy and aortic stenosis), hip dysplasia, and bloat. Buy from health-tested lines and keep up regular vet checks.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Boxers good family dogs?**
Excellent — playful, patient with kids, and protective.

**Do Boxers calm down?**
They mature slowly and stay energetic; consistent exercise helps.

## Conclusion

The Boxer is a joyful, loyal and protective companion that bonds deeply with its family. Give them exercise, training and plenty of togetherness, and you'll have a devoted, fun-loving friend.
