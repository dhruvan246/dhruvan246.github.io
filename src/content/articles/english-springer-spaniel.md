Cheerful, energetic and eager to please, the English Springer Spaniel is a classic gundog and a wonderful active family companion. Affectionate and trainable, the "Springer" loves nothing more than being busy with its people.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (18–25 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–14 years |
| Coat | Medium, feathered |
| Energy | High |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Springers are well-built with a medium feathered coat (often liver-and-white) and long ears. They are happy, affectionate and sociable — eager to please and very people-focused, disliking being left alone.

## Exercise & training

- Need plenty of daily exercise — walks, fetch, swimming and sniffing games.
- Highly trainable and eager; respond beautifully to gentle, positive methods.

## Grooming & care

- Brush several times a week; trim feathering as needed.
- **Clean the long ears regularly** to prevent infections, especially after swimming.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for ear infections, hip dysplasia, eye conditions, and certain hereditary issues. Choose health-tested lines and keep up regular ear care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Springers good family dogs?**
Yes — affectionate, trainable and great with children, for active homes.

**Do they need a lot of exercise?**
Yes — they're energetic gundogs that need daily activity and engagement.

## Conclusion

The English Springer Spaniel is a joyful, affectionate and active companion for a busy family. Give them exercise, gentle training and diligent ear care, and they'll be a devoted, happy friend.
