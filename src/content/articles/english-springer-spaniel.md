Cheerful, energetic and devoted, the English Springer Spaniel is a classic flushing gundog that has become a popular active family companion. Bred to work closely with hunters, the "Springer" is eager to please, highly trainable and happiest when it has a job to do and people to do it with.

## Appearance & temperament

The English Springer Spaniel is a compact, well-balanced dog with a moderately long, weather-resistant double coat and characteristic feathering on the ears, legs, chest and underside. Common colours include liver-and-white and black-and-white, often with tan markings or ticking. The long, low-set ears and gentle expression are hallmarks of the breed.

Temperamentally, Springers are friendly, affectionate and sociable. They form strong bonds with their families, tend to get along well with children and other dogs, and are typically more outgoing than aloof. Because they are so people-focused, they can struggle with being left alone for long periods and may become bored or anxious without enough company and activity.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food appropriate to your Springer's life stage and activity level. As an active breed, working or highly exercised individuals may need more energy than a couch-companion of the same size. Divide the daily ration into two meals and measure portions to keep your dog at a healthy weight, since excess weight adds strain to the joints. Provide constant access to fresh water, and check with your veterinarian before making major dietary changes.

## Exercise & training

This is a genuinely high-energy breed that needs substantial daily activity. Aim for a good amount of vigorous exercise each day, such as brisk walks, off-lead running in safe areas, fetch and swimming, plus sniffing and search games that engage the breed's strong nose. A bored Springer can become restless or develop nuisance behaviours.

Springers are bright and eager to please, which makes them very trainable with consistent, reward-based methods. Early socialisation and basic obedience pay off, and many excel at activities like gundog work, agility, scent work and obedience.

## Grooming & care

The medium-length coat needs regular brushing, ideally several times a week, to prevent mats and tangles, especially in the feathered areas. Many owners have the coat tidied or trimmed periodically. Because of the long, pendulous ears, ear care is especially important: check and gently clean the ears regularly, and dry them thoroughly after swimming or bathing, as the breed is prone to ear infections. Routine nail trimming, dental care and parasite prevention round out basic maintenance.

## Health & lifespan

English Springer Spaniels generally live around 12 to 14 years. Like all breeds, they have some health considerations owners should be aware of, including ear infections, hip and elbow dysplasia, and certain hereditary eye conditions. Responsible breeders screen their breeding dogs for known inherited problems. Regular veterinary check-ups, a healthy weight, dental care and diligent ear maintenance all support a long, comfortable life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are English Springer Spaniels good family dogs?**
Yes. They are affectionate, sociable and typically good with children, making them excellent companions for active households that can meet their exercise and companionship needs.

**How much exercise does an English Springer Spaniel need?**
A lot. As an energetic gundog breed, they thrive on vigorous daily activity plus mental stimulation, and they can become bored or frustrated without enough.

## Conclusion

The English Springer Spaniel is a joyful, hard-working and loving companion for an active home. Give it plenty of exercise, engaging training, regular grooming and attentive ear care, and you will have a devoted, cheerful friend for many years.
