Bred in southern Africa to track lions, the Rhodesian Ridgeback is a powerful, dignified hound named for the distinctive ridge of backward-growing hair along its spine. Loyal and independent, the Ridgeback is a devoted guardian for an experienced owner.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Large (29–41 kg) |
| Lifespan | 10–12 years |
| Coat | Short, sleek |
| Energy | High |
| Good with kids | Yes, with supervision |

## Appearance & temperament

Ridgebacks are athletic and muscular with a short wheaten coat and the trademark ridge. They are confident, independent and loyal — affectionate with family, reserved with strangers, and strong-willed.

## Exercise & training

- Need plenty of daily exercise — running, hiking; they have stamina and a prey drive.
- Intelligent but independent; consistent, positive training and **early socialisation** are essential. Best for experienced owners.

## Grooming & care

- Very easy — the short coat needs only weekly brushing.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for hip and elbow dysplasia, bloat, hypothyroidism, and dermoid sinus (a breed-specific skin condition). Choose health-tested lines and keep up regular vet care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Rhodesian Ridgebacks good for first-time owners?**
Challenging — their size, independence and strength suit experienced owners.

**Do all of them have the ridge?**
The ridge is the breed hallmark, though occasional pups are born "ridgeless."

## Conclusion

The Rhodesian Ridgeback is a dignified, athletic and loyal companion for an experienced, active owner. Provide exercise, early socialisation and consistent training, and you'll have a devoted, noble guardian.
