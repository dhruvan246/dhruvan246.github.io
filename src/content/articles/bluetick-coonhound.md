Named for its striking mottled "blue" coat, the Bluetick Coonhound is an American scent hound bred to trail and tree raccoons and other game. Tireless, devoted and famously vocal, it suits active homes that can channel its powerful nose and stamina.

## Appearance & temperament

The Bluetick's coat is its signature: a dark, heavily ticked or mottled "blue" pattern created by black speckling over a white base, usually with black and tan markings on the head and body. The build is muscular and athletic, with long ears and a deep, far-carrying voice. Temperament is affectionate, loyal and people-oriented at home, but determined, alert and single-minded on a trail. They are intelligent yet independent, and their strong prey drive and loud bawling bay are hard-wired traits to expect rather than train away.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced diet suited to a large, active dog, adjusting portions to age, workload and condition. Working or heavily exercised individuals may need more calories, while less active pets can gain weight easily, so monitor body condition. Divide food into two meals a day, keep treats modest, and provide constant fresh water. As with many deep-chested breeds, avoid heavy exercise right after large meals. Ask your veterinarian for guidance tailored to your dog.

## Exercise & training

This is a high-energy hound that needs substantial daily exercise: long walks, jogging, hiking or supervised time to use its nose. A bored, under-exercised Bluetick can become noisy and destructive. **Always exercise on a secure leash or in a fenced area** — once a scent is found, recall is unreliable and they will follow it for miles. Training should be patient, consistent and reward-based; they are smart but stubborn. Early socialisation and scent-based games or tracking work help satisfy their instincts.

## Grooming & care

The short, dense coat is low-maintenance: weekly brushing removes loose hair and keeps it healthy, with more frequent attention during seasonal shedding. Bathe only as needed. **Check and clean the long, drop ears regularly**, as the breed's floppy ears can trap moisture and lead to infection. Trim nails routinely and maintain regular dental care. After outdoor outings, inspect for ticks, burrs and minor scrapes.

## Health & lifespan

The Bluetick Coonhound is generally a hardy, healthy breed with a typical lifespan of around 11 to 12 years. Like other large hounds, it can be prone to hip dysplasia, ear infections related to its pendulous ears, and eye issues. Deep-chested dogs may also carry some risk of bloat (gastric torsion). Buying from breeders who health-test their stock, keeping your dog at a healthy weight, and scheduling routine veterinary check-ups all support a long, active life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Bluetick Coonhounds loud?**
Yes. They were bred to bay loudly on a trail, and many are vocal at home too, which can be a challenge for close neighbours.

**Can a Bluetick Coonhound be off-leash?**
Rarely safely. A strong scent easily overrides recall, so secure fencing and leashed walks are recommended.

## Conclusion

The Bluetick Coonhound is a handsome, hard-working scent hound that thrives with active owners who appreciate its nose, stamina and distinctive voice. Give it ample exercise, secure containment, consistent positive training and routine ear care, and you will have a loyal, characterful companion.
