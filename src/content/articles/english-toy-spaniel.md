Gentle, affectionate and content to be a devoted lap dog, the English Toy Spaniel is a true companion breed. Often confused with the larger Cavalier King Charles Spaniel, this smaller toy spaniel has its own distinct domed head and quiet, dignified charm.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Small (3.5–6.5 kg) |
| Lifespan | 10–12 years |
| Coat | Silky, medium-length, feathered |
| Energy | Low to moderate |
| Good with kids | Good (best with gentle, older children) |

## Appearance & temperament

The English Toy Spaniel is a compact, square-bodied toy dog with a rounded, domed head, a short upturned muzzle and large, dark, expressive eyes. The silky coat is straight or slightly wavy with feathering on the ears, legs and tail, and appears in several recognised colour varieties such as Blenheim, Prince Charles, King Charles and Ruby. Temperamentally they are calm, gentle and affectionate, bonding closely with their families. They tend to be reserved or shy around strangers and are happiest in quiet, settled households.

## Diet & nutrition

- Feed a good-quality diet appropriate for small or toy breeds, in measured portions.
- Their small size means calories add up quickly, so weigh portions and limit treats to avoid obesity.
- Provide fresh water at all times and divide food into two meals a day.
- Ask your veterinarian for advice tailored to your dog's age and weight.

## Exercise & training

- This is a low- to moderate-energy breed; short daily walks plus indoor play usually meet their needs.
- They enjoy gentle games and time with their owners but are not demanding athletes.
- Use patient, reward-based training; this sensitive breed responds poorly to harsh corrections.
- Early, gentle socialisation helps counter their natural shyness with new people and situations.

## Grooming & care

- Brush the silky coat several times a week to prevent mats and tangles, especially in the ear and leg feathering.
- Check and gently clean the long, low-set ears regularly, as they can trap moisture and debris.
- Wipe facial folds and around the eyes to keep them clean and dry.
- Trim nails routinely and maintain regular dental care, as small breeds are prone to dental issues.

## Health & lifespan

The English Toy Spaniel typically lives around 10 to 12 years. As a flat-faced (brachycephalic) breed, it can be sensitive to heat and may experience breathing difficulties, so avoid overexertion in hot weather. Other considerations sometimes seen in toy spaniels include heart conditions such as mitral valve disease, patellar luxation, eye problems and dental disease. Choosing a responsible breeder who screens breeding dogs, keeping your pet at a healthy weight and scheduling regular veterinary check-ups all support a long, comfortable life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the English Toy Spaniel the same as the Cavalier King Charles Spaniel?**
No. They are separate breeds. The English Toy Spaniel is smaller with a more domed head and shorter muzzle, while the Cavalier is larger with a flatter head and longer nose.

**Are English Toy Spaniels good apartment dogs?**
Yes. Their small size, low exercise needs and quiet nature make them well suited to apartments, provided they get daily attention and gentle activity.

## Conclusion

The English Toy Spaniel is a loving, peaceful companion that thrives on closeness with its people. With consistent grooming, gentle handling, weight management and attentive veterinary care for its brachycephalic and heart-health needs, this charming toy spaniel makes a devoted lifelong friend.
