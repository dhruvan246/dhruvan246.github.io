The African Grey Parrot is widely regarded as one of the most intelligent companion birds in the world. Famous for its remarkable talking ability and problem-solving skills, this medium-sized parrot forms deep bonds with its keepers but demands a serious, long-term commitment.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Size | Around 12–14 inches (medium parrot) |
| Lifespan | Commonly 40–60 years with good care |
| Noise level | Moderate; less screechy than many parrots |
| Talking ability | Exceptional; among the best mimics |
| Care level | Advanced |

## Appearance & temperament

African Greys have soft grey plumage, a pale face patch, and a distinctive bright red tail (in the Congo subspecies; the smaller Timneh has a darker maroon tail). They are sensitive, observant, and emotionally complex birds. Many bond strongly with one person and can become anxious or develop feather-plucking habits if neglected, bored, or stressed. They thrive on routine, gentle handling, and consistent social interaction, and they read human moods keenly.

## Housing & cage setup

Provide the largest cage you can, with bar spacing suitable for a medium parrot so the head cannot become trapped. The cage should allow full wing movement and room for several perches of varying diameters and natural textures to support foot health. Include sturdy toys, a feeding station, and a quiet sleeping area. Place the cage in a family room away from drafts, direct sun, and kitchen fumes (non-stick cookware fumes are deadly to birds). Daily out-of-cage time in a safe, supervised space is essential.

## Diet & nutrition

A balanced diet centers on a high-quality formulated pellet, supplemented with a wide variety of fresh vegetables and smaller amounts of fruit. Offer healthy seeds and nuts sparingly as treats. African Greys are prone to calcium and vitamin deficiencies, so dietary variety matters. Always provide fresh, clean water. Never feed avocado, chocolate, caffeine, alcohol, or salty and sugary human foods, all of which are toxic or harmful to parrots.

## Enrichment & handling

These parrots need constant mental stimulation. Foraging toys, puzzles, shreddable items, and training sessions help prevent boredom and behavioral problems. Teaching tricks, words, and simple tasks channels their intelligence positively. Handle them gently and build trust slowly; rough handling erodes their confidence. Rotate toys regularly to keep interest high, and aim for several hours of interaction and supervised freedom each day.

## Health & lifespan

With excellent care, African Greys can live for many decades, making them a true lifetime pet. Watch for signs of illness such as fluffed feathers, appetite loss, lethargy, or changes in droppings, and seek an avian veterinarian promptly since birds hide sickness well. Feather-plucking often signals stress, poor diet, or boredom. Routine wellness checks with a vet experienced in birds support a long, healthy life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are African Grey Parrots good for beginners?** Not usually. Their intelligence, sensitivity, long lifespan, and high care needs make them better suited to experienced, committed keepers.

**Are African Grey Parrots legal to keep?** Legality varies by region, and some areas require permits because of conservation protections. Always check local laws and buy only from reputable sources, as they need specialist care.

## Conclusion

The African Grey Parrot is a brilliant, affectionate companion that rewards dedicated owners with decades of interaction and remarkable communication. Anyone considering one should be ready to provide spacious housing, a varied diet, daily enrichment, and ongoing specialist care for the bird's entire long life.
