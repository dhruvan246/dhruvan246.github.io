The Peruvian Inca Orchid is an elegant, ancient sighthound from Peru, prized for its smooth, mostly hairless skin and sensitive, devoted nature. Often called the Peruvian Hairless Dog (or "Perro sin Pelo del Perú"), the breed comes in both hairless and coated varieties and has accompanied Andean peoples for centuries.

## Appearance & temperament

The Peruvian Inca Orchid has a slender, athletic frame built for speed, much like other sighthounds. The hairless variety has soft, warm skin that may carry tufts of hair on the head, feet, and tail, while the coated variety has a short, close coat. Skin color ranges from pink to dark brown or mottled. Bred in three size classes, the medium type stands roughly 16 to 20 inches at the shoulder.

Temperamentally, this breed is lively, affectionate, and loyal, bonding closely with its family. It can be reserved or wary around strangers and benefits from early socialization. Intelligent and alert, it makes a good companion and watchful housemate, though its sensitive nature means harsh handling should be avoided.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food suited to your dog's age, size, and activity level. Because the breed is athletic and lean by nature, monitor portions to maintain a trim, healthy weight rather than letting ribs disappear under excess fat. Fresh water should always be available. Some owners divide the daily ration into two meals to support steady energy. Consult your veterinarian about specific amounts and any dietary adjustments.

## Exercise & training

The Peruvian Inca Orchid is energetic and enjoys daily walks plus opportunities to run safely in a secure area. As a sighthound, it may chase small moving animals, so a fenced space or leash is wise. Mental stimulation through games and gentle training keeps this clever dog content.

Training works best with positive, reward-based methods and patience. The breed's sensitivity means it responds poorly to scolding but well to encouragement and consistency.

## Grooming & care

Hairless dogs need particular skin care. Protect bare skin from sunburn with shade and pet-safe sunblock, and shield the dog from cold with a sweater in chilly weather. Bathe occasionally and keep skin clean to prevent blackheads or irritation, applying a gentle moisturizer if the skin becomes dry. The coated variety needs only routine brushing. For all dogs, trim nails, clean ears, and care for teeth regularly.

## Health & lifespan

The Peruvian Inca Orchid is generally a hardy breed with a typical lifespan of about 12 to 14 years. Hairless dogs commonly have missing or incomplete teeth, a trait linked to the hairless gene. Skin conditions such as sunburn, dryness, and acne can occur and are largely preventable with good skin care. Responsible breeders screen breeding dogs, and routine veterinary checkups help catch issues early.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Peruvian Inca Orchids hypoallergenic?**
No dog is fully hypoallergenic, but the hairless variety sheds very little and may suit some allergy-sensitive households better than heavily shedding breeds.

**Do Peruvian Inca Orchids get cold easily?**
Yes. With little or no coat, hairless dogs feel cold readily and benefit from sweaters outdoors in cool weather and warm bedding indoors.

## Conclusion

The Peruvian Inca Orchid is a graceful, affectionate companion with a rich heritage and distinctive hairless appearance. With attentive skin care, daily exercise, gentle training, and regular veterinary attention, this devoted breed can thrive as a loving family member for many years.
