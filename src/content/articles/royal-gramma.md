The Royal Gramma (*Gramma loreto*) is one of the most popular small marine fish for home saltwater aquariums. Native to the western Atlantic and Caribbean reefs, it is prized for its vivid colour, hardy nature and modest space requirements, making it a favourite for beginners moving into reef keeping.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Adult size | About 3 inches (8 cm) |
| Lifespan | Around 5 years (sometimes longer) |
| Minimum tank size | 30 gallons (114 litres) |
| Water type | Saltwater (marine) |
| Care level | Easy to moderate |

## Appearance & behaviour

The Royal Gramma has a striking two-tone body: the front half is bright violet-purple and the rear half is golden yellow, with the two colours blending in the middle. A thin dark stripe runs through the eye, and a small black spot often marks the front of the dorsal fin. Males and females look similar, though males tend to grow slightly larger.

In the wild these fish shelter in caves and overhangs, frequently swimming upside down to orient against rock surfaces, so seeing them hover at odd angles in the aquarium is completely normal. They are generally peaceful but will defend a chosen cave or crevice, flaring and gaping at intruders without usually causing real harm.

## Tank setup & water parameters

Provide a mature, well-established marine tank of at least 30 gallons with plenty of live rock arranged to create caves, ledges and hiding spots. A secure, tight-fitting lid is essential, as Royal Grammas are accomplished jumpers.

Aim for stable, reef-appropriate conditions:

- Temperature: 72-78°F (22-26°C)
- pH: 8.1-8.4
- Specific gravity: 1.020-1.025
- Ammonia and nitrite: 0 ppm; nitrate low

Steady parameters matter more than chasing exact numbers, so avoid sudden swings. Royal Grammas are fully reef-safe and will not bother corals or clean-up invertebrates.

## Diet & feeding

Royal Grammas are carnivores. In the wild they pick zooplankton from the water column and act as cleaners for larger fish. In the aquarium offer a varied meat-based diet: frozen mysis shrimp, brine shrimp, finely chopped seafood and quality marine flakes or pellets. Feed small amounts once or twice a day, only what the fish can consume in a couple of minutes, to keep water quality high. Newly added or shy individuals often respond best to enriched frozen foods.

## Tank mates & compatibility

This is a peaceful community fish that suits most reef tanks. Good companions include clownfish, firefish, gobies, blennies, cardinalfish and other calm species. Avoid large or aggressive predators such as bigger groupers, lionfish or eels that may eat them.

House only one Royal Gramma per tank unless you have a large system, as they can squabble over caves with their own kind and with the similar-looking Royal Dottyback, which is more aggressive. Keeping a single specimen with non-competing tank mates avoids most conflict.

## Health & common issues

Royal Grammas are hardy but, like all marine fish, are susceptible to common saltwater ailments such as marine ich (*Cryptocaryon*) and marine velvet, especially after stress from poor water quality or rough shipping. Quarantining new arrivals before adding them to your display tank greatly reduces the risk of introducing disease.

The best prevention is stable water chemistry, a varied diet and low stress. Watch for clamped fins, rapid breathing, faded colour or white spots, which signal a problem. Address issues early through improved husbandry, and consult marine-aquarium resources or an aquatic specialist for treatment guidance rather than dosing blindly.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Royal Grammas reef safe?** Yes. They do not nip corals or harm invertebrates, making them an excellent and colourful choice for reef aquariums.

**Can I keep two Royal Grammas together?** It is risky in smaller tanks because they defend caves aggressively toward their own kind. Keep just one unless your system is large with multiple separated hiding spots.

## Conclusion

With its brilliant purple-and-gold colouring, easygoing temperament and reef-safe habits, the Royal Gramma is an outstanding choice for marine hobbyists. Give it a stable, well-cooked tank with caves to call home, a varied carnivorous diet and a secured lid, and this little Caribbean beauty will reward you with years of colour and character.
