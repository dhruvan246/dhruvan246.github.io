The Green Anole (*Anolis carolinensis*) is a small, active arboreal lizard native to the southeastern United States. Famous for its ability to shift between bright green and brown, it is an affordable, widely available reptile. While often marketed as a beginner pet, it has real care needs and is better watched than handled.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Adult size | Around 5-8 inches including tail |
| Lifespan | Roughly 4-7 years in captivity |
| Enclosure size | 10-20 gallon vertical tank for 1-2 anoles |
| Temperament | Shy, fast, easily stressed |
| Care level | Beginner to intermediate |

## Appearance & temperament

Green Anoles are slender lizards with long tails, sticky toe pads, and a pink throat fan (dewlap) that males display during courtship and territory disputes. They change color between vivid green and dull brown depending on temperature, mood, and lighting, not to match their surroundings like a chameleon.

They are diurnal and constantly on the move, climbing and basking. Anoles are nervous by nature and may drop their tail if grabbed. Males are territorial, so house only one male per enclosure to avoid fighting.

## Habitat & enclosure

Because anoles climb, choose a tall, well-ventilated terrarium rather than a wide one. A single anole can live in a 10-gallon vertical tank, while a small group benefits from 20 gallons or more. Provide plenty of branches, vines, and foliage (live or artificial) for climbing and hiding.

Use a substrate that holds humidity, such as coconut fiber or cypress mulch. A secure, escape-proof lid is essential, as these agile lizards can slip through small gaps.

## Heating, lighting & humidity

Create a thermal gradient with a basking spot around 85-90°F (29-32°C) and a cooler end in the mid-70s°F (around 24°C). Temperatures can drop slightly at night. Use a basking bulb during the day and avoid overheating the small enclosure.

Green Anoles need UVB lighting to process calcium and prevent metabolic bone disease; replace UVB bulbs as the manufacturer recommends. Maintain humidity around 60-70% by misting once or twice daily, which also provides water droplets the anoles drink from leaves.

## Diet & feeding

Green Anoles are insectivores. Offer appropriately sized live insects such as crickets, small roaches, fruit flies, and the occasional waxworm as a treat. Feed prey no larger than the space between the lizard's eyes.

Dust insects with a calcium supplement at most feedings and a vitamin/mineral supplement less often, following product guidance. Adults typically eat several insects every other day; younger anoles eat more frequently. Provide drinking water through misting and a shallow dish.

## Handling & health

Green Anoles tolerate gentle, occasional handling at best and are easily stressed by frequent contact. Never grab the tail, and support the body when interacting. Many owners enjoy them as display animals rather than hands-on pets.

Watch for warning signs such as persistent dark coloration, lethargy, weight loss, sunken eyes, swollen limbs, or refusal to eat, which can indicate poor husbandry or illness. Metabolic bone disease, dehydration, and mouth infections are common when temperatures, UVB, or humidity are wrong. Consult a reptile-experienced veterinarian when problems arise.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Green Anoles legal to keep?** They are widely available and commonly kept, but laws on capturing or selling reptiles vary by region. Check local regulations and buy captive-bred animals from reputable sources.

**Can Green Anoles live together?** Females and juveniles often cohabit peacefully, but two males will fight. Keep only one male per enclosure and provide enough space and hiding spots.

## Conclusion

The Green Anole is a lively, low-cost reptile that rewards careful husbandry. With a tall planted enclosure, proper heat, UVB, humidity, and a varied insect diet, these color-changing lizards thrive as fascinating display pets best appreciated through observation rather than handling.
