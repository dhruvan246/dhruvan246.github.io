The Smooth Fox Terrier is a lively, fearless English breed developed to flush foxes from their dens during the hunt. Quick, confident and endlessly curious, it packs a big personality into a compact, athletic frame.

## Overview

| Trait | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Small to medium; about 15–18 lb (7–8 kg), up to roughly 15.5 in tall |
| Lifespan | 12–15 years |
| Coat | Short, flat, dense and weather-resistant smooth coat |
| Energy | High |
| Good with kids | Yes, especially with older, active children |

## Appearance & temperament

The Smooth Fox Terrier has a distinctive long, wedge-shaped head, dark alert eyes and small V-shaped folded ears. Its body is balanced and muscular, set on straight legs, with a high-set tail traditionally carried upright. The coat is predominantly white, often marked with black, tan or both.

Temperamentally, this is a bold, alert and tireless companion. Bred to work independently underground, the breed is brave and self-assured, with a strong prey drive and a tendency to dig and bark. Smooth Fox Terriers are affectionate and playful with their families but can be feisty toward unfamiliar dogs.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food appropriate for the breed's life stage and high activity level. Most adults do well on two measured meals a day. Because Smooth Fox Terriers are compact and food-motivated, they gain weight easily, so monitor body condition and keep treats modest. Always provide fresh water, and consult your veterinarian about portion sizes for puppies, seniors or less active individuals.

## Exercise & training

This is an energetic breed that needs daily vigorous activity, including brisk walks, off-leash running in safe areas, fetch and interactive games. Mental stimulation is just as important; puzzle toys, scent games and trick training help prevent boredom-driven digging and barking.

Smooth Fox Terriers are smart but independent and can be stubborn, so use consistent, reward-based training and start socialization early. A securely fenced yard is essential, as their hunting instinct can send them chasing or tunneling. Keep them leashed near small animals.

## Grooming & care

The smooth coat is low-maintenance. Weekly brushing with a bristle brush or hound glove removes loose hair and keeps the coat shiny; expect moderate seasonal shedding. Bathe only as needed. Routine care includes regular nail trims, dental brushing and checking the ears. Their short coat offers little protection from cold, so a sweater may help in harsh winter weather.

## Health & lifespan

Smooth Fox Terriers are generally hardy and typically live 12 to 15 years. Like many breeds, they can be prone to certain conditions, including patellar luxation, lens luxation and other eye disorders, and occasional deafness. Responsible breeders screen breeding stock for hereditary issues. Routine veterinary checkups, dental care, parasite prevention and weight management support a long, healthy life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Smooth Fox Terriers good apartment dogs?**
They can adapt to apartments if given plenty of daily exercise and mental enrichment, but their barking and high energy mean they thrive best with active owners and access to safe outdoor space.

**Do Smooth Fox Terriers shed a lot?**
They shed moderately year-round with some seasonal increase. Weekly brushing keeps loose hair under control, and their short coat needs far less grooming than many wire-coated terriers.

## Conclusion

The Smooth Fox Terrier is a spirited, intelligent and devoted companion for active households. With consistent training, secure containment and plenty of exercise, this charismatic little hunter rewards its family with years of energetic, affectionate company.
