The Portuguese Sheepdog, known in its homeland as the Cão da Serra de Aires, is a medium-sized herding breed developed in the Serra de Aires region of southern Portugal. Prized by shepherds for its agility and devotion, it earned the affectionate nickname "monkey dog" for its lively expression and shaggy coat. Today it is a spirited, loyal companion for active homes.

## Appearance & temperament

The Portuguese Sheepdog is a rustic, well-proportioned dog typically weighing 17–27 kg (37–60 lb). Its most distinctive feature is a long, single-layered coat with a goat-like texture that forms a beard, moustache, and bushy eyebrows, often partly covering the eyes. Coat colors include yellow, chestnut, grey, fawn, wolf-grey, and black, frequently with tan markings.

Temperamentally, this breed is intelligent, alert, and intensely devoted to its family. It tends to bond closely with one person while remaining affectionate toward the whole household. Lively and watchful, it makes an attentive watchdog. It can be wary of strangers and somewhat strong-willed, so consistent leadership and early socialization help it become a balanced companion.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a high-quality, complete dog food appropriate to the dog's age, weight, and activity level. As an energetic working breed, an active Portuguese Sheepdog benefits from a diet with adequate protein and fat to fuel its stamina. Most adults do well on two measured meals a day rather than free-feeding.

Monitor body condition closely, as the dense coat can hide weight gain. Adjust portions for less active or older dogs to prevent obesity, which strains joints. Always provide fresh water, and introduce any diet changes gradually. Consult your veterinarian for guidance tailored to your individual dog.

## Exercise & training

This is a high-energy breed that needs substantial daily activity, ideally an hour or more of brisk walks, running, play, or dog sports. Bred to work all day on the move, it thrives with a job to do and can become bored or destructive without enough physical and mental stimulation.

Highly intelligent and eager to please its person, the Portuguese Sheepdog excels at obedience, agility, and herding trials. It responds best to positive, reward-based training that channels its quick mind. Begin socialization early to temper its natural wariness, and keep sessions varied to hold its lively attention.

## Grooming & care

The breed's long, goat-textured coat requires regular brushing, roughly once or twice a week, to prevent mats and tangles, with extra attention behind the ears and on the legs. The coat is not typically clipped, as its natural shaggy look is a breed hallmark. Seasonal shedding is moderate.

Bathe only as needed to preserve natural coat oils. Check and clean the ears regularly, trim nails as they grow, and maintain dental hygiene with routine tooth brushing. Keep the hair around the eyes tidy so the dog can see clearly.

## Health & lifespan

The Portuguese Sheepdog is generally a hardy, robust breed with a typical lifespan of around 12 to 14 years. As with many active medium-sized dogs, responsible breeders screen for hip dysplasia and eye conditions. Its active build and modest size help it stay sound into older age.

Routine veterinary checkups, vaccinations, parasite prevention, dental care, and weight management support a long, healthy life. Keeping the coat and ears clean reduces the risk of skin and ear irritation.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Portuguese Sheepdog a good family pet?**
Yes. It is affectionate and loyal, and does well with children when socialized early. Its high energy suits active families who can provide regular exercise and mental engagement.

**Does the Portuguese Sheepdog need a lot of grooming?**
It needs moderate, consistent grooming. Brushing once or twice weekly keeps the long, goat-like coat free of mats, and the coat is left natural rather than clipped.

## Conclusion

The Portuguese Sheepdog is a spirited, intelligent herder that rewards an active owner with deep loyalty and an engaging personality. With daily exercise, early socialization, consistent training, and regular coat care, this charming "monkey dog" makes a devoted and lively companion.
