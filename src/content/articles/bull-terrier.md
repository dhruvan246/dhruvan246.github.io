Unmistakable with its egg-shaped head and triangular eyes, the Bull Terrier is a muscular, comical and energetic breed. Playful and affectionate, the "Bully" is a clownish companion that thrives on attention and activity.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (22–38 kg) |
| Lifespan | 11–14 years |
| Coat | Short, flat |
| Energy | High |
| Good with kids | Yes, with supervision and exercise |

## Appearance & temperament

Bull Terriers are powerfully built with a distinctive long, curved head. They are playful, mischievous and devoted — full of energy and personality, often described as "three-year-old children in dog suits."

## Exercise & training

- Need plenty of daily exercise plus mental stimulation; they get bored and destructive without it.
- Strong-willed but trainable; consistent, positive training and early socialisation are important.

## Grooming & care

- Easy — the short coat needs only weekly brushing.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for deafness (especially in whites — BAER testing), heart and kidney conditions, skin allergies, and luxating patella. Choose health-tested lines and keep up regular vet care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Bull Terriers good family dogs?**
Yes for active families who can supervise and provide exercise; they're playful and devoted.

**Do they need lots of exercise?**
Yes — they're energetic and need physical and mental activity to stay balanced.

## Conclusion

The Bull Terrier is a comical, energetic and affectionate companion bursting with personality. Provide exercise, training and plenty of engagement, and this lovable clown will be a devoted friend.
