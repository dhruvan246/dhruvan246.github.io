The Swedish Lapphund (Svensk Lapphund) is one of Sweden's native breeds, a spitz-type dog developed by the Sami people of Lapland to herd and guard reindeer. Bright, hardy, and devoted, it is sometimes called the "black beauty of Norrland" and remains a rare but cherished companion today.

## Appearance & temperament

The Swedish Lapphund is a compact, square-built spitz with erect triangular ears, a profuse ruff, and a curled tail carried over the back. The breed is most often solid black, though dark brown and a small amount of white markings occur. Weight typically falls around 13-20 kg (28-45 lb).

Temperamentally, this is an alert, friendly, and lively dog with a strong herding heritage. It is known for being attentive and quick to learn, deeply attached to its family, and naturally watchful, which makes it an effective alarm barker. Bred to work alongside people in harsh conditions, it thrives on companionship and dislikes being isolated for long periods.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food appropriate to your dog's age, size, and activity level. As an energetic working breed, an active Swedish Lapphund may need calorie-dense, protein-rich nutrition, while a less active or older dog needs careful portion control to prevent weight gain.

Divide the daily amount into two meals and measure portions rather than free-feeding. Always provide fresh water, and adjust intake seasonally as activity changes. Your veterinarian can recommend the right diet and quantity for your individual dog.

## Exercise & training

This is a high-energy, intelligent breed that needs substantial daily exercise plus mental stimulation. Long walks, off-lead runs in safe areas, hiking, and active play help burn energy. Without enough outlet, a bored Lapphund may bark excessively or become restless.

The breed excels at dog sports such as obedience, agility, herding, and tracking, which channel its working drive. Training should be positive, consistent, and engaging, since these dogs are sensitive and respond poorly to harsh methods. Early socialisation helps temper their natural wariness of strangers and their tendency to vocalise.

## Grooming & care

The dense double coat sheds steadily and "blows" heavily once or twice a year. Brush at least weekly, increasing to several times a week during seasonal shedding, to remove loose undercoat and prevent matting. The coat is fairly self-cleaning, so bathe only when necessary.

Check and clean the ears regularly, trim nails as needed, and maintain a routine dental-care habit. The thick coat suits cold climates well; in hot weather, provide shade, water, and exercise during cooler parts of the day.

## Health & lifespan

The Swedish Lapphund is generally a robust, healthy breed with a typical lifespan of about 12-14 years. As with many breeds, hip dysplasia can occur, and a hereditary eye condition (progressive retinal atrophy) has been documented, so responsible breeders screen their stock.

Routine veterinary checkups, vaccinations, parasite prevention, dental care, and weight management all support a long, healthy life. Choosing a puppy from health-tested parents reduces the risk of inherited problems.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Swedish Lapphunds good family dogs?**
Yes. They are affectionate, loyal, and good with children when properly socialised and supervised, though their energy and alertness suit active households best.

**Do Swedish Lapphunds bark a lot?**
They can. Bred as watchful herders, they are naturally vocal, so consistent training and adequate exercise are important to keep barking manageable.

## Conclusion

The Swedish Lapphund is a hardy, intelligent, and devoted spitz with deep roots in Lapland's reindeer-herding tradition. Given plenty of exercise, mental challenge, regular coat care, and close human companionship, this rare breed makes a loving and spirited partner for an active home.
