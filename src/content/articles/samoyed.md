With their fluffy white coats and famous "Sammy smile," Samoyeds are joyful, friendly dogs bred to work and live closely with people in the Arctic. Sociable and gentle, they make wonderful — if high-maintenance — companions.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (16–30 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–14 years |
| Coat | Thick, fluffy double coat |
| Energy | High |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Samoyeds have a dense white double coat and an upturned mouth that gives their trademark smile. They are friendly, gentle and sociable — bred to live among people and unhappy when isolated.

## Exercise & training

- Need plenty of daily exercise — walks, play and activity.
- Intelligent but independent; consistent, positive training and early socialisation help. They can be vocal.

## Grooming — a big commitment

- **Brush several times a week**, daily during their dramatic seasonal shed.
- The coat is high-maintenance; never shave the double coat.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for hip dysplasia, eye conditions (PRA), and a breed-specific kidney disease (hereditary nephritis). Choose health-tested lines and keep up regular vet care. They tolerate cold far better than heat.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Samoyeds shed a lot?**
Enormously, especially seasonally — heavy grooming is essential.

**Are they friendly?**
Very — sociable, gentle and great with families; not good guard dogs.

## Conclusion

The Samoyed is a joyful, friendly and beautiful companion for an active owner ready for serious grooming. Keep up the brushing, give them exercise and company, and you'll have a smiling, devoted friend.
