Famous as the gentle alpine rescue dog, the Saint Bernard is a massive, calm and supremely affectionate companion. Behind that enormous frame is a patient, loving family dog with a soft heart.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Giant (55–90 kg) |
| Lifespan | 8–10 years |
| Coat | Smooth or rough, dense |
| Energy | Low to moderate |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Saint Bernards are powerfully built giants with a kind, expressive face and a dense coat (smooth or rough). They are calm, friendly and gentle — patient with children and devoted to their families.

## Diet & nutrition

- A quality large/giant-breed diet; puppies need giant-breed formula for slow, healthy growth.
- Feed measured meals and **take bloat precautions** (raised feeding, no exercise right after meals).

## Exercise

- Moderate walks and gentle play; they're not high-energy.
- Avoid heat and high-impact exercise — their size and coat make them prone to overheating.

## Grooming & care

- Brush 2–3 times a week; expect heavy seasonal shedding.
- **Be prepared for drooling** — keep a cloth handy.
- Large, supportive bedding protects their joints.

## Health & lifespan

Giant-breed health needs apply: hip and elbow dysplasia, bloat (GDV), heart conditions, and a shorter lifespan. Choose health-tested lines, keep them lean and cool, and maintain regular vet care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Saint Bernards drool a lot?**
Yes — especially the loose-jowled type. It comes with the breed.

**Do they need a lot of space?**
Room to move and sturdy furniture help; they're calm but very large.

## Conclusion

The Saint Bernard is a magnificent, gentle and devoted companion for a family with the space and means to care for a giant breed. Feed carefully, keep them cool, and you'll have a loving, legendary friend.
