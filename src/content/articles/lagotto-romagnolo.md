The Lagotto Romagnolo is an ancient Italian breed from the Romagna region, originally bred as a water retriever and later prized as a specialist truffle-hunting dog. Instantly recognizable by its dense, woolly curls, this medium-sized working dog combines a keen nose, a tireless work ethic and an affectionate, eager-to-please nature that makes it a devoted companion.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
|---|---|

| Energy | Moderate to high; needs daily activity |
| Good with kids | Yes; affectionate and playful with children |

## Appearance & temperament

The Lagotto is a sturdy, well-proportioned dog of rustic, robust build. Its hallmark is a thick coat of tight, ring-shaped curls covering the body, including the head, with characteristic eyebrows, whiskers and a beard. Common colors include off-white, white with brown or orange markings, solid brown in various shades, and roan, sometimes with a darker mask.

Temperamentally, the Lagotto is keen, intelligent and undemanding in the home. It is affectionate toward its family, generally good with children, and naturally driven to use its remarkable sense of smell. Bred to search rather than to be aggressive, it tends to be alert and can be reserved with strangers, making it a watchful but not typically sharp companion.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food suited to the Lagotto's age, size and activity level. Divide the daily amount into two meals and measure portions, since this food-motivated breed can gain weight easily if overfed or under-exercised. Maintaining a lean body condition supports joint health and overall wellbeing.

Active working or sporting Lagottos may need more calories than companion dogs that live a quieter life. Always provide fresh water, introduce any diet changes gradually, and consult your veterinarian about the right food and feeding amounts for your individual dog.

## Exercise & training

The Lagotto has moderate to high exercise needs and thrives on having a job to do. Daily walks, play, and scent-based activities such as nose work, hide-and-seek games or scent trails satisfy both body and mind. A bored Lagotto with too little stimulation may dig or develop other unwanted habits, so mental work matters as much as physical exercise.

This breed is highly trainable, quick to learn and responsive to positive, reward-based methods. Early socialization and consistent, gentle training help channel its energy and intelligence. Its strong nose and natural retrieving instincts make it well suited to scent sports and trick training.

## Grooming & care

The curly, woolly coat does not shed much but requires regular care to prevent matting. The curls should be combed out and the coat kept relatively short through periodic clipping; left to grow, it can felt into cords or tangles. Many owners have the coat professionally trimmed several times a year and check regularly for mats behind the ears and on the legs.

Routine care also includes cleaning the ears, since hair and moisture can build up under the drop ears, along with regular nail trims and dental care.

## Health & lifespan

The Lagotto Romagnolo is generally a healthy, long-lived breed, often reaching around 15 to 17 years. Responsible breeders screen for hip dysplasia and for inherited conditions linked to the breed, including certain forms of juvenile epilepsy and a storage-disease type condition, as well as some eye disorders. DNA testing is available for several of these.

Regular veterinary checkups, weight management, dental care and routine preventive care support a long, comfortable life. Choosing a breeder who health-tests their breeding stock reduces the risk of inherited problems.

## Frequently asked questions

**Does the Lagotto Romagnolo shed a lot?**
No. Its woolly, curly coat sheds very little, but it is not maintenance-free and needs regular combing and periodic clipping to prevent matting.

**Is the Lagotto Romagnolo a good family dog?**
Yes. The breed is affectionate, intelligent and generally good with children, though it does best with daily exercise, mental stimulation and early socialization.

## Conclusion

The Lagotto Romagnolo is a charming blend of capable working dog and loving family companion, famous for its curly coat and extraordinary nose. Given daily exercise, engaging mental work, consistent positive training, regular grooming and attentive health care, this hardy Italian breed can offer many happy, devoted years as part of the family.
