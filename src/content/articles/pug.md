With their wrinkled faces, curly tails and comical personalities, Pugs are little companions bred purely to be loved. Affectionate, sociable and endlessly entertaining, they thrive on human company.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Small (6–8 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–15 years |
| Coat | Short, smooth (sheds a lot) |
| Energy | Low to moderate |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Pugs have a stocky body, a deeply wrinkled flat face and big soulful eyes. They are gentle, even-tempered and love being the centre of attention — wonderful family pets.

## Diet & nutrition

- Pugs **love food and gain weight easily** — measure meals carefully.
- Obesity worsens their breathing, so keep them lean.

## Exercise — keep it gentle

- Short walks and play are enough.
- **Avoid heat and overexertion** — their flat faces make breathing and cooling difficult. Walk during cool hours and never leave them in warm spaces.

## Grooming & care

- They shed a surprising amount — brush weekly.
- **Clean the facial wrinkles regularly** and dry them to prevent infection.
- Clean ears and trim nails routinely.

## Health & lifespan

Be aware of brachycephalic airway syndrome (breathing), eye injuries (their eyes protrude), skin-fold infections, and heat sensitivity. Buy from responsible breeders and keep them cool and slim.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Pugs have breathing problems?**
Many do, due to their flat faces. Keep them lean and cool to help.

**Are Pugs good for families?**
Excellent — gentle, patient and great with children.

## Conclusion

The Pug is an affectionate, comical companion that asks only for your company. Protect them from heat, keep their wrinkles clean and their weight down, and they'll fill your home with joy.
