The Emperor Scorpion (*Pandinus imperator*) is one of the most popular pet scorpions, prized for its large size, glossy black body and famously calm disposition. Native to the rainforests and savannas of West Africa, it is a hardy, low-maintenance invertebrate that suits keepers wanting an unusual but manageable display animal.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Size | Around 13-20 cm (5-8 in) including the tail |
| Lifespan | Roughly 5-8 years in captivity |
| Housing | Warm, humid terrarium with deep substrate |
| Temperament | Docile and slow-moving; rarely stings |
| Care level | Beginner to intermediate |

## Appearance & behaviour

Emperor Scorpions are among the largest scorpions in the world, with a heavy, jet-black exoskeleton, thick pincers (pedipalps) and a relatively slender tail. Under ultraviolet light their cuticle glows a striking blue-green. They are primarily nocturnal, spending the day hidden in burrows and emerging at night to hunt. Despite their intimidating look, they are remarkably gentle. They rely far more on their powerful claws than their venom, and their sting is generally mild, often compared to a bee sting, though reactions vary between individuals.

## Housing & environment

A single adult does well in a terrarium of around 38 litres (10 gallons) or larger, with more floor space than height. Provide 8-15 cm of moisture-retaining substrate such as coconut fibre or a coir-and-soil mix so the scorpion can burrow. Include cork bark, half-logs or rock caves for hiding. Maintain warm temperatures of about 24-29 C (75-84 F), using an under-tank heater on the side if needed, and keep humidity high at roughly 70-80% by lightly misting and providing a shallow water dish. Always ensure a secure, well-ventilated lid. Emperor Scorpions can sometimes be kept communally, but this requires extra space and food to avoid cannibalism.

## Diet & feeding

These scorpions are insectivorous ambush predators. Offer appropriately sized live feeder insects such as crickets, roaches (dubia), locusts and mealworms, gut-loaded before feeding. Juveniles may eat a few small insects several times a week, while adults typically need feeding only once or twice a week. Remove uneaten prey that may stress or injure a moulting scorpion. Provide fresh water at all times in a shallow dish the animal cannot drown in.

## Handling & care

Although docile, Emperor Scorpions are best treated as a look-but-don't-touch pet. Handling stresses them and risks dropping or injuring the animal, and even a calm scorpion may pinch or sting if startled. If you must move one, gently coax it into a container rather than picking it up. Use slow movements during maintenance, and take extra care after a moult, when the new exoskeleton is soft and the animal is especially vulnerable.

## Health

A healthy scorpion is active at night, eats regularly and moults cleanly. Most problems stem from incorrect conditions: low humidity can cause failed moults, while overly wet, dirty substrate encourages mould and mites. Keep the enclosure clean, remove waste and uneaten food, and maintain stable heat and humidity. Sluggishness, refusal to eat or difficulty moulting can signal husbandry issues. Specialist exotic-pet veterinarians who treat invertebrates are uncommon, so prevention through good care is essential.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Emperor Scorpions dangerous?** They are considered one of the least dangerous scorpions. Their venom is mild for most people, but anyone can have an allergic reaction, so respect them and avoid unnecessary handling.

**Do I need a permit to keep one?** Possibly. Emperor Scorpions are CITES-listed, and legality and import rules vary by country and region. Always check local laws and buy captive-bred animals from reputable sources.

## Conclusion

The Emperor Scorpion is a fascinating, beginner-friendly invertebrate that rewards keepers with years of low-key but captivating behaviour. With a warm, humid burrowing setup, a simple insect diet and a hands-off approach, this gentle giant makes a striking and surprisingly easy exotic pet for those willing to provide proper specialist care.
