The English Toy Terrier (Black & Tan) is a rare, refined toy breed descended from the old Manchester Terrier. Compact and graceful, it combines a true terrier spirit with the affectionate, easy-care nature of a lap dog, making it a devoted companion for the right household.

## Appearance & temperament

The English Toy Terrier is a small, sleek dog, typically standing about 25-30 cm (10-12 in) at the shoulder and weighing roughly 2.7-3.6 kg (6-8 lb). Its hallmark is a jet-black coat with sharply defined rich tan (mahogany) markings on the muzzle, chest, legs, and above the eyes. The breed is best known for its distinctive "candle-flame" ears, which are pointed, erect, and set high on a long, narrow head.

In temperament, this is an alert, intelligent, and affectionate breed. It bonds closely with its family and often attaches strongly to one person. Though small, it retains a keen terrier instinct and was historically a capable ratter. English Toy Terriers are generally friendly but can be reserved with strangers and make watchful little alarm dogs.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a high-quality dog food formulated for small or toy breeds, as their small mouths and high metabolism suit small kibble and energy-dense diets. Because the breed is tiny, portions are modest; measure meals carefully and divide the daily amount into two servings to maintain steady energy and avoid digestive upset.

Monitor body condition closely, since even a little extra weight is significant on such a small frame. You should be able to feel the ribs easily. Limit treats to a small fraction of daily calories, and always provide fresh water.

## Exercise & training

Despite its size, the English Toy Terrier is lively and enjoys daily activity. A couple of short walks plus some play and exploration each day usually satisfies its needs. The breed delights in chasing toys and benefits from a securely fenced area, as its terrier prey drive can prompt it to dash after small animals.

These dogs are intelligent and eager to please, responding well to positive, reward-based training. Early, gentle socialisation helps temper any wariness toward strangers and new situations. Keep sessions short and upbeat, and avoid harsh corrections, which a sensitive toy breed does not tolerate well.

## Grooming & care

Grooming needs are minimal. The short, smooth coat requires only a weekly brush or wipe with a soft cloth to keep it glossy and remove loose hair. Bathe only occasionally, when the dog is dirty.

As with most small breeds, dental care is important; brush the teeth regularly to reduce tartar. Trim nails every few weeks, check the ears, and protect this thin-coated breed from cold weather with a coat or sweater in winter.

## Health & lifespan

The English Toy Terrier is generally a healthy, long-lived breed, often reaching 12-13 years or more. Like many toy breeds, it can be prone to certain conditions, including luxating patella (slipping kneecaps), dental issues due to crowding in a small mouth, and occasionally eye problems. Some lines may carry a risk of inherited conditions affecting the heart or joints.

Choosing a responsible breeder who screens breeding stock, keeping your dog at a healthy weight, and maintaining routine veterinary checkups all support a long, comfortable life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the English Toy Terrier the same as the Manchester Terrier?**
They share a common ancestry. The English Toy Terrier is the toy-sized descendant of the old black-and-tan terrier and is closely related to the larger Manchester Terrier, but it is now recognised as a separate breed.

**Are English Toy Terriers good apartment dogs?**
Yes. Their small size, moderate exercise needs, and quiet, affectionate nature make them well suited to apartment living, provided they get daily walks and companionship.

## Conclusion

The English Toy Terrier is a charming, low-maintenance companion that pairs elegant looks with a spirited terrier heart. Devoted, alert, and easy to groom, it thrives on close human company and suits owners seeking a small, affectionate dog. With good nutrition, gentle training, and routine care, this rare breed makes a loyal lifelong friend.
