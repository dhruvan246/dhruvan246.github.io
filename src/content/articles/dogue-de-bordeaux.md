The Dogue de Bordeaux, often called the French Mastiff, is one of the oldest French breeds and an unmistakable giant with a massive, wrinkled head and devoted nature. Powerful yet affectionate, this breed forms deep bonds with its family while remaining calm and watchful at home.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Size | Large (typically 99-110 lb / 45-50 kg, 23-27 in tall) |
| Lifespan | 5-8 years |
| Coat | Short, fine, soft |
| Energy | Low to moderate |
| Good with kids | Yes, with supervision |

## Appearance & temperament

The Dogue de Bordeaux is instantly recognizable by its enormous, broad head, which is among the largest in the canine world relative to body size. The face carries deep wrinkles, a pronounced stop, and an undershot jaw, while the body is muscular, thickset, and close to the ground. The short coat comes in shades of fawn, ranging from light to rich mahogany, often with a black or brown mask.

Despite their imposing looks, these dogs are typically calm, even-tempered, and deeply loyal. They are affectionate companions who thrive on closeness to their people and can be sensitive to harsh handling. Naturally protective, they make confident watchdogs but should be well socialized from puppyhood to ensure balanced behavior.

## Diet & nutrition

A high-quality diet formulated for large or giant breeds supports the Dogue de Bordeaux's heavy frame. Because rapid growth can stress developing joints, puppies should be fed a large-breed puppy food that promotes steady, controlled growth rather than maximum size.

Portion control is essential, as this breed gains weight easily and excess pounds strain the heart and joints. Feeding two measured meals a day, rather than free-feeding, helps prevent obesity. Like many deep-chested breeds, the Dogue de Bordeaux may be prone to bloat, so avoid heavy exercise right before and after meals. Always provide fresh water, and consult your veterinarian about appropriate amounts.

## Exercise & training

This is not a high-energy breed, but the Dogue de Bordeaux still needs daily moderate exercise to stay fit and maintain a healthy weight. A couple of relaxed walks plus gentle play are usually enough. Avoid strenuous activity in hot or humid weather, as the short muzzle makes this breed prone to overheating.

Training should begin early and rely on patient, positive, consistent methods. These dogs are intelligent but can be stubborn and independent, so firm yet gentle leadership works best. Early socialization with people and other animals is key to raising a confident, well-mannered adult. Their size alone means good manners and reliable recall are important.

## Grooming & care

The short coat is low maintenance and needs only weekly brushing to control shedding and keep it healthy. The breed's defining wrinkles, however, require regular attention: clean the facial folds and skin creases routinely to prevent moisture buildup and irritation.

Dogues de Bordeaux are known to drool, especially after eating or drinking, so keeping a cloth handy is practical. Check and clean the ears, trim nails regularly, and brush the teeth to support overall health.

## Health & lifespan

The Dogue de Bordeaux has a relatively short lifespan, generally around 5 to 8 years. The breed can be prone to hip and elbow dysplasia, heart conditions such as dilated cardiomyopathy, and the breathing and heat sensitivity associated with a short muzzle. Bloat (gastric torsion) is another serious concern for this deep-chested breed.

Responsible breeding, routine veterinary checkups, weight management, and protection from extreme heat all help support a healthier life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Dogue de Bordeaux good family dogs?**
Yes. They are affectionate, loyal, and typically gentle with their families. Supervision around small children is wise given their large size and strength, and early socialization helps ensure good behavior.

**Do Dogue de Bordeaux drool a lot?**
Yes. Their loose jowls mean they are heavy droolers, particularly after eating or drinking water, so owners should expect and accept regular slobber.

## Conclusion

The Dogue de Bordeaux is a loyal, gentle giant that rewards committed owners with deep devotion. With proper diet, moderate exercise, attentive wrinkle care, and protection from heat, this powerful French Mastiff makes a calm and loving companion for the right home.
