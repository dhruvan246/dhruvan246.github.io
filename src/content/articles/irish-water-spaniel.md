The Irish Water Spaniel is the tallest of the spaniels and one of the most distinctive water dogs, instantly recognizable by its crisp, liver-colored curls and its smooth, hairless "rat tail." Bred in Ireland to flush and retrieve waterfowl, it is an athletic, clever companion that thrives with an active family.

## Overview

| Trait | Details |
|---|---|
| Size | Large; roughly 21–24 in (53–61 cm) tall, about 45–68 lb (20–31 kg) |
| Lifespan | Around 10–12 years |
| Coat | Dense, water-repellent liver curls; low-shedding |
| Energy | High |
| Good with kids | Yes, with supervision and early socialization |

## Appearance & temperament

This breed has a sturdy, well-balanced build covered in tight, crisp curls of a rich liver (puce) color, topped by a curly "topknot" and a tapering, nearly bare tail. The face and lower legs are smooth. Temperament is alert, intelligent and playful, often described as the clown of the spaniel family. Irish Water Spaniels tend to be devoted and a bit reserved with strangers, so early, positive socialization helps them grow into confident, friendly adults.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food appropriate for a large, active breed and for your dog's life stage. Divide the daily amount into two meals and adjust portions based on body condition, age and activity level, since working-line dogs may need more energy than couch companions. As a deep-chested breed, the Irish Water Spaniel may benefit from avoiding heavy exercise right around mealtimes. Keep fresh water available and ask your veterinarian for tailored feeding guidance.

## Exercise & training

This is a high-energy sporting dog that needs substantial daily exercise: long walks, running, retrieving games and, ideally, swimming, which it loves. Aim for at least an hour of activity plus mental challenges each day. Their sharp minds respond well to positive, reward-based training, but they can be sensitive and somewhat independent, so keep sessions varied, patient and consistent. Dog sports such as obedience, agility, dock diving and field work suit them well.

## Grooming & care

The curly coat needs regular attention. Brush or comb every week or two to prevent mats, paying attention to the topknot, ears and leg feathering. Many owners have the coat trimmed and shaped by a groomer every couple of months. The coat sheds little, which some allergy-prone owners appreciate, but low shedding is not a guarantee against allergies. Check and gently clean the long, hanging ears regularly to reduce moisture buildup, and keep nails trimmed and teeth brushed.

## Health & lifespan

Irish Water Spaniels are generally hardy, with a typical lifespan of about 10–12 years. Like many breeds, they can be prone to certain conditions, including hip dysplasia, eye disorders and ear infections related to their pendulous ears. Some individuals show sensitivity to certain medications, so always inform your veterinarian of the breed. Responsible breeders screen breeding dogs, and routine veterinary checkups, parasite prevention and a healthy weight support a long, comfortable life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Irish Water Spaniels hypoallergenic?** No dog is truly hypoallergenic, but their low-shedding, curly coat may be tolerated better by some allergy-prone people. Spend time with the breed before committing.

**Do Irish Water Spaniels need a lot of exercise?** Yes. They are energetic working dogs that need vigorous daily activity and mental stimulation, and they especially enjoy swimming and retrieving.

## Conclusion

The Irish Water Spaniel is a smart, spirited and affectionate water retriever best suited to active owners who can meet its exercise, training and grooming needs. Given regular activity, consistent positive training and routine veterinary care, this curly-coated companion can be a loyal and entertaining family member for many years.
