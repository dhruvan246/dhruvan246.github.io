The crested gecko (*Correlophus ciliatus*) is one of the most popular pet reptiles for beginners. Native to New Caledonia, this arboreal lizard is hardy, docile, and undemanding, thriving at room temperature on a simple powdered diet.

## Overview

| Trait | Details |
|---|---|
| Adult size | 4-4.5 inches body, 7-9 inches with tail |
| Lifespan | 15-20 years in captivity |
| Enclosure size | 18x18x24 inches minimum for one adult |
| Temperament | Calm, docile, mostly nocturnal |
| Care level | Beginner-friendly |

## Appearance & temperament

Crested geckos are named for the crest of fringed scales running from above each eye down the neck and back. They come in many colors and patterns, including buckskin, harlequin, pinstripe, and flame, the result of decades of selective breeding. A notable trait is that they can drop their tail if stressed, and unlike many lizards, they do not regrow it, so most adults are naturally tailless.

These geckos are gentle and rarely bite. They are crepuscular to nocturnal, becoming most active in the evening. Special toe pads and a prehensile tail make them skilled climbers and surprisingly capable jumpers.

## Habitat & enclosure

Because they are arboreal, crested geckos need vertical space. A single adult does well in an 18x18x24-inch terrarium with plenty of climbing branches, cork bark, and foliage. Live or artificial plants provide hiding spots and security, which reduces stress.

Use a moisture-retaining substrate such as coconut fiber or a bioactive soil mix in adult enclosures. Avoid loose substrate with hatchlings to prevent accidental ingestion; paper towel is a safe alternative for young geckos. House males separately, as they are territorial and will fight.

## Heating, lighting & humidity

One of the crested gecko's biggest advantages is that it does not require high heat. They prefer temperatures of 72-78°F and can suffer at sustained temperatures above 82°F. In most homes, no supplemental heating is needed; if your room runs cold, a low-wattage source can gently warm one side.

While they are nocturnal and were long kept without UVB, low-level UVB lighting can support vitamin D3 synthesis and overall health. Maintain humidity around 50-70 percent by misting the enclosure once or twice daily, allowing it to dry out partially between mistings to prevent mold.

## Diet & feeding

Modern commercial crested gecko diets (CGD) are powdered, nutritionally complete meal-replacement formulas that you mix with water. These make feeding simple and balanced, and should form the foundation of the diet. Offer fresh food in a shallow dish every other day, removing uneaten portions.

You can supplement with appropriately sized, gut-loaded insects such as crickets or dubia roaches a couple of times a week for variety and enrichment. Dust insects with calcium. Avoid feeding insects larger than the space between the gecko's eyes.

## Handling & health

Crested geckos tolerate gentle handling but should be allowed to settle in for a couple of weeks after arriving home. Handle low over a soft surface, since they are quick and may leap. Let the gecko walk hand over hand rather than gripping it.

Common health issues include metabolic bone disease from poor calcium intake, "floppy tail syndrome," and shedding problems linked to low humidity. A healthy gecko has clear eyes, full toes, and a good appetite. Consult an exotics veterinarian if you notice lethargy or weight loss.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do crested geckos need to eat live insects?** No. A quality powdered crested gecko diet can meet all their nutritional needs, though occasional insects offer enrichment and variety.

**Is owning a crested gecko legal?** They are widely kept and legal in most regions, but laws vary by country and locality, so check your local exotic-pet regulations before purchasing.

## Conclusion

Crested geckos are an ideal first reptile: long-lived, low-maintenance, and content at room temperature with a simple diet. With a vertical planted enclosure, proper humidity, and gentle handling, your gecko can be a rewarding companion for well over a decade.
