The Braque Saint-Germain is an elegant French pointing breed developed in the 19th century, with roots tracing to crosses between French braques and imported English Pointers. Friendly, sensitive and eager to please, it remains rare even in its native France but makes a devoted companion and capable gundog for active homes.

## Overview

| Trait | Details |

| Lifespan | Around 12–14 years |

| Energy | High; bred as an active hunting dog |
| Good with kids | Yes, gentle and affectionate when socialized |

## Appearance & temperament

The Braque Saint-Germain is a well-balanced, athletic dog of medium-large build with a noble, refined outline. Its short, close-lying coat is white with characteristic fawn-orange or russet markings, and the ears, eyes and nose often share warm orange tones. Most dogs stand roughly 22–24 inches at the shoulder, with males a little taller than females.

Temperamentally, this breed is known for being gentle, sociable and people-oriented. It tends to form close bonds with its family, is generally good with children, and usually gets along with other dogs. Because it is sensitive, it responds far better to patient, positive handling than to harsh correction. Its hunting heritage means a strong nose and a tendency to track scents enthusiastically.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced diet appropriate for an active large-breed dog, divided into two meals a day for adults. As a working-type breed, the Braque Saint-Germain may need more calories during periods of heavy activity and fewer when resting, so adjust portions to keep the dog lean. You should be able to feel the ribs easily without a thick fat layer.

Provide constant access to fresh water, especially after exercise. Because deep-chested dogs can be at some risk of bloat, avoid heavy meals immediately before or after vigorous activity. Discuss specific dietary needs and any weight concerns with your veterinarian.

## Exercise & training

This is a high-energy breed that thrives on plenty of daily exercise. Long walks, off-leash runs in safe areas, hiking and scent-based games help satisfy both its physical and mental needs. Without enough activity, the breed can become restless or develop unwanted habits.

Training tends to be rewarding, as the Braque Saint-Germain is intelligent and naturally willing to cooperate. Start socialization and basic obedience early, use reward-based methods, and keep sessions upbeat. A reliable recall is especially valuable given the breed's instinct to follow scents over long distances.

## Grooming & care

The short coat is low-maintenance and needs only weekly brushing to remove loose hair and keep the skin healthy. Bathe occasionally, as needed. Check the pendant ears regularly and keep them clean and dry, since drop ears can trap moisture and debris.

Trim the nails as needed to prevent overgrowth, and brush the teeth regularly to support dental health. After outdoor outings, inspect the coat, ears and paws for burrs, ticks or minor cuts.

## Health & lifespan

The Braque Saint-Germain is generally a hardy, healthy breed with a typical lifespan of around 12 to 14 years. As with many active, larger sporting dogs, responsible owners should be aware of conditions such as hip dysplasia and the ear infections that can affect drop-eared breeds. Maintaining a healthy weight protects the joints over time.

Routine veterinary checkups, vaccinations, parasite prevention and dental care all support a long, comfortable life. Choosing a responsible breeder who screens breeding dogs can help reduce the risk of inherited problems.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Braque Saint-Germain a good family dog?**
Yes. It is typically gentle, affectionate and sociable, and usually does well with children and other dogs when properly socialized. It does best in an active household that can meet its exercise needs.

**Does the Braque Saint-Germain need a lot of exercise?**
Yes. As an active pointing breed, it needs substantial daily physical activity and mental stimulation, such as long walks, running and scent games, to stay happy and well-behaved.

## Conclusion

The Braque Saint-Germain is a rare but rewarding French pointing breed that pairs a striking white-and-orange coat with a gentle, devoted personality. Given enough exercise, patient positive training and routine care, it makes a loyal companion and a capable working dog for owners who can match its energy and affection.
