The Domestic Pigeon (*Columba livia domestica*) is one of the oldest domesticated birds, kept for thousands of years for racing, showing, and companionship. Calm, intelligent, and remarkably tame when hand-raised, pigeons make rewarding pets for keepers who can provide an outdoor loft or aviary and daily care.

## Overview

| Trait | Details |
|---|---|
| Size | Roughly 29–37 cm long; about 300–500 g |
| Lifespan | Commonly 8–15 years with good care |
| Noise level | Low to moderate (soft cooing) |
| Talking ability | None (they coo, not mimic speech) |
| Care level | Beginner to intermediate |

## Appearance & temperament

Domestic pigeons come in hundreds of breeds and colors, from the familiar blue-bar wild type to fancy fantails, tumblers, and racing homers. Most have a plump body, small head, short legs, and a smooth, dense plumage. They are sociable flock birds that thrive on companionship, and pigeons raised by people often become very gentle, learning to perch on a hand or shoulder. They are generally quiet, with a pleasant cooing call rather than the loud screeching of parrots.

## Housing & cage setup

Pigeons need far more space than a typical cage allows; an outdoor loft or large aviary is ideal. Provide ample room to fly or at least move freely, along with sheltered, draft-free roosting space. Furnish the loft with perches, nest boxes or shelves, and a clean, predator-proof structure. Good ventilation without cold drafts is important, as is protection from rain, extreme heat, cats, and rodents. Keep the floor clean with regular removal of droppings, and offer a shallow bath dish a few times a week, as pigeons love to bathe.

## Diet & nutrition

A pigeon's diet centers on a varied mix of grains and seeds, such as corn, wheat, peas, sorghum, milo, and millet, ideally from a commercial pigeon blend. Supplement with grit and a calcium source like crushed oyster shell to support digestion and eggshell formation. Offer small amounts of leafy greens and provide fresh, clean water daily, since pigeons drink frequently and uniquely by suction. Avoid feeding bread, salty foods, chocolate, or moldy grain. Pelleted pigeon diets are also available and can simplify balanced feeding.

## Enrichment & handling

Pigeons are intelligent and benefit from mental and physical stimulation. Supervised flight time, foraging for scattered grain, and the company of other pigeons all keep them content. Handle birds gently and regularly from a young age to build trust; support the body and avoid squeezing. Many keepers train homing pigeons to fly and return, which provides excellent exercise. Always introduce new birds carefully and watch for bullying within the flock.

## Health & lifespan

With proper care, domestic pigeons commonly live 8–15 years. Keep them healthy with clean housing, fresh food and water, and routine observation for signs of illness such as ruffled feathers, weight loss, diarrhea, labored breathing, or discharge. Common concerns include canker, coccidiosis, worms, and external parasites like mites and lice. Quarantine new arrivals and consult an avian veterinarian for diagnosis and treatment rather than self-medicating.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are domestic pigeons good pets for beginners?**
Yes. They are hardy, gentle, and relatively easy to care for, provided you can supply a loft or aviary, a grain-based diet, and daily attention.

**Can a pet pigeon live indoors?**
A single pigeon can live indoors with supervised out-of-cage time, though many owners use special pigeon "flight suits" for diapering; most pigeons still benefit from outdoor flight and fresh air.

## Conclusion

Domestic pigeons are affectionate, low-noise companions with a long history alongside humans. Give them spacious, clean housing, a balanced grain diet, fresh water, regular gentle handling, and prompt veterinary care, and these charming birds can be devoted pets for many years.
