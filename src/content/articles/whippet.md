Often called the perfect house dog, the Whippet is a gentle, affectionate sighthound that's calm and quiet indoors yet astonishingly fast outdoors. They combine athletic grace with an easy, loving temperament.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (9–18 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–15 years |
| Coat | Short, fine (low-maintenance) |
| Energy | Moderate (sprinter, not stamina) |
| Good with kids | Yes — gentle and tolerant |

## Appearance & temperament

Whippets are slender and elegant with a deep chest and smooth coat. They are quiet, affectionate and remarkably calm at home — happy to curl up for hours — but love a daily sprint.

## Diet & nutrition

- Quality food; their lean build means ribs may show slightly — that's normal.
- Avoid overfeeding, but they rarely become obese.

## Exercise

- A couple of walks plus a chance to **run flat-out** in a safe, enclosed area.
- They're sprinters: short bursts of speed, then long rests.
- **Strong prey drive** — keep them leashed or in secure spaces; recall can be unreliable.

## Grooming & care

- Very easy — a quick weekly brush.
- They feel the cold (thin coat, low body fat) — a coat helps in winter.
- Provide soft bedding, as their bony frames appreciate cushioning.

## Health & lifespan

Generally very healthy. Be cautious with anaesthesia (sighthound sensitivity — use a knowledgeable vet), and watch for skin cuts due to thin skin. Otherwise a robust, long-lived breed.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Whippets calm indoors?**
Remarkably so — they're famous couch potatoes between runs.

**Do they bark a lot?**
No — they're one of the quietest breeds.

## Conclusion

The Whippet is a gentle, low-maintenance and affectionate companion that suits both active people and relaxed homes. Give them a daily run and a soft, warm bed, and they're content.
