The Chinese Water Dragon (*Physignathus cocincinus*) is a striking bright-green, semi-arboreal lizard native to the forests and waterways of Southeast Asia. Active, alert, and capable of becoming tame with patience, it is a rewarding pet for keepers ready to provide a large, warm, and humid environment.

## Overview

| Trait | Details |
|---|---|
| Adult size | 2–3 ft long (tail is roughly two-thirds of length) |
| Lifespan | 10–15 years with good care |
| Enclosure size | Minimum 4 ft tall x 4 ft long for an adult; bigger is better |
| Temperament | Active, alert, can become tame but may be skittish |
| Care level | Intermediate to advanced |

## Appearance & temperament

Chinese Water Dragons are vivid green, sometimes with turquoise or teal tones, and pale diagonal bands along the body. They have a prominent crest of spines running down the neck and back, a long whip-like tail used for balance and swimming, and a third "parietal eye" on top of the head that senses light. Males grow larger, with bigger crests and jowls. They are diurnal and naturally cautious, so consistent gentle interaction is needed to build trust. Stressed or untamed dragons may rub their snouts against the glass, so soft barriers and ample cover help reduce injury.

## Habitat & enclosure

Because they climb and need vertical space, a tall enclosure is essential. Provide sturdy branches, vines, and broad-leaved (live or artificial) plants for climbing and hiding. As semi-aquatic animals, they require a large water area big enough to soak and submerge in, kept clean and changed frequently since dragons often defecate in water. Use a moisture-retaining substrate such as cypress mulch, coconut fiber, or soil mixes that hold humidity without growing mold. Keep the enclosure visually screened on several sides to help the dragon feel secure.

## Heating, lighting & humidity

Maintain a basking spot of about 88–95°F (31–35°C), with a cooler ambient gradient around 75–85°F (24–29°C) and a nighttime drop into the low-to-mid 70s°F. A high-output UVB tube is critical for synthesizing vitamin D3 and preventing metabolic bone disease; replace UVB bulbs on the manufacturer's schedule, typically every 6–12 months. Humidity should stay high, around 60–80%, achieved through misting, a large water feature, and live plants. Use thermometers and a hygrometer to monitor conditions accurately.

## Diet & feeding

Chinese Water Dragons are primarily insectivorous and somewhat omnivorous. Offer a rotation of gut-loaded insects such as crickets, dubia roaches, locusts, earthworms, and the occasional waxworm as a treat. Larger adults may take pinky mice sparingly and small amounts of leafy greens or chopped vegetables. Dust feeders with calcium powder at most feedings and a vitamin/D3 supplement a couple of times weekly. Juveniles eat daily, while adults can be fed every other day. Always provide fresh drinking water.

## Handling & health

Approach calmly and support the body fully when handling; avoid grabbing the tail, which can be injured. Regular, low-stress handling helps them acclimate. Watch for common health issues including snout abrasions from rubbing on glass, metabolic bone disease from poor UVB or calcium, respiratory infections from cold or stale conditions, and mouth or skin infections. A reptile-experienced veterinarian should be consulted for any signs of lethargy, swelling, weight loss, or trouble shedding.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Chinese Water Dragons good for beginners?** They are best suited to intermediate keepers because they need a large, tall, humid enclosure with strong UVB and consistent handling to stay tame.

**Do Chinese Water Dragons need water to swim in?** Yes. As semi-aquatic lizards they need a sizable, clean water area deep enough to soak and submerge, which also supports hydration and shedding.

## Conclusion

A Chinese Water Dragon can be a beautiful, engaging companion for years when its needs are met: a tall, planted enclosure, ample water, proper heat, UVB, and high humidity, plus a varied insect-based diet and patient handling. As an exotic reptile, ownership rules vary by region, so check local laws before bringing one home. With the right setup, these spirited lizards thrive and reward attentive keepers.
