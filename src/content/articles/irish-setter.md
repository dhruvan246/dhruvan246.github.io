With a gleaming mahogany-red coat and an irrepressibly cheerful personality, the Irish Setter is one of the most recognizable and joyful gundogs. Originally bred in Ireland to locate and "set" game birds for hunters, this elegant, athletic sporting dog combines beauty with boundless energy. Affectionate, sociable and famously playful, the Irish Setter thrives in active homes that can match its enthusiasm.

## Appearance & temperament

The Irish Setter is a tall, lean and graceful dog built for stamina and speed. Its hallmark is a rich chestnut to mahogany-red coat that lies flat and silky, with attractive feathering on the ears, chest, legs and tail. Temperamentally, the breed is outgoing, affectionate and high-spirited. Irish Setters are known for maturing slowly, often retaining a puppy-like exuberance well into adulthood. They are people-oriented, generally friendly toward strangers and other dogs, and can become unhappy or destructive if left alone for long stretches.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced diet formulated for active large-breed dogs, with portions matched to age, weight and activity level. Because the breed is deep-chested, dividing the daily ration into two or more smaller meals rather than one large meal is a sensible precaution. Avoid heavy exercise immediately before and after eating. Keep fresh water available, monitor body condition to prevent excess weight, and consult your veterinarian about any breed- or life-stage-specific dietary needs.

## Exercise & training

This is a high-energy working breed that needs substantial daily activity — long walks, running, fetch, swimming or canine sports such as agility and field work. A bored or under-exercised Irish Setter may become restless or mischievous. They are intelligent but can be easily distracted and slow to settle, so patient, consistent, reward-based training started early works best. Early socialization helps channel their sociable nature into good manners.

## Grooming & care

The silky coat needs brushing several times a week to prevent tangles and mats, especially in the feathered areas behind the ears, on the legs and under the body. Periodic trimming keeps the coat neat. Bathe as needed, trim nails regularly, and brush teeth routinely. Their pendant ears trap moisture, so check and gently clean them often to reduce the risk of ear infections.

## Health & lifespan

Irish Setters are generally robust but, like many large breeds, have some predispositions. Owners should be aware of hip dysplasia, gastric dilatation-volvulus (bloat), progressive retinal atrophy and other eye conditions, and hypothyroidism. Choosing a breeder who performs recommended health screening, maintaining a healthy weight, and keeping up routine veterinary checkups all support a long, healthy life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Irish Setters good family dogs?**
Yes. For active families they are affectionate, playful and patient companions that typically get along well with children and other pets.

**Do Irish Setters calm down with age?**
They mature slowly and stay lively for years. Ample daily exercise and mental stimulation help them settle into calmer, well-mannered adults.

## Conclusion

The Irish Setter is a beautiful, devoted and energetic companion best suited to households that can provide plenty of exercise, training and companionship. Meet those needs, and this gleaming red gundog rewards its family with affection, fun and years of lively, loyal friendship.
