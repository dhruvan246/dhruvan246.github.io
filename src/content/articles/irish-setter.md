With a gleaming mahogany coat and an exuberant personality, the Irish Setter is one of the most striking and joyful gundogs. Friendly, energetic and a little goofy, the Irish Setter brings warmth and liveliness to an active home.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Large (25–32 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–15 years |
| Coat | Medium-long, silky, feathered |
| Energy | Very high |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Irish Setters are elegant and athletic with a flowing red coat. They are outgoing, affectionate and playful — famously high-spirited and slow to mature, staying puppy-like for years. They love people and dislike being alone.

## Exercise & training

- Need **a great deal of daily exercise** — running, fetch, dog sports.
- Intelligent but exuberant and easily distracted; patient, positive, consistent training works best.

## Grooming

- Brush several times a week to prevent tangles in the feathering; trim as needed.
- Check the ears regularly to prevent infections.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for hip dysplasia, bloat (feed smaller meals), eye conditions (PRA), and hypothyroidism. Choose health-tested lines and keep up regular vet care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Irish Setters good family dogs?**
Yes for active families — they're affectionate, playful and great with children.

**Do they calm down?**
They mature slowly and stay lively for years; plenty of exercise helps.

## Conclusion

The Irish Setter is a joyful, affectionate and energetic companion for an active household. Provide ample exercise, patient training and regular grooming, and this gleaming red beauty will be a devoted, lively friend.
