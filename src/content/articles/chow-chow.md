With a lion-like mane and a distinctive blue-black tongue, the Chow Chow is an ancient, dignified breed. Independent and reserved, the Chow is loyal to its family but famously cat-like and aloof.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (20–32 kg) |
| Lifespan | 9–15 years |
| Coat | Thick, rough or smooth double coat |
| Energy | Low to moderate |
| Good with kids | Best with respectful, older children |

## Appearance & temperament

Chows are sturdy and squarely built with a profuse mane, deep-set eyes and that unique dark tongue. They are independent, dignified and reserved — devoted to their own people but often standoffish with strangers. Early socialisation is essential.

## Exercise & training

- Modest daily walks suit their lower energy.
- Independent and stubborn; patient, consistent, positive training and **early socialisation** are vital for a well-mannered Chow.

## Grooming

- **Brush several times a week** to manage the dense coat and prevent mats; heavier during seasonal sheds.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for hip and elbow dysplasia, eye conditions (entropion), and heat sensitivity (thick coat + sometimes flat-ish face). Keep them cool and maintain regular vet care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Chow Chows aggressive?**
They're aloof and protective, not inherently aggressive — socialisation and training are key.

**Are they affectionate?**
Devoted but reserved — more dignified companion than cuddly lap dog.

## Conclusion

The Chow Chow is a dignified, loyal and striking companion for an owner who appreciates an independent dog. Socialise early, keep up grooming, protect them from heat, and respect their unique personality.
