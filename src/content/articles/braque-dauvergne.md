The Braque d'Auvergne is a French pointing breed that hails from the Auvergne region in central France. Built as an all-round gundog, it is admired for its keen nose, steady working style and its striking black-and-white "charbonné" coat. Friendly and even-tempered, the Braque d'Auvergne is equally at home in the field and as a devoted family companion.

## Appearance & temperament

The Braque d'Auvergne is a well-proportioned, athletic dog of medium-to-large size, with males generally standing around 57–63 cm at the shoulder and females a little smaller. The short coat is its hallmark: white heavily ticked or roaned with black, often with a solid black head and ears, creating a distinctive blue-grey "charbonné" appearance.

In temperament, the breed is gentle, affectionate and eager to please. Braques d'Auvergne bond closely with their people, tend to get along well with children and other dogs, and are generally calm indoors. They are intelligent and sensitive, responding far better to encouragement than to harsh handling.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced diet suited to an active, large breed, split into two meals a day rather than one large serving. Match the portion to your dog's age, weight and workload, and use a large-breed puppy formula during growth to support steady development.

Because this is a deep-chested breed, it can be at some risk of bloat (gastric dilatation-volvulus), so avoid vigorous activity right before and after meals. Keep fresh water available and monitor body condition, since excess weight adds strain to the joints. Ask your veterinarian for advice tailored to your individual dog.

## Exercise & training

Bred to hunt and range over varied terrain, the Braque d'Auvergne has plenty of stamina and needs substantial daily exercise. Long walks, off-lead running in safe areas, scent work and ideally some fieldwork help keep this dog satisfied. Without enough activity it can become bored and restless.

Training should begin early and rely on positive, reward-based methods. These dogs are clever and willing but sensitive, so calm, consistent sessions are most effective. Early socialisation helps produce a confident, well-mannered adult that settles easily at home.

## Grooming & care

The short, dense coat is low-maintenance and needs only a weekly brush to remove loose hair and keep it healthy, with baths given as needed. The breed's pendulous ears can trap moisture and debris, so check and gently clean them regularly to help prevent ear infections.

Routine care also includes nail trimming and regular dental hygiene. Keep an eye on the eyes and skin, and inspect the coat and paws after time spent in the field for burrs, ticks or minor scrapes.

## Health & lifespan

The Braque d'Auvergne is generally a robust, healthy breed with a typical lifespan of around 11 to 13 years. As with many large, deep-chested gundogs, possible concerns include hip dysplasia, ear infections related to its drop ears, and a risk of bloat.

Choosing a responsible breeder who health-tests breeding stock, along with routine veterinary check-ups, supports a long and healthy life. Always seek professional veterinary advice for any specific health concern.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Braque d'Auvergne a good family dog?**
Yes. The breed is affectionate, gentle and sociable, usually getting along well with children and other dogs when properly socialised. It enjoys being part of family life and is typically calm and settled indoors.

**How much exercise does a Braque d'Auvergne need?**
A good deal. As a working gundog it benefits from at least one to two hours of varied daily activity, including walks, free running and mental stimulation such as scent games, to stay content and well-behaved.

## Conclusion

The Braque d'Auvergne is a handsome, good-natured French pointer that rewards active owners with loyalty and a calm home presence. Given ample exercise, gentle training and attentive care of its ears and joints, this versatile gundog makes a wonderful, devoted companion.
