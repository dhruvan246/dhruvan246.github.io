The Braque Francais is a classic French pointing dog, an old gundog breed developed for hunting over varied terrain. Existing in two distinct types, the larger Gascogne and the smaller Pyrenean, it is valued for its keen nose, biddable nature and an easygoing temperament that translates well into family life.

## Overview

| Trait | Details |
|---|---|
| Size | Large (typically 47–69 cm tall depending on type) |
| Lifespan | About 12–14 years |
| Coat | Short, fine and dense |
| Energy | High |
| Good with kids | Yes, gentle and friendly |

## Appearance & temperament

The Braque Francais is an athletic, well-proportioned dog with a noble head, fairly long ears and a muscular frame built for endurance in the field. The Gascogne type is the larger of the two, standing around 58–69 cm, while the Pyrenean type is lighter and smaller. The short coat is typically chestnut-brown and white, often with ticking or roan patterning.

In temperament the breed is gentle, sociable and eager to please. Braques bond closely with their people, tend to be patient with children and usually get on well with other dogs. They are sensitive and intelligent rather than dominant, which makes them pleasant companions when given attention and inclusion in daily life.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced diet formulated for an active dog of its size, split into two meals a day rather than one large portion. Match the amount to your dog's age, weight and workload, using a large-breed puppy formula during growth to support steady development.

As an active, fairly deep-chested breed, take care to avoid vigorous exercise immediately before and after eating. Keep fresh water available and monitor body condition, since excess weight adds strain to the joints. Your veterinarian can advise on feeding tailored to your individual dog.

## Exercise & training

Bred to range and point game across open country, the Braque Francais has real stamina and needs plenty of daily activity. Long walks, free running in safe areas, scent work and ideally some fieldwork keep this dog content. Without enough physical and mental outlet, a Braque can become restless and frustrated.

Training is generally straightforward thanks to the breed's willingness and intelligence. Reward-based, consistent methods suit its sensitive nature far better than harsh handling. Early socialisation helps produce a calm, confident and well-mannered adult dog.

## Grooming & care

The short coat is low-maintenance and benefits from a weekly brush to remove loose hair and keep it glossy, with baths only when needed. The breed's longer, hanging ears can trap moisture and debris, so check and clean them regularly to reduce the chance of ear infections.

Routine care also includes regular nail trimming and dental hygiene. After working or walking in rough country, check the coat, paws and ears for grass seeds, ticks or minor scrapes.

## Health & lifespan

The Braque Francais is generally a robust, healthy breed with a typical lifespan of around 12 to 14 years. As with many larger active dogs, it can be predisposed to hip dysplasia, and its pendulous ears may make ear infections more likely without routine care.

Choosing a responsible breeder who health-screens breeding stock, along with regular veterinary check-ups, supports a long and healthy life. Always seek professional veterinary advice for any specific health concern.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Braque Francais a good family dog?**
Yes. The breed is affectionate, gentle and sociable, usually getting along well with children and other dogs when properly socialised. It thrives on companionship and likes to be part of everyday family activity.

**How much exercise does a Braque Francais need?**
A good deal. As a working gundog it needs at least one to two hours of varied daily activity, including walks, off-lead running and scent or mental work, to stay happy and well-behaved.

## Conclusion

The Braque Francais is a versatile, good-natured French pointer that rewards active owners with affection and an easy temperament at home. Given ample exercise, gentle training and routine care of its ears and joints, this capable gundog makes a devoted and adaptable companion.
