Cockatoos are striking, crested parrots native to Australia, Indonesia, and surrounding islands. Known for their expressive head crests and deep need for companionship, they are among the most affectionate and demanding birds kept as pets. Anyone considering one should understand that these are highly intelligent, long-lived, and noisy animals requiring an enormous commitment of time and attention.

## Overview

| Trait | Typical Range |
|-------|---------------|
| Size | Roughly 12–24 inches, depending on species |
| Lifespan | Commonly 40–70+ years with good care |
| Noise level | Very high; capable of piercing screams |
| Talking ability | Limited to moderate; better at mimicry than clear speech |
| Care level | Advanced; not suited to beginners |

## Appearance & temperament

Cockatoos are usually white, pink, grey, or black, and all share a movable crest they raise when excited, alarmed, or curious. Popular pet species include the Umbrella, Sulphur-crested, Galah, and the smaller Goffin's cockatoo. They are famously affectionate, often described as "velcro birds" because they crave constant physical closeness. This same intensity can lead to screaming, feather plucking, and aggression if they feel neglected. They bond strongly and need consistent, patient interaction.

## Housing & cage setup

Because cockatoos are large and active, they need the biggest cage you can provide, with strong bars and secure locks, as they are skilled escape artists. The cage should allow full wing stretching and room to climb. Include several perches of varying diameters, ideally natural wood, plus sturdy, frequently rotated toys. Place the cage in a busy family area away from drafts, kitchen fumes, and direct sun. Daily out-of-cage time in a safe, supervised space is essential for exercise and mental health.

## Diet & nutrition

A balanced diet centers on a high-quality formulated pellet, supplemented with a variety of fresh vegetables, leafy greens, and limited amounts of fruit. Seeds and nuts may be offered sparingly as treats, since a seed-only diet is fatty and leads to malnutrition and obesity. Always provide clean, fresh water. Avoid avocado, chocolate, caffeine, alcohol, and salty or sugary human foods, all of which can be toxic or harmful to birds.

## Enrichment & handling

Cockatoos are extremely intelligent and become bored, destructive, or self-harming without stimulation. Provide foraging toys, shreddable materials, puzzles, and training sessions to keep their minds engaged. Gentle, consistent handling builds trust; teach step-up and basic commands using positive reinforcement. Be cautious about over-cuddling, which can encourage hormonal behavior and excessive bonding to one person. Plan for several hours of interaction and supervised activity every day.

## Health & lifespan

With proper care, cockatoos often live for many decades, making them a lifelong commitment that may outlive their owner. Common concerns include feather-destructive behavior, respiratory issues, obesity, and psittacine beak and feather disease. Watch for changes in appetite, droppings, posture, or feathering, as birds hide illness well. Schedule regular checkups with an avian veterinarian, and provide a clean, smoke-free environment. Note that legality of ownership varies by region, and these exotic birds require specialist care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are cockatoos good pets for beginners?** Generally no. Their loud calls, emotional intensity, long lifespan, and need for constant attention make them better suited to experienced, dedicated bird owners.

**Why do cockatoos scream so much?** Some calling is natural, but excessive screaming usually signals boredom, loneliness, or a learned demand for attention. Adequate enrichment and routine help reduce it.

## Conclusion

Cockatoos are loving, captivating companions, but they demand more time, space, and patience than almost any other pet bird. With the right environment, diet, and daily engagement, a well-cared-for cockatoo can be a devoted friend for decades. Anyone considering one should be fully prepared for the noise, mess, and decades-long commitment involved.
