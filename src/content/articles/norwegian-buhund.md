The Norwegian Buhund is a compact, energetic spitz from Norway, where it has long worked as an all-purpose farm dog herding livestock and guarding the homestead. Lively, affectionate and quick to learn, this medium-sized companion thrives in active homes that can match its tireless enthusiasm.

## Appearance & temperament

The Buhund is a typical northern spitz: square in build, with a wedge-shaped head, prickly erect ears and a tightly curled tail carried over the back. Most stand roughly 41–47 cm (16–18.5 inches) at the shoulder. The weather-resistant double coat comes in two colours, wheaten (pale cream to bright tan) and black, lying smooth and close over a soft, dense undercoat.

Temperamentally, the Buhund is cheerful, confident and devoted to its family. Bred to think on its feet while herding, it is intelligent and eager to please but can be vocal and independent. The breed is naturally alert and makes a good watchdog without being aggressive. It bonds closely with children and other pets when raised with them.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, high-quality dog food formulated for the breed's life stage and activity level. As an athletic working breed, the Buhund does well on diets with quality protein to support lean muscle. Most adults eat in two measured meals daily; portion according to body condition rather than the bag's maximum, as the breed gains weight easily if under-exercised. Keep fresh water available at all times, and use part of the daily ration for training treats rather than adding extras.

## Exercise & training

This is a high-energy breed that needs at least an hour of vigorous activity each day, including walks, runs and active play. The Buhund also relishes dog sports such as agility, rally, herding and obedience, which channel both body and mind. A bored Buhund may bark excessively or become destructive.

Highly trainable and food-motivated, the breed responds best to upbeat, reward-based methods and consistent short sessions. Early socialisation helps temper its watchful, talkative nature. Because it is sensitive, harsh corrections are counterproductive.

## Grooming & care

The double coat sheds steadily year-round and "blows" heavily twice a year. Weekly brushing keeps it healthy, increasing to several times a week during seasonal sheds to remove loose undercoat. The coat is naturally clean and odour-resistant, so bathing is only needed occasionally. Check and trim nails regularly, brush teeth often, and keep the ears clean and dry.

## Health & lifespan

The Norwegian Buhund is generally a robust, long-lived breed, typically living 12 to 15 years. Like many breeds, it can be prone to hip dysplasia and certain eye conditions such as hereditary cataracts. Responsible breeders screen breeding stock for hip and eye health. Maintaining a healthy weight, providing regular exercise and scheduling routine veterinary check-ups all support a long, active life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Norwegian Buhunds bark a lot?**
They can be vocal. Bred to alert and herd, Buhunds use their voice readily, but consistent training, plenty of exercise and mental stimulation help keep barking in check.

**Are Norwegian Buhunds good family dogs?**
Yes. They are affectionate, playful and patient with children when properly socialised, and their watchful nature makes them devoted, alert companions for active households.

## Conclusion

The Norwegian Buhund is a bright, hardworking spitz that rewards owners willing to meet its high energy and need for companionship. With daily exercise, positive training and routine grooming, this hardy Nordic breed makes a loyal, joyful family member for years to come.
