The Lapponian Herder, known in its homeland as the Lapinporokoira, is a medium-sized working dog bred by the Sami people of northern Finland to herd reindeer across the harsh Arctic landscape. Developed into its modern form in the mid-20th century, it is a calm, tireless and intelligent breed that remains relatively rare outside Scandinavia but is prized for its steady temperament and remarkable endurance.

## Appearance & temperament

The Lapponian Herder is slightly longer than it is tall, with a strong, muscular build suited to long days of work in cold conditions. It has a medium-length double coat that is coarse on the outside with a soft, insulating undercoat, most commonly seen in shades of black, grey or dark brown, often with lighter or tan markings. The expression is alert and friendly, with erect or semi-erect ears.

In temperament, the breed is calm, docile and willing to work, with a strong herding instinct. These dogs are intelligent and energetic, eager to please and quick to learn. They tend to be friendly toward people, including children, and are not typically aggressive, though they can be vocal and may try to herd by nudging.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced diet formulated for active medium-sized dogs, with portions matched to age, weight and activity level. As a working breed, an adult Lapponian Herder doing regular hard exercise may need more calories than a less active companion dog. Divide food into two daily meals, provide constant access to fresh water, and monitor body condition to avoid weight gain. Consult your veterinarian about the most suitable diet for each life stage.

## Exercise & training

This is a high-energy breed that needs substantial daily exercise, ideally an hour or more of active work, walking, running or play. Without enough physical and mental stimulation, a bored Lapponian Herder may become restless or develop nuisance behaviours such as excessive barking. They excel at dog sports like agility, herding trials and obedience.

Training is generally straightforward thanks to the breed's intelligence and willingness to please. Use consistent, positive, reward-based methods, and begin socialisation early to build a confident, well-mannered adult.

## Grooming & care

The double coat is fairly low-maintenance for most of the year and benefits from weekly brushing to remove loose hair and keep it clean. During the twice-yearly seasonal shed, more frequent brushing helps manage the heavy loss of undercoat. Bathe only as needed, check ears regularly, trim nails when they grow long, and maintain routine dental care to support overall health.

## Health & lifespan

The Lapponian Herder is generally a hardy, healthy breed with a typical lifespan of around 12 to 14 years. As with many medium-sized dogs, responsible breeders screen for conditions such as hip and elbow dysplasia and certain hereditary eye disorders. Keeping your dog at a healthy weight, providing regular exercise and scheduling routine veterinary check-ups are the best ways to support a long, active life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Lapponian Herder a good family pet?**
Yes. The breed is generally calm, friendly and good with children, making it a suitable family companion provided its high exercise needs are met.

**Does the Lapponian Herder shed a lot?**
It sheds moderately year-round and heavily during two seasonal coat changes. Regular brushing, increasing in frequency during shedding seasons, keeps loose hair under control.

## Conclusion

The Lapponian Herder is a versatile, even-tempered working dog that thrives in an active home willing to give it plenty of exercise and engagement. With its weatherproof coat, friendly nature and strong work ethic, it rewards committed owners with loyalty, intelligence and years of energetic companionship.
