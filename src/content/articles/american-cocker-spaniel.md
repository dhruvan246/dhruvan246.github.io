The American Cocker Spaniel is the smallest member of the sporting group, known for its rounded head, gentle expression and flowing, feathered coat. Originally developed in the United States from English spaniel stock to flush and retrieve birds, today it is mostly a beloved, merry companion.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Small (roughly 7–14 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–15 years |
| Coat | Silky, medium-to-long, feathered |
| Energy | Moderate |
| Good with kids | Very good |

## Appearance & temperament

The American Cocker Spaniel has a compact, sturdy body, a domed skull and long, low-set ears covered in feathering. Its silky coat comes in many colours, including black, buff, parti-color and roan. Famously sweet-natured and eager to please, the breed is affectionate, sociable and gentle, though it can be sensitive and is sometimes wary of rough handling.

## Diet & nutrition

- Feed a complete, good-quality dog food in measured portions appropriate for a small, moderately active breed.
- These dogs gain weight easily, so watch portions and keep treats modest.
- Provide fresh water at all times and divide food into two daily meals.
- Ask your veterinarian to recommend a diet suited to your dog's age, weight and activity level.

## Exercise & training

- Aim for about 30–60 minutes of daily activity, such as walks, play and games that engage their nose.
- Mental stimulation through scent work or gentle retrieving suits their sporting heritage.
- They respond best to patient, positive, reward-based training; harsh corrections can upset this sensitive breed.
- Early, gentle socialisation builds a confident, well-mannered companion.

## Grooming & care

The luxurious coat is high-maintenance and one of the breed's defining care needs.

- Brush several times a week, or daily, to prevent mats and tangles; many owners have the coat professionally clipped for easier upkeep.
- **The long, hanging ears trap moisture and need regular attention** — check and clean them often, and keep them dry after eating, drinking or swimming to reduce infection risk.
- Keep up with nail trims, dental care and routine eye cleaning.

## Health & lifespan

With typical lifespans of around 12–15 years, American Cocker Spaniels are generally hardy but have some breed-linked concerns. Ear infections are very common due to the heavy ear flaps, and the breed can be prone to several eye conditions such as cataracts and progressive retinal atrophy. Some lines may experience hip dysplasia, certain heart conditions or skin issues. Choosing a responsible breeder who screens their dogs, plus regular veterinary checkups, helps support a long, healthy life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are American Cocker Spaniels good family dogs?**
Yes. They are gentle, affectionate and typically do well with children and other pets when properly socialised.

**Do they need a lot of grooming?**
Yes. Their silky, feathered coat requires frequent brushing and often professional grooming, and their ears need regular cleaning.

## Conclusion

The American Cocker Spaniel is a loving, merry companion that thrives on attention and gentle handling. With consistent grooming, diligent ear care, moderate daily exercise and routine veterinary attention, this small spaniel can be a devoted family member for many years.
