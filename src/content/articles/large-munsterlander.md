The Large Munsterlander is a versatile German gundog bred to point, track and retrieve game on land and in water. Known for its striking black-and-white coat and affectionate, people-oriented nature, this athletic breed thrives with active families who can give it plenty of work and companionship.

## Appearance & temperament

The Large Munsterlander is a well-balanced, muscular dog standing roughly 58–65 cm at the shoulder, with males generally larger than females. Its medium-length coat is dense and slightly wavy, with feathering on the legs, tail and ears. The breed is distinctively colored in black and white, often with a solid black head and a patched, ticked or roan body.

Bred as an all-round hunting companion, the Large Munsterlander is intelligent, trainable and deeply attached to its family. The breed is typically friendly, gentle and good-natured, getting along well with children and other dogs. These dogs dislike being left alone for long stretches and do best as full members of an active household.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced diet suited to an active large-breed dog, divided into two meals a day rather than one large portion. Adjust portions to your dog's age, weight and activity level, as a working Munsterlander needs more calories than a less active pet. Keep your dog at a lean, healthy body condition to protect its joints, provide fresh water at all times, and consult your veterinarian before making significant diet changes.

## Exercise & training

This is a high-energy, athletic breed that needs substantial daily exercise — typically well over an hour of running, swimming, retrieving or field work. A short walk around the block is not enough. Large Munsterlanders excel at gundog work, tracking, agility and other dog sports, and they are happiest when both body and mind are engaged.

Eager to please and quick to learn, the breed responds best to consistent, positive, reward-based training begun early. Early socialization builds a confident, well-mannered adult. A bored or under-exercised Munsterlander may become restless or destructive, so channel that drive into structured activity.

## Grooming & care

The medium-length coat is relatively easy to maintain but benefits from brushing once or twice a week to remove loose hair and prevent the feathering from matting. Check the feathered, drop ears regularly and keep them clean and dry, especially after swimming, to help prevent infections. Routine nail trimming, dental care and seasonal coat checks for burrs and debris round out a straightforward grooming routine.

## Health & lifespan

The Large Munsterlander is generally a healthy, hardy breed with a typical lifespan of around 11–13 years. Like many large dogs, it can be predisposed to hip dysplasia, and the breed's pendulous ears can make it prone to ear infections. Choosing a responsible breeder who health-tests their breeding dogs, keeping your dog at a healthy weight and maintaining regular veterinary checkups all support a long, active life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Large Munsterlanders good family dogs?**
Yes, for active families. They are friendly, gentle and affectionate, generally good with children and other dogs, but they need plenty of exercise and companionship.

**Do Large Munsterlanders shed a lot?**
They shed moderately. Their medium-length coat needs brushing once or twice a week, with extra attention to the feathered areas to prevent matting.

## Conclusion

The Large Munsterlander is an intelligent, versatile and devoted gundog that bonds closely with its people. Give this breed ample exercise, early training and plenty of companionship, and it will reward you with a loyal, even-tempered partner for many years.
