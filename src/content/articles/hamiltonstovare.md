The Hamiltonstovare, also known as the Hamilton Hound, is a large Swedish scenthound bred to track hare and fox over long distances. Named after Count Adolf Patrick Hamilton, who helped develop the breed in the late 19th century, it remains popular in Sweden as a hunting companion while also making an affectionate, even-tempered family dog.

## Appearance & temperament

The Hamiltonstovare is a well-balanced, athletic hound with a deep chest, straight legs and a confident, level gait. Its most recognisable feature is the striking tricolour coat: a black saddle, rich tan on the head and legs, and white markings on the muzzle, neck, feet and tail tip. Males generally stand a little taller than females.

In temperament, the breed is friendly, calm and devoted to its family. Bred to work independently in the field, it has a strong nose and a tendency to follow scents, but it is not typically aggressive. Hamiltonstovares are usually good with children and sociable with people, though their hunting drive means small pets should be introduced carefully.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced diet appropriate for a large, active dog, choosing a food suited to the dog's age and activity level. Because this is a working breed, dogs that hunt or exercise heavily may need more calories than companion dogs of the same size. Divide the daily ration into two meals to support steady energy and reduce the risk of bloat, which can affect deep-chested breeds. Keep fresh water available at all times and monitor body condition, adjusting portions to maintain a healthy weight rather than free-feeding. Consult your veterinarian about the right diet for your individual dog.

## Exercise & training

This is an energetic hound that needs substantial daily exercise: long walks, runs and opportunities to use its nose all help keep it content. A bored or under-exercised Hamiltonstovare may become restless or vocal. Because the breed follows scents enthusiastically, a secure garden and a reliable recall are important, and many owners keep them leashed in unfenced areas.

Hamiltonstovares are intelligent and willing but can be independent thinkers. Positive, reward-based training with consistency works best, ideally started early alongside good socialisation. Scent games and tracking activities are especially rewarding for this breed.

## Grooming & care

The short, dense double coat is low-maintenance. Weekly brushing removes loose hair and keeps the coat healthy, with more frequent brushing during seasonal shedding. Bathe only as needed. As with all dogs, check and clean the ears regularly, trim the nails, and maintain a routine of dental care. The breed tolerates cold weather well thanks to its protective coat but should still have shelter and warmth in harsh conditions.

## Health & lifespan

The Hamiltonstovare is generally a robust, healthy breed with a typical lifespan of around 12 to 15 years. As a large, deep-chested dog it can be susceptible to bloat (gastric dilatation-volvulus), and like many larger breeds it may experience joint issues such as hip dysplasia. Responsible breeders screen breeding stock for relevant conditions. Routine veterinary check-ups, parasite prevention, weight management and prompt attention to any signs of illness all help support a long, healthy life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Hamiltonstovares good family dogs?**
Yes. They are typically friendly, calm and affectionate with their families and usually good with children, provided they receive enough exercise and early socialisation.

**Do Hamiltonstovares need a lot of exercise?**
Yes. As an active hunting breed they need substantial daily activity, including long walks or runs and mentally stimulating scent work, to stay happy and well-behaved.

## Conclusion

The Hamiltonstovare is a handsome, good-natured Swedish scenthound that combines a strong working drive with a gentle home temperament. Given plenty of exercise, secure containment, consistent training and routine care, it rewards its owners with loyalty and an easygoing companionship that suits active households well.
