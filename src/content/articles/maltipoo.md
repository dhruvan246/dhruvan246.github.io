Soft, playful and devoted to their people, the Maltipoo is a popular "designer" crossbreed produced by mating a Maltese with a Toy or Miniature Poodle. Bred mainly as an affectionate companion, the Maltipoo is prized for its gentle temperament, low-shedding coat and small, apartment-friendly size.

## Appearance & temperament

Because the Maltipoo is a cross rather than a standardized breed, appearance varies between individuals and even within a single litter. Most are small dogs with rounded heads, dark expressive eyes and a soft coat that can be white, cream, apricot, silver or grey. The coat texture ranges from loosely wavy to tightly curly depending on which parent it favors.

Temperamentally, Maltipoos are typically friendly, affectionate and eager to please. They bond closely with their families and often dislike being left alone for long periods, which can lead to separation anxiety. Their alert nature makes them quick to bark at visitors, so early socialization helps prevent excessive yapping.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a high-quality dog food formulated for small breeds, which provides appropriately sized kibble and the calorie density a tiny dog needs. Because Maltipoos are small, portions are modest, and it is easy to overfeed them with treats and table scraps. Measure meals and divide them into two servings a day to maintain a steady weight.

Very small or young puppies can be prone to low blood sugar, so frequent small meals are recommended in early life. Always provide fresh water, and ask your veterinarian for guidance on portion sizes suited to your dog's weight and activity level.

## Exercise & training

Maltipoos have moderate energy and are satisfied with one or two short daily walks plus some indoor play. A couple of 20- to 30-minute sessions of walking, fetch or puzzle games usually meets their needs. Their small size makes them well suited to apartments, but they still benefit from daily activity to stay fit and mentally stimulated.

Thanks to their Poodle heritage, Maltipoos are intelligent and respond well to positive, reward-based training. Keep sessions short and consistent, focus on housetraining patience, and prioritize early socialization to build confidence around new people, dogs and environments.

## Grooming & care

The Maltipoo's coat is low-shedding but not maintenance-free. Brush several times a week to prevent mats and tangles, especially around the ears, legs and face. Many owners schedule a professional grooming trim every six to eight weeks to keep the coat manageable.

Routine care also includes regular nail trimming, dental brushing to protect small teeth, and gentle cleaning of the eye area, as tear staining is common in light-colored coats. Check and clean the ears regularly to reduce the risk of infection.

## Health & lifespan

Maltipoos generally enjoy a long lifespan of roughly 12 to 16 years. As a cross of two small breeds, they may inherit conditions seen in either parent, including patellar luxation, dental disease, progressive retinal atrophy, and white shaker syndrome. Their small jaws make overcrowded teeth and tartar buildup a frequent concern.

Routine veterinary checkups, dental care, weight management and parasite prevention all support a healthy life. Choosing a responsible breeder who screens parent dogs for hereditary issues can reduce some risks.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Maltipoos hypoallergenic?**
No dog is truly hypoallergenic, but the Maltipoo's low-shedding coat often produces less loose hair and dander, which some allergy sufferers tolerate better. Spending time with a Maltipoo before adopting is the best way to gauge your own reaction.

**Do Maltipoos bark a lot?**
They can be alert barkers who announce visitors and unfamiliar sounds. Early socialization and consistent training help keep barking in check and prevent nuisance noise.

## Conclusion

The Maltipoo is a loving, adaptable companion that fits well into homes ranging from apartments to family houses. With regular grooming, modest daily exercise, attentive dental care and plenty of companionship, this charming Maltese-Poodle cross can be a delightful friend for many years.
