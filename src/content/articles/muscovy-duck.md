The Muscovy Duck (*Cairina moschata*) is a large, distinctive domestic waterfowl prized for its quiet nature, excellent foraging, and hardy constitution. Unlike most domestic ducks descended from Mallards, the Muscovy is a separate species native to the Americas, which gives it several unusual traits that make it a favorite on small farms and homesteads.

## Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|

## Appearance & temperament

Muscovy Ducks are easily recognized by the fleshy red wattle-like caruncles around their face and bill. Plumage varies widely, from glossy black-and-white to all-white, chocolate, blue, and pied patterns. Drakes are noticeably larger than hens, sometimes nearly double the weight.

They are famously quiet. Rather than the loud quacking of typical ducks, Muscovies communicate with soft hisses, tail wags, and gentle chirps, making them well suited to properties where noise is a concern. Most are calm and curious, though drakes can become territorial during breeding season.

## Housing & space

Muscovies need a predator-proof shelter for nighttime, with clean, dry bedding such as straw. During the day they thrive on a spacious run or free-range yard where they can forage for insects, slugs, and greens.

A notable advantage is that they require very little water. Unlike Mallard-derived ducks, Muscovies do not need a swimming pond. A shallow basin deep enough to dip and clean their head, bill, and nostrils is sufficient, though they enjoy occasional bathing. They are strong fliers, so wing clipping or a covered run may be needed to keep them home.

## Diet & nutrition

A quality waterfowl or poultry feed should form the base of the diet, with niacin levels important for healthy leg development. Muscovies are exceptional foragers and will eat large quantities of insects, mosquito larvae, slugs, and weeds, which makes them popular for natural pest control.

Supplement with leafy greens, vegetables, and occasional grains. Always provide clean drinking water alongside feed, as ducks need water to swallow. Offer poultry grit to aid digestion and crushed oyster shell for laying hens.

## Care & handling

Muscovies are generally low-maintenance. Keep their bedding dry, refresh water frequently, and check the run for hazards. They tolerate handling but prefer not to be picked up; support the body and keep the strong wings controlled if you must lift one.

Hens are devoted, broody mothers and will hatch and raise ducklings reliably with minimal intervention. Because drakes have sharp claws, handle them with care to avoid scratches.

## Health & lifespan

With good care, Muscovy Ducks commonly live 8-12 years. They are notably hardy and cold-tolerant, though their facial caruncles can be susceptible to frostbite in harsh winters, so a draft-free shelter helps.

Watch for common waterfowl issues such as bumblefoot from rough or wet surfaces, niacin deficiency causing leg weakness, and external parasites. Keeping housing clean and dry prevents most problems. Consult an avian or poultry veterinarian if a bird shows lethargy, lameness, or appetite loss.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Muscovy Ducks loud?** No. They are among the quietest of all ducks, using soft hisses and chirps instead of loud quacking, which makes them ideal for backyards.

**Do Muscovy Ducks need a pond?** No. They need only shallow water to clean their bill and eyes, though they enjoy bathing. Note that Muscovies are considered invasive in some areas, so check local regulations before keeping them.

## Conclusion

The Muscovy Duck is a hardy, quiet, and self-sufficient choice for farms and homesteads. With a predator-proof shelter, fresh water, good feed, and room to forage, these gentle birds reward keepers with pest control, eggs, and years of low-fuss companionship.
