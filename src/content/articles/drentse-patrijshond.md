The Drentse Patrijshond (Dutch Partridge Dog) is a versatile hunting and companion breed from the Drenthe province of the Netherlands. Calm at home yet capable in the field, it suits active families who want a loyal, soft-natured all-rounder.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (roughly 19–24 kg) |
| Lifespan | 11–14 years |
| Coat | Medium-length, dense |
| Energy | Moderate to high |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

The Drentse Patrijshond is a balanced, medium-sized gundog, typically standing around 55–62 cm at the shoulder. The coat is medium-length and slightly wavy on the chest, with longer feathering on the ears, legs and tail. Colouring is white with brown (and sometimes orange) markings, often with a mantle.

Temperament is a defining trait: this is a gentle, loyal, intelligent dog that bonds closely with its family. It is affectionate and patient with children, generally sociable with other dogs, and more sensitive than a typical pointer — harsh handling damages the relationship quickly.

## Diet & nutrition

- Feed a complete, good-quality diet appropriate to age, weight and activity level.
- Split the daily amount into measured meals rather than free-feeding.
- Adjust portions when working or sporting activity rises or falls with the seasons.
- Keep treats modest and counted toward the daily total to avoid excess weight.
- Provide constant access to fresh water, especially after exercise.

## Exercise & training

As a working gundog, the Drentse needs real daily activity — typically an hour or more of walking, play, scent work and free running in a safe area. Mental stimulation matters as much as physical exercise; bored dogs may become restless.

This breed is intelligent and eager to please but also independent and sensitive. Reward-based, consistent and patient training works best. Early socialisation helps it stay confident and friendly. Many enjoy retrieving, tracking, agility and other canine sports.

## Grooming & care

- Brush the coat about once or twice a week to control shedding and prevent mats behind the ears and on the feathering.
- Bathe only when needed; the coat is fairly weather-resistant.
- Check and clean the ears regularly, as drop ears can trap moisture and debris.
- Keep nails trimmed and maintain a routine dental-care habit.

## Health & lifespan

The Drentse Patrijshond is generally a hardy, healthy breed with a typical lifespan of around 11–14 years. As with many medium and larger dogs, responsible breeders screen for hip dysplasia, and the breed can be linked to hereditary conditions such as progressive retinal atrophy. Routine veterinary check-ups, weight management and good ear care support a long, comfortable life. Always consult your veterinarian about specific health concerns.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Drentse Patrijshond a good family dog?**
Yes. It is known for being gentle, loyal and patient, making it well suited to active families with children.

**Does it need a lot of exercise?**
It does. As a working gundog it needs daily physical exercise plus mental challenges such as scent games or training.

## Conclusion

The Drentse Patrijshond is a versatile, affectionate Dutch gundog that blends field ability with a calm, devoted home life. Give it daily exercise, kind and consistent training, and regular grooming, and it rewards you with a sensitive, loyal companion for many years.
