The Broholmer is a large Danish mastiff-type breed once used to guard estates and hunt large game. Named after Sehestedt Castle on the Broholm estate, it nearly disappeared after the World Wars before dedicated Danish breeders revived it in the 1970s. Today it is a calm, dignified family guardian known for its impressive size paired with a gentle, even-tempered nature.

## Appearance & temperament

The Broholmer is a powerfully built, rectangular dog standing roughly 70-75 cm (27-30 in) at the shoulder for males, with females slightly smaller. It has a broad head, strong neck with some loose skin, and a confident, unhurried gait. The short coat is most often yellow with a black mask, but golden-red and black are also recognized.

Despite its guarding heritage, the breed is calm, friendly and self-assured rather than aggressive. Broholmers tend to be affectionate with their families, tolerant of children, and naturally watchful without being nervous or noisy. They form strong bonds and dislike being left alone for long periods.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced diet formulated for large or giant breeds. Because Broholmers grow slowly and reach a great size, puppies should be raised on large-breed puppy food with controlled calcium and energy levels to support steady, healthy bone development.

Divide daily food into two meals rather than one large portion to reduce the risk of bloat, a serious concern in deep-chested breeds. Monitor body condition closely, as excess weight strains joints and shortens lifespan. Provide constant access to fresh water and avoid heavy exercise immediately before or after eating.

## Exercise & training

The Broholmer has modest exercise needs for its size. Daily walks plus access to a securely fenced yard usually suffice; this is not a high-stamina working breed and it can overheat or stress growing joints with excessive activity. Avoid forced running and repetitive jumping while the dog is still maturing.

Early socialization is essential to produce a stable, confident adult. Broholmers are intelligent and willing but can be independent, so calm, consistent, positive training works best. Their size makes leash manners and reliable recall important to establish young, while harsh methods tend to be counterproductive.

## Grooming & care

Grooming is straightforward. The short double coat needs only weekly brushing, increasing during seasonal sheds when a rubber curry or shedding tool helps remove loose hair. Bathe only as needed.

Check and wipe any facial or neck folds to keep them clean and dry, and tend to ears, teeth and nails on a regular schedule. Like many large breeds, Broholmers can drool somewhat, so keep a cloth handy. They tolerate heat poorly, so provide shade and water in warm weather.

## Health & lifespan

The Broholmer typically lives 8-11 years, in line with other giant breeds. Thanks to a carefully managed revival, the population is relatively healthy, but the gene pool remains small. Responsible breeders screen for hip and elbow dysplasia and heart conditions.

Owners should also be aware of bloat (gastric torsion), joint problems, and general giant-breed concerns. Keeping the dog lean, feeding measured meals, and scheduling routine veterinary checkups all support a longer, more comfortable life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Broholmers good family dogs?**
Yes. They are typically calm, patient and affectionate with their families, including children, while their watchful nature makes them natural guardians. Early socialization helps ensure a well-rounded adult.

**Do Broholmers need a lot of exercise?**
No. They have low to moderate energy and are content with daily walks and yard time. Over-exercising, especially during growth, can harm their joints, so moderate activity is best.

## Conclusion

The Broholmer is a gentle giant: a calm, loyal Danish guardian that combines impressive size with an easygoing temperament. With moderate exercise, sensible large-breed feeding, early socialization and attention to giant-breed health, it makes a devoted and dignified family companion for owners prepared to meet its space and care needs.
