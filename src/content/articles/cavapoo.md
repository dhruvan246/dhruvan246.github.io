The Cavapoo is a popular companion crossbreed that pairs the Cavalier King Charles Spaniel with the Poodle (usually a Miniature or Toy Poodle). Also called the Cavoodle, it is prized for being gentle, sociable, and well suited to family life.

## Overview

| Trait | Details |

| Lifespan | Typically 12–15 years |

| Energy | Moderate |

## Appearance & temperament

Cavapoos are small dogs that usually weigh between roughly 9 and 25 pounds (4–11 kg), with size depending on whether a Toy or Miniature Poodle was used. Their coats range from loosely wavy to tightly curled and come in many colors, including apricot, cream, chestnut, black, and parti combinations. They have round, expressive eyes and softly feathered ears.

In temperament, the Cavapoo tends to be affectionate, eager to please, and people-oriented. They generally bond closely with their families, get along with children and other pets, and dislike being left alone for long stretches. This loving nature makes them appealing companions but also means they can be prone to separation anxiety.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food formulated for small breeds and appropriate to your dog's life stage. Because Cavapoos are small, portions should be measured carefully to prevent weight gain, which can strain joints and overall health. Divide the daily amount into two meals.

Fresh water should always be available. Treats are useful for training but should make up only a small share of daily calories. If you are unsure about portion sizes or considering a diet change, ask your veterinarian for guidance tailored to your individual dog.

## Exercise & training

Cavapoos have moderate energy and typically thrive on around 30–60 minutes of activity each day. This can include walks, play sessions, and games that engage their minds, such as puzzle feeders or fetch. Mental stimulation is just as important as physical exercise for this intelligent crossbreed.

Thanks to their Poodle heritage and willingness to please, Cavapoos generally respond well to positive, reward-based training. Early socialization with people, places, and other animals helps them grow into confident adults. Consistent, gentle training also reduces nuisance behaviors like excessive barking.

## Grooming & care

The Cavapoo's coat needs regular attention. Brush several times a week, ideally daily for curlier coats, to prevent mats and tangles. Many owners schedule professional grooming or clipping every six to eight weeks to keep the coat manageable.

Check and clean the ears regularly, since their floppy shape can trap moisture and lead to infections. Trim nails as needed, and brush the teeth frequently to support dental health. While often described as low-shedding, no dog is truly hypoallergenic.

## Health & lifespan

Cavapoos generally live about 12 to 15 years. As a cross of two breeds, they can inherit conditions seen in either parent line, including mitral valve heart disease (associated with Cavaliers), eye disorders such as progressive retinal atrophy, luxating patellas, hip issues, and ear infections.

Choosing a responsible breeder who health-tests parent dogs lowers the risk of inherited problems. Routine veterinary checkups, dental care, parasite prevention, and weight management all support a long, healthy life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Cavapoos hypoallergenic?**
No dog is fully hypoallergenic, but Cavapoos often shed less than many breeds thanks to their Poodle ancestry, which some allergy sufferers tolerate better. Spending time with the breed before adopting is wise.

**Do Cavapoos bark a lot?**
They are not known as heavy barkers, but they may bark when bored, anxious, or under-stimulated. Adequate exercise, mental enrichment, and training help keep barking in check.

## Conclusion

The Cavapoo is an affectionate, adaptable small companion that fits well into families and varied households. With regular grooming, moderate exercise, consistent training, and routine veterinary care, this friendly crossbreed can be a devoted companion for many years.
