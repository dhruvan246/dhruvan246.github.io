Friendly, curious and ruled by their nose, Beagles are one of the most popular family dogs in the world. Bred to hunt in packs, they're sociable, energetic and endlessly food-motivated.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (9–14 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–15 years |
| Coat | Short, dense |
| Energy | High |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Beagles are compact scent hounds with droopy ears, soulful eyes and a classic tricolour or tan-and-white coat. They are happy, gentle and great with children and other dogs — but that powerful nose means they'll follow a scent anywhere.

## Diet & nutrition

- Measure meals carefully — Beagles are prone to obesity and will eat anything.
- Secure food and bins; they're notorious scavengers.
- Use food rewards for training, but count them in the daily total.

## Exercise & training

- Need at least an hour of exercise daily — walks, play and sniffing games.
- Always keep them on a lead or in a secure yard; their nose overrides recall.
- Training takes patience and high-value treats. They can be stubborn but respond well to reward-based methods.

## Grooming & care

- Short coat needs only weekly brushing (they do shed).
- Check and clean those long ears regularly to prevent infections.
- Routine nail trims and dental care.

## Health & lifespan

Generally healthy, but watch for obesity, ear infections, epilepsy and a condition called "Chinese Beagle Syndrome." Keeping them lean and active prevents many problems.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Beagles bark a lot?**
They're vocal — known for a distinctive bay or howl. Training and exercise help.

**Can Beagles be left off-lead?**
Rarely safe — their nose leads them astray. Use secure areas only.

## Conclusion

The Beagle is a cheerful, affectionate family dog that thrives on company and activity. Give them plenty of exercise, watch their waistline, and secure your fences — and you'll have a merry companion for years.
