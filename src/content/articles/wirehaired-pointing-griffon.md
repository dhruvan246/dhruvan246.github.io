The Wirehaired Pointing Griffon, sometimes called the Korthals Griffon, is a versatile hunting dog developed in continental Europe to point and retrieve on land and in water. Known for its harsh, weatherproof coat and shaggy beard and eyebrows, this breed is a hardworking field companion and an affectionate, people-oriented family dog.

## Appearance & temperament

The Wirehaired Pointing Griffon is a medium-to-large, sturdily built dog standing roughly 50–60 cm at the shoulder. Its hallmark is a functional double coat: a soft, dense undercoat beneath a harsh, straight wiry outer coat that resists water and thorny cover. Prominent eyebrows, a bushy beard and mustache give the breed a friendly, almost human expression. Coats are typically steel gray with brown markings, though chestnut and roan shades are also common.

Bred as a close-working gundog, the Griffon is intelligent, eager to please and notably devoted to its family. The breed is generally gentle and outgoing, often described as a sensitive "supreme gun dog" that thrives on companionship and dislikes being left alone for long.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced diet suited to an active large-breed dog, divided into two daily meals rather than one large portion. Adjust amounts to body condition, age and activity level, since working dogs need more calories than less-active pets. As a deep-chested breed, the Griffon can be vulnerable to bloat, so avoid vigorous exercise right before and after meals. Keep fresh water available at all times, and consult your veterinarian before making diet changes.

## Exercise & training

This is an athletic, high-energy breed that needs substantial daily activity — typically more than an hour of running, swimming, retrieving or dog sports. A brief walk around the block is rarely enough. Griffons love water and excel at field work, agility and tracking, and they are happiest when both body and mind are engaged.

Highly trainable and naturally cooperative, the breed responds best to gentle, consistent, reward-based methods begun early. They are sensitive dogs that do not respond well to harsh handling. Early socialization builds confidence, and a bored or under-exercised Griffon may resort to nuisance behaviors, so channel that drive into structured activity.

## Grooming & care

The wiry coat is relatively low-maintenance but benefits from weekly brushing to remove loose hair, dirt and burrs. Many owners hand-strip the coat periodically to preserve its harsh, weather-resistant texture rather than clipping it. Clean and dry the beard after meals and water work, and inspect the drop ears regularly to help prevent infections. Routine nail trimming and dental care complete a simple grooming schedule.

## Health & lifespan

The Wirehaired Pointing Griffon is generally a healthy, hardy breed with a typical lifespan of around 12–15 years. Like many large, deep-chested dogs, it can be predisposed to hip dysplasia, gastric bloat (gastric dilatation-volvulus), ear infections and certain eye conditions. Selecting a responsible breeder who health-tests their lines, keeping your dog at a healthy weight and maintaining regular veterinary checkups all help support a long, active life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Wirehaired Pointing Griffons good family dogs?**
Yes. They are affectionate, gentle and bond closely with their families, doing well with children when interactions are supervised. They do need plenty of daily exercise and companionship.

**Do Wirehaired Pointing Griffons shed a lot?**
They shed relatively little. The harsh coat needs weekly brushing and occasional hand-stripping rather than heavy grooming.

## Conclusion

The Wirehaired Pointing Griffon is a rugged, intelligent and devoted gundog that pairs serious field ability with a loving, family-friendly nature. Give this breed ample exercise, early positive training and close companionship, and it will reward you with a versatile, loyal partner for many years.
