Instantly recognisable by its silhouette, beard and bushy eyebrows, the Scottish Terrier — the "Scottie" — is a dignified, confident little dog. Independent and loyal, the Scottie is famous for its determined, sometimes stubborn character.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Small (8.5–10 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–15 years |
| Coat | Harsh, wiry double coat |
| Energy | Moderate |
| Good with kids | Best with older, respectful children |

## Appearance & temperament

Scotties have a compact body, distinctive beard and a hard, wiry coat (often black). They are confident, independent and loyal — devoted to their family, reserved with strangers, and bold for their size. Nicknamed the "Diehard" for their tenacity.

## Diet & nutrition

- Quality small-breed food in measured portions.

## Grooming

- Brush a few times a week; the wiry coat is traditionally hand-stripped, though many pets are clipped.

## Exercise & training

- Daily walks and play; they're moderately active with a strong prey drive.
- Independent and stubborn; patient, consistent, positive training and early socialisation help.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for "Scottie cramp" (a movement disorder), von Willebrand's disease (clotting), bladder cancer (the breed has higher risk), and skin issues. Choose reputable breeders and keep up vet care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Scotties stubborn?**
Yes — independent and strong-willed; patient, positive training works best.

**Are they good watchdogs?**
Yes — alert and confident, they make excellent little watchdogs.

## Conclusion

The Scottish Terrier is a dignified, loyal and tenacious companion with unmistakable style. Respect their independence, keep up grooming, provide training, and you'll have a devoted little character for years.
