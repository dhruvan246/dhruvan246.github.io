Strong, athletic and intensely loyal, the American Pit Bull Terrier is an affectionate and people-loving breed often misunderstood. Well-bred and well-raised, the "Pit Bull" is a devoted, fun-loving family companion.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (14–27 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–14 years |
| Coat | Short, smooth |
| Energy | High |
| Good with kids | Often excellent, with supervision |

## Appearance & temperament

Pit Bulls are muscular and athletic with a short coat and a broad, smiling face. They are affectionate, eager to please and devoted to their people — typically very friendly toward humans, though some can be dog-selective, so socialisation matters.

## Exercise & training

- Need plenty of daily exercise and mental work.
- Smart and eager to please; they respond very well to positive, reward-based training. **Early socialisation is important**, especially around other dogs.

## Grooming & care

- Easy — the short coat needs only weekly brushing.

## Legal note

Some areas have breed-specific legislation affecting ownership or insurance — check local laws before getting one.

## Health & lifespan

Generally healthy and robust. Watch for hip dysplasia, skin allergies, and certain heart conditions. Choose responsible breeders and keep up regular vet care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Pit Bulls good family dogs?**
Many are wonderful, affectionate family dogs — temperament depends heavily on breeding, socialisation and training.

**Do they get along with other dogs?**
Some are dog-selective; early socialisation and careful management help.

## Conclusion

The American Pit Bull Terrier is a loyal, affectionate and athletic companion for a responsible, committed owner. Provide exercise, positive training and good socialisation, and you'll have a devoted, loving friend.
