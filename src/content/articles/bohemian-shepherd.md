The Bohemian Shepherd, known in its native Czech Republic as the Chodský pes or Chodenhund, is an old herding and guarding breed from the Chodsko region near the Bavarian border. Once used to watch flocks and patrol borders, this medium-sized, long-coated dog is prized today as a versatile, devoted family companion.

## Appearance & temperament

The Bohemian Shepherd is a well-proportioned, agile dog that often resembles a smaller, lighter German Shepherd with a fuller coat. The breed is almost always black with rich tan or rust markings on the face, legs and chest. A thick ruff frames the neck, and the bushy tail is carried low at rest. Ears are erect and triangular, giving an alert, intelligent expression.

In character, these dogs are friendly, lively and deeply loyal. They bond closely with their families, are gentle with children, and tend to be playful well into adulthood. As a herding and watchdog breed, they are naturally attentive and will bark to alert, but they are not typically aggressive. Early socialisation helps them remain confident and relaxed around strangers and other animals.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, good-quality diet appropriate for an active medium-sized dog. Most adults do well on two measured meals a day, with portions adjusted for age, weight and activity level. Puppies need a growth-formula food split across several smaller meals. Because the breed is energetic and athletic, obesity is uncommon, but treats should still be counted toward daily calories. Always provide fresh water, and introduce any diet changes gradually.

## Exercise & training

This is an energetic working breed that needs substantial daily activity, ideally an hour or more of walks, running, fetch or off-lead play. Bohemian Shepherds excel at dog sports such as agility, obedience, herding trials and tracking, which channel both body and mind. Mental stimulation is as important as physical exercise, so puzzle toys and training games help prevent boredom.

Highly intelligent and eager to please, they respond very well to positive, reward-based training. They learn quickly and thrive on having a job to do. Consistent, kind handling from puppyhood produces a well-mannered, biddable companion.

## Grooming & care

The long double coat is surprisingly low-maintenance for its length. Brush once or twice a week to remove loose hair and prevent tangles, increasing to several times weekly during the heavier seasonal sheds in spring and autumn. Bathe only when needed. Routine care includes regular nail trimming, dental brushing and checking the ears for dirt or moisture.

## Health & lifespan

The Bohemian Shepherd is considered a generally hardy, healthy breed, helped by a relatively diverse gene pool. A typical lifespan is around 12 to 15 years. As with many medium and larger breeds, responsible breeders screen for hip and elbow dysplasia, and owners should watch for general issues such as ear infections in any active outdoor dog. Routine veterinary check-ups, vaccinations, parasite prevention and dental care all support a long, healthy life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Bohemian Shepherds good family dogs?**
Yes. They are loyal, gentle and playful, usually getting on well with children and bonding strongly with their household, which makes them excellent active family companions.

**Do Bohemian Shepherds need a lot of exercise?**
They do. As an energetic herding breed they need at least an hour of daily activity plus mental challenges, so they suit owners with an active lifestyle rather than a sedentary home.

## Conclusion

The Bohemian Shepherd is a loyal, intelligent and athletic breed that combines a striking long black-and-tan coat with a friendly, trainable nature. Given enough exercise, mental engagement and companionship, this Czech herder makes a devoted, versatile partner for active individuals and families alike.
