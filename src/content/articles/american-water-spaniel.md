The American Water Spaniel is a versatile, medium-sized gundog developed in the upper Midwest of the United States to hunt and retrieve waterfowl and upland game from boats and marshes. Compact, eager and affectionate, this relatively rare breed is the state dog of Wisconsin and makes a devoted companion for active owners.

## Appearance & temperament

The American Water Spaniel is a sturdy, well-balanced dog, with both sexes generally standing around 15 to 18 inches at the shoulder. Its defining feature is a coat that ranges from tightly curled to wavy ("marcel"), dense enough to protect against cold water and rough cover. The coat comes in solid shades of brown, liver or chocolate, sometimes with small white markings on the chest or toes. The ears are long and feathered, and the overall build reflects a working dog bred for both water and land.

Temperamentally, this breed is friendly, intelligent and eager to please, while retaining an independent, sometimes sensitive streak. American Water Spaniels typically bond closely with their families and can be reserved with strangers. Early, consistent socialization helps them become confident, well-mannered companions.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced diet formulated for an active medium-sized dog, with portions matched to age, weight and activity level. Because these are working spaniels, their needs may be higher during hunting or training seasons and lower during quieter periods. Keep fresh water available, monitor body condition to avoid excess weight, and divide the daily ration into two meals for many adults. Ask your veterinarian for guidance on the most appropriate food and feeding amount.

## Exercise & training

This is an energetic, athletic breed that needs substantial daily activity to stay content. Brisk walks, retrieving games, hunting work and especially swimming are ideal outlets, as the breed was developed to work eagerly in water. Without enough physical and mental stimulation, an American Water Spaniel can become bored, vocal or mischievous.

Training tends to be rewarding because the breed is bright and willing, though some individuals can be sensitive or a little stubborn. They respond best to patient, positive, reward-based methods begun early. They enjoy having a job, whether hunting, obedience, dock diving or other canine sports.

## Grooming & care

The water-repellent coat needs regular attention to stay healthy. Weekly brushing helps prevent mats and tangles, especially in the curlier areas and feathering. Bathe only as needed, since over-bathing can strip the coat's natural oils that aid water resistance. Routine care also includes regular nail trimming, dental care and checking the long, hanging ears, particularly after swimming, to help keep them clean and dry and reduce the chance of ear infections.

## Health & lifespan

The American Water Spaniel is generally a hardy breed with a typical lifespan of roughly 10 to 14 years. Like many dogs, it can be prone to hip dysplasia, certain eye conditions and recurrent ear infections linked to its drop ears. Responsible breeders screen their dogs for known issues. Regular veterinary checkups, a sensible diet, a healthy weight and consistent exercise all support a long, comfortable life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are American Water Spaniels good family dogs?**
Yes. They are affectionate and loyal with their families and can do well with children, though their energy and sometimes sensitive nature mean early socialization and supervision around young kids are recommended.

**Do American Water Spaniels need a lot of exercise?**
Absolutely. As an active gundog, this breed needs vigorous daily activity, with swimming and retrieving being especially well-suited outlets for its energy and working drive.

## Conclusion

The American Water Spaniel is a capable, devoted companion best suited to active owners who can provide plenty of exercise, kind and consistent training and a sense of purpose. In return, this rare American water dog offers loyalty, enthusiasm and a close bond with its family.
