The Braque du Bourbonnais is an old French pointing breed from the Bourbonnais region of central France, prized as a versatile hunting dog and an affectionate family companion. Known for its ticked or roan coat and a famously short (sometimes naturally absent) tail, this medium-sized gundog combines a strong nose and steady pointing instinct with a gentle, people-focused temperament.

## Overview

| Trait | Details |

## Appearance & temperament

The Braque du Bourbonnais is a balanced, muscular dog of moderate size, slightly longer than tall. Its short, dense coat shows fine ticking or roaning, typically in fawn ("faded lilac") or liver shades, often described in French as peach-blossom or wine-dregs coloring. Some puppies are born with a naturally short tail or no tail at all.

Temperamentally, the breed is affectionate, eager to please and strongly bonded to its people. These dogs are intelligent and sensitive, tending to "stick close" to their owner in the field and at home. They are typically friendly with children and other dogs, though their hunting drive means small animals may trigger chasing.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food appropriate to the breed's age, weight and high activity level. Active adults and working dogs need quality protein and fat to fuel their stamina, while less-active pets need portions controlled to avoid excess weight. Divide the daily ration into two meals, provide fresh water at all times, and adjust amounts based on body condition. Because deep-chested, active breeds can be prone to digestive upset, avoid heavy exercise immediately before or after meals. Ask your veterinarian for guidance suited to your individual dog.

## Exercise & training

This is an athletic, energetic gundog that needs substantial daily exercise, ideally an hour or more of running, hiking, fieldwork or active play. A bored or under-exercised Braque can become restless. Scent games, retrieving and structured activities such as field trials or agility suit the breed well.

The Braque du Bourbonnais is intelligent and very willing to learn, but it is also sensitive, so positive, reward-based training works far better than harsh corrections. Early socialization and consistent, gentle guidance produce a confident, well-mannered companion.

## Grooming & care

Grooming needs are modest thanks to the short coat. Weekly brushing removes loose hair and keeps the coat healthy; bathe only as needed. Check and clean the ears regularly, as drop ears can trap moisture and debris. Trim nails routinely and maintain a dental-care routine. As an active outdoor dog, it should be checked for ticks, burrs and minor cuts after time in the field.

## Health & lifespan

The Braque du Bourbonnais is generally a hardy, healthy breed with a typical lifespan of around 12 to 15 years. As with many active, deep-chested breeds, responsible care includes attention to joint health (such as hip soundness) and eye and ear health. Buying from breeders who health-test their stock, keeping your dog at a healthy weight, and scheduling regular veterinary checkups all support a long, active life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Braque du Bourbonnais a good family dog?**
Yes. It is affectionate, gentle and typically very good with children, bonding closely with its family. It does best with people who can meet its high exercise needs.

**Does the Braque du Bourbonnais really have no tail?**
Many are born with a naturally short tail or are tailless, a long-standing trait of the breed. Others have longer tails, and both occur naturally.

## Conclusion

The Braque du Bourbonnais is a rare, versatile French pointer that pairs serious hunting ability with a warm, devoted personality. For an active owner or family who can provide ample exercise, training and companionship, this affectionate gundog makes a loyal and rewarding partner.
