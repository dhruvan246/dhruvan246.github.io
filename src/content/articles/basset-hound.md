With those long velvety ears, droopy eyes and a nose second only to the Bloodhound's, the Basset Hound is a gentle, easy-going companion with a stubborn streak and a big heart.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (20–29 kg) |
| Lifespan | 10–13 years |
| Coat | Short, dense |
| Energy | Low to moderate |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Bassets are long, low and heavy-boned, with the breed's trademark long ears and soulful expression. They are friendly, patient and devoted — relaxed at home but ruled by their powerful nose outdoors.

## Diet & nutrition

- Measured meals — **Bassets gain weight easily**, which strains their long back and joints.
- Limit treats and secure food (they're food-driven scavengers).

## Exercise & training

- Moderate daily walks keep them fit and prevent obesity.
- Keep them leashed — a scent will lead them astray, and recall is poor.
- Training takes patience and tasty rewards; they're smart but stubborn.

## Grooming & care

- Short coat needs weekly brushing (they shed).
- **Clean those long ears regularly** to prevent infections, and wipe facial folds and droopy eyes.
- Routine nail trims and dental care.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for obesity, ear infections, back problems (long spine), bloat, and eye conditions. Keeping them lean is the single best thing you can do for their health.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Basset Hounds bark a lot?**
They have a loud, distinctive bay and can be vocal.

**Are they good family dogs?**
Yes — gentle, tolerant and great with children.

## Conclusion

The Basset Hound is a charming, mellow companion with a comical personality. Keep them lean, clean those ears, and secure your fences against that famous nose, and you'll have a devoted friend.
