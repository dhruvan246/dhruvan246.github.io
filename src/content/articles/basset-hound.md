Famous for their long velvety ears, soulful droopy eyes and short, sturdy legs, the Basset Hound is a gentle, easy-going scent hound. Bred to track game by smell, they pair a powerful nose with a famously laid-back, affectionate nature.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Large-boned but short; typically 20–30 kg |
| Lifespan | 10–13 years |
| Coat | Short, smooth and dense |
| Energy | Low to moderate |
| Good with kids | Excellent — patient and tolerant |

## Appearance & temperament

The Basset Hound is long and low to the ground, with a heavy, dense body carried on short legs. The breed is heavy-boned for its height, with loose skin, long drooping ears and a kind, wrinkled expression. Temperamentally they are friendly, calm and devoted, fitting in well with families and other pets. They can be notably stubborn, and outdoors their nose tends to rule their decisions.

## Diet & nutrition

Bassets are food-motivated and prone to weight gain, which is a real concern for a breed already carrying a long back on short legs. Feed measured portions of a complete, balanced dog food appropriate to their age and activity level, and keep treats modest. Because they are determined scavengers, store food securely and discourage counter-surfing. Always provide fresh water.

## Exercise & training

Despite their relaxed reputation, Bassets need regular moderate exercise to stay fit and avoid obesity. A couple of steady daily walks usually suffice; avoid overexerting puppies while their joints develop. Keep them on a lead or in a securely fenced area, because a captivating scent can lead them off and recall is often unreliable. Training works best with patience, consistency and tasty, positive rewards rather than repetition or pressure.

## Grooming & care

The short coat is low-maintenance but does shed, so a weekly brush helps manage loose hair. Their long ears have limited airflow and need regular checking and gentle cleaning to reduce the risk of infection. Wipe facial folds and the area around the eyes to keep them clean and dry. Routine nail trims and regular tooth brushing round out basic care.

## Health & lifespan

Most Basset Hounds live around 10–13 years. Their build and traits make certain issues more common, including obesity, ear infections, eye conditions, and back or joint strain linked to their long spine and heavy frame. Like many deep-chested breeds, they can be susceptible to bloat. Keeping your dog lean, scheduling regular veterinary checkups and choosing a responsible breeder are the best general steps for long-term health.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Basset Hounds bark a lot?**
They are known for a loud, deep bay and can be quite vocal, especially when they catch an interesting scent or are left alone.

**Are Basset Hounds good with children?**
Yes. They are typically gentle, patient and tolerant, which makes them a popular choice for families with kids.

## Conclusion

The Basset Hound is a mellow, affectionate companion with a comical streak and a world-class nose. Keep them at a healthy weight, clean those long ears regularly, and secure your yard against their scent-driven wanderlust, and you will have a devoted, good-natured friend for years.
