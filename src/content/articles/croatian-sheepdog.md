The Croatian Sheepdog (Hrvatski Ovčar) is an agile, alert herding breed native to Croatia, where it has worked alongside shepherds for centuries. Compact, quick, and famously devoted, it remains a hardworking dog that thrives when given a job and an active home.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Size | Medium (16–21 in / 40–53 cm tall, 13–20 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–14 years |
| Coat | Short on the face and legs, longer wavy or curly black coat on the body |
| Energy | Very high |
| Good with kids | Yes, with early socialization and supervision |

## Appearance & temperament

The Croatian Sheepdog is a lightly built, square-proportioned dog with a foxy, wedge-shaped head and watchful expression. Its hallmark is a predominantly black coat, sometimes with small white markings on the chest or toes, that is short on the head and legs but soft, wavy, or curly across the body. Pricked or semi-pricked ears and an alert stance give it a lively look.

In temperament, this breed is highly intelligent, energetic, and intensely loyal to its family. Bred to move flocks independently, it is courageous, vigilant, and naturally protective without being aggressive. It bonds closely with its owner, can be reserved with strangers, and makes an excellent watchdog.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, high-quality diet formulated for active dogs of this size. Because the Croatian Sheepdog burns a lot of energy, working or sport dogs may need more calories than a companion living a quieter life, so adjust portions to maintain a lean, athletic build.

Divide the daily amount into two meals to support steady energy. Always provide fresh water, keep treats to a small share of daily calories, and monitor body condition since this breed can stay slim but should never look bony. Consult your veterinarian about life-stage and activity-based feeding.

## Exercise & training

This is a demanding breed when it comes to activity. A Croatian Sheepdog needs vigorous daily exercise, ideally an hour or more, combined with mental challenges. Long walks, running, fetch, and herding or dog sports such as agility, obedience, and flyball suit it well. Without enough stimulation it may become bored, vocal, or destructive.

Training is usually rewarding because the breed learns quickly and is eager to work with its handler. Use consistent, positive, reward-based methods and start socialization early to temper natural wariness toward strangers. Its herding instinct can lead to nipping at heels, so redirect this behavior with games and structured tasks.

## Grooming & care

The Croatian Sheepdog is low-maintenance to groom. Brush the coat about once a week to remove loose hair and prevent tangles in the longer body fur; brushing more often during seasonal sheds helps control hair around the home. The breed is naturally clean, so bathe only when needed.

Check and trim nails regularly, brush the teeth several times a week, and inspect the ears for debris or signs of irritation. As a hardy outdoor worker, it tolerates varied weather but still appreciates shelter and companionship rather than isolation.

## Health & lifespan

The Croatian Sheepdog is generally a robust, healthy breed with a typical lifespan of around 12 to 14 years. Its working heritage and modest popularity have helped keep it relatively free of widespread inherited disorders.

As with many active dogs, responsible breeders may screen for hip dysplasia and eye conditions, and owners should watch for everyday issues affecting joints, ears, and dental health. Routine veterinary checkups, parasite prevention, vaccinations, and weight management are the best ways to support a long, active life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Croatian Sheepdogs good family pets?**
Yes. They are devoted and affectionate with their families and can do well with children when socialized early. However, their high energy and herding drive mean they suit active households that can provide plenty of exercise and engagement.

**Do Croatian Sheepdogs bark a lot?**
They can be vocal, as alert herders that bark to signal activity and warn of strangers. Early training, adequate exercise, and mental stimulation help keep barking under control.

## Conclusion

The Croatian Sheepdog is a bright, energetic, and loyal herding companion best suited to owners who can meet its substantial exercise and mental needs. With early socialization, consistent training, and a job to do, this hardy Croatian native rewards its family with devotion, agility, and years of enthusiastic partnership.
