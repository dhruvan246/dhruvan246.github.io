A fluffy, friendly spitz from the Netherlands, the Keeshond is famous for its plush grey coat, expressive "spectacles" markings and warm, sociable nature. Devoted and people-loving, the "Kees" makes a wonderful family companion.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (16–20 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–15 years |
| Coat | Thick, plush double coat |
| Energy | Moderate |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Keeshonden have a stand-off grey, black and cream coat, a curled tail and dark markings around the eyes (the "spectacles"). They are friendly, affectionate and outgoing — true companion dogs that bond closely with the family and dislike being left alone.

## Exercise & training

- Moderate daily exercise — walks and play.
- Intelligent and eager to please; they train easily with positive methods and enjoy activities. They can be vocal.

## Grooming

- **Brush 2–3 times a week**, daily during heavy seasonal sheds.
- Never shave the double coat.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for hip dysplasia, luxating patella, heart conditions, and a hormonal condition (primary hyperparathyroidism) in some lines. Choose health-tested lines and keep up regular vet care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Keeshonden friendly?**
Very — outgoing, affectionate and great with families; they're companions, not guard dogs.

**Do they shed a lot?**
Yes — the plush coat sheds heavily, especially seasonally; regular brushing is essential.

## Conclusion

The Keeshond is a friendly, affectionate and beautiful companion that thrives on family life. Keep up the grooming, provide moderate exercise and plenty of company, and you'll have a smiling, devoted friend.
