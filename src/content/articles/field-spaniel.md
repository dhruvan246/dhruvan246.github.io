The Field Spaniel is a calm, affectionate gundog that often flies under the radar compared with its Cocker and Springer cousins. Bred in Britain to flush and retrieve game, it pairs a gentle, devoted nature with a love of outdoor work, making it a steady companion for an active, attentive home.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (around 18–25 kg) |
| Lifespan | Roughly 10–13 years |
| Coat | Single, moderately long, glossy and feathered |
| Energy | Moderate to high |
| Good with kids | Yes, gentle and sociable |

## Appearance & temperament

The Field Spaniel is a well-balanced, moderately built spaniel, noticeably longer in body than a Cocker and a touch more substantial. Its single coat is glossy and silky, lying close to the body with feathering on the ears, chest, legs and underside. Common colours include solid black, liver, and roan, sometimes with limited tan markings. The long, low-set ears and soft, expressive eyes give the breed a kind, thoughtful look.

In temperament, Field Spaniels are typically calm, sensitive and deeply attached to their people. They tend to be friendly with children and other dogs, though their loyalty can make them reserved with strangers at first. They thrive on companionship and may become anxious if left alone for long stretches.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food appropriate to the Field Spaniel's age, size and activity level. Active working or sporting dogs may need more calories than a primarily companion dog, so adjust portions to keep your dog lean. Because the breed can be prone to weight gain when under-exercised, measure meals rather than free-feeding and limit treats. Always provide fresh water, and consult your veterinarian about the best diet for your individual dog, especially during growth or in the senior years.

## Exercise & training

As a sporting breed, the Field Spaniel needs regular daily exercise: brisk walks, off-lead runs in safe areas, and games such as fetch all suit it well. It also enjoys having a job to do, and channels its natural instincts beautifully into scent work, retrieving games and canine sports. Mental stimulation matters as much as physical activity.

These are intelligent, willing dogs that respond best to patient, reward-based training. Because they are sensitive, harsh corrections can backfire. Start socialisation and gentle training early to build a confident, well-mannered adult.

## Grooming & care

The silky coat needs brushing a few times a week to prevent mats and tangles, with extra attention to the feathered areas. Trimming around the feet and ears helps keep the dog tidy. The long, pendulous ears trap moisture and debris, so check and clean them regularly to reduce the risk of ear infections. Routine nail trims and dental care round out a sensible grooming routine.

## Health & lifespan

Field Spaniels generally live around 10 to 13 years. Like many breeds, they can be predisposed to certain conditions, including hip dysplasia, eye disorders, and ear infections related to their drop ears. Buying from a responsible breeder who screens breeding stock, plus routine veterinary check-ups, helps catch problems early. Keeping your dog at a healthy weight supports joint and overall health throughout life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Field Spaniels good family dogs?** Yes. They are typically gentle, affectionate and sociable, getting along well with children and other pets when properly socialised, though they prefer not to be left alone for long periods.

**Do Field Spaniels need a lot of grooming?** They need regular care, including brushing several times a week, occasional trimming, and routine ear cleaning, but their coat is more manageable than that of some heavier-coated spaniels.

## Conclusion

The Field Spaniel is a loyal, even-tempered gundog that rewards owners who can provide daily exercise, consistent positive training and steady companionship. With good grooming, attentive ear care and regular veterinary attention, this charming and somewhat rare spaniel makes a devoted, well-rounded family member.
