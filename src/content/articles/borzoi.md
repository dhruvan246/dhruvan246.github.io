The Borzoi is a tall, graceful sighthound developed in Russia, where it was historically prized by the nobility for coursing game such as hares and wolves across open country. Sometimes still called the Russian Wolfhound, the breed combines breathtaking speed with a quiet, dignified manner that makes it a surprisingly gentle companion at home.

## Overview

## Appearance & temperament

The Borzoi has the classic sighthound silhouette: a long, narrow head, deep but tucked chest, arched loin, and long legs built for galloping. Its silky coat comes in many colors and patterns and forms a fuller frill around the neck and feathering on the legs and tail. Despite its size, the breed moves with light, flowing elegance.

Temperamentally, Borzoi are calm, affectionate, and somewhat reserved. They tend to bond closely with their families while remaining polite but aloof with strangers. They are quiet indoors and rarely prone to nuisance barking. Like most sighthounds, they have an independent streak and a powerful prey drive, so small running animals can trigger an instinctive chase.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food appropriate for the Borzoi's age, size, and activity level. Because they are deep-chested, dividing the daily ration into two meals and avoiding heavy exercise right around mealtimes is a sensible precaution. Maintaining a lean body condition is important, as their slender frame is not meant to carry excess weight. Always provide fresh water, and consult your veterinarian about the right portions and any diet changes.

## Exercise & training

Borzoi are often described as "couch potatoes" that nonetheless need real opportunities to run. Daily walks plus regular chances to sprint in a securely fenced area usually keep them content. Off-leash freedom in unfenced spaces is risky because their chase instinct can override recall.

Training works best with gentle, patient, reward-based methods. They are intelligent but independent and respond poorly to harsh corrections. Early socialization and reliable leash manners are especially valuable for such a large, fast dog.

## Grooming & care

The silky coat needs brushing a couple of times a week, with more frequent attention during seasonal shedding to manage loose hair and prevent tangles in the feathering. Routine care also includes regular nail trims, dental hygiene, and ear checks. The Borzoi's coat is relatively low-odor and stays fairly clean between baths.

## Health & lifespan

Borzoi are generally healthy, with a typical lifespan of around 9 to 12 years. As a deep-chested breed, they can be susceptible to bloat (gastric dilatation-volvulus), a serious emergency. Owners should also be aware of breed-associated concerns such as heart issues and sensitivity to certain anesthetics. Routine veterinary checkups support a long, comfortable life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Borzoi good apartment dogs?** They can adapt to apartment living because they are quiet and calm indoors, provided they get daily walks and regular access to a safe space where they can run.

**Do Borzoi get along with cats and small pets?** It varies by individual and early socialization, but their strong prey drive means small, fast-moving animals can trigger chasing, so introductions should be careful and supervised.

## Conclusion

The Borzoi is an elegant, gentle giant that pairs athletic grace with a calm household demeanor. With a secure place to run, patient training, routine grooming, and attentive veterinary care, this dignified sighthound can be a rewarding and affectionate companion.
