With their soulful eyes, silky ears and gentle nature, Cocker Spaniels are one of the most affectionate family dogs around. Originally bred to flush and retrieve birds, they're active, loving and eager to please.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (12–16 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–15 years |
| Coat | Silky, medium-length, feathered |
| Energy | Moderate to high |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Cockers have a compact, sturdy build, a beautiful feathered coat and famously long, silky ears. They are sweet-natured, sociable and sensitive dogs that bond deeply with their families.

## Diet & nutrition

- A quality diet in measured portions — Cockers can gain weight easily.
- Keep treats modest and meals consistent.

## Exercise & training

- Need around an hour of daily exercise plus play and sniffing.
- Respond beautifully to gentle, positive training — harsh methods upset this sensitive breed.
- Early socialisation builds confidence.

## Grooming — coat and ears

- Brush several times a week; many owners clip the coat for easier care.
- **Those long ears need special attention** — clean weekly to prevent infections, and keep them dry after eating or swimming.
- Regular professional grooming, nail trims and dental care.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for ear infections, eye conditions, hip dysplasia and certain heart issues. Some lines can show "rage syndrome," so choose a reputable breeder. Regular ear care prevents the most common problem.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Cocker Spaniels good family dogs?**
Yes — gentle, affectionate and great with children.

**Do they need a lot of grooming?**
Yes — regular brushing and diligent ear care are essential.

## Conclusion

The Cocker Spaniel is a loving, gentle companion that rewards kindness with deep devotion. Stay on top of grooming and ear care, give them daily activity, and they'll be a treasured family member for years.
