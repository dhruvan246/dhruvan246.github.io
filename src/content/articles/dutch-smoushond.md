The Dutch Smoushond (Hollandse Smoushond) is a small, rare working terrier-type breed from the Netherlands. Once kept around stables and shops to control rats, it nearly disappeared during the twentieth century and was rebuilt through a careful breeding program. Today it is prized as a cheerful, friendly companion with a distinctive shaggy yellow coat.

## Appearance & temperament

The Dutch Smoushond is a compact, nearly square little dog weighing around 9–10 kg (20–22 lb). Its hallmark is a rough, harsh, slightly tousled coat in shades of yellow, from straw to deep reddish-gold, with a charming "mustache" and beard that frame an expressive face. The ears are small and dropped, and the overall look is rustic rather than refined.

In temperament, the Smoushond is good-natured, lively, and affectionate. Unlike many terriers, it tends to be gentle and easygoing rather than feisty, though it remains alert and will announce visitors. It bonds closely with its family, gets along well with children and other dogs, and generally adapts well to both town and country living.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food appropriate for a small to medium breed and the dog's life stage. Because the Smoushond is a modestly sized, moderately active dog, portion control matters; measure meals rather than free-feeding to prevent weight gain, which strains joints and shortens lifespan.

Most adults do well on two measured meals a day. Use treats sparingly, especially during training, and keep them within roughly ten percent of daily calories. Always provide fresh water, and consult your veterinarian before making major diet changes or if your dog gains or loses weight unexpectedly.

## Exercise & training

The Dutch Smoushond has moderate energy needs. A couple of daily walks plus some active play or a safe romp in a fenced yard usually keeps it happy and fit. It enjoys games, sniffing exploration, and dog sports such as agility, but it is not a high-octane dog and adapts to a relaxed household.

Training is generally straightforward thanks to the breed's biddable, people-pleasing nature. It responds best to positive, reward-based methods and consistency. Early socialization with people, other animals, and new environments helps produce a confident, well-mannered adult. Mental enrichment through puzzle toys and short training sessions keeps this clever little dog engaged.

## Grooming & care

The harsh, wiry coat is fairly low-maintenance but does require routine attention. Brush once or twice a week to remove loose hair and prevent matting, paying attention to the beard and leg furnishings. The traditional coat is hand-stripped rather than clipped to preserve its texture and weather-resistant quality; many owners learn to do this or visit a groomer a few times a year.

Bathe only as needed, since over-washing softens the coat. Check and clean the ears regularly, trim nails as they grow, and maintain dental hygiene with regular tooth brushing.

## Health & lifespan

The Dutch Smoushond is generally a hardy breed with a typical lifespan of about 12 to 15 years. Because the modern population was rebuilt from a small number of dogs, responsible breeders work to maintain genetic diversity and health.

As with any breed, routine veterinary care matters: keep vaccinations and parasite prevention current, monitor weight, and watch for common small-dog concerns such as dental disease. Choosing a breeder who health-tests their stock and provides early socialization gives a puppy the best start. Discuss any specific health questions with your veterinarian.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Dutch Smoushonds rare?** Yes. The breed nearly went extinct in the twentieth century and remains uncommon, with most dogs found in the Netherlands and parts of Europe. It is not widely registered elsewhere.

**Is the Dutch Smoushond good for families?** Generally, yes. It is friendly, gentle for a terrier-type, and typically good with children and other pets, making it a pleasant companion for many household types.

## Conclusion

The Dutch Smoushond is a rare, cheerful little dog that combines a rustic, shaggy charm with a famously friendly disposition. Moderate exercise, weekly brushing with occasional hand-stripping, and steady positive training keep it thriving. For families seeking a sociable, adaptable companion and willing to seek out a responsible breeder, this Dutch original is a delightful choice.
