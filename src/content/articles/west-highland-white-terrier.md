Bright, confident and full of terrier spirit, the West Highland White Terrier — the "Westie" — is a sturdy little Scottish breed with a big personality. Friendly and lively, they make charming, characterful companions.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Small (6–10 kg) |
| Lifespan | 13–15 years |
| Coat | Harsh, white double coat |
| Energy | Moderate to high |
| Good with kids | Yes |

## Appearance & temperament

Westies have a bright white, harsh-textured coat and a confident, alert expression. They are friendly, spirited and self-assured — independent little terriers that are affectionate but not clingy.

## Diet & nutrition

- Quality small-breed food in measured portions; watch for weight gain.

## Grooming

- Brush a few times a week; the harsh coat is traditionally hand-stripped, though many pets are clipped.
- Keep the coat clean and check skin (they're prone to skin issues).

## Exercise & training

- Daily walks and play; they're lively and enjoy activity.
- Smart but independent; consistent, positive training and early socialisation help. They can be vocal and have a prey drive.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for skin allergies/dermatitis (common in the breed), luxating patella, and certain hereditary conditions. Regular skin and dental care help keep them healthy.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Westies bark a lot?**
They can be vocal little watchdogs; training helps manage it.

**Are they good family dogs?**
Yes — friendly, sturdy and lively, good with respectful children.

## Conclusion

The West Highland White Terrier is a bold, friendly and characterful companion full of terrier charm. Keep up coat and skin care, provide exercise and training, and you'll enjoy this spirited little Scot for years.
