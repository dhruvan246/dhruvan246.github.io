Known for its thick, woolly curls and cheerful disposition, the Barbet is an old French water dog traditionally used to retrieve waterfowl from marshes and ponds. Affectionate, athletic and eager to please, this versatile breed has grown in popularity as a friendly, sporty family companion.

## Appearance & temperament

The Barbet is a sturdy, medium-to-large dog covered in a thick, curly coat that may be black, brown, grey, fawn or pied, often with white markings. The signature beard that frames the muzzle gives the breed its name, derived from the French word "barbe" (beard). The coat is water-resistant and helped the dog work comfortably in cold, wet conditions.

In temperament, Barbets are typically friendly, joyful and devoted to their people. They are intelligent and sociable, generally getting along well with children and other dogs when properly introduced. The breed can be sensitive and thrives on companionship, so it dislikes being left alone for long periods.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food suited to your dog's age, size and activity level. As an active, water-loving breed, the Barbet does well on quality food that supports lean muscle and steady energy. Measure portions and avoid overfeeding, since extra weight places unnecessary strain on the joints of any larger dog.

Dividing the daily amount into two meals and keeping fresh water available is a sensible routine. Your veterinarian can recommend the right diet and portion size for your individual dog.

## Exercise & training

The Barbet is an energetic sporting breed that needs plenty of daily activity. Long walks, running, swimming and retrieving games suit its background as a water dog and help satisfy both body and mind. A Barbet that lacks exercise or stimulation may become bored and restless.

These dogs are clever and people-oriented, which makes them responsive to patient, positive, reward-based training. Early socialisation and basic obedience set the foundation for a well-mannered adult. Many Barbets enjoy canine sports such as agility, obedience and water work.

## Grooming & care

The Barbet's dense, curly coat needs regular care to stay healthy. Frequent brushing and combing help prevent the coat from matting, and many owners keep it trimmed to a manageable length. Because the coat tends to hold dirt and water, attention after swimming or muddy outings is important. The breed sheds relatively little, with loose hair often caught in the curls rather than dropped.

As with all dogs, check and clean the ears regularly, especially after swimming, trim the nails as needed, and keep up routine dental care.

## Health & lifespan

The Barbet is generally a robust breed with a typical lifespan of around 12 to 14 years. Like many medium-to-large dogs, it can be prone to conditions such as hip dysplasia and certain eye disorders, while ear infections are a consideration in any breed with hanging, hairy ears. Responsible breeders screen their breeding stock for known hereditary issues.

Routine veterinary check-ups, parasite prevention, appropriate exercise and a healthy weight all support a long, comfortable life. Always consult your veterinarian about any specific health concerns.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Barbet hypoallergenic?**
The Barbet sheds very little and its curls tend to trap loose hair, which some allergy-prone owners find easier to live with. However, no dog is truly hypoallergenic, so spend time with the breed before committing.

**Does the Barbet make a good family dog?**
Yes. Barbets are usually friendly, gentle and devoted, and tend to do well with children when socialised and supervised. Their high energy and need for companionship suit active households that can include them in daily life.

## Conclusion

The Barbet is a cheerful, athletic and affectionate companion for owners who can meet its exercise, grooming and social needs. With early training, regular activity and consistent coat care, this charming French water dog can be a loyal and rewarding member of an active family.
