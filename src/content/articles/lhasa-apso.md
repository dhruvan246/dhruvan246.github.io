Bred for centuries as a sentinel in Tibetan monasteries, the Lhasa Apso is a confident, dignified little dog with a flowing coat and a watchful nature. Independent yet devoted, the Lhasa is a charming companion with a regal air.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Small (5.5–8 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–15 years |
| Coat | Long, dense, heavy |
| Energy | Low to moderate |
| Good with kids | Best with older, respectful children |

## Appearance & temperament

Lhasas have a long, heavy coat and a self-assured manner. They are independent, alert and loyal — affectionate with their family but often aloof with strangers, and naturally good little watchdogs.

## Diet & nutrition

- Quality small-breed food in measured portions.

## Grooming

- The long coat needs **regular brushing (several times a week)**; many owners choose a short "puppy cut" for ease.
- Keep hair out of the eyes and maintain dental care.

## Exercise & training

- Short daily walks and play are plenty.
- Smart but independent and a touch stubborn; patient, consistent, positive training works best.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for eye conditions (including dry eye and progressive retinal atrophy), luxating patella, and kidney issues in some lines. Regular vet care keeps them healthy.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Lhasa Apsos good guard dogs?**
For their size, yes — they're alert and make excellent little watchdogs.

**Do they need a lot of grooming?**
Yes if kept long; a shorter clip greatly reduces the work.

## Conclusion

The Lhasa Apso is a dignified, loyal and characterful companion with an independent streak. Commit to coat care, provide gentle training, and you'll enjoy this regal little watchdog for years.
