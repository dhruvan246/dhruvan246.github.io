A massive, sweet-natured working dog bred for water rescue, the Newfoundland is the epitome of the gentle giant. Devoted, patient and famously good with children, the "Newfie" is a loving family guardian.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Giant (45–70 kg) |
| Lifespan | 9–10 years |
| Coat | Thick, water-resistant double coat |
| Energy | Low to moderate |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Newfoundlands are huge and powerful with a heavy double coat (often black) and webbed feet for swimming. They are sweet, calm and patient — devoted to family, wonderful with children, and natural swimmers and lifesavers.

## Diet & nutrition

- A quality large/giant-breed diet; puppies need controlled, slow growth.
- Feed measured meals and **take bloat precautions** (rest after meals).

## Exercise

- Moderate exercise — walks and swimming, which they love.
- Avoid high-impact activity in puppies to protect growing joints.

## Grooming & care

- **Brush several times a week**; expect heavy shedding and drooling.
- Keep them cool — that heavy coat makes them heat-sensitive.

## Health & lifespan

Giant-breed risks: hip and elbow dysplasia, heart conditions (subaortic stenosis), bloat, and a shorter lifespan. Choose health-tested lines and keep them lean.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Newfoundlands good with children?**
Famously so — gentle, patient "nanny dogs," though supervision is always wise given their size.

**Do they drool?**
Yes — quite a bit. Keep a cloth handy.

## Conclusion

The Newfoundland is a sweet, devoted and heroic gentle giant for a family with the space and means to care for a giant breed. Keep up grooming, feed carefully, keep them cool, and treasure this loving companion.
