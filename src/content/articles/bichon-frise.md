A cheerful little powder-puff of a dog, the Bichon Frise is famous for its fluffy white coat and sunny, affectionate personality. Playful and sociable, the Bichon makes a delightful companion for families and city dwellers alike.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Small (3–5 kg) |
| Lifespan | 14–16 years |
| Coat | Curly, fluffy, low-shedding |
| Energy | Moderate |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Bichons have a soft, curly white coat and a perky, rounded look. They are happy, affectionate and playful — sociable little dogs that love people and adapt well to apartments.

## Diet & nutrition

- Small portions of quality small-breed food; watch treats to avoid weight gain.

## Grooming — the main commitment

- The curly coat needs **regular brushing (several times a week)** and professional grooming every 4–6 weeks to prevent matting.
- Clean around the eyes to reduce tear staining.

## Exercise & training

- Daily walks and play are plenty.
- Smart and eager to please, but house-training takes patience and consistency.

## Health & lifespan

Generally long-lived. Watch for allergies, dental disease, luxating patella, and bladder stones. Regular grooming, dental care and vet checks keep them healthy.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Bichons shed?**
Very little — often suitable for shedding-sensitive homes, but they need lots of grooming.

**Are they good apartment dogs?**
Excellent — small, cheerful and adaptable.

## Conclusion

The Bichon Frise is a joyful, affectionate and adaptable companion that brightens any home. Commit to regular grooming, watch their dental health, and you'll have a sunny little friend for many years.
