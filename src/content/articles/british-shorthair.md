With their dense plush coats, round faces and famously calm nature, British Shorthairs are like living teddy bears. Easy-going and undemanding, they're one of the most popular cat breeds in the world.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium–large (4–8 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–17 years |
| Coat | Short, dense, plush |
| Energy | Low to moderate |
| Good with kids | Yes — calm and tolerant |

## Appearance & temperament

British Shorthairs are sturdy and rounded, most famous in "British Blue" (blue-grey) with copper eyes. They are calm, dignified and affectionate — but on their own terms, often preferring to sit near you rather than on your lap.

## Diet & nutrition

- Quality cat food in measured portions — they're prone to weight gain, especially as indoor cats.
- Provide fresh water and avoid overfeeding.

## Grooming & care

- Brush weekly (more during seasonal shedding) — the dense coat needs help removing loose hair.
- Provide scratching posts, perches and gentle play.

## Health & lifespan

Generally robust. Watch for obesity and **hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM)** — a heart condition seen in the breed. Choose breeders who screen for HCM, and keep your cat at a healthy weight.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are British Shorthairs cuddly?**
Affectionate but independent — they often prefer companionship beside you over being held.

**Do they shed much?**
Moderately, with heavier seasonal sheds; weekly brushing helps.

## Conclusion

The British Shorthair is a calm, affectionate and low-maintenance companion ideal for relaxed households. Keep them lean, brush weekly, and enjoy this gentle, plush charmer for years.
