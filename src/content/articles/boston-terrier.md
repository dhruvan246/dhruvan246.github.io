Nicknamed the "American Gentleman" for its tuxedo-like markings and polite manners, the Boston Terrier is a friendly, adaptable little dog that fits city and family life beautifully.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Small (5–11 kg) |
| Lifespan | 11–15 years |
| Coat | Short, smooth |
| Energy | Moderate |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Compact and muscular with a short muzzle and large round eyes, Bostons sport a distinctive black-and-white "tuxedo" coat. They are friendly, lively and gentle — great with children and other pets.

## Diet & nutrition

- Quality food in measured portions.
- Some Bostons have sensitive stomachs and can be gassy — a slow-feeder bowl helps.

## Exercise & training

- Daily walks and play keep them happy; they're moderately active.
- Intelligent and eager to please, they respond well to gentle, reward-based training.

## Grooming & care

- Easy coat — weekly brushing.
- Clean facial folds and wipe their prominent eyes gently.
- Routine nail trims and dental care.

## Health & lifespan

As a short-faced breed, watch for breathing issues and overheating. Their large eyes are prone to injury and conditions like corneal ulcers. Also watch for patellar luxation. Keep them cool in hot weather.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Boston Terriers good apartment dogs?**
Excellent — compact, friendly and adaptable to small spaces.

**Do they bark much?**
Generally quiet — one of the calmer small breeds.

## Conclusion

The Boston Terrier is a polite, affectionate and easy-going companion ideal for families and city dwellers. Protect them from heat, mind those big eyes, and enjoy a delightful little friend.
