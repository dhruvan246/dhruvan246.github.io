The Bristlenose Pleco (genus *Ancistrus*) is one of the most popular algae-eating catfish in the aquarium hobby. Smaller and hardier than the common pleco, it stays a manageable size and earns its keep cleaning surfaces, making it a favourite for beginners and experienced keepers alike.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Adult size | 4-5 inches (10-13 cm) |
| Lifespan | 5-12 years |
| Minimum tank size | 25-30 gallons |
| Water type | Freshwater |
| Care level | Easy |

## Appearance & behaviour

Bristlenose Plecos have a flattened body, broad head and armoured plates instead of scales. The standout feature is the cluster of fleshy tentacle-like "bristles" on the snout of mature males; females have shorter, sparser bristles or none at all. Colour ranges from mottled brown, grey and black to selectively bred albino, calico and "longfin" varieties.

They are peaceful, mostly nocturnal bottom-dwellers that spend the day grazing on surfaces or hiding in caves and under driftwood. Males can be territorial toward other male plecos but generally ignore other fish.

## Tank setup & water parameters

A single Bristlenose does well in a 25-30 gallon tank with plenty of horizontal floor space. Provide a soft substrate, robust plants, and at least one cave per fish, since they value secure hiding spots. Driftwood is especially important: the fish rasp on it, and the fibre aids their digestion.

Aim for these parameters:

- Temperature: 73-81°F (23-27°C)
- pH: 6.5-7.5
- Hardness: 6-15 dGH

They produce a fair amount of waste, so strong filtration and regular partial water changes (around 25% weekly) keep conditions stable. Good oxygenation and some water flow are appreciated.

## Diet & feeding

Bristlenose Plecos are primarily herbivores. While they graze on aquarium algae, this rarely supplies enough food. Supplement with sinking algae wafers and spirulina pellets, plus blanched vegetables such as zucchini, cucumber, spinach and peas. Driftwood provides essential fibre. Offer occasional protein in small amounts, but avoid overfeeding meaty foods. Feed once daily, ideally after lights-out when the fish are most active.

## Tank mates & compatibility

Their peaceful nature makes them excellent community fish. Good companions include tetras, rasboras, danios, guppies, mollies, peaceful gouramis, corydoras and most community-sized fish. Avoid housing them with aggressive cichlids or fin-nippers, and do not keep them with large fish that might view them as prey. Keep only one male per tank unless it is large, as males defend caves during breeding.

## Health & common issues

Bristlenose Plecos are hardy, but poor water quality is the most common cause of problems. Watch for ich (white spots), fin rot, and bloating from constipation when fed too little fibre. A sunken belly often signals starvation, a reminder that algae alone is not enough. Quarantine new arrivals, maintain stable parameters, and ensure a varied vegetable-rich diet to prevent most issues. If a fish stops grazing or hides constantly, test your water first.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Bristlenose Plecos really keep a tank clean?** They graze algae from glass, plants and decor, which helps, but they are not a substitute for filtration and water changes, and they add to the bioload.

**How can I tell males from females?** Mature males grow prominent branching bristles across the snout, while females have very short bristles confined to the edge of the head, if any at all.

## Conclusion

The Bristlenose Pleco is a rewarding, low-maintenance catfish that suits most peaceful freshwater communities. Give it a cave, driftwood and a vegetable-based diet, keep the water clean, and this hardworking little fish will thrive for many years.
