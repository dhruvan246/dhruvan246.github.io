The Eastern newt (*Notophthalmus viridescens*) is a small, hardy North American amphibian best known for its striking bright-orange juvenile stage. Adaptable and long-lived, it is a popular choice for keepers who want an aquatic amphibian with personality and easy-to-meet needs.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Size | 2.5–5 inches (6.5–12.5 cm) |
| Lifespan | 10–15 years in captivity |
| Enclosure | 10–20 gallon aquatic tank |
| Temperament | Active, curious, non-aggressive |
| Care level | Beginner to intermediate |

## Appearance & behaviour

Eastern newts pass through a famous three-stage life cycle. After hatching, the aquatic larva metamorphoses into a terrestrial juvenile called an "eft," which is bright orange-red with rows of black-bordered red spots. After one to three years on land, the eft transforms into the aquatic adult, which is olive-green to brownish above with a yellow belly and retains the spotted pattern. Adults are mostly aquatic and swim actively, often resting on plants or surfacing for air. Their skin secretes mild toxins (tetrodotoxin), a defense that makes them unpalatable to most predators.

## Habitat & water

Adult Eastern newts are primarily aquatic and need a tank with clean, dechlorinated water 4–8 inches deep, plus an easily accessible land area or floating platform where they can climb out. A 10-gallon tank suits one or two newts, with a tight-fitting lid since they are skilled escape artists. Keep water cool, around 60–72°F (16–22°C); they tolerate cooler temperatures well and dislike heat above the mid-70s. Use a gentle filter to maintain water quality without strong currents, and provide live or artificial plants, smooth rocks, and hiding spots. Perform regular partial water changes to keep ammonia and nitrite at zero. Land-stage efts, by contrast, need a moist terrestrial setup with damp substrate and a shallow water dish.

## Diet & feeding

Eastern newts are carnivores. Adults readily accept bloodworms, blackworms, brine shrimp, small earthworms, daphnia, and chopped nightcrawlers. Many will also learn to take sinking carnivore or newt pellets. Feed adults every two to three days, offering only what they finish in a few minutes to avoid fouling the water. Terrestrial efts eat small live prey such as pinhead crickets, springtails, fruit flies, and tiny worms. Variety supports good health, and live or frozen foods are generally preferred over dry pellets alone. Remove uneaten food promptly to protect water quality.

## Handling & health

Like all amphibians, Eastern newts have permeable skin that absorbs substances easily, so handling should be minimal. When you must move one, wet your hands first or use a soft net, and never handle them with lotion, soap, or oil residue on your skin. Always wash your hands before and after contact, as their skin toxins can irritate eyes and mucous membranes. Healthy newts are alert and feed regularly; warning signs include lethargy, skin discoloration, bloating, refusing food, or excessive shedding. Most health problems trace back to poor water quality or overly warm temperatures, so stable, cool, clean water is the best preventive care. Consult an exotic-animal veterinarian for persistent illness.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Eastern newts poisonous to touch?** Their skin secretes tetrodotoxin, which is harmless through brief, careful contact but can irritate your eyes or mouth. Never handle them with broken skin, and always wash your hands thoroughly afterward.

**Can Eastern newts live with fish?** It is generally not recommended. Fish may nip at newts, compete for food, or be eaten, and many fish prefer warmer water than newts tolerate. A species-only setup is safest.

## Conclusion

Eastern newts are rewarding, long-lived amphibians that thrive with simple but consistent care: cool clean water, an accessible land area, a varied carnivorous diet, and minimal handling. Note that collecting or keeping native amphibians is regulated in many areas, so check local laws before acquiring one. With a properly maintained aquatic setup, these charming spotted newts can be fascinating companions for well over a decade.
