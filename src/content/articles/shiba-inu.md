Bold, alert and famously independent, the Shiba Inu is Japan's most popular companion dog. With a fox-like face and a confident, cat-like personality, the Shiba is charming — but it has a strong will of its own.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (8–11 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–15 years |
| Coat | Thick double coat |
| Energy | Moderate |
| Good with kids | Yes, with early socialisation |

## Appearance & temperament

Shibas are compact and athletic with pricked ears, a curled tail and a plush double coat. They are spirited, clean and independent — loyal to their family but often aloof with strangers and other dogs.

## Diet & nutrition

- Quality food in measured portions; Shibas are usually not greedy but can gain weight.

## Exercise & training

- Daily walks and play keep them satisfied.
- **Training takes patience** — Shibas are intelligent but stubborn and independent. Start socialisation early.
- **Recall is unreliable** and they have a strong prey drive — keep them leashed or in secure areas.

## Grooming

- Brush weekly, and daily during their heavy seasonal shed ("blowing coat").
- Otherwise very clean and low-odour, with cat-like self-grooming habits.

## Health & lifespan

Generally healthy and long-lived. Watch for allergies, luxating patella, hip dysplasia and eye conditions. Routine vet care keeps them thriving.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Shiba Inus good for first-time owners?**
Their independence and stubbornness make them challenging for beginners.

**Do they get along with other dogs?**
They can be aloof or assertive; early socialisation is important.

## Conclusion

The Shiba Inu is a beautiful, dignified and spirited companion for an owner who appreciates an independent dog. Socialise early, keep them secure, and respect their unique personality.
