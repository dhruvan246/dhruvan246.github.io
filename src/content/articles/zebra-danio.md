The Zebra Danio (*Danio rerio*) is one of the most popular freshwater aquarium fish, prized for its hardiness, energetic swimming, and striking horizontal stripes. Native to South Asia, it adapts to a wide range of conditions, making it a classic choice for first-time aquarists.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Adult size | Around 4-5 cm (1.5-2 in) |
| Lifespan | Typically 3-5 years |
| Minimum tank size | About 38 litres (10 gallons) |
| Water type | Freshwater |
| Care level | Easy / beginner |

## Appearance & behaviour

Zebra Danios are slender, torpedo-shaped fish marked by five dark blue-purple horizontal stripes running from gill to tail against a silvery-gold body. Long-finned and various colour-enhanced varieties also exist. They are constantly active, darting around the upper and middle levels of the tank.

This is a true schooling species and should be kept in groups of at least six. Smaller groups can lead to stress, fin-nipping, and skittish behaviour. In a proper shoal they are peaceful, confident, and entertaining to watch.

## Tank setup & water parameters

Because they are such fast, restless swimmers, Zebra Danios appreciate length over height. A long tank of 38 litres or more gives them room to swim. Provide a secure lid, as they are accomplished jumpers.

Aim for the following:

- Temperature: roughly 18-24°C (64-75°F); they tolerate cooler water than many tropicals
- pH: around 6.5-7.5
- Hardness: soft to moderately hard

Use a gentle to moderate current, planted areas, and some open swimming space. Regular weekly partial water changes and good filtration keep them healthy.

## Diet & feeding

Zebra Danios are omnivores with hearty appetites. A quality flake or small pellet food forms a good staple. Supplement with frozen or live foods such as brine shrimp, daphnia, and bloodworms for colour and condition, plus occasional blanched vegetables.

Feed small amounts once or twice a day, only what they finish in a couple of minutes. Their surface-feeding habits make them quick to respond at mealtime; avoid overfeeding to keep water quality high.

## Tank mates & compatibility

Their peaceful, active nature suits community tanks. Good companions include other danios, tetras, rasboras, platies, Corydoras catfish, and similarly sized non-aggressive fish. Keeping a full shoal reduces the fin-nipping they sometimes show when underpopulated.

Avoid pairing them with slow, long-finned fish like bettas or fancy guppies, whose trailing fins may be nipped, and steer clear of large or aggressive species.

## Health & common issues

Zebra Danios are remarkably hardy but can suffer the usual freshwater ailments, including ich (white spot), fin rot, and fungal infections, often linked to poor water quality or stress. Stable parameters, a varied diet, and routine maintenance prevent most problems.

Quarantine new fish before adding them, and watch for clamped fins, loss of appetite, or unusual spots. If illness appears, consult a knowledgeable aquatics retailer or aquatic veterinarian.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Zebra Danios need a heater?** Not always. They tolerate room-temperature water better than most tropical fish, but a heater helps keep temperatures stable in cooler homes.

**How many Zebra Danios should I keep together?** Keep at least six, and ideally more. As schooling fish they are calmer, bolder, and less nippy in a larger group.

## Conclusion

Zebra Danios are forgiving, lively, and inexpensive, making them an excellent starter fish or a dynamic addition to a peaceful community tank. Give them a long tank, a proper shoal, clean water, and a varied diet, and they will reward you with years of constant activity and shimmering colour.
