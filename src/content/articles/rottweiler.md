Powerful, confident and deeply loyal, the Rottweiler is a natural guardian with a surprisingly calm and affectionate side. In the right hands — those of a committed, experienced owner — the "Rottie" is a devoted and gentle family companion.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Large (35–60 kg) |
| Lifespan | 9–10 years |
| Coat | Short, dense |
| Energy | Moderate to high |
| Good with kids | Yes, with training and supervision |

## Appearance & temperament

Rottweilers are robust, muscular dogs with a black coat and distinctive tan markings. They are confident, calm and self-assured — protective of their family yet affectionate and even goofy at home. Good breeding and upbringing are everything.

## Training & socialisation — essential

- **Early, thorough socialisation is critical** to raise a stable, friendly adult.
- Use firm, consistent, reward-based training; they're intelligent and eager to work.
- A well-trained Rottie is calm and reliable; an unsocialised one can be a problem. This is a breed for committed owners.

## Exercise

- Need around an hour or more of daily exercise plus mental engagement.
- They enjoy having a job — obedience, cart-pulling, tracking.

## Diet & nutrition

- A quality large-breed diet, portioned to keep them lean and muscular.
- Large-breed puppy food supports healthy joint development.

## Grooming & care

- Short coat needs only weekly brushing (they shed moderately).
- Routine nail trims, ear checks and dental care.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for hip and elbow dysplasia, heart conditions (aortic stenosis), certain cancers and bloat. Keep them lean, choose health-tested lines, and maintain regular vet visits.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Rottweilers dangerous?**
No more than any large powerful breed when well-bred, socialised and trained. Responsible ownership is key.

**Are they good family dogs?**
Yes, with proper training — they're loyal, affectionate and protective.

## Conclusion

The Rottweiler is a loyal, capable guardian that thrives with an experienced, dedicated owner. Invest in early socialisation and consistent training, and you'll be rewarded with a calm, devoted and loving companion.
