The Dutch Shepherd (Hollandse Herdershond) is a versatile herding breed developed in the rural Netherlands, where it worked as an all-purpose farm dog tending sheep, guarding property, and pulling carts. Recognizable by its distinctive brindle coat, this athletic, sharp-minded dog has become a favorite for dog sports, police, and military work.

## Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|---|---|

| Lifespan | 11–14 years |

| Energy | Very high |

## Appearance & temperament

The Dutch Shepherd is a medium-to-large, well-muscled dog with a wedge-shaped head and erect ears. Its hallmark is the brindle coat, ranging from gold to silver with dark striping, and it comes in three varieties: short-haired, long-haired, and rough-haired (wire-coated). Build is lean and agile rather than bulky.

In temperament, this breed is intelligent, alert, and intensely loyal. Dutch Shepherds are affectionate with their families and tend to be more biddable and less independent than some related working breeds. They are naturally watchful and make capable watchdogs, but they thrive on partnership and being given a job.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced diet formulated for active large-breed dogs. Because Dutch Shepherds are highly energetic, working or sport dogs may need more calories than companion dogs, so adjust portions to body condition rather than relying solely on bag guidelines.

Divide daily food into two meals to support steady energy and reduce the risk of bloat in deep-chested dogs. Puppies benefit from large-breed growth formulas that control calcium and energy for steady skeletal development. Always provide fresh water, and keep treats to a reasonable share of daily calories to prevent weight gain.

## Exercise & training

This is not a low-maintenance breed. Dutch Shepherds need substantial daily exercise, typically one to two hours of vigorous activity such as running, hiking, fetch, or structured play. Just as important is mental work; they excel at obedience, agility, tracking, herding, and protection sports.

Training is generally a pleasure because the breed is quick to learn and eager to please. Use positive, reward-based methods and start socialization early to channel their alertness appropriately. Without enough physical and mental stimulation, a bored Dutch Shepherd may become restless or develop nuisance behaviors.

## Grooming & care

Grooming needs depend on coat type. The short-haired variety requires only weekly brushing and sheds seasonally. The long-haired coat benefits from regular brushing to prevent tangles, while the rough-haired coat needs occasional hand-stripping and combing to maintain texture.

All varieties are fairly low-maintenance for bathing; wash only when dirty to preserve natural oils. Routine care includes checking and cleaning ears, trimming nails regularly, and brushing teeth several times a week to support dental health.

## Health & lifespan

The Dutch Shepherd is considered a hardy, relatively healthy breed, partly because it has not been heavily over-bred. Typical lifespan is around 11 to 14 years. As with many large, active breeds, potential concerns include hip and elbow dysplasia, and some lines may carry inherited conditions such as goniodysgenesis (an eye disorder) or inflammatory myopathy.

Choosing a breeder who screens breeding stock helps reduce risk. Keep your dog at a healthy weight, maintain routine veterinary checkups and vaccinations, and stay alert to changes in movement, vision, or appetite.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Dutch Shepherds good family dogs?**
Yes. With early socialization and adequate exercise, they are loyal, affectionate companions that bond closely with their families and can be good with children when supervised.

**How are Dutch Shepherds different from German Shepherds?**
Dutch Shepherds are typically leaner and more agile, always have a brindle coat, and are often regarded as healthier overall, while German Shepherds are larger, come in more colors, and have a sloped-back show variety.

## Conclusion

The Dutch Shepherd is a brilliant, athletic, and devoted breed best suited to active owners who can meet its considerable exercise and training needs. Given a job, structure, and companionship, it rewards its family with loyalty, versatility, and years of capable partnership.
