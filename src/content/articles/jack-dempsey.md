The Jack Dempsey (*Rocio octofasciata*) is a striking Central American cichlid named after the famous boxer, a nod to its bold, sometimes pugnacious personality. Native to slow rivers and lakes of Mexico and Central America, it has become a popular choice for hobbyists who want a characterful, colorful fish with plenty of attitude.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Adult size | Around 8-10 inches (20-25 cm) |
| Lifespan | Roughly 8-12 years with good care |
| Minimum tank size | About 55 gallons for one adult |
| Water type | Freshwater |
| Care level | Easy to moderate |

## Appearance & behaviour

Jack Dempseys are best known for the iridescent blue, green and gold flecks that cover a darker body, with eight faint vertical bars that intensify with age and mood. Males tend to grow larger and develop brighter, more pointed fins, while females are usually smaller. Colours deepen during breeding and can fade when the fish is stressed or unwell.

Behaviourally, they are intelligent, curious and territorial. They readily recognise their keeper and may "beg" at feeding time. Aggression increases with maturity and especially during spawning, so they are not a community fish in the traditional sense.

## Tank setup & water parameters

Provide a single adult with at least a 55-gallon tank; pairs or tank mates need more. Use a soft sandy or fine-gravel substrate, as Jack Dempseys love to dig, and include caves, driftwood and sturdy rockwork to create territories and hiding spots. Anchor or pot any plants, since they may be uprooted.

Aim for the following stable conditions:

- Temperature: about 72-82°F (22-28°C)
- pH: roughly 6.5-7.5
- Hardness: soft to moderately hard

These fish are messy eaters, so strong filtration and regular weekly water changes of around 25-30% are important to keep nitrates low and water quality high.

## Diet & feeding

The Jack Dempsey is an omnivore that leans carnivorous. Offer a staple of quality cichlid pellets and supplement with frozen or live foods such as bloodworms, brine shrimp, krill and earthworms. Occasional blanched vegetables like peas or spinach add variety and fibre.

Feed adults once or twice a day, giving only what they finish in a couple of minutes. Avoid overfeeding, which fouls the water and contributes to obesity and bloating.

## Tank mates & compatibility

Jack Dempseys can be kept with other robust, similarly sized fish, but never with small or timid species that may be bullied or eaten. Suitable companions include larger cichlids, big catfish such as plecos, and other sturdy fish, provided the tank is spacious enough to spread out territories.

Watch closely when introducing new fish, and always have a backup plan if aggression escalates. Many keepers find a single specimen or a bonded breeding pair the simplest arrangement.

## Health & common issues

With clean, stable water Jack Dempseys are fairly hardy. The most common problems stem from poor water quality, including fin damage, bacterial infections and "hole-in-the-head" disease, which is linked to nutrition and water conditions. Ich (white spot) can appear after stress or temperature swings.

Quarantine new arrivals, maintain good filtration and observe daily for changes in colour, appetite or behaviour. If a fish shows persistent signs of illness, consult an aquatic veterinarian or experienced specialist rather than guessing at treatments.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Jack Dempseys aggressive?** They are territorial and can be aggressive, particularly as adults and when breeding. Ample space, hiding spots and careful tank-mate choices help manage this behaviour.

**How big do Jack Dempseys get?** Most reach around 8-10 inches in the aquarium, with males generally larger than females. Their full size means they need a tank of at least 55 gallons.

## Conclusion

The Jack Dempsey rewards attentive keepers with bold colours, real personality and an engaging presence in the aquarium. Give it a spacious, well-filtered tank, a varied carnivore-leaning diet and carefully chosen tank mates, and this classic cichlid can thrive for a decade or more. Regulations on fishkeeping vary by region, so check local rules before purchasing.
