The Bergamasco Sheepdog is an ancient herding breed from the Italian Alps near Bergamo, best known for its distinctive coat that naturally forms flat mats, or "flocks." Patient, intelligent, and deeply devoted, the Bergamasco was bred to think independently while guarding flocks, traits that make it a steady, watchful family companion today.

## Appearance & temperament

The Bergamasco is a solidly built, muscular dog of medium-large size, with males generally standing 23–24 inches and females 22–23 inches at the shoulder. Its hallmark is the unique coat made of three different hair textures that weave together into loose, flat layers called flocks, giving the dog a shaggy, almost matted look that protected it from cold and predators in the mountains.

In temperament, the breed is calm, observant, and patient. Centuries of working closely with shepherds gave the Bergamasco a problem-solving mind and a strong bond with its people. It is affectionate with family, reserved with strangers, and naturally protective without being aggressive. The breed tends to be gentle with children and other animals it has grown up alongside.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced diet appropriate for a large-breed dog and the individual's age, weight, and activity level. Because the Bergamasco has a moderate, steady energy level rather than a high-octane one, portions should be measured to prevent weight gain that strains joints. Divide daily food into two meals to support digestion. Always provide fresh water, and consult your veterinarian about feeding amounts, large-breed puppy formulas, and any weight management needs.

## Exercise & training

The Bergamasco needs moderate daily exercise: brisk walks, play, and opportunities to use its mind through herding games, scent work, or puzzle activities. It is not a hyperactive breed, but it does need a job or regular engagement to stay content. Because it was bred to make decisions on its own, the Bergamasco can seem stubborn; training works best with patience, consistency, and positive, reward-based methods. Early socialization helps this watchful breed become a confident, well-mannered adult.

## Grooming & care

Coat care is unique to this breed. During the first year, the flat flocks gradually form as the three hair types intertwine; owners help by separating the growing mats by hand so the flocks develop evenly rather than into a single felted mass. Once formed, the coat needs surprisingly little brushing and does not shed in the usual sense. Bathing is infrequent but the coat takes a long time to dry thoroughly. Routine nail trims, ear checks, and dental care round out basic maintenance.

## Health & lifespan

The Bergamasco is generally a hardy, long-lived breed, with a typical lifespan of about 13 to 15 years. As with most large dogs, responsible breeders screen for hip dysplasia, and owners should watch for eye conditions and bloat. Keeping your dog at a healthy weight, providing regular exercise, and scheduling routine veterinary checkups support a long, comfortable life. Discuss appropriate screenings with your vet.

## Frequently asked questions

**Does the Bergamasco Sheepdog shed a lot?**
No. Once the flocked coat matures, it sheds very little and is often described as low-shedding, though the unique mats still require occasional hand care.

**Is the Bergamasco good for first-time owners?**
It can be, for committed owners. The breed is calm and people-oriented, but its independent thinking and special coat call for patience and consistent, positive training.

## Conclusion

The Bergamasco Sheepdog pairs a one-of-a-kind flocked coat with a calm, loyal, and intelligent personality. For owners who appreciate its independent nature and are ready for its distinctive grooming needs, this ancient Italian herder makes a devoted, hard-working, and gentle companion.
