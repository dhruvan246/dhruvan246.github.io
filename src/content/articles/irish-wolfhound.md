Among the tallest dog breeds in the world, the Irish Wolfhound combines commanding size with a famously gentle, easygoing nature. Bred in Ireland as a hunting and coursing hound, today this calm sighthound is treasured as a dignified, affectionate family companion.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |

| Lifespan | 6–10 years |
| Coat | Rough, harsh, medium-length |
| Energy | Low to moderate |
| Good with kids | Very good (supervise due to size) |

## Appearance & temperament

The Irish Wolfhound is a giant, rough-coated sighthound with a long head, deep chest and powerful, far-reaching stride. Males commonly stand around 32 inches or more at the shoulder. The wiry coat comes in colours such as grey, brindle, wheaten, red, black and white. Temperamentally, the breed is calm, patient and good-natured — gentle with family and typically friendly rather than guard-like, though their sheer presence is impressive.

## Diet & nutrition

- Feed a quality diet suited to large and giant breeds, particularly during the long growth period of puppyhood, to support steady bone and joint development.
- Measure meals to maintain a lean body condition; excess weight strains a giant frame.
- As a deep-chested breed, they may face an elevated bloat (GDV) risk, so dividing food into more than one meal a day and avoiding vigorous activity right around mealtimes is sensible. Always ensure fresh water.

## Exercise & training

- Despite their size, adults are content with moderate daily exercise — regular walks plus opportunities to stretch out and gallop in a safe, securely fenced area.
- As sighthounds, they may instinctively chase fast-moving animals, so a fenced space and reliable leash control matter.
- Avoid strenuous, high-impact exercise in growing puppies to protect developing joints.
- Begin gentle, positive, consistent training and socialisation early — a giant dog must learn good manners while still manageable in size.

## Grooming & care

- The harsh, rough coat is fairly low-maintenance; brush roughly weekly to remove loose hair and prevent matting, with occasional tidying.
- Check ears, trim nails and attend to dental care routinely.
- Provide large, supportive bedding to cushion joints, plus ample indoor space simply to move and turn around comfortably.

## Health & lifespan

Like many giant breeds, the Irish Wolfhound has a relatively short lifespan, often around 6 to 10 years. Breed-linked health considerations can include heart disease such as dilated cardiomyopathy (DCM), the giant-breed emergency bloat (gastric dilatation-volvulus), bone cancer (osteosarcoma) and joint conditions like hip dysplasia. Choosing puppies from health-tested parents, keeping your dog lean and scheduling regular veterinary check-ups all support a healthier life. Learn the warning signs of bloat so you can act quickly.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Irish Wolfhounds good family dogs?**
Yes. They are known for a calm, gentle and affectionate temperament and generally do well with children, though their large size means young kids should always be supervised around them.

**Do Irish Wolfhounds need a lot of exercise?**
Less than their size suggests. Adults are usually satisfied with moderate daily walks plus a chance to run safely, and tend to be calm and relaxed when indoors.

## Conclusion

The Irish Wolfhound is a gentle giant — dignified, affectionate and surprisingly mellow at home. They need space, sensible feeding, early training and attentive health care to thrive. For owners ready to embrace a giant breed's needs and shorter years, this ancient hound offers a uniquely loving companionship.
