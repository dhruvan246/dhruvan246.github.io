Strikingly beautiful and bred to run for miles pulling sleds, the Siberian Husky is a high-energy, independent dog with a friendly, mischievous spirit. They're stunning — but they're a serious commitment.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Large (16–27 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–15 years |
| Coat | Thick double coat |
| Energy | Very high |
| Good with kids | Yes, with supervision and exercise |

## Appearance & temperament

Huskies have a dense double coat, erect ears and often piercing blue (or multi-coloured) eyes. They are friendly, outgoing and pack-oriented — but also independent, stubborn and famous escape artists.

## Exercise & training — a big commitment

- Need **a great deal of daily exercise** — running, hiking, sledding or dog sports.
- **Master escape artists:** secure, high fences are essential, and recall is unreliable due to strong prey drive — keep them leashed.
- Independent and stubborn; training requires patience and consistency.

## Diet & nutrition

- A quality, active-dog diet; many huskies are surprisingly "easy keepers" and eat less than expected.

## Grooming

- Brush 2–3 times a week, and daily during their dramatic seasonal shed ("blowing coat").
- **Never shave the double coat** — it insulates against heat and cold.

## Health & lifespan

Generally healthy. Watch for eye conditions (cataracts, corneal issues) and hip dysplasia. They tolerate cold far better than heat — keep them cool in summer.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Huskies good for first-time owners?**
Not usually — their energy, escaping and independence challenge beginners.

**Do they shed a lot?**
Enormously, especially seasonally. Regular brushing is a must.

## Conclusion

The Siberian Husky is a gorgeous, energetic and free-spirited dog for an active, committed owner with secure fencing. Meet their exercise needs and respect their independence, and they're a joyful companion.
