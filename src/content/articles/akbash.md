The Akbash is a large, white livestock guardian dog from western Turkey, where it has protected sheep and goats from predators for centuries. Calm, watchful, and fiercely loyal, the Akbash blends the lean athleticism of sighthounds with the heavy build of mountain guardian breeds, making it a capable working dog and a devoted, if strong-willed, companion.

## Appearance & temperament

The Akbash is a tall, powerfully built dog standing roughly 28-34 inches at the shoulder, with males larger than females. Its coat is always white, ranging from short and flat to medium-length with light feathering, and it carries a dense undercoat that insulates against weather. The breed has a wedge-shaped head, dark almond eyes, and pendant ears, giving it an alert yet composed expression.

Temperamentally, the Akbash is independent, intelligent, and deeply protective. Bred to work alone with livestock, it makes its own decisions and is naturally suspicious of strangers and unfamiliar animals. With its own family it is gentle, steady, and affectionate. This is a guardian, not a herder, so it bonds closely with whatever it considers its charges.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a high-quality diet formulated for large breeds, with controlled protein and calcium levels during puppyhood to support slow, steady bone growth. Because the Akbash is a giant working breed, portion sizes should match its activity level; many adults need only moderate calories despite their size. Divide daily food into two meals rather than one large feeding to reduce the risk of bloat, a serious concern in deep-chested breeds. Always provide fresh water and avoid heavy exercise immediately before or after meals.

## Exercise & training

Despite its size, the Akbash has moderate exercise needs and tends to conserve energy until action is required. Daily walks and access to a large, securely fenced yard keep it healthy and content. It is not a jogging or dog-park breed; its guarding instincts and independence make off-leash freedom risky.

Training requires patience and consistency. The Akbash responds to calm, confident leadership and positive reinforcement but will ignore repetitive obedience drills it sees no purpose in. Early, extensive socialization is essential so the dog learns to distinguish genuine threats from normal visitors.

## Grooming & care

The white double coat sheds steadily year-round and heavily during seasonal coat changes. Weekly brushing, increasing to several times a week during shedding seasons, removes loose hair and keeps the coat clean. The Akbash is naturally clean and needs only occasional baths. Routine care includes nail trimming, ear checks, and dental hygiene. Provide shade and fresh water in warm weather, as this breed is built for cold climates.

## Health & lifespan

The Akbash is generally a hardy, long-lived breed for its size, typically living 10-12 years. Like many large dogs, it can be prone to hip and elbow dysplasia, and its deep chest puts it at risk for bloat (gastric dilatation-volvulus). Hypothyroidism and cardiac issues are also seen occasionally. Choosing a breeder who screens breeding stock, maintaining a healthy weight, and scheduling regular veterinary checkups all support a long, healthy life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Akbash a good family dog?**
Yes, for the right home. It is loyal and gentle with its family, but its size, protective nature, and independence make it best suited to experienced owners with space and a secure yard.

**Is the Akbash the same as the Anatolian Shepherd?**
No. Although both are Turkish livestock guardians and are sometimes crossed, the Akbash is a distinct, always-white breed with its own type and history.

## Conclusion

The Akbash is a remarkable working guardian that combines strength, intelligence, and unwavering loyalty. It thrives with owners who respect its independence, provide firm and patient guidance, and give it a job or family to protect. For those prepared to meet its needs, the Akbash is a calm, dignified, and devoted companion.
