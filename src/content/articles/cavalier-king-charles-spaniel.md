Affectionate, graceful and wonderfully gentle, the Cavalier King Charles Spaniel is one of the friendliest companion breeds you can find. They adore people and adapt happily to almost any home.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Small (5.5–8 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–15 years |
| Coat | Silky, medium-length, feathered |
| Energy | Moderate |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Cavaliers have soft, expressive eyes and a silky coat in four classic colours. They are sweet, sociable and eager to please — equally happy on a walk or curled in your lap. They get along with children, strangers and other pets.

## Diet & nutrition

- Quality food in measured portions — they can overeat.
- Keep them lean to protect their heart and joints.

## Exercise & training

- Moderate daily walks and play; they enjoy activity but aren't demanding.
- Gentle, reward-based training works best for this sensitive, willing breed.

## Grooming

- Brush several times a week to prevent tangles in the feathering.
- Clean ears regularly (long ears trap moisture) and trim nails.

## Health & lifespan

This breed needs informed health awareness: **mitral valve heart disease** is common, as is **syringomyelia** (a neurological condition), plus ear and eye issues. Choose a breeder who screens for heart and neurological conditions, and have your vet monitor the heart regularly.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Cavaliers good for first-time owners?**
Yes — their gentle, trainable nature makes them ideal beginners' dogs.

**Can they be left alone?**
They strongly prefer company and can develop separation anxiety.

## Conclusion

The Cavalier King Charles Spaniel is a loving, adaptable companion that asks mainly for your time and affection. Stay on top of heart health and grooming, and you'll have a devoted friend.
