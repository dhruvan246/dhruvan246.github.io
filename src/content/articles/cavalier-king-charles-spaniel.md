With their soft, expressive eyes and silky coats, Cavalier King Charles Spaniels are among the most affectionate of all toy breeds. Bred as companion lapdogs, they thrive on human company and bring a gentle, easygoing charm to almost any home.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Small / toy (around 5.5–8 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–15 years |
| Coat | Medium-length, silky, feathered |
| Energy | Low to moderate |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

The Cavalier is a small, well-balanced spaniel with a gently domed head, large round eyes and long, feathered ears. The silky coat comes in four recognised colours: Blenheim (chestnut and white), tricolour, black and tan, and ruby. Famously sweet-natured, sociable and adaptable, Cavaliers are happy companions that get along well with children, strangers and other pets. They form strong bonds and can dislike being left alone for long periods.

## Diet & nutrition

- Feed a quality complete dog food in measured portions suited to their small size and age.
- Cavaliers enjoy their food and can gain weight easily, so monitor body condition and keep treats modest.
- Divide the daily amount into two meals and provide fresh water at all times.
- Check with your vet if you are unsure about the right portion or diet for your individual dog.

## Exercise & training

- Around 30–60 minutes of daily activity, split into walks and play, suits most Cavaliers.
- They enjoy gentle adventures but are equally content relaxing at home, making them flexible companions.
- Intelligent and eager to please, they respond very well to reward-based, positive training.
- Early socialisation helps them grow into confident, well-mannered adults.

## Grooming & care

- Brush the silky coat several times a week to prevent tangles, paying attention to the feathering on the ears, legs and tail.
- Check and gently clean the long, drop ears regularly, as their shape can trap moisture and debris.
- Keep nails trimmed, teeth brushed and eyes wiped clean of any discharge.
- Cavaliers are seasonal shedders, so regular grooming keeps loose hair under control.

## Health & lifespan

Cavaliers typically live around 12–15 years. As a breed they are predisposed to certain inherited conditions, most notably mitral valve disease (a heart condition) and syringomyelia, a neurological problem linked to skull shape. They can also experience eye conditions and luxating patellas. Choosing a responsible breeder who health-tests their dogs, keeping up with routine veterinary check-ups, and maintaining a healthy weight all help support a long, comfortable life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Cavalier King Charles Spaniels good for first-time owners?**
Yes. Their gentle, trainable and adaptable nature makes them an excellent choice for novice owners, provided they receive companionship, modest exercise and regular grooming.

**Do Cavaliers like being left alone?**
Not particularly. They are devoted companions that prefer human company and may become unhappy if left alone for long stretches, so they suit households where someone is often around.

## Conclusion

The Cavalier King Charles Spaniel is a loving, adaptable companion that fits beautifully into family life. With regular grooming, gentle daily exercise, sensible feeding and attentive veterinary care, these affectionate spaniels reward their owners with years of devoted companionship.
