With their bat ears and squishy faces, French Bulldogs have become one of the most popular city dogs in the world. They're affectionate, low-energy and great for apartments — but their flat faces mean they need careful, informed care.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Small (8–14 kg) |
| Lifespan | 10–12 years |
| Coat | Short, smooth |
| Energy | Low to moderate |
| Good with kids | Yes — gentle and patient |

## Appearance & temperament

Frenchies are compact, muscular dogs with signature "bat ears" and a wrinkled, flat face (brachycephalic). They are playful, affectionate lap dogs that love human company and dislike being left alone.

## Diet & nutrition

- Feed a quality diet in measured portions — Frenchies gain weight easily.
- Slow-feeder bowls help, as they can gulp air and gas.
- Keep them lean to reduce strain on joints and breathing.

## Exercise — keep it gentle

- Short walks and play are plenty; they tire quickly.
- **Avoid heat and overexertion** — their flat faces make breathing and cooling hard. Walk in the cool parts of the day.
- Most Frenchies cannot swim — supervise near water.

## Grooming & care

- Wipe and dry facial wrinkles regularly to prevent infection.
- Short coat needs only weekly brushing.
- Clean ears and trim nails routinely.

## Health & lifespan

Be aware of breed-specific issues: brachycephalic airway syndrome (breathing), spinal problems, allergies, and heat sensitivity. Buy from responsible breeders and keep your Frenchie cool and lean.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are French Bulldogs high maintenance?**
Low grooming, but high health awareness — heat and breathing need real care.

**Can they be left alone?**
Not for long; they're prone to separation anxiety.

## Conclusion

The French Bulldog is a loving, comical companion perfectly suited to indoor life. Protect them from heat, keep them slim, and stay on top of their health needs, and you'll enjoy a devoted little friend.
