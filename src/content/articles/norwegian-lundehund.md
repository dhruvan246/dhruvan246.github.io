The Norwegian Lundehund is one of the world's rarest and most physically unusual dog breeds. Developed on remote Norwegian islands to hunt puffins along steep sea cliffs, this small spitz-type dog has a remarkable set of anatomical adaptations that make it unlike any other breed.

## Appearance & temperament

The Lundehund is a small, agile spitz with prick ears, a wedge-shaped head, and a tail often carried curled over the back. Its most famous features are functional adaptations for cliff climbing: at least six toes on each foot (polydactyly), extremely flexible shoulder joints that let the front legs extend outward to the sides, a neck that can bend backward to touch the spine, and ears that can fold closed to protect the canals. The coat is dense and weather-resistant, commonly reddish-brown to fallow with black tips and white markings.

Temperamentally, Lundehunds are alert, curious, and affectionate with their families. They tend to be reserved or wary around strangers and can be quite independent, a legacy of their solo working history. They are generally friendly without being aggressive, though their primitive nature means they can be stubborn and hard to housetrain.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a balanced, high-quality dog food appropriate for a small, moderately active breed. Portion according to weight, age, and activity level, and split daily food into two meals to support steady energy. Because the breed is prone to a digestive disorder affecting nutrient absorption (see Health), owners should monitor weight, stool quality, and body condition closely. Some Lundehunds benefit from easily digestible diets recommended by a veterinarian. Always provide fresh water and avoid overfeeding treats.

## Exercise & training

Lundehunds have moderate energy and enjoy daily walks, exploration, and play. They are natural climbers and jumpers, so secure fencing and supervision are wise. Around 30–60 minutes of activity per day usually keeps them content. Mentally, they thrive on puzzle games and scent work that echo their hunting heritage.

Training requires patience. This independent, primitive breed responds best to short, positive, reward-based sessions. Housetraining is often the biggest challenge and may take longer than with other breeds, so consistency and crate routines help.

## Grooming & care

The double coat sheds seasonally, with heavier "blow-outs" once or twice a year. Weekly brushing keeps loose hair under control, increasing to several times a week during shedding periods. Bathe only as needed. Routine care includes nail trimming (mindful of the extra toes), ear checks, and regular dental care. The flexible joints rarely need special handling but should be observed for any signs of discomfort.

## Health & lifespan

Most Norwegian Lundehunds live 12–15 years. The breed's defining health concern is Lundehund syndrome, a group of digestive disorders that can impair protein absorption and lead to weight loss or gastrointestinal upset. Because the breed descends from a very small population, genetic diversity is limited, and responsible breeding aims to manage this. Work with a veterinarian on early detection, dietary management, and routine wellness checks to support a long, comfortable life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Why does the Norwegian Lundehund have six toes?**
The extra toes are an adaptation for gripping steep, slippery cliffs while hunting puffins. Each foot typically has at least six toes, including extra functional toe pads that improve traction and stability.

**Are Norwegian Lundehunds good family pets?**
Yes. They are affectionate and playful with their families and can do well with children when socialized. Their independence and challenging housetraining mean they suit patient owners who enjoy a quirky, primitive breed.

## Conclusion

The Norwegian Lundehund is a living piece of history, shaped by a unique island environment into a dog with extraordinary flexibility and a friendly, independent spirit. With patient training, attentive nutrition, and proactive health care, this rare breed makes a fascinating and devoted companion.
