Calm, courageous and famously laid-back, the Bulldog is a gentle companion with an unmistakable wrinkled face. Once bred for sport, today's Bulldog is a placid, affectionate family friend.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (18–25 kg) |
| Lifespan | 8–10 years |
| Coat | Short, smooth |
| Energy | Low |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Bulldogs are stocky and muscular with loose, wrinkled skin and a pushed-in face. They are gentle, friendly and dependable — content to lounge with the family and great with children.

## Diet & nutrition

- Measured, quality meals — Bulldogs gain weight easily, which strains breathing and joints.
- A slow feeder helps, as they can gulp air.

## Exercise — keep it gentle

- Short walks and gentle play are enough.
- **Avoid heat and overexertion** — their flat faces make breathing and cooling hard. Exercise in cool parts of the day, and never leave them in warm spaces.

## Grooming & care

- Short coat needs weekly brushing.
- **Clean and dry the skin folds and wrinkles regularly** to prevent infection.
- Clean ears, trim nails, maintain dental care.

## Health & lifespan

Bulldogs need committed health care: brachycephalic airway syndrome (breathing), skin-fold infections, hip and joint issues, and heat sensitivity. Buy from responsible breeders, keep them lean and cool, and budget for vet care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Bulldogs lazy?**
They're low-energy and love lounging — perfect for a relaxed household.

**Can Bulldogs swim?**
Most cannot — their build makes swimming dangerous, so supervise near water.

## Conclusion

The Bulldog is a calm, loving companion ideal for families and quieter homes. Protect them from heat, keep their wrinkles clean and weight down, and you'll enjoy a devoted, gentle friend.
