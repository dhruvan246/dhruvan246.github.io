Famous for their unique spotted coat, Dalmatians are athletic, energetic dogs originally bred to run alongside carriages. Behind those spots is a loyal, lively companion that needs a very active home.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium–large (16–32 kg) |
| Lifespan | 11–14 years |
| Coat | Short, dense (sheds year-round) |
| Energy | Very high |
| Good with kids | Yes, with enough exercise |

## Appearance & temperament

Dalmatians are sleek and muscular with the breed's iconic black or liver spots on a white coat. They are energetic, intelligent and loyal — devoted to their families but needing lots of activity to stay balanced.

## Exercise & training

- Require **substantial daily exercise** — running, hiking, dog sports. Bored Dalmatians become destructive.
- Intelligent but independent; consistent, positive training and early socialisation are important.

## Grooming & care

- Short coat is easy to brush but **sheds heavily year-round** — frequent brushing helps.

## Health & lifespan

A notable proportion are born **deaf** (in one or both ears) — reputable breeders test puppies (BAER test). They're also prone to **urinary stones**, so fresh water and the right diet matter. Watch for skin allergies too.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Dalmatians good family dogs?**
Yes for active families who can meet their high exercise needs.

**Do Dalmatians shed a lot?**
Yes — those short white hairs shed constantly.

## Conclusion

The Dalmatian is a striking, energetic and loyal companion for a very active household. Provide ample exercise, training and proper health care, and this spotted athlete will be a devoted friend.
