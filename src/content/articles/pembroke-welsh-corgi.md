Famous for their short legs, big ears and bright personalities, Pembroke Welsh Corgis are herding dogs in a compact package. Smart, lively and affectionate, the Corgi is a charming and surprisingly energetic companion.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Small–medium (10–14 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–15 years |
| Coat | Short–medium double coat |
| Energy | Moderate to high |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Corgis are long and low with upright ears and a fox-like face. They are intelligent, bold and affectionate — true herding dogs that are alert, active and eager to be involved in everything.

## Diet & nutrition

- **Watch the weight carefully** — Corgis love food and gain easily, which strains their long back.
- Measured meals and limited treats are essential.

## Exercise & training

- Need regular daily exercise and mental stimulation — they're working dogs at heart.
- Smart and trainable, but can be stubborn and vocal; positive training and early socialisation help.
- Discourage jumping on/off furniture to protect their back.

## Grooming

- Brush a few times a week; expect heavy seasonal shedding.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for obesity, back problems (long spine), hip dysplasia, and degenerative myelopathy in some lines. Keeping them lean is the single best thing for their health.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Corgis shed a lot?**
Yes — their double coat sheds heavily, especially seasonally.

**Are they good family dogs?**
Yes — smart, affectionate and great with children, though they may try to "herd."

## Conclusion

The Pembroke Welsh Corgi is a smart, lively and affectionate companion with a huge personality. Keep them lean, protect that long back, provide exercise and training, and you'll have a delightful, devoted friend.
