Smart, athletic and devoted, the Australian Shepherd is a herding dog that thrives on work and activity. Despite the name, the breed was refined in the United States — and it remains one of the most capable working companions around.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (16–32 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–15 years |
| Coat | Medium, double coat |
| Energy | Very high |
| Good with kids | Yes, with enough exercise |

## Appearance & temperament

"Aussies" have a striking medium coat, often in blue or red merle, and intelligent, expressive eyes. They are loyal, energetic and eager to please — but they need a purpose and plenty of activity to be content.

## Exercise & mental stimulation

- Require **vigorous daily exercise** plus mental work — herding, agility, fetch, training.
- A bored Aussie becomes anxious and destructive. This is not a dog for a sedentary home.

## Diet & nutrition

- A quality, active-dog diet portioned to their workload.
- Keep them lean and muscular.

## Grooming

- Brush 2–3 times weekly; expect seasonal shedding.
- Routine nail trims, ear checks and dental care.

## Training

Highly intelligent and quick to learn — excellent at dog sports and obedience. Channel their drive constructively with jobs and games.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for hip dysplasia, eye conditions, epilepsy, and the **MDR1 gene** drug sensitivity (tell your vet). Choose health-tested breeding lines.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Australian Shepherds good pets?**
For active, engaged owners — yes, wonderful. For quiet homes — they'll be frustrated.

**Do they need a lot of exercise?**
Yes — a great deal, every single day.

## Conclusion

The Australian Shepherd is a brilliant, energetic partner for an active owner. Give them exercise, training and a job to do, and you'll have a loyal, accomplished companion.
