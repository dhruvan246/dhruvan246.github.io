Gentle, hardworking and devoted, the Epagneul Bleu de Picardie (Blue Picardy Spaniel) is a French gundog prized by hunters for its calm temperament and tireless stamina in the field. Developed in the Picardy region of northern France, this versatile pointing and retrieving spaniel combines a soft, biddable nature at home with genuine working drive outdoors, making it a rewarding companion for active, attentive owners.

## Overview

| Trait | Details |
|---|---|
| Size | Medium; typically around 22–24 in (56–60 cm) at the shoulder |
| Lifespan | Roughly 12–14 years |
| Coat | Medium-length, flat or slightly wavy; speckled blue-grey and black |
| Energy | High; bred for long days afield |
| Good with kids | Yes; gentle and patient with respectful children |

## Appearance & temperament

The breed's most distinctive feature is its coat: a dense roan of black and grey speckling that creates the bluish appearance giving the dog its name, usually with darker black patches. The coat is medium in length, lying flat or slightly wavy, with feathering on the ears, legs, chest and tail. The ears are set fairly low and well feathered, and the overall build is balanced, athletic and sturdy without being heavy.

Temperamentally, the Blue Picardy Spaniel is known for being calm, gentle and affectionate. It bonds closely with its family, is generally sociable with other dogs, and tends to be sensitive rather than headstrong. This soft disposition makes it pleasant to live with, though the breed thrives on companionship and can become unhappy if left isolated for long stretches.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food appropriate for the dog's age, size and activity level. Because this is an active working breed, dogs that hunt or exercise heavily may need more calories than a sedentary pet of the same size. Divide the daily ration into two meals to support steady energy, and measure portions to prevent excess weight, which strains joints. Provide constant access to fresh water, especially after exertion. If you hunt or run your dog hard, avoid feeding a large meal immediately before or after intense activity. Consult your veterinarian for guidance tailored to your individual dog.

## Exercise & training

This is not a breed suited to apartment idleness. The Blue Picardy Spaniel needs substantial daily exercise, ideally including off-lead running, scent work, swimming or retrieving games that engage its hunting instincts. An under-exercised dog may become restless or anxious. Mental stimulation matters as much as physical activity.

Training is generally straightforward thanks to the breed's eagerness to please, but its sensitivity means harsh corrections backfire. Positive, reward-based methods with patience and consistency work best. Early socialization and basic obedience help channel its natural drive into a polite, confident adult.

## Grooming & care

The medium-length coat is relatively easy to maintain. Brush a couple of times a week to remove loose hair, prevent mats in the feathering, and keep the coat healthy. After outings in fields or water, check the coat and feet for burrs, seeds and debris. The pendulous, feathered ears trap moisture and should be checked and gently cleaned regularly to reduce the risk of infection. Trim nails as needed and maintain routine dental care.

## Health & lifespan

The Blue Picardy Spaniel is generally a hardy breed with a typical lifespan of about 12 to 14 years. As with many spaniels and medium-sized dogs, owners should be attentive to ear health given the long, hanging ears, and to joint conditions such as hip dysplasia. Maintaining a healthy weight, providing regular exercise, and keeping up with veterinary checkups, vaccinations and parasite prevention all support a long, comfortable life. Choosing a responsible breeder who screens breeding stock can further reduce inherited risks.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Epagneul Bleu de Picardie a good family dog?**
Yes. Its gentle, affectionate and patient nature suits families, and it is typically good with respectful children and other dogs. It does best with active households that can meet its considerable exercise needs and provide regular companionship.

**Does this breed need a lot of exercise?**
Very much so. Bred as a working gundog, it has high stamina and needs vigorous daily activity along with mental engagement. Without enough outlet for its energy, it can become bored, restless or anxious.

## Conclusion

The Epagneul Bleu de Picardie is a calm, devoted and capable spaniel that rewards owners who can match its energy and appreciate its gentle character. With ample exercise, consistent positive training, routine ear and coat care, and good preventive veterinary attention, this distinctive blue-coated French gundog makes a loyal companion both in the field and at home.
