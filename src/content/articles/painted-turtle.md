The Painted Turtle (*Chrysemys picta*) is one of North America's most widespread freshwater turtles and a popular choice for aquatic turtle keepers. Hardy, active, and attractively marked, it can thrive in captivity for decades when given a properly sized aquatic setup, basking access, and UVB lighting.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Adult size | Around 4-7 inches (10-18 cm) shell length; females usually larger |
| Lifespan | Commonly 20-30+ years with good care |
| Enclosure size | 40+ gallon aquarium for one adult; bigger is better |
| Temperament | Active, alert, semi-social but not a cuddly pet |
| Care level | Intermediate (commitment to water quality and lighting) |

## Appearance & temperament

Painted Turtles have a smooth, oval, dark olive to black carapace, often bordered with red or orange markings. Their most distinctive feature is the bright red, orange, and yellow striping on the head, neck, legs, and shell margins. They are diurnal and spend much of the day swimming, foraging, and basking. Curious and food-motivated, they often recognize their keeper at feeding time, but they generally dislike being held and may scratch or retreat when handled.

## Habitat & enclosure

These are highly aquatic turtles that need a large water area for swimming, plus a dry basking spot. A single adult needs at least a 40-gallon tank, with larger or multiple turtles requiring proportionally more space. Provide a water depth that allows full swimming, at least as deep as the turtle's shell is long. Include a secure basking platform or ramp that lets the turtle climb fully out of the water and dry off. Strong filtration (rated above the tank volume) is essential, since turtles are messy and produce a lot of waste. Avoid small gravel that can be swallowed.

## Heating, lighting & humidity

Painted Turtles need a temperature gradient. Keep water around 70-78°F (21-26°C), using an aquarium heater if the room is cool. The basking area should reach roughly 88-95°F (31-35°C) under a heat lamp so the turtle can thermoregulate. UVB lighting is critical: a quality UVB tube or bulb helps the turtle synthesize vitamin D3 and absorb calcium, preventing metabolic bone disease. Replace UVB bulbs on schedule, as output fades before the light visibly dims. Because these are aquatic turtles, ambient humidity is not a major concern, but stable water quality is.

## Diet & feeding

Painted Turtles are omnivores, and their diet shifts with age. Juveniles are more carnivorous, while adults eat more plant matter. Offer a varied diet of a quality commercial aquatic turtle pellet as a base, plus protein items like earthworms, insects, and occasional small feeder portions, alongside leafy greens and aquatic plants. Provide a calcium source such as a cuttlebone. Feed juveniles daily and adults every other day or so, removing uneaten food to protect water quality. Avoid overfeeding, which is a common cause of obesity and shell problems.

## Handling & health

Handle Painted Turtles only when necessary, supporting the body and washing hands afterward, as turtles can carry Salmonella. Common health issues include shell rot, respiratory infections, and metabolic bone disease, often linked to poor water quality, inadequate UVB, or improper temperatures. Signs of trouble include lethargy, swollen eyes, soft shell, or loss of appetite. Keep water clean and seek a reptile-experienced veterinarian for any concerns. Always check local laws, as collecting or keeping native turtles is regulated in many regions.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Painted Turtles need a heat lamp and UVB light?**
Yes. They need a warm basking spot from a heat lamp plus UVB lighting to process calcium properly. Without UVB, they commonly develop metabolic bone disease.

**How big a tank does a Painted Turtle need?**
A single adult needs at least a 40-gallon aquarium with deep swimming water and a dry basking area. More space and strong filtration are always better.

## Conclusion

Painted Turtles are rewarding, long-lived aquatic pets for keepers willing to maintain clean water, proper heating, and reliable UVB lighting. With a spacious tank, a varied omnivorous diet, and responsible handling, a healthy Painted Turtle can be an engaging companion for decades. Confirm local regulations before acquiring one.
