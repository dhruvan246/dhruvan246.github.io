The Ibizan Hound is a lean, agile sighthound known for its large upright ears, amber eyes, and remarkable jumping ability. Originating in the Balearic Islands of Spain, this ancient hunting breed is friendly, clever, and athletic, making a striking yet relatively low-maintenance companion.

## Appearance & temperament

Ibizan Hounds are tall, slim, and finely built, with long legs, a narrow head, and distinctive large, erect ears that move expressively. The coat comes in two varieties, smooth and wire-haired, usually in red, white, or a combination of the two. They are quiet, even-tempered dogs at home but lively and athletic outdoors. Intelligent and somewhat independent, they bond closely with their families and are generally polite with strangers, though they can be reserved. Their famous athleticism includes the ability to leap great heights from a standstill.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food appropriate to your hound's age, weight, and activity level. Because they are naturally lean and deep-chested, a slight visibility of the ribs is normal and overfeeding should be avoided. Divide meals into two portions a day and provide constant access to fresh water, especially after exercise. As with other deep-chested breeds, avoid heavy exercise immediately before or after eating. Consult your veterinarian before making significant diet changes.

## Exercise & training

Ibizan Hounds are energetic and need a good amount of daily activity, including walks and opportunities to run safely. Because they are sighthounds with a strong prey drive, they should be exercised on a lead or within a securely fenced area, as they may chase small animals and can clear ordinary fences with ease. They enjoy activities like lure coursing and agility. Training should be patient, positive, and reward-based; this sensitive, intelligent breed responds poorly to harsh methods. Early socialization helps them grow into confident, well-mannered adults.

## Grooming & care

Grooming needs are modest. The smooth coat requires only occasional brushing, while the wire-haired variety benefits from slightly more frequent attention to remove loose hair. Both shed moderately. Bathe only as needed, check and clean the large ears regularly, trim nails, and maintain dental hygiene. With little body fat and a fine coat, Ibizan Hounds can feel the cold, so a dog coat is helpful in chilly weather, and they appreciate soft, padded bedding.

## Health & lifespan

The Ibizan Hound is generally a healthy, hardy breed with a typical lifespan of around 12 to 14 years. As with many sighthounds, individuals can be sensitive to certain anaesthetics due to low body fat, so use a veterinarian familiar with the breed. Responsible breeders screen for conditions such as eye disorders and, less commonly, hip or seizure-related issues. Routine veterinary checkups, parasite prevention, and dental care support a long, healthy life. Always seek professional advice for any specific health concern.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can Ibizan Hounds be let off-lead?**
Generally no in unsecured areas. Their strong prey drive and speed mean reliable recall is difficult, so a securely fenced space is safest for free running.

**Do Ibizan Hounds need a lot of grooming?**
No. Both the smooth and wire-haired coats are low-maintenance, needing only occasional brushing, regular ear checks, and routine nail and dental care.

## Conclusion

The Ibizan Hound is an elegant, athletic, and affectionate companion that thrives with active owners who can provide secure space to run. With modest grooming needs and a friendly disposition, this ancient sighthound rewards patient, positive care with years of loyal companionship.
