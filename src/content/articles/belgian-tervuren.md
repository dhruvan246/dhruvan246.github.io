The Belgian Tervuren is the elegant long-haired variety of the Belgian Shepherd, named after the Belgian village of Tervuren. Graceful, intensely alert and deeply devoted to its family, it was bred as a versatile herding and working dog. Today it excels at herding, obedience, agility and protection sports, thriving when given a job and close companionship.

## Appearance & temperament

The Tervuren is a square, well-balanced dog standing roughly 56–66 cm at the shoulder. Its hallmark is a rich fawn-to-mahogany coat with a black overlay, a darker "mask" on the face and ears, and a fuller ruff and "breeches." The long outer coat and dense undercoat give it a proud, elegant silhouette.

Temperamentally, the Tervuren is highly intelligent, watchful and sensitive. It bonds strongly with its people and can be reserved or aloof with strangers, making early socialisation essential. Naturally protective and observant, it makes an attentive watchdog but needs firm, kind guidance to avoid nervous or overprotective behaviour.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced diet formulated for an active large breed. Because the Tervuren burns considerable energy, working or sport dogs may need higher-calorie food, while less active pets need careful portioning to prevent weight gain. Divide the daily amount into two meals rather than one large serving.

As with other deep-chested breeds, avoid heavy exercise immediately before or after eating. Choose a food appropriate to your dog's life stage, and always provide fresh water. Consult your veterinarian about portion sizes and any supplements rather than guessing.

## Exercise & training

This is a demanding breed for activity. A Tervuren needs well over an hour of vigorous daily exercise plus mental challenges. Long walks or runs, fetch, herding, agility, scent work and obedience all help channel its energy. A bored, under-exercised Tervuren can become anxious, vocal or destructive.

Training should start early and stay positive. The breed is quick to learn but sensitive, so reward-based methods work far better than harsh corrections. Consistent structure, varied tasks and ongoing socialisation produce a confident, well-mannered companion.

## Grooming & care

The double coat sheds steadily year-round with heavier seasonal moults. Brush at least twice weekly, and more often during shedding, to remove loose hair and prevent matting behind the ears and on the legs. Bathe only occasionally, as the coat is fairly weather-resistant and self-cleaning.

Round out care with routine nail trimming, dental brushing and regular checks of the ears and eyes. The Tervuren's strong bond means it also needs plenty of companionship and should not be left isolated for long periods.

## Health & lifespan

The Belgian Tervuren is generally a hardy, long-lived breed, typically reaching 12–14 years. Like many large herding breeds, it can be prone to hip and elbow dysplasia, certain eye conditions such as progressive retinal atrophy, epilepsy and, less commonly, thyroid issues.

Buying from a breeder who screens breeding dogs reduces these risks. Keep your dog at a healthy weight, maintain routine veterinary care and stay alert to any changes in movement, vision or behaviour.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Belgian Tervuren a good family dog?**
Yes, for active, committed households. It is loyal and affectionate with its family but needs early socialisation, training and ample exercise, and supervision around very young children.

**What is the difference between a Tervuren and a Belgian Malinois?**
They are varieties of the same Belgian Shepherd type. The Tervuren has a long fawn-to-mahogany coat with a black overlay, while the Malinois has a short coat and is often more drive-focused for working roles.

## Conclusion

The Belgian Tervuren is a beautiful, brilliant and energetic companion that rewards owners who can meet its considerable needs for exercise, training and closeness. Give it a job, consistent guidance and devoted companionship, and it will return that effort with loyalty, elegance and years of partnership.
