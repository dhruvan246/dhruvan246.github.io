The Pharaoh Hound is a graceful, athletic sighthound prized for its striking tan coat, amber eyes, and lively, affectionate personality. Originally developed as a hunting and coursing dog, it remains the national hound of Malta and makes a clean, devoted companion for active homes.

## Appearance & temperament

The Pharaoh Hound is a sleek, elegant dog built for speed, with a lean, muscular frame, long legs, and a deep chest. Its short, fine coat ranges from rich tan to chestnut, often with small white markings on the chest, toes, or tail tip. One of the breed's most endearing traits is "blushing": when happy or excited, the nose and ears flush a deep rose color.

Temperamentally, Pharaoh Hounds are friendly, playful, and affectionate with their families. They are intelligent and somewhat independent, with a strong prey drive typical of sighthounds. Many are reserved or aloof with strangers but rarely aggressive. They enjoy human company and do not like being left alone for long stretches.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food appropriate for your dog's age, weight, and activity level. Because these are lean, athletic dogs, monitor body condition closely and avoid overfeeding, as excess weight strains their fine-boned frame. Divide daily portions into two meals and provide constant access to fresh water. Treats should stay within roughly ten percent of daily calories. If you have questions about a specific diet, consult your veterinarian.

## Exercise & training

This is a high-energy breed that needs daily vigorous exercise, including brisk walks and opportunities to run safely. A securely fenced yard is ideal, since their strong prey drive and remarkable speed mean they may chase small animals and ignore recall. For this reason, keep them leashed in open or unfenced areas.

Pharaoh Hounds are smart but can be sensitive and independent, so training works best with patient, positive, reward-based methods. Early socialization and consistent, gentle guidance help them grow into well-mannered adults. They generally respond poorly to harsh corrections.

## Grooming & care

Grooming needs are minimal. The short coat requires only occasional brushing to remove loose hair and an occasional bath. Routine care includes regular nail trims, dental brushing, and ear checks. Their thin coat offers little insulation, so they feel the cold easily; a dog coat and limited time outdoors help in chilly weather.

## Health & lifespan

The Pharaoh Hound is generally a healthy, hardy breed with a typical lifespan of about 12 to 14 years. As with many breeds, responsible breeders screen for hip dysplasia and patellar (kneecap) issues. Sighthounds can also be sensitive to certain anesthetics, so make sure your veterinarian is aware of the breed. Regular wellness exams, dental care, and parasite prevention support a long, healthy life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Pharaoh Hounds good family dogs?**
Yes. They are affectionate and playful with their families and generally do well with children when interactions are supervised. Their high energy suits active households.

**Do Pharaoh Hounds really blush?**
Yes. When excited or happy, their nose and the insides of their ears flush a deep rose color, a charming and well-known feature of the breed.

## Conclusion

The Pharaoh Hound is an elegant, energetic, and affectionate companion that thrives with active owners who can provide daily exercise, secure spaces, and gentle training. With minimal grooming and a generally robust constitution, this ancient sighthound rewards a committed family with years of loyal, joyful companionship.
