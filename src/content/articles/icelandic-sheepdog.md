The Icelandic Sheepdog is Iceland's only native dog breed, a hardy Nordic spitz developed to herd sheep, horses, and cattle across rugged terrain. Cheerful, alert, and deeply people-oriented, it remains a beloved farm companion and family dog.

## Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|

| Lifespan | 12–14 years |
| Coat | Thick double coat, short or long variety |
| Energy | High |
| Good with kids | Yes, very friendly and tolerant |

## Appearance & temperament

The Icelandic Sheepdog is a compact, slightly rectangular spitz standing roughly 16.5–18 inches at the shoulder and weighing about 20–30 pounds, with males larger than females. It carries a curled tail over the back, prick ears, and an expressive face that often appears to be smiling. The weather-resistant double coat comes in short-haired and long-haired varieties and in colors such as tan, reddish-brown, chocolate, grey, and black, usually with white markings. Many individuals have distinctive dewclaws, sometimes double, on the hind legs.

Temperamentally this is an upbeat, affectionate, and sociable breed. It bonds closely with its family, greets people warmly, and is rarely aggressive. As a herding dog it is naturally alert and vocal, often barking to announce visitors or anything overhead—a trait that traces back to guarding flocks from birds of prey.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food appropriate for the breed's age, size, and activity level. Active working or sporting Icelandic Sheepdogs may need more calories than companion pets. Divide the daily amount into two meals and measure portions, since the breed can gain weight if overfed and under-exercised. Keep fresh water available, and use treats in moderation, accounting for them within daily calories. Consult your veterinarian about the best diet and portion sizes for your individual dog.

## Exercise & training

Bred for long days herding in harsh conditions, this breed has notable energy and stamina. Aim for at least an hour of activity daily through walks, play, hikes, fetch, or dog sports like agility, rally, and herding trials. Mental stimulation matters just as much as physical exercise; bored dogs may bark excessively or become restless.

Icelandic Sheepdogs are intelligent and eager to please, making them responsive to positive, reward-based training. Early socialization and consistent, gentle guidance help channel their enthusiasm and curb nuisance barking.

## Grooming & care

The double coat sheds steadily and heavily during seasonal shedding periods, typically twice a year. Brush weekly most of the year and more frequently during heavy sheds to remove loose undercoat and keep the coat healthy. The coat is self-cleaning and needs only occasional bathing. Trim nails regularly, brush teeth often, and check ears for debris. Because they are so people-focused, Icelandic Sheepdogs do best living indoors with their family rather than isolated outdoors.

## Health & lifespan

The Icelandic Sheepdog is generally a robust, healthy breed with a typical lifespan of around 12 to 14 years. Responsible breeders screen for conditions seen in the breed, including hip dysplasia, elbow dysplasia, patellar luxation, and certain eye disorders such as cataracts. Routine veterinary checkups, parasite prevention, dental care, and weight management support a long, active life. Ask breeders about health testing of the parents.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Icelandic Sheepdogs good family pets?**
Yes. They are friendly, gentle, and devoted, generally getting along well with children and other pets when properly socialized, which makes them excellent family companions.

**Do Icelandic Sheepdogs bark a lot?**
They can be quite vocal, as alert barking was prized in their herding work. Training, exercise, and mental enrichment help keep barking at a reasonable level.

## Conclusion

The Icelandic Sheepdog is a cheerful, energetic, and affectionate companion suited to active households that can provide daily exercise, regular brushing, and plenty of togetherness. In return, this hardy Nordic herder offers loyalty, smiles, and a charming, spirited personality.
