One of the oldest toy breeds, the Maltese has charmed companions for over two thousand years. With a flowing white coat and a sweet, gentle nature, this little dog is a devoted lap companion.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Tiny (2–4 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–15 years |
| Coat | Long, silky, white (low-shedding) |
| Energy | Low to moderate |
| Good with kids | Best with gentle, older children |

## Appearance & temperament

The Maltese is famous for its pure white, silky coat that hangs to the floor. They are gentle, affectionate and playful, bonding closely with their family and happiest by your side.

## Diet & nutrition

- Small portions of quality small-breed food.
- Watch for fussy eating; keep meals consistent.
- Provide fresh water and dental-friendly options.

## Grooming

- **Daily brushing** keeps the long coat mat-free; many owners opt for a short trim.
- Clean around the eyes daily to reduce tear staining.
- Regular baths, ear cleaning, nail trims and dental care.

## Exercise & training

Short walks and indoor play suit them well. They're intelligent and eager to please but can be hard to house-train — be patient and consistent.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for dental disease, luxating patella, collapsing trachea (use a harness), and tear staining. With attentive care, the Maltese enjoys a long life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Does the Maltese shed?**
Very little — often considered a good match for shedding-sensitive households.

**Are they good for first-time owners?**
Yes, as long as you commit to daily grooming.

## Conclusion

The Maltese is an elegant, loving companion that thrives on closeness and care. Keep that beautiful coat groomed and their teeth healthy, and you'll have a gentle friend for years.
