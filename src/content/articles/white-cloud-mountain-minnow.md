The White Cloud Mountain Minnow (*Tanichthys albonubes*) is a small, peaceful schooling fish that has earned the nickname "the poor man's neon tetra" for its bright colours and easygoing nature. Hardy, inexpensive, and tolerant of cooler temperatures, it is one of the best fish for beginners and for unheated nano aquariums.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Adult size | Around 1.5 inches (3–4 cm) |
| Lifespan | Roughly 5–7 years with good care |
| Minimum tank size | About 10 gallons (38 litres) |
| Water type | Freshwater, cool to room temperature |
| Care level | Easy / beginner-friendly |

## Appearance & behaviour

White Cloud Mountain Minnows have a slender, streamlined body with a coppery-brown to olive back and a bright iridescent stripe running along each flank, ending in a red-tipped tail. Fins are often edged in red, and the colours intensify when fish are kept in groups and displaying to one another. Popular varieties include the standard form, a golden ("gold cloud") strain, and longer-finned "meteor minnow" types.

They are active, constantly swimming shoaling fish that feel safest in groups of at least six to eight. In a proper school they are bold and visible; kept alone or in small numbers they become shy and faded. Males may gently spar and display brighter colours, but they are non-aggressive and never harm one another.

## Tank setup & water parameters

A tank of around 10 gallons or larger gives a small school room to swim. Provide a secure lid, as these minnows are capable jumpers. Decorate with live or artificial plants, some open swimming space, and a gentle to moderate current.

Unusually for tropical fish, White Clouds prefer cooler water and thrive at roughly 64–72°F (18–22°C). They can even live in unheated rooms, making them a rare choice for tanks without a heater. Aim for a pH near neutral (about 6.5–7.5) and soft to moderately hard water. Always cycle the aquarium before adding fish, and perform regular partial water changes to keep ammonia and nitrite at zero and nitrate low.

## Diet & feeding

White Cloud Mountain Minnows are easy-to-feed omnivores. A good-quality micro pellet or crushed flake food forms a solid staple, since their mouths are small. Supplement with frequent small offerings of live or frozen foods such as daphnia, brine shrimp, and bloodworms to boost colour and condition. Feed once or twice a day, giving only what the school can finish in a couple of minutes to avoid fouling the water.

## Tank mates & compatibility

Their peaceful temperament makes them excellent community fish, especially in cooler setups. Good companions include other small, non-aggressive species that tolerate similar temperatures, such as zebra danios, certain rasboras, dwarf corydoras, and shrimp. Avoid large, boisterous, or fin-nipping fish, and avoid pairing them long-term with strictly warm-water species whose temperature needs conflict with theirs.

## Health & common issues

White Clouds are robust and rarely sick when kept in clean, well-cycled, appropriately cool water. Most problems stem from poor water quality, overfeeding, or temperatures kept too warm for too long, which shortens their lifespan. Watch for common freshwater ailments such as ich (white spots), fin rot, and fungal infections, which usually appear when fish are stressed. Quarantine new arrivals, keep up with maintenance, and respond early to any signs of illness by checking and correcting water parameters first.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do White Cloud Mountain Minnows need a heater?**
Usually not. They prefer cool to room temperatures and do well in unheated tanks in most homes, though a heater can help in very cold rooms to avoid sudden swings.

**Can I keep just one or two?**
No. They are schooling fish and become stressed and dull when kept alone. Always keep a group of at least six so they feel secure and show their best colours.

## Conclusion

The White Cloud Mountain Minnow is a colourful, hardy, and genuinely beginner-friendly fish that shines in a cool, planted aquarium. Give it a school of companions, stable water quality, and cooler temperatures, and it will reward you with years of active, eye-catching display.
