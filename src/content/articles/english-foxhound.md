The English Foxhound is a large, sturdy scenthound developed in England over centuries to hunt foxes in packs alongside mounted riders. Built for stamina rather than speed, it is friendly, sociable and tireless, making it a rewarding companion for active homes with the space and time it needs.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Large (roughly 25–34 kg; 58–64 cm tall) |
| Lifespan | About 10–13 years |
| Coat | Short, dense, weatherproof; typically tricolour or tan and white |
| Energy | High — bred for hours of work |
| Good with kids | Yes — gentle and tolerant with proper exercise |

## Appearance & temperament

The English Foxhound is a balanced, muscular dog with straight legs, a deep chest and a moderately long head with low-set, rounded ears. The short, hard coat is easy to recognise in classic hound colours such as black, white and tan tricolour, or various pied patterns. Bred to work in large packs, the breed is exceptionally sociable and gets along well with other dogs and people. It is even-tempered and rarely aggressive, but it is also independent and driven by its nose, so it can wander or follow a scent if given the chance.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, good-quality diet appropriate for a large, active dog, divided into two meals a day. Active working hounds may need more calories than a pet kept at lighter exercise, so adjust portions to keep the dog lean — you should be able to feel the ribs easily. Because deep-chested breeds can be prone to bloat (gastric torsion), avoid heavy exercise immediately before and after eating. Always provide fresh water, and consult your veterinarian for guidance on amounts and any weight concerns.

## Exercise & training

This is a high-stamina breed that needs substantial daily exercise — long walks, runs or off-lead time in a secure area. A bored or under-exercised Foxhound can become restless and vocal. The breed is intelligent but independent, having been bred to work at a distance from handlers, so training rewards patience, consistency and positive, food-based motivation. A strong recall is hard to guarantee once a scent is found, so keep the dog on lead or in fenced areas near roads or livestock. Early, ongoing socialisation helps channel its naturally friendly nature.

## Grooming & care

Grooming needs are low. The short, dense coat needs only a weekly brush to remove loose hair and keep it healthy, with more frequent brushing during seasonal shedding. Bathe only when necessary. Check and clean the pendant ears regularly, as their shape can trap moisture and debris, and keep nails trimmed and teeth brushed. As a pack-bred dog, the English Foxhound dislikes being left alone for long periods and is happiest with company, whether human or canine.

## Health & lifespan

The English Foxhound is generally a hardy, healthy breed with a typical lifespan of about 10 to 13 years. As with many large, deep-chested dogs, owners should be aware of the risk of bloat. Hip dysplasia can occur in larger breeds, and the long ears benefit from routine checks to prevent infection. Keeping the dog at a healthy weight, providing regular exercise and scheduling routine veterinary check-ups support a long, comfortable life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the English Foxhound a good family pet?** It can be, for active families with space. The breed is gentle, sociable and tolerant, but it needs a great deal of exercise and ideally the company of other dogs or people.

**Is the English Foxhound the same as the American Foxhound?** No. They are separate breeds. The English Foxhound is generally heavier and more substantially built, while the American Foxhound tends to be taller, lighter and faster.

## Conclusion

The English Foxhound is a friendly, hardworking scenthound that thrives on activity and companionship. With plenty of daily exercise, secure spaces to explore and minimal grooming, it makes a loyal, easygoing companion for owners who can match its energy and social nature.
