The mynah bird, especially the popular hill mynah, is one of the most accomplished mimics in the avian world. Renowned for crisp, human-like speech, these glossy black starlings make engaging companions for dedicated keepers who can meet their demanding social, dietary, and space requirements.

## Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|---|---|
| Size | Roughly 9–12 inches (23–30 cm), depending on species |
| Lifespan | Commonly 10–15 years, sometimes longer with excellent care |
| Noise level | High — loud whistles, squawks, and constant chatter |
| Talking ability | Excellent; among the clearest mimics of human speech |
| Care level | Intermediate to advanced |

## Appearance & temperament

Mynahs are sturdy, glossy black songbirds with bright orange-yellow bills and legs. The hill mynah is distinguished by yellow wattles and skin patches behind the eyes, while the common mynah shows brown plumage with a dark head. They are intelligent, curious, and highly social, often bonding closely with their caregivers. Mynahs are active and vocal throughout the day, and many learn to mimic words, household sounds, and tunes with surprising clarity.

## Housing & cage setup

Because mynahs are active flyers and prolific eaters, they need a large cage — wider rather than tall, ideally several feet across to allow short flights. Provide multiple perches of varying diameters, including natural branches, plus sturdy toys for chewing and exploration. Mynahs are notoriously messy eaters and produce loose droppings, so easy-clean trays, washable surfaces, and frequent cleaning are essential. Place the cage in a warm, draft-free room away from kitchen fumes, and offer a shallow dish or daily misting for bathing, which they enjoy.

## Diet & nutrition

Mynahs are softbill, fruit-eating birds with specialized dietary needs. Their core diet should be a low-iron commercial softbill or mynah pellet, since these birds are prone to iron-storage disease. Supplement with a variety of fresh fruits such as papaya, melon, banana, grapes, and berries, plus occasional cooked vegetables. Small amounts of soaked pellets or insects can add protein. Avoid foods high in iron, like fortified cereals and excessive citrus, and never feed avocado, chocolate, caffeine, or salty human snacks. Provide fresh, clean water daily.

## Enrichment & handling

Mynahs thrive on interaction and mental stimulation. Spend time talking and whistling to them daily, as repetition encourages their mimicry. Rotate toys, offer foraging opportunities, and allow supervised out-of-cage time in a bird-safe room. Hand-raised mynahs can be tame and enjoy gentle handling, though they are generally less cuddly than parrots and prefer companionship over constant petting. Consistent routines and positive attention help prevent boredom and screaming.

## Health & lifespan

With good husbandry, mynahs can live well over a decade. The most significant species-specific concern is iron-storage disease, which a low-iron diet helps prevent. Watch for signs of illness such as fluffed feathers, lethargy, weight loss, or changes in droppings, and consult an avian veterinarian promptly. Routine check-ups, a clean environment, and a balanced diet support long-term health. As an exotic species, mynahs need specialist care, and ownership legality varies by region, so confirm local regulations before acquiring one.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can mynah birds really talk?**
Yes. Hill mynahs are exceptional mimics and can reproduce human speech, whistles, and sounds with remarkable clarity, often rivaling or surpassing many parrots in tone and pronunciation.

**Are mynah birds good pets for beginners?**
Not usually. Their loud voices, messy habits, specialized low-iron diet, and need for daily interaction make them better suited to experienced, dedicated keepers.

## Conclusion

Mynah birds reward attentive owners with charm, intelligence, and unmatched vocal talent. Meeting their needs for spacious housing, a careful low-iron diet, and daily companionship is demanding but deeply rewarding. For keepers prepared to commit, a mynah can be a lively, talkative friend for many years.
