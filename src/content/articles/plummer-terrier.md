The Plummer Terrier is a small, hardy working terrier developed in England by Brian Plummer in the latter half of the 20th century. Bred for ratting and bolting quarry, it combines drive, courage and a striking tan-and-white coat. It remains a working and companion terrier rather than a show breed, prized by enthusiasts for its tenacity and intelligence.

## Appearance & temperament

The Plummer Terrier is a compact, well-muscled dog with a wedge-shaped head, dark almond eyes and a keen, alert expression. The hallmark coloring is a rich fiery tan and white, typically with a white blaze, chest and feet. The coat is short and dense; some individuals have a slightly broken or harsher texture.

In temperament, this is a bold, energetic and intelligent terrier with strong working instincts. Plummers are affectionate and loyal with their families but retain a powerful prey drive toward small animals. They are alert and make good watchdogs, though they can be vocal and stubborn without consistent guidance.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food appropriate for an active small breed. Because Plummer Terriers are working dogs with a fast metabolism, they often do well on a moderately protein-rich diet that supports muscle and energy. Divide the daily amount into two meals and adjust portions based on age, weight and activity level.

Monitor body condition closely; you should be able to feel the ribs without a thick fat layer. Avoid overfeeding and limit treats, especially for less active pets. Always provide fresh water, and consult your veterinarian before making significant dietary changes.

## Exercise & training

This breed needs substantial daily exercise to stay balanced and content. Expect to provide at least an hour of activity, combining brisk walks, off-lead running in secure areas, and games that engage the mind. Plummers excel at earthdog trials, ratting, agility and scent work, all of which channel their natural drive.

Training should be early, consistent and reward-based. Plummer Terriers are quick learners but can be independent and willful, so firm, patient guidance works best. Prioritize recall and impulse control, and socialize widely with people and dogs from puppyhood. A secure garden is essential, as these terriers are determined diggers and escape artists.

## Grooming & care

Grooming needs are modest. The short coat requires only a weekly brush to remove loose hair and keep the skin healthy; brushing increases during seasonal shedding. Bathe only when necessary to preserve natural coat oils.

Check and clean the ears regularly, trim nails every few weeks, and brush the teeth several times a week to support dental health. After working or country walks, inspect for grass seeds, ticks and minor cuts.

## Health & lifespan

The Plummer Terrier is generally a robust, healthy breed with a typical lifespan of 12 to 15 years. As a relatively recent and working-focused breed, it benefits from a varied gene pool. Owners should still watch for common terrier issues such as luxating patella, dental disease and minor skin or ear problems.

Routine veterinary checkups, vaccinations, parasite control and a healthy weight all support a long life. Responsible breeders screen breeding stock for soundness.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Plummer Terriers good family pets?**
Yes. They are affectionate and loyal companions, but their high energy and prey drive mean they suit active homes and need supervision around very small children and small pets.

**Do Plummer Terriers bark a lot?**
They can be vocal, especially when alert or under-stimulated. Plenty of exercise, mental enrichment and consistent training help keep barking in check.

## Conclusion

The Plummer Terrier is a courageous, intelligent and tireless little working terrier that thrives with an active, committed owner. Given ample exercise, firm training and routine care, it rewards its family with loyalty, character and years of spirited companionship.
