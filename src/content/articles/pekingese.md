An ancient breed once cherished by Chinese royalty, the Pekingese is a dignified, lion-like little dog with a bold personality. Loyal and self-assured, the "Peke" carries itself with regal confidence.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Small (3–6 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–14 years |
| Coat | Long, thick, mane-like |
| Energy | Low |
| Good with kids | Best with gentle, older children |

## Appearance & temperament

Pekingese have a flat face, a long flowing coat and a distinctive lion's mane. They are confident, loyal and independent — affectionate with their chosen people but dignified and sometimes stubborn.

## Diet & nutrition

- Quality small-breed food in measured portions; keep them lean to ease breathing.

## Exercise — keep it gentle

- Short walks and gentle play are enough.
- **Avoid heat and overexertion** — as a flat-faced breed, they struggle to breathe and cool in warm weather.

## Grooming

- The heavy coat needs **regular brushing (several times a week)** to prevent mats.
- Clean facial wrinkles and keep the eye area clean.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for brachycephalic airway syndrome (breathing), eye problems (their eyes can protrude and injure easily), skin-fold infections, and heat sensitivity. Keep them cool and lean.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Pekingese affectionate?**
Loyal and devoted to their people, though dignified rather than overly cuddly.

**Do they need much exercise?**
No — low-energy, but they do need careful grooming and heat protection.

## Conclusion

The Pekingese is a regal, loyal and characterful companion with real presence. Keep them cool, groom that coat regularly, protect their eyes, and you'll enjoy this dignified little lion dog.
