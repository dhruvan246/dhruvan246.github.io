The Basset Fauve de Bretagne is a small, short-legged scent hound from Brittany in France, prized for its rich fawn coat, courageous hunting heritage, and friendly, energetic personality.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Small to medium (roughly 10–16 kg) |
| Lifespan | About 12–14 years |
| Coat | Short, harsh, wiry; fawn-colored |
| Energy | Moderate to high |
| Good with kids | Generally very good |

## Appearance & temperament

Despite the "basset" name, this is a compact, well-muscled hound that stands low to the ground on short, sturdy legs. The breed's hallmark is its harsh, wiry coat in shades of fawn, from golden-wheaten to red. The face carries a keen, alert expression with dropped ears of moderate length.

Temperamentally, the Fauve is lively, affectionate, and courageous — bred to follow game through dense Breton cover. They are sociable and people-oriented, but the strong hunting instinct means they can be independent-minded and easily distracted by interesting scents.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food appropriate to the dog's age, size, and activity level. Because this is an active hound that loves food, monitor portions and treats to prevent weight gain, which strains the back and joints. Divide the daily ration into measured meals, provide constant fresh water, and adjust amounts as your dog's activity changes through the seasons.

## Exercise & training

This is an energetic working breed that needs more activity than its size suggests. Aim for daily walks plus opportunities to explore, sniff, and play. Scent-based games and secure off-lead time satisfy their natural drive.

- Keep them on lead or in a fenced area — a tempting scent can override recall.
- Use positive, reward-based training; they respond well to food and praise.
- Start socialization and consistent routines early to channel their independent streak.

## Grooming & care

The harsh coat is relatively low-maintenance but benefits from regular care:

- Brush weekly to remove loose hair and debris.
- Hand-stripping (or occasional tidying) helps maintain coat texture.
- Check and clean the ears regularly, as drop ears can trap moisture.
- Keep nails trimmed and maintain routine dental care.

## Health & lifespan

The Basset Fauve de Bretagne is generally a hardy, robust breed with a typical lifespan of around 12 to 14 years. As with many drop-eared dogs, ear infections can occur if ears are not kept clean and dry. Their long-backed, short-legged build means it is sensible to keep them lean and avoid excess strain on the spine and joints. Routine veterinary check-ups, parasite prevention, and weight management support a long, healthy life. Always consult your veterinarian about any specific health concerns.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Basset Fauve de Bretagnes good for first-time owners?**
They can be, for active owners willing to provide exercise and consistent, patient training, but their hunting instinct and independence require commitment.

**Do they get along with other pets?**
They are usually sociable with other dogs. Caution is wise around small pets, given their strong prey drive as a hunting breed.

## Conclusion

The Basset Fauve de Bretagne is a cheerful, hardy little hound that combines a rugged working background with an affectionate family temperament. Give them daily exercise, secure boundaries, and regular ear and coat care, and they make spirited, devoted companions.
