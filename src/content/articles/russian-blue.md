Quiet, elegant and devoted, the Russian Blue is a beautiful breed known for its shimmering blue-grey coat and vivid green eyes. Gentle and a little reserved, they bond deeply with their chosen people.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (3–5.5 kg) |
| Lifespan | 15–20 years |
| Coat | Short, dense, plush double coat |
| Energy | Moderate |
| Good with kids | Yes, in calm homes |

## Appearance & temperament

Russian Blues have a striking silvery-blue double coat and bright green eyes. They are gentle, intelligent and somewhat shy — often reserved with strangers but affectionate and loyal with their family. They like routine and a calm environment.

## Diet & nutrition

- Quality cat food in measured portions; they enjoy their food, so watch for weight gain.

## Grooming & care

- The plush double coat needs only weekly brushing.
- Provide quiet retreats, perches and gentle play; they appreciate predictability.

## Health & lifespan

One of the healthier, long-lived breeds. Watch for obesity and routine feline issues like dental disease. Regular vet care and weight control keep them in great shape.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Russian Blues shy?**
Often reserved with strangers, but devoted and affectionate with their family.

**Are they good for quiet homes?**
Ideal — they thrive in calm, predictable environments.

## Conclusion

The Russian Blue is a graceful, gentle and loyal companion for a calm household. Respect their reserved nature, keep them lean, and you'll earn the deep devotion of this elegant breed.
