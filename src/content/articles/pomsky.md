The Pomsky is a designer crossbreed created by mixing the Siberian Husky with the Pomeranian, usually via artificial insemination because of the size difference between the two parent breeds. The result is a small, plush-coated dog that often resembles a miniature Husky, with the striking looks and lively personality that have made the cross popular over the past decade.

## Overview

| Trait | Typical range |

## Appearance & temperament

Because the Pomsky is a first- or later-generation cross rather than a standardized breed, appearance varies widely even within a single litter. Most are small to medium dogs with a fox-like or Husky-like face, erect ears, a curled or plumed tail and a dense double coat. Coat colors include grey-and-white, black-and-white, red, and sable, and some individuals inherit the Husky's blue or parti-colored eyes.

Temperamentally, Pomskies tend to be playful, affectionate and intelligent. They often combine the Pomeranian's alertness with the Husky's sociability, which can make them confident and outgoing. Many are talkative, "speaking" with howls and chatter, and they can become attached to their families. Early socialization helps prevent the wariness or excessive barking that small, alert dogs sometimes develop.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food formulated for small breeds and appropriate to your dog's life stage. Because Pomskies are small and can gain weight easily, measure portions and divide the daily amount into two meals rather than free-feeding. Treats should make up only a small share of daily calories. Always provide fresh water, and ask your veterinarian for guidance on the right food type and quantity for your individual dog's size, age and activity level.

## Exercise & training

Despite their small stature, Pomskies are energetic and need daily activity to stay happy. A couple of walks plus play sessions usually satisfy them, and they enjoy games, fetch and puzzle toys that engage their sharp minds. Without enough stimulation they may bark, dig or become destructive.

Both parent breeds are intelligent but can be independent, so training works best with short, consistent, reward-based sessions. Start socialization and basic obedience early, and be patient with housetraining, which some small dogs find slower to master.

## Grooming & care

The Pomsky's double coat needs regular care. Brush several times a week, and more often during seasonal shedding periods when the undercoat blows. Routine grooming keeps the coat free of mats and reduces loose hair around the home. Bathe only as needed to preserve natural coat oils. Round out care with regular nail trims, dental brushing and ear checks. Like Huskies, Pomskies can be sensitive to heat, so avoid heavy exercise in hot weather.

## Health & lifespan

Pomskies are generally healthy and typically live around 12 to 15 years. As a cross, they can inherit conditions seen in either parent, including dental disease, luxating patella, eye problems and skin or coat issues. Buying from a responsible breeder who screens parent dogs, and keeping up with routine veterinary visits, vaccinations and parasite prevention, supports a long, healthy life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Pomskies hypoallergenic?**
No. Pomskies have a thick double coat and shed, especially seasonally, so they are not considered hypoallergenic.

**How big does a Pomsky get?**
Sizes vary, but most Pomskies stand roughly 10 to 15 inches tall and weigh about 15 to 30 pounds when fully grown.

## Conclusion

The Pomsky is an affectionate, energetic and eye-catching small dog that brings together the looks of a Husky and the size of a Pomeranian. With daily exercise, consistent positive training, regular grooming and routine veterinary care, a Pomsky can be a devoted and entertaining companion for many years.
