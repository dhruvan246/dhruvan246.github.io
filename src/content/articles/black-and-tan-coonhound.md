The Black and Tan Coonhound is a large American scenthound bred to trail and tree raccoons and other game by following ground scent over long distances. Easygoing at home but tireless on a trail, this breed rewards owners who appreciate its mellow nature and powerful nose.

## Overview

| Trait | Typical range |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Large, roughly 23-27 inches at the shoulder; about 65-110 lb |
| Lifespan | Around 10-12 years |
| Coat | Short, dense, glossy black coat with rich tan markings |
| Energy | Moderate to high; built for endurance |
| Good with kids | Generally yes; gentle and tolerant when socialized |

## Appearance & temperament

This is an imposing, athletic hound with a deep chest, long muscular legs, and the long, low-set ears characteristic of trailing breeds. The coat is its hallmark: coal black with clearly defined tan markings above the eyes, on the muzzle, chest, legs, and under the tail. The expression is alert and friendly.

Temperamentally, the Black and Tan Coonhound is even-tempered, affectionate, and sociable with its family, often described as laid-back indoors. It can be reserved with strangers but is rarely aggressive. The breed is famous for its deep, melodious bay, used to signal a trail or a treed animal. That voice, combined with a strong prey drive, means a coonhound may follow its nose for considerable distances if given the chance.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food appropriate for a large, active breed and adjust portions to your dog's age, weight, and activity level. Because coonhounds are food-motivated and can overeat, measure meals rather than free-feeding, and keep treats modest to prevent excess weight that strains joints. Splitting the daily ration into two meals is sensible for a deep-chested dog. Always provide fresh water, and consult your veterinarian about specific dietary needs at each life stage.

## Exercise & training

This breed needs substantial daily exercise: long walks, jogs, hikes, or secure off-leash running to satisfy its stamina. A well-exercised coonhound is calm and content; an under-exercised one may bay, dig, or become restless. Because the nose can override recall, exercise in fenced areas or on a long lead near roads or wildlife.

Training works best with patient, consistent, reward-based methods. Coonhounds are intelligent but independent and can be distractible when a scent captures their attention. Early socialization and basic obedience help, and scent games or tracking activities channel their instincts productively.

## Grooming & care

The short coat is low-maintenance: weekly brushing removes loose hair and keeps it glossy, with more frequent brushing during seasonal shedding. Bathe as needed. The long, drooping ears trap moisture and debris, so check and gently clean them regularly to reduce the risk of ear infections. Trim nails routinely and maintain dental care.

## Health & lifespan

The Black and Tan Coonhound is generally a hardy breed with a typical lifespan of roughly 10 to 12 years. As a large, deep-chested dog, it can be predisposed to bloat (gastric dilatation-volvulus), as well as hip dysplasia and ear infections related to its pendulous ears. Eye conditions are also seen in the breed. Choosing a responsible breeder who screens breeding stock, keeping your dog at a healthy weight, and maintaining routine veterinary care all support long-term health. Discuss any specific concerns with your veterinarian.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Black and Tan Coonhounds loud?**
They can be. The breed is known for a deep, carrying bay used while trailing or alerting, and many are vocal at home, so they suit households where some noise is acceptable.

**Do Black and Tan Coonhounds make good family pets?**
Yes, for active families. They are gentle and affectionate, generally good with children when socialized, but need plenty of exercise and secure containment because of their strong scent drive.

## Conclusion

The Black and Tan Coonhound is a devoted, gentle giant for owners who can meet its need for exercise, secure space, and patient training. Reward its remarkable nose with activity and companionship, and you will have a calm, loyal hound at your side for years.
