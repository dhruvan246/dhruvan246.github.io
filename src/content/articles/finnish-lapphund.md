The Finnish Lapphund is a medium-sized spitz breed developed in the Arctic regions of Finland, where it was traditionally used by the Sámi people to herd reindeer. Friendly, alert, and remarkably adaptable to cold, it has become a beloved companion dog known for its sweet expression and willingness to please.

## Overview

| Trait | Details |

| Energy | Moderate to high |

## Appearance & temperament

The Finnish Lapphund is a strongly built, slightly longer-than-tall dog standing roughly 16–21 inches at the shoulder. It carries a profuse double coat that comes in nearly every color, often with distinctive markings, and a plumed tail that curls over the back. The face is framed by a ruff and topped with erect, mobile ears that give an eager, intelligent look.

Temperamentally, this breed is courageous yet calm, affectionate with its family, and notably friendly toward strangers. Bred to work closely with herders, it is sensitive, eager to learn, and forms strong bonds. Many retain a herding instinct and a tendency to bark, which historically warned of predators.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food appropriate to your Lapphund's age, weight, and activity level. As an active medium breed, it does well on quality kibble or a vet-approved fresh diet, typically divided into two meals a day. Because the heavy coat can hide weight gain, monitor body condition by feel rather than appearance. Provide constant access to fresh water, and keep treats to a small portion of daily calories.

## Exercise & training

Finnish Lapphunds are working dogs at heart and need daily physical and mental activity—brisk walks, play, and opportunities to run safely. They excel at canine sports such as agility, herding, obedience, and rally, which channel their energy and intelligence productively.

Training should be positive and reward-based; this sensitive breed responds poorly to harsh methods. They learn quickly but can be independent, so consistency and early socialization help. Redirecting their natural barking and herding tendencies early prevents nuisance habits.

## Grooming & care

The double coat requires regular upkeep. Weekly brushing keeps the coat healthy, with more frequent attention during the twice-yearly heavy shed, when the dense undercoat blows out. Never shave a Lapphund, as the coat insulates against both cold and heat. Bathe occasionally as needed. Routine care includes nail trimming, dental brushing, and checking the ears, especially given the breed's furnishings.

## Health & lifespan

The Finnish Lapphund is generally a hardy, long-lived breed, often reaching 12–15 years. Responsible breeders screen for conditions seen in the breed, including hip dysplasia and inherited eye disorders such as progressive retinal atrophy. Maintaining a healthy weight, providing regular exercise, and scheduling routine veterinary checkups support a long, comfortable life. Always consult your veterinarian about screening and preventive care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Finnish Lapphunds good family dogs?**
Yes. They are gentle, patient, and affectionate, generally getting along well with children and other pets when properly socialized.

**Do Finnish Lapphunds shed a lot?**
Yes. They shed year-round and blow their dense undercoat heavily twice a year, so expect regular brushing and seasonal cleanup.

## Conclusion

The Finnish Lapphund is a versatile, good-natured companion that blends Arctic hardiness with a loving disposition. With regular exercise, consistent positive training, and routine coat care, this adaptable spitz thrives as both an active partner and a devoted family dog.
