Bred to guard estates by quietly pinning intruders, the Bullmastiff is a powerful yet calm and affectionate guardian. Devoted to family and naturally protective, this large breed is gentle at home with the right training.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Large (45–59 kg) |
| Lifespan | 8–10 years |
| Coat | Short, dense |
| Energy | Low to moderate |
| Good with kids | Yes, with supervision |

## Appearance & temperament

Bullmastiffs are powerfully built with a broad head and a short fawn, red or brindle coat with a dark mask. They are calm, confident and devoted — affectionate with family and naturally watchful, without being hyperactive.

## Exercise & training

- Moderate daily walks; they're not high-energy.
- **Early socialisation and consistent training are essential** for a dog this size and strength. They can be strong-willed; use calm, positive methods.

## Diet & nutrition

- A quality large-breed diet; puppies need controlled growth.
- Feed measured meals and **take bloat precautions**.

## Grooming & care

- Easy — weekly brushing.
- Expect some drooling; keep them cool (short face + bulk = heat-sensitive).

## Health & lifespan

Giant-breed risks: hip and elbow dysplasia, bloat, heart conditions, certain cancers, and a shorter lifespan. Choose health-tested lines and keep them lean.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Bullmastiffs good family dogs?**
Yes — devoted and gentle with family, but they need training, socialisation and supervision.

**Do they need much exercise?**
No — modest exercise suits them, but early training is a must.

## Conclusion

The Bullmastiff is a calm, devoted and protective companion for an owner prepared for a powerful giant breed. Socialise and train early, feed carefully, keep them lean and cool, and you'll have a loyal guardian.
