The potbellied pig is an intelligent, affectionate companion animal that became popular as a "miniature" pet. While smaller than commercial farm hogs, these pigs are still substantial animals with complex needs, and they require a long-term commitment that many owners underestimate.

## Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Size | 16-26 in tall, 60-175 lb typical |
| Lifespan | 12-18 years |
| Space needs | Secure yard plus sheltered indoor area |
| Temperament | Smart, social, curious, food-motivated |
| Care level | Intermediate to advanced |

## Appearance & temperament

Potbellied pigs are recognizable by their sagging belly, swayed back, short legs, straight tail, and upright ears. Coats are usually black, but white, gray, and spotted patterns occur. Despite marketing terms like "teacup" or "micro," there is no genuinely tiny breed; most healthy adults weigh well over 60 pounds.

They are remarkably intelligent, often compared to dogs in problem-solving ability. Pigs are highly social, form strong bonds with their keepers, and can learn names, tricks, and routines. They can also be stubborn and territorial, and intact animals may become aggressive, so neutering or spaying is strongly recommended.

## Housing & space

Pigs need a draft-free, insulated shelter with deep bedding such as straw, plus access to a secure outdoor area for rooting and grazing. Rooting is a natural behavior, so expect digging; a designated dirt patch helps protect lawns and gardens. Fencing must be sturdy and low to the ground, as pigs push under barriers.

Provide shade and a wallow or mud area in warm weather, since pigs cannot sweat and overheat easily. Indoor pigs need non-slip flooring to protect their joints. Check local zoning laws first, as many areas classify pigs as livestock and restrict or prohibit them.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a commercial mini-pig pellet formulated for potbellied pigs, supplemented with leafy greens and limited vegetables. Pigs are prone to obesity, which strains joints and can cause "fat blindness" when facial fat blocks vision, so portion control is essential. Avoid dog and cat food, salty snacks, and excess fruit.

Provide constant access to fresh water and offer fiber-rich foods to keep them satisfied. Never free-feed. Use food puzzles and scatter-feeding to satisfy foraging instincts and slow eating.

## Care & handling

Pigs dislike being picked up and may squeal loudly when restrained, so teach them to walk on a harness and follow rather than lifting them. Daily enrichment, training sessions, and social interaction prevent boredom and destructive behavior.

Hooves need periodic trimming, and tusks in males may require occasional filing. Skin can become dry and flaky, so brushing and the occasional gentle bath help. Establish a consistent routine, as pigs thrive on predictability.

## Health & lifespan

With good care, potbellied pigs commonly live 12 to 18 years. Find an exotic or farm veterinarian before bringing one home, as not all clinics treat pigs. Routine care includes vaccinations, deworming, hoof trimming, and weight monitoring.

Common issues include obesity, arthritis, respiratory infections, skin conditions, and dental problems. Spaying females greatly reduces the risk of uterine and mammary tumors. Watch for changes in appetite, breathing, or mobility, and seek prompt veterinary care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do potbellied pigs stay small?** No. "Teacup" and "micro" pigs are marketing labels, not real breeds. Most potbellied pigs reach 60 to 175 pounds at maturity, so plan for a large, long-lived animal.

**Can a potbellied pig live indoors?** They can, but they still need outdoor access for rooting, exercise, and sunlight. Indoor pigs require non-slip floors, a consistent toilet routine, and plenty of enrichment to stay healthy and content.

## Conclusion

Potbellied pigs are clever, charming companions, but they demand space, specialized diets, legal clearance, and a decade-plus commitment. Owners who provide proper housing, weight control, and veterinary care are rewarded with a deeply social and engaging pet.
