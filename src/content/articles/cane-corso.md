A majestic Italian mastiff-type breed, the Cane Corso is a powerful, intelligent guardian. Confident and deeply devoted to its family, this is a serious working dog that demands an experienced, committed owner.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Large (40–50 kg) |
| Lifespan | 9–12 years |
| Coat | Short, stiff |
| Energy | Moderate to high |
| Good with kids | With family, yes — supervised |

## Appearance & temperament

Cane Corsos are large and muscular with a short coat and an imposing presence. They are confident, intelligent and intensely loyal — protective of family yet calm and steady when well-raised. Proper socialisation and training are non-negotiable.

## Exercise & training

- Need daily exercise plus mental work — they're working dogs.
- **Experienced ownership, early socialisation and firm, consistent, positive training are essential.** A dog this powerful must be well-mannered and confident.

## Diet & nutrition

- A quality large-breed diet; puppies need controlled growth.
- Feed measured meals and **take bloat precautions**.

## Grooming & care

- Easy — weekly brushing.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for hip and elbow dysplasia, bloat, eye conditions (entropion/ectropion), and heart issues. Choose health-tested lines and keep them lean.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Cane Corsos good for first-time owners?**
No — their size, power and guarding instincts require experienced handling.

**Are they aggressive?**
Not when well-bred, socialised and trained — but they are serious guardians needing responsible ownership.

## Conclusion

The Cane Corso is a powerful, intelligent and devoted guardian for an experienced, committed owner. Invest in early socialisation and consistent training, and you'll have a steady, loyal protector.
