A powerful and dignified breed from Japan, the Akita is famous for its deep loyalty — immortalised by the legendary Hachikō. Devoted and courageous, the Akita is a serious dog for an experienced, committed owner.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Large (32–59 kg) |
| Lifespan | 10–13 years |
| Coat | Thick double coat |
| Energy | Moderate |
| Good with kids | With supervision; better in adult homes |

## Appearance & temperament

Akitas are large, bear-like dogs with a plush double coat and a curled tail. They are loyal, calm and protective — devoted to family but often aloof or assertive with strangers and other dogs. Early, thorough socialisation is essential.

## Exercise & training

- Moderate daily exercise — walks and play.
- Intelligent but strong-willed and independent; **experienced ownership, early socialisation and consistent training** are crucial. They can be dog-aggressive, especially with the same sex.

## Grooming

- Brush 2–3 times a week, daily during heavy seasonal sheds.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for hip dysplasia, hypothyroidism, eye conditions, bloat, and autoimmune issues. Choose health-tested lines and maintain regular vet care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Akitas good for first-time owners?**
Generally no — their size, strength and strong will suit experienced owners.

**Are they good with other dogs?**
Often not — many are dog-selective or dog-aggressive; careful management is needed.

## Conclusion

The Akita is a loyal, dignified and powerful companion for an experienced, committed owner. Provide early socialisation, consistent training and proper care, and you'll have a devoted, noble guardian.
