The Syrian hamster, also called the golden or teddy bear hamster, is one of the most popular small pets in the world. Solitary, hardy, and big enough to handle comfortably, it makes an excellent first pet for older children and adults willing to learn its needs.

## Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Size | Around 5-7 inches (13-18 cm) long |
| Lifespan | Typically 2-3 years |
| Housing | Large solo enclosure, never housed in pairs |
| Temperament | Curious, mostly solitary, nocturnal |
| Care level | Beginner-friendly |

## Appearance & temperament

Syrian hamsters are noticeably larger than dwarf species, with a stocky body, short tail, and roomy cheek pouches used to carry food and bedding. The wild "golden" coat is the most common, but selective breeding has produced long-haired (teddy bear), satin, and many color variations.

By nature they are solitary and territorial. Unlike some dwarf hamsters, Syrians must live alone, as adults housed together will fight, sometimes fatally. They are crepuscular to nocturnal, becoming most active in the evening and overnight. With gentle, regular handling they often become tame and enjoy interacting with their owners.

## Housing & environment

Provide the largest enclosure you can, far bigger than the small cages often sold in pet shops. A solid-bottom tank or a cage with a deep base allows generous, burrowable bedding. Aim for plenty of unbroken floor space rather than relying on tubes and multiple levels.

Fill the enclosure with several inches of paper-based or aspen bedding so your hamster can dig and tunnel. Add a solid exercise wheel large enough that the back stays straight (typically 8 inches or more), a hideout, chew items, and a sand bath. Keep the habitat indoors, out of direct sun and drafts, at a comfortable room temperature. Avoid cedar and pine shavings, which can irritate airways.

## Diet & nutrition

A quality commercial hamster mix or pellet forms the foundation of the diet. Pelleted food prevents selective feeding, where a hamster eats only its favorite pieces and ignores the rest. Supplement with small amounts of fresh vegetables and occasional fruit, plus the rare protein treat such as a mealworm or plain cooked egg.

Offer fresh water at all times from a bottle or shallow dish. Provide safe chew material, since a hamster's teeth grow continuously. Remove uneaten fresh food daily to prevent spoilage, and feed treats sparingly to avoid obesity.

## Handling & enrichment

Let a new hamster settle for several days before handling. Always approach during waking hours, never startle a sleeping hamster, and scoop gently with both hands rather than grabbing from above. Sit low to the ground in case of a fall, and supervise any interaction with children.

Enrichment keeps hamsters mentally and physically healthy. Rotate toys, cardboard tubes, foraging puzzles, and digging areas. A securely fenced floor playpen offers safe exploration outside the cage. Hiding food around the enclosure encourages natural foraging behavior.

## Health & lifespan

Most Syrian hamsters live around 2-3 years. Common health concerns include wet tail (a serious digestive illness needing prompt veterinary care), overgrown teeth, respiratory infections, and skin or fur problems. Older hamsters can develop tumors.

Watch for warning signs such as appetite loss, lethargy, diarrhea, labored breathing, or sudden weight change, and seek an exotics-savvy veterinarian when they appear. Good hygiene, dry clean bedding, a balanced diet, and a stress-free environment go a long way toward preventing illness.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can Syrian hamsters live together?** No. Syrian hamsters are strictly solitary and must be kept one per enclosure. Housing them together leads to serious, often fatal, fighting once they mature.

**Are Syrian hamsters good pets for children?** They can be, with adult supervision. Their larger size makes them easier to hold than dwarf hamsters, but their nocturnal schedule and need for gentle handling suit older children best.

## Conclusion

The Syrian hamster is a rewarding, low-cost companion that thrives on a spacious solo enclosure, deep bedding, a balanced diet, and patient handling. Meet these straightforward needs and you will enjoy a curious, charming little pet for its full natural life.
