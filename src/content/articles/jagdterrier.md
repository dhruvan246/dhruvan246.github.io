The Jagdterrier, also called the German Hunting Terrier, is a small, powerfully built working dog developed in Germany in the early 20th century. Bred specifically for versatile hunting, it remains an intensely driven, courageous breed that thrives when given a serious job to do.

## Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|---|---|
| Size | Small (about 13-16 in / 33-40 cm tall) |
| Lifespan | 12-15 years |
| Coat | Short, dense; smooth (rough) or broken-coated varieties |
| Energy | Very high |
| Good with kids | Best with older, respectful children |

## Appearance & temperament

The Jagdterrier is compact and athletic, typically weighing around 17-22 pounds (8-10 kg). Its coat is hard and weather-resistant, usually black, dark brown, or grizzled black-and-tan, with tan markings on the muzzle, chest, legs, and over the eyes. The body is slightly longer than tall, with a deep chest and strong jaws suited to its working heritage.

Temperamentally, this is a bold, tenacious, and highly intelligent breed. Bred to hunt fox, badger, wild boar, and small game both above and below ground, the Jagdterrier shows fearless determination and a strong prey drive. It bonds closely with its handler but can be reserved with strangers and assertive with other dogs.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a high-quality diet formulated for active small-to-medium dogs, with adequate protein and fat to fuel its considerable energy. Most adults do well on two measured meals per day; portion according to weight, age, and activity level. Because the breed is compact and athletic, monitor body condition closely to prevent excess weight, which can strain joints. Provide constant access to fresh water, especially after vigorous exercise or fieldwork. Treats are useful for training but should stay within roughly ten percent of daily calories.

## Exercise & training

The Jagdterrier needs substantial daily exercise, far more than its small size suggests. Long walks, running, hiking, and structured activities such as earthdog trials, tracking, agility, or scent work help satisfy its working instincts. A bored or under-exercised Jagdterrier can become destructive or vocal.

Training requires consistency, confidence, and early socialization. This breed is smart and eager but independent and strong-willed, so positive, reward-based methods combined with firm, fair boundaries work best. Recall and impulse control deserve special attention given the powerful prey drive, and a securely fenced yard is essential since the breed will chase and dig.

## Grooming & care

Grooming needs are modest. The short, dense coat requires only weekly brushing to remove loose hair and distribute oils; rough-coated individuals may benefit from occasional hand-stripping. Bathe only when necessary to preserve the coat's natural weatherproofing. Routine care includes regular nail trimming, ear checks (particularly after fieldwork or swimming), and dental brushing several times a week to support long-term oral health.

## Health & lifespan

The Jagdterrier is generally a hardy, robust breed with a typical lifespan of about 12 to 15 years. As with many small working dogs, responsible breeders screen for issues such as luxating patella and eye conditions including primary lens luxation. Keeping the dog at a healthy weight, maintaining dental hygiene, and scheduling regular veterinary checkups all support a long, active life. Always discuss any health concerns or preventive care with a qualified veterinarian.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Jagdterriers good apartment dogs?**
Generally no. Their very high energy and strong working drive make them better suited to active homes with space and a securely fenced yard, plus an owner committed to daily vigorous exercise and mental stimulation.

**Is the Jagdterrier a good first dog?**
Usually not. Their independence, intensity, and prey drive can overwhelm inexperienced owners. They suit confident handlers who can provide consistent training, firm structure, and plenty of purposeful activity.

## Conclusion

The Jagdterrier is a small dog with an outsized work ethic. For an active, experienced owner who can channel its drive through exercise, training, and a sense of purpose, this courageous German terrier makes a loyal, capable, and rewarding companion.
