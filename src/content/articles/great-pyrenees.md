Bred to guard flocks in the mountains, the Great Pyrenees is a majestic, calm and protective livestock guardian. Gentle with family and independent by nature, this giant white dog is devoted, patient and watchful.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Giant (38–54+ kg) |
| Lifespan | 10–12 years |
| Coat | Thick, weatherproof double coat |
| Energy | Low to moderate |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Pyrs are large and powerful with a beautiful white double coat. They are calm, gentle and devoted guardians — patient with family and livestock, independent thinkers, and naturally protective and watchful (often barking, especially at night).

## Exercise & training

- Moderate daily exercise — walks; they're calm but need a secure, fenced space (they roam and guard territory).
- Independent guardians by instinct; patient, consistent, positive training and **early socialisation** are essential. Recall can be unreliable.

## Grooming

- **Brush 2–3 times a week**, more during heavy seasonal sheds.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for hip and elbow dysplasia, bloat, patellar luxation, and heart conditions. Choose health-tested lines, keep them lean, and protect from heat.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Great Pyrenees bark a lot?**
Yes — guarding instinct makes them vocal, especially at night.

**Are they good family dogs?**
Yes — gentle and devoted, but they need space, training and an understanding of their independent guardian nature.

## Conclusion

The Great Pyrenees is a majestic, calm and protective companion for a family with space and an appreciation for guardian breeds. Provide secure fencing, early socialisation and regular grooming, and you'll have a devoted, gentle guardian.
