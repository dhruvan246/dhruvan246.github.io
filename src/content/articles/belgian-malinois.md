The Belgian Malinois is the elite athlete of the dog world — the breed of choice for police and military work. Intensely driven, intelligent and tireless, the "Mal" is a phenomenal partner for the right owner, and a poor fit for almost everyone else.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Large (18–36 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–14 years |
| Coat | Short, dense |
| Energy | Extremely high |
| Good with kids | With training, exercise and supervision |

## Appearance & temperament

Malinois resemble a lighter, more intense German Shepherd, with a fawn coat and black mask. They are brilliant, energetic and work-obsessed — happiest with a demanding job and constant engagement. Without it, they become destructive and anxious.

## Exercise & training — extreme needs

- Require **hours of vigorous exercise and intensive mental work** every day.
- Exceptionally trainable and driven; they excel at protection sports, agility, scent work and obedience.
- **Not a casual pet** — they need an experienced, very active handler.

## Grooming & care

- Easy — the short coat needs only weekly brushing.

## Health & lifespan

Generally healthy and athletic. Watch for hip and elbow dysplasia and eye conditions. Choose health-tested lines and keep up regular vet care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Belgian Malinois good family pets?**
Only for very active, experienced owners who can meet their intense needs; they're working dogs at heart.

**Do they need a job?**
Absolutely — without serious physical and mental work they become difficult.

## Conclusion

The Belgian Malinois is a brilliant, intense and tireless working dog for a dedicated, experienced handler. Give them a real job, abundant exercise and training, and you'll have an unmatched canine partner.
