Unique and endearing, the Chinese Crested comes in two varieties — the (mostly) hairless and the fully-coated "Powderpuff." Affectionate and devoted, this little companion bonds intensely with its people.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Tiny (3.5–5.5 kg) |
| Lifespan | 13–18 years |
| Coat | Hairless (with tufts) or full "Powderpuff" |
| Energy | Moderate |
| Good with kids | Best with gentle, older children |

## Appearance & temperament

The Hairless variety has smooth skin with tufts of hair on the head, feet and tail; the Powderpuff has a full soft coat. Both are affectionate, playful and devoted "velcro" companions that crave closeness.

## Skin & coat care

- **Hairless dogs need skin care:** gentle washing, moisturising, and **sun protection** (they can sunburn).
- They feel the cold — provide warmth and clothing.
- Powderpuffs need regular brushing to prevent mats.
- Both are prone to dental issues — diligent dental care is important.

## Exercise & training

- Daily walks and play are plenty.
- Sensitive and clever; gentle, positive training works best.

## Health & lifespan

Long-lived. Watch for dental disease (especially in hairless), skin issues, luxating patella, and eye conditions. Regular vet and dental care keep them healthy.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Chinese Crested dogs get sunburned?**
Yes — hairless dogs need sunscreen or shade and protection from cold too.

**Are they hypoallergenic?**
No dog truly is, but the hairless variety sheds little; they still produce allergens.

## Conclusion

The Chinese Crested is an affectionate, devoted and distinctive companion with special skin-care needs. Protect their skin from sun and cold, keep up dental care, and you'll have a loving little friend for many years.
