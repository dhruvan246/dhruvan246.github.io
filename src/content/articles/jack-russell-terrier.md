Small in size but enormous in energy, the Jack Russell Terrier is a fearless, intelligent working dog. Bred to hunt foxes, this little terrier needs an active owner who can keep up with its tireless drive.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Small (5–8 kg) |
| Lifespan | 13–16 years |
| Coat | Smooth, rough or broken |
| Energy | Very high |
| Good with kids | Yes, with active, older children |

## Appearance & temperament

Jack Russells are compact, athletic and mostly white with tan or black markings. They are bold, clever and confident — and they need a job to do. Without enough activity, they get bored and mischievous.

## Diet & nutrition

- Quality food matched to their high energy.
- Measured portions to keep them lean and athletic.

## Exercise & training — the key to a happy Jack Russell

- They need **plenty of daily exercise** plus mental challenges — long walks, fetch, training games, even dog sports.
- Highly intelligent but independent and strong-willed; consistent, positive training is essential.
- A bored, under-exercised Jack Russell will dig, bark and escape.

## Grooming

- Smooth and rough coats both need only weekly brushing (they shed).
- Routine nail trims, ear checks and dental care.

## Health & lifespan

Generally robust and long-lived. Watch for luxating patella, eye conditions, and deafness in some lines. Their fearlessness can lead to injuries — supervise around larger animals.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Jack Russells good for first-time owners?**
Not the easiest — their energy and stubbornness suit active, experienced owners best.

**Do they get along with other pets?**
They have a strong prey drive, so care is needed around small animals.

## Conclusion

The Jack Russell Terrier is a brilliant, energetic companion for an active household. Give them plenty of exercise, training and mental stimulation, and they'll reward you with years of fun and loyalty.
