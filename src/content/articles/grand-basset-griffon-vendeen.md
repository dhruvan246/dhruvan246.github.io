The Grand Basset Griffon Vendeen is a long, low French scenthound bred for hunting rabbit and hare over the rough terrain of the Vendee region. The largest of the four Vendeen griffon breeds, the "GBGV" pairs a shaggy, weatherproof coat with an alert, outgoing personality that has won it a devoted following as both a hunter and a family companion.

## Appearance & temperament

The GBGV is noticeably longer than it is tall, with a deep chest, straight legs and a proud, slightly tousled look. Its rough outer coat sits over a thick undercoat and is accented by the bushy eyebrows, beard and mustache typical of the Vendeen griffons. Colors are usually a base of white combined with markings in lemon, orange, black, sable or tricolor.

In temperament the breed is happy, confident and independent. As a pack hound it tends to be sociable with people and other dogs, and it is known for an enthusiastic, sometimes vocal personality. That independence and strong nose mean a GBGV can be willful and easily distracted by interesting scents.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food appropriate to the dog's age, size and activity level. Active hunting or sporting dogs may need more calories than companion dogs, so adjust portions to keep your GBGV in lean body condition with a visible waist. Because the breed enjoys food and can be food-motivated, measure meals and account for training treats to prevent unwanted weight gain. Always provide fresh water, and ask your veterinarian for guidance on amounts and any life-stage changes.

## Exercise & training

This is an energetic working breed that thrives on substantial daily exercise: long walks, hikes, scent games and secure off-lead running all suit it well. A bored or under-exercised GBGV may become noisy or dig. Because its hunting drive and nose are powerful, keep the dog on lead or in a securely fenced area, as it may follow a scent and ignore recall. Training works best with patient, positive, reward-based methods and short, engaging sessions; consistency and early socialization help channel the breed's independent streak.

## Grooming & care

The harsh double coat is fairly low-maintenance but not no-maintenance. Brush weekly to remove dead hair, prevent matting and keep the coat tidy; the breed is not heavily trimmed and should keep its natural rough texture. Check and clean the long, low-set ears regularly, since drop ears can trap moisture and debris. Routine nail trims, dental care and skin checks after time outdoors round out basic grooming.

## Health & lifespan

The GBGV is generally a hardy, healthy breed with a typical lifespan of about 12 to 14 years. As with many long-eared hounds, owners should watch for ear infections, and like many breeds it can be affected by issues such as hip or joint problems and eye conditions. Buying from a responsible breeder who screens breeding stock, keeping the dog at a healthy weight, and scheduling regular veterinary checkups all support a long, comfortable life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Grand Basset Griffon Vendeen a good family dog?** Yes. It is friendly, sociable and generally good with children and other dogs when properly socialized, though its energy and voice mean it suits an active household.

**Does the GBGV need a lot of exercise?** Yes. It was bred for stamina and needs vigorous daily activity and mental stimulation; a securely contained space is important given its strong hunting drive.

## Conclusion

The Grand Basset Griffon Vendeen is a cheerful, hard-working scenthound that rewards owners willing to meet its needs for exercise, secure containment and patient training. With weekly coat care, attention to its ears and a healthy lifestyle, this characterful French hound can be a fun, affectionate companion for an active home.
