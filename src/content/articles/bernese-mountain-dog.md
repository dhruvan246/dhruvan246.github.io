Big, beautiful and wonderfully gentle, the Bernese Mountain Dog is a Swiss farm breed turned beloved family companion. With their striking tricolour coat and affectionate nature, "Berners" are devoted, easy-going giants.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Large (35–55 kg) |
| Lifespan | 7–10 years |
| Coat | Thick, long, tricolour |
| Energy | Moderate |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Berners have a gorgeous black, white and rust coat and a sturdy, powerful build. They are calm, affectionate and devoted — gentle with children and happiest when included in family life. They bond deeply and dislike being left alone.

## Diet & nutrition

- A quality large-breed diet; large-breed puppy food supports healthy growth.
- Feed measured meals and **take bloat precautions** (don't exercise right after eating).

## Exercise

- Moderate daily exercise — walks and play; they enjoy cooler weather.
- Avoid high-impact activity in puppies to protect growing joints.

## Grooming

- **Brush 2–3 times a week** — that thick coat mats and sheds heavily, especially seasonally.
- Routine nail trims, ear checks and dental care.

## Health & lifespan

Sadly, Berners have a shorter lifespan and notable health risks: certain **cancers** (relatively common in the breed), hip and elbow dysplasia, and bloat. Choose a reputable, health-testing breeder and keep up regular vet care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Bernese Mountain Dogs shed a lot?**
Yes — heavily. Regular brushing is essential.

**Are they good family dogs?**
Wonderful — gentle, affectionate and great with children.

## Conclusion

The Bernese Mountain Dog is a loving, gentle giant that thrives as part of the family. Keep up the grooming, take bloat precautions, choose a responsible breeder, and treasure your time with these devoted companions.
