The American Hairless Terrier is a curious, energetic small dog that descends from the Rat Terrier. It is unusual among terriers because it comes in both a truly hairless variety and a short, smooth coated variety, making it a favorite among people who want a lively companion with minimal shedding.

## Overview

| Trait | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Small (about 7-16 lb; 12-16 in tall) |
| Lifespan | 14-16 years |
| Coat | Hairless or short, smooth coated |
| Energy | Moderate to high |
| Good with kids | Yes, with supervision |

## Appearance & temperament

The American Hairless Terrier has a compact, muscular body, a deep chest, and large, erect or button ears. The hairless variety is born with a soft downy fuzz that is shed within a few weeks, leaving warm, smooth skin that may be solid or spotted. The coated variety carries a dense, glossy short coat. Both share an alert, expressive face.

Temperament-wise, these dogs are smart, playful, and affectionate. They bond closely with their families and are often described as inquisitive and a little mischievous. As true terriers, they can be vocal and tenacious, but they generally lack the strong prey-driven digging instinct of some hunting terriers because the hairless variety was not bred to go to ground.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a high-quality dog food formulated for small breeds and your dog's life stage. Because they are small and active, portion control matters; measure meals rather than free-feeding to prevent excess weight, which strains small joints. Most adults do well on two measured meals per day. Always provide fresh water, and consult your veterinarian before adding supplements or changing diets.

## Exercise & training

American Hairless Terriers are spirited and need daily activity to stay content. A couple of brisk walks plus active play or fetch usually satisfies their needs. They enjoy dog sports such as agility, rally, and earthdog-style games that channel their intelligence.

Training should be positive, consistent, and reward-based. These terriers are quick learners but can be independent, so keep sessions short and engaging. Early socialization helps them grow into confident, well-mannered adults.

## Grooming & care

The hairless variety needs little brushing but requires special skin care. Bathe occasionally with a gentle shampoo and keep the skin moisturized to prevent dryness. Because they lack a protective coat, they are sensitive to sunburn, cold, and scrapes; apply pet-safe sunscreen on bright days and use a sweater in cold weather. The coated variety needs only routine weekly brushing. For both, trim nails, brush teeth regularly, and check ears to keep them clean and dry.

## Health & lifespan

The American Hairless Terrier is generally a healthy, long-lived breed, often reaching 14 to 16 years. Responsible breeders screen for conditions that can occur in the breed, including luxating patella, certain heart concerns, and eye issues. Skin care is the most breed-specific concern for the hairless variety, since cuts, rashes, and sun exposure require attention. Routine veterinary checkups, dental care, and weight management support a long, healthy life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are American Hairless Terriers hypoallergenic?**
No dog is truly hypoallergenic, but the hairless variety sheds essentially no hair and produces little dander, which some allergy sufferers tolerate better. Spending time with the breed before adopting is wise.

**Do they need protection from the weather?**
Yes. The hairless variety has no coat for insulation, so they can sunburn easily and feel the cold. Sunscreen, shade, and warm clothing in winter help keep them comfortable and safe.

## Conclusion

The American Hairless Terrier is a smart, affectionate, and energetic small companion that suits active households, including some allergy-conscious owners. With attentive skin care, regular exercise, and positive training, this distinctive terrier makes a devoted and long-lived family pet.
