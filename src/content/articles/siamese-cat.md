Striking, intelligent and famously chatty, the Siamese is one of the oldest and most recognisable cat breeds. With their blue eyes and colour-point coats, they're as devoted as they are vocal.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (3–5 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–20 years |
| Coat | Short, fine, colour-pointed |
| Energy | High |
| Good with kids | Yes — sociable and playful |

## Appearance & temperament

Siamese cats are sleek and elegant with striking blue eyes and dark "points" on the ears, face, paws and tail. They are extremely social, intelligent and **very vocal** — they'll hold conversations with you and crave constant interaction.

## Diet & nutrition

- Quality cat food in measured portions; they're active and rarely overweight.

## Enrichment & care

- **They hate being bored or alone** — provide lots of play, puzzle toys and climbing space.
- Many do best with a feline companion for company.
- Easy grooming: a weekly brush is plenty for the short coat.

## Health & lifespan

Often long-lived. Watch for dental disease, respiratory issues, and certain genetic conditions (including some eye and heart problems). Choose a reputable breeder and keep up vet care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Siamese cats loud?**
Yes — famously talkative, with a loud, distinctive voice. They love to "chat."

**Can they be left alone?**
Not happily — they're social and prone to loneliness. Company or a companion cat helps.

## Conclusion

The Siamese is an intelligent, affectionate and wonderfully vocal companion for someone who wants an interactive, people-focused cat. Give them attention, play and company, and they'll be devoted for life.
