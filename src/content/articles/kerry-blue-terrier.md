The Kerry Blue Terrier is a spirited, versatile working terrier from County Kerry in Ireland, famous for its soft, wavy blue-grey coat and bold, affectionate personality. Once an all-purpose farm dog used for hunting vermin, herding, and guarding, it remains a confident and devoted companion today.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Size | Medium (18–19.5 in tall; 33–40 lb) |
| Lifespan | 12–15 years |
| Coat | Soft, dense, wavy; non-shedding |
| Energy | High |
| Good with kids | Yes, with supervision |

## Appearance & temperament

The Kerry Blue is a sturdy, well-proportioned terrier with a long head, small dark eyes, and a distinctive soft coat that does not shed. Puppies are born black and gradually "clear" to shades of blue-grey, usually completing the change by around 18 months to two years.

Temperamentally, the breed is alert, intelligent, and full of self-assurance. Kerries are deeply loyal and affectionate with their families and make watchful, capable guardians. Like most terriers, they can be strong-willed and may be assertive toward unfamiliar dogs, so early socialization is important. With people they tend to be loving, playful, and good-natured.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a high-quality complete dog food appropriate to your Kerry's age, size, and activity level. As an active medium breed, most do well on two measured meals a day rather than free-feeding, which helps prevent weight gain. Choose a formula with quality protein to support their muscular, athletic build.

Monitor body condition regularly; you should be able to feel the ribs without pressing hard. Adjust portions for activity, and limit treats to a small fraction of daily calories. Always provide fresh water, and consult your veterinarian before making significant dietary changes.

## Exercise & training

Kerry Blues are energetic, athletic dogs that need daily exercise to stay happy and well-behaved. Aim for at least an hour of activity through brisk walks, play sessions, and opportunities to run safely. They excel at dog sports such as agility, obedience, and earthdog trials, which also engage their sharp minds.

Training should be consistent, positive, and engaging. These intelligent terriers learn quickly but can be independent and stubborn, so reward-based methods work far better than harsh corrections. Early socialization with people and other animals helps temper their natural terrier feistiness.

## Grooming & care

The non-shedding coat is one of the breed's signature features, but it requires real commitment. Brush or comb several times a week to prevent matting, and plan on professional grooming or hand-tidying roughly every four to six weeks to maintain the classic outline.

Routine care includes regular nail trims, dental brushing, and checking the ears, since the hair-covered drop ears can trap moisture. Because the coat keeps growing rather than dropping out, neglecting it leads quickly to tangles, so build grooming into a steady routine.

## Health & lifespan

The Kerry Blue Terrier is generally a hardy breed with a typical lifespan of about 12 to 15 years. As with many breeds, certain conditions can occur, including eye issues, hip dysplasia, skin cysts, and occasionally hereditary disorders affecting the nervous system. Responsible breeders screen their stock to reduce these risks.

Keep your Kerry healthy with routine veterinary checkups, vaccinations, parasite prevention, dental care, and weight management. Early attention to any changes in behavior, appetite, skin, or movement gives the best chance of catching problems early.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Kerry Blue Terriers shed?**
No, the Kerry Blue is considered a low-shedding breed, which is part of its appeal. However, the coat grows continuously and needs frequent brushing plus regular professional grooming to stay healthy and mat-free.

**Are Kerry Blue Terriers good family dogs?**
Yes, they are loyal, affectionate, and protective companions that bond closely with their families. With early socialization and supervision around young children and other pets, they make devoted and lively household dogs.

## Conclusion

The Kerry Blue Terrier rewards an active, committed owner with loyalty, courage, and plenty of personality. If you can meet its exercise and grooming needs and provide consistent, positive training, this striking Irish terrier makes a devoted and entertaining lifelong companion.
