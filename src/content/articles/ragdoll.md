Named for their tendency to go limp and relaxed when picked up, Ragdolls are among the most docile and affectionate of all cat breeds. Big, beautiful and devoted, they're the ultimate gentle companion.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Large (4.5–9 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–17 years |
| Coat | Semi-long, silky, colour-pointed |
| Energy | Low to moderate |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Ragdolls have striking blue eyes, a silky colour-pointed coat and a large, sturdy body. They are exceptionally gentle, calm and people-oriented — following you around, greeting you at the door and happily flopping into your arms.

## Diet & nutrition

- A quality diet portioned for their large frame; they mature slowly over 3–4 years.
- Watch weight, as they're laid-back.

## Grooming & care

- Brush 2–3 times a week; the silky coat mats less than a Persian's but still needs regular care.
- Keep them indoors — their trusting, docile nature makes them vulnerable outside.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for **hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM)** — a known concern in the breed — plus bladder stones and obesity. Choose breeders who screen for HCM and keep up regular vet care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Ragdolls good lap cats?**
Famously so — they're among the most affectionate, cuddly breeds.

**Should Ragdolls go outside?**
Best kept indoors; their trusting nature puts them at risk outdoors.

## Conclusion

The Ragdoll is a serene, loving and beautiful companion perfect for those wanting an affectionate lap cat. Brush regularly, keep them indoors and lean, watch their heart, and enjoy this gentle, devoted breed.
