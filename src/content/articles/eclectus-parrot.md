The Eclectus Parrot is a striking medium-to-large parrot famous for its dramatic sexual dimorphism: males are emerald green while females are red and purple. Calm and intelligent, they make rewarding companions for owners willing to meet their fairly specialized dietary and social needs.

## Overview

| Trait | Typical range |
|-------|---------------|
| Size | Medium-large, around 30-38 cm (12-15 in) long |
| Lifespan | Commonly 30+ years with good care |
| Noise level | Moderate; can be loud at dawn and dusk |
| Talking ability | Excellent; often clear, expressive speech |
| Care level | Intermediate to advanced |

## Appearance & temperament

Eclectus Parrots are best known for looking like two different species. Males are vivid green with candy-corn orange beaks, while females are rich red with deep blue or purple chests and black beaks. Their feathers have a distinctive hair-like, almost fur-like quality.

Temperamentally they are often described as laid-back, thoughtful, and observant rather than hyperactive. Many bond closely with their people and enjoy gentle interaction, though individuals vary. They tend to be sensitive birds that respond poorly to harsh handling or chaotic households.

## Housing & cage setup

Provide the largest cage you can, with bar spacing appropriate for a medium parrot so they cannot get their head stuck. Width matters more than height because these birds move horizontally and stretch their wings. Include several perches of varying diameter and natural materials to keep feet healthy.

Place the cage in a draft-free area where the bird is part of family life but can also retreat. Daily out-of-cage time in a safe, supervised, bird-proofed room is essential for exercise and mental stimulation.

## Diet & nutrition

Diet is the single most important Eclectus care topic. They have an unusually long digestive tract and thrive on a high-fiber, relatively low-fat diet rich in fresh fruits and vegetables. Offer plenty of leafy greens, vegetables, and a moderate amount of fruit daily.

Many keepers limit or avoid heavily fortified, artificially colored pellets, as Eclectus are thought to be sensitive to excess synthetic vitamins and additives; discuss the right pellet ratio with an avian vet. Provide clean water daily and offer a varied, colorful plate. Avoid avocado, chocolate, caffeine, alcohol, and excessively salty or fatty foods, which are toxic or harmful to parrots.

## Enrichment & handling

These intelligent birds need daily mental and physical enrichment. Rotate foraging toys, shreddable items, and puzzle feeders, and teach simple tricks or step-up training using positive reinforcement. Consistent, calm handling builds trust.

Because they are sensitive, avoid sudden changes and loud confrontation. Boredom or stress can lead to feather-destructive behavior, so a predictable routine and regular interaction matter greatly.

## Health & lifespan

With proper care, Eclectus Parrots commonly live 30 years or more. Establish care with an avian veterinarian and schedule routine wellness checks. Watch for signs of illness such as fluffed posture, appetite loss, or changes in droppings, and seek prompt veterinary attention.

Diet-related issues, vitamin imbalances, and feather plucking are among the more common concerns. As exotic birds, their legality and import rules vary by region, and they benefit from specialist avian care, so research local regulations before acquiring one.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are male and female Eclectus really different colors?** Yes. Males are mainly green and females are red and purple. This is genuine sexual dimorphism, not separate species.

**Do Eclectus Parrots talk well?** Many are excellent talkers with surprisingly clear voices, though talking ability varies by individual and the time spent interacting with them.

## Conclusion

The Eclectus Parrot rewards dedicated owners with beauty, intelligence, and impressive speech. If you can provide a spacious cage, a fresh whole-food diet, daily enrichment, and specialist avian veterinary care, this calm and charismatic parrot can be a decades-long companion.
