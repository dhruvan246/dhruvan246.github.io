One of the most versatile sporting breeds, the German Shorthaired Pointer (GSP) is an athletic, intelligent and affectionate gundog. Tireless on the field and loving at home, the GSP thrives with an active family.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Large (20–32 kg) |
| Lifespan | 10–14 years |
| Coat | Short, dense |
| Energy | Very high |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

GSPs are lean and powerful with a short liver-and-white coat. They are friendly, eager and energetic — devoted to their family and happiest when active. They bond closely and dislike being left alone for long.

## Exercise & training

- Need **a great deal of daily exercise** — running, swimming, fetch, dog sports.
- Smart and trainable; respond well to positive, consistent training. Bored GSPs become destructive.

## Grooming & care

- Easy — the short coat needs only weekly brushing.

## Health & lifespan

Generally healthy and athletic. Watch for hip dysplasia, bloat (feed smaller meals), ear infections and certain eye conditions. Choose health-tested lines.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are GSPs good family dogs?**
Yes — for active families who can meet their substantial exercise needs.

**Can they be left alone?**
Not for long — they crave activity and company and may develop anxiety.

## Conclusion

The German Shorthaired Pointer is an athletic, intelligent and affectionate companion for an active household. Meet their exercise needs and they'll be a versatile, devoted family friend.
