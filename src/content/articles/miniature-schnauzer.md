With their distinctive beard, bushy eyebrows and bright, alert expression, Miniature Schnauzers are smart, spirited little dogs. Bred as farm ratters, they're energetic, loyal and endlessly entertaining.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Small (5–9 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–15 years |
| Coat | Wiry double coat (low-shedding) |
| Energy | Moderate to high |
| Good with kids | Yes |

## Appearance & temperament

Miniature Schnauzers have a wiry coat (often salt-and-pepper) and the breed's iconic beard and eyebrows. They are intelligent, alert and affectionate, with a bold, watchful streak that makes them good little watchdogs.

## Diet & nutrition

- Quality food in measured portions.
- **Watch fat intake** — the breed is prone to high blood fats (hyperlipidaemia) and pancreatitis. Avoid fatty treats.

## Grooming

- Brush 2–3 times a week to prevent matting, especially in the beard and legs.
- The coat needs regular clipping or hand-stripping every few months.
- Keep the beard clean after meals.

## Exercise & training

- Daily walks and play; they're lively and enjoy games.
- Very trainable but can be vocal — early training curbs excessive barking.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for pancreatitis, high blood fats, bladder stones, eye conditions and diabetes. A low-fat diet and regular vet checks help keep them healthy.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Miniature Schnauzers shed?**
Very little — a popular choice for shedding-sensitive homes, but they need regular grooming.

**Are they good family dogs?**
Yes — loyal, playful and good with respectful children.

## Conclusion

The Miniature Schnauzer is a clever, charming companion with bags of personality. Mind their diet (low fat), keep up the grooming, and you'll have a devoted and lively friend.
