Originating on the Isle of Man, the Manx is famous for being tailless — the result of a natural genetic mutation. Behind that unusual silhouette is a friendly, playful and intelligent cat often described as dog-like.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (3.5–5.5 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–16 years |
| Coat | Short or long, dense double coat |
| Energy | Moderate to high |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Manx cats have a rounded body, hind legs longer than the front, and tails ranging from completely absent ("rumpy") to a short stub. They are playful, affectionate and clever — many enjoy fetch, follow their owners around, and are known for being good "watch-cats."

## Diet & nutrition

- A quality diet in measured portions; watch weight to protect their back end.

## Grooming & care

- Brush weekly (more for longhaired Manx, sometimes called "Cymric").
- Provide play and climbing; they're active and engaging.

## Health & lifespan — important

The taillessness gene can cause **"Manx syndrome"** — spinal and nerve defects affecting the bladder, bowel and hind legs, usually appearing early in life. Responsible breeders manage this carefully. Choose a reputable breeder and have kittens vet-checked.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are all Manx cats completely tailless?**
No — they range from fully tailless to having a short stub or near-normal tail.

**Are Manx cats healthy?**
Most are, but the tailless gene carries risks (Manx syndrome), so responsible breeding and vet checks matter.

## Conclusion

The Manx is a playful, friendly and distinctive companion with a dog-like charm. Choose a responsible breeder, watch for breed-specific health issues, and you'll enjoy this clever, affectionate island cat.
