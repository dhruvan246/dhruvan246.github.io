The Shichon is a small, affectionate designer dog created by crossing the Bichon Frise with the Shih Tzu. Also known as the Zuchon or Teddy Bear dog, this hybrid was developed primarily in the United States as a gentle, family-friendly companion. With a soft, rounded face and a plush coat, the Shichon has become a popular choice for households wanting an easygoing lap dog.

## Appearance & temperament

Shichons are small, sturdy dogs that typically weigh between 9 and 15 pounds (4-7 kg). They have a round, expressive face, dark eyes, and a fluffy double coat that comes in a wide range of colors including white, cream, apricot, black, gray, and various combinations. Their teddy-bear appearance is one of their biggest draws.

Temperamentally, the Shichon is gentle, outgoing, and people-oriented. They thrive on companionship and tend to bond closely with their families, getting along well with children, seniors, and other pets. Because they dislike being left alone for long periods, they can be prone to separation anxiety. They are generally not aggressive and make poor guard dogs, though they may alert-bark at visitors.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed your Shichon a high-quality dog food formulated for small breeds, which provides the calorie density and small kibble size suited to little mouths. Most adult Shichons do well on roughly 1/2 to 1 cup of food per day, split into two meals, though the exact amount depends on weight, age, and activity level.

Because small breeds can be prone to weight gain, measure portions and limit treats to about 10 percent of daily calories. Always provide fresh water, and consult your veterinarian before making significant dietary changes.

## Exercise & training

Despite their small size, Shichons benefit from daily activity to stay healthy and content. A couple of short walks plus some indoor play usually meets their needs. They adapt well to apartment living and do not require a large yard.

Shichons are intelligent and eager to please, which makes them fairly easy to train. Use positive reinforcement with treats and praise, and keep sessions short and upbeat. Early socialization and consistent, gentle housetraining are important, as small breeds can sometimes be slower to house-train. Avoid harsh corrections, which can make these sensitive dogs anxious.

## Grooming & care

The Shichon's coat is low-shedding but requires regular maintenance to prevent mats and tangles. Brush several times a week, or daily if the coat is kept long. Many owners opt for a professional grooming trim every four to six weeks to keep the coat manageable.

Routine care also includes regular nail trims, ear cleaning to reduce the risk of infection, and frequent tooth brushing, as small breeds are especially prone to dental disease. Wipe the area around the eyes to manage tear staining.

## Health & lifespan

Shichons are generally healthy and often live 12 to 15 years. As a hybrid, they can inherit conditions seen in either parent breed, including patellar luxation (slipping kneecaps), dental problems, ear infections, allergies, and eye conditions such as cataracts. Their small size can also make them sensitive to extreme temperatures.

Choosing a responsible breeder who health-tests parent dogs, plus regular veterinary checkups, supports a long and healthy life. Maintaining a healthy weight reduces strain on joints and the heart.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Shichons hypoallergenic?**
No dog is truly hypoallergenic, but Shichons are low-shedding and often tolerated better by people with mild allergies. Regular grooming helps reduce dander.

**Do Shichons bark a lot?**
They are not excessive barkers, but they may bark to alert you to visitors or out of boredom. Adequate companionship, exercise, and training keep barking in check.

## Conclusion

The Shichon is a loving, adaptable little companion that suits families, singles, and seniors alike. With modest exercise needs, a friendly nature, and a low-shedding coat, this teddy-bear hybrid rewards owners who provide consistent grooming, training, and plenty of togetherness.
