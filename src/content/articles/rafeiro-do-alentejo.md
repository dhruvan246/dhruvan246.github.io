The Rafeiro do Alentejo is a large, powerful livestock guardian breed from the Alentejo region of southern Portugal. Traditionally used to protect flocks and farmsteads from wolves and intruders, this mastiff-type dog is calm, watchful, and deeply devoted to its family.

## Appearance & temperament

The Rafeiro do Alentejo is a substantial, bear-like dog with a broad head, a deep chest, and a solid, slightly rectangular body. Males typically stand around 66–74 cm and females a little smaller, with weights commonly between 35 and 50 kg. The coat is short to medium in length, thick, and comes in colors such as black, fawn, wolf-grey, and brindle, usually with white markings.

In temperament, the breed is calm, serious, and naturally suspicious of strangers. Bred to work independently while guarding livestock, the Rafeiro is confident and territorial, becoming more alert and active at night. With its own family it is loyal, affectionate, and gentle, but it needs firm, consistent leadership and thorough socialization to remain well-mannered around visitors and other animals.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a high-quality diet formulated for large or giant breeds, with controlled calories to prevent excess weight that can stress the joints. Because the Rafeiro is a slow-maturing large dog, large-breed puppy food with balanced calcium and phosphorus supports healthy bone growth during the first one to two years.

Adults usually do well on two measured meals per day rather than free-feeding. As a deep-chested breed, the Rafeiro may face a higher risk of bloat, so avoid heavy exercise immediately before and after eating. Always provide fresh water, and adjust portions based on age, activity level, and body condition with guidance from your veterinarian.

## Exercise & training

Despite its guardian heritage, the Rafeiro do Alentejo has fairly moderate energy needs. Daily walks plus access to a secure, spacious yard generally keep it content. It is not built for intense, prolonged athletic activity, and overexertion should be avoided in young, growing dogs.

Training requires patience and consistency. This is an independent thinker that responds best to calm, reward-based methods and a confident handler. Early, ongoing socialization with people, children, and other dogs is essential to temper its protective instincts. Secure fencing is a must, as the breed takes territorial boundaries seriously.

## Grooming & care

The dense double coat is low-maintenance but does shed, with heavier seasonal shedding in spring and autumn. Weekly brushing keeps the coat healthy and reduces loose hair, increasing to several times a week during heavy sheds. Bathe only as needed, since the coat naturally repels dirt.

Routine care includes checking and cleaning the ears, trimming nails regularly, and brushing the teeth to support dental health. Given the breed's size, it is wise to introduce handling and grooming routines while the dog is young.

## Health & lifespan

The Rafeiro do Alentejo is generally a hardy, robust breed with a typical lifespan of around 12 to 14 years. As with most large breeds, the main health concerns relate to the joints and digestion. Hip and elbow dysplasia can occur, and the deep chest carries a risk of gastric bloat (gastric dilatation-volvulus).

Maintaining a healthy weight, providing appropriate exercise, and scheduling regular veterinary checkups all help protect long-term health. Buying from breeders who screen for joint conditions further reduces the risk of inherited problems.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Rafeiro do Alentejo a good family dog?**
Yes, for the right home. It is devoted and protective of its family and can be gentle with children it knows. However, its size, guarding instincts, and need for socialization make it best suited to experienced owners with space.

**Does the Rafeiro do Alentejo bark a lot?**
As a guardian breed, it is alert and will bark to warn of anything unusual, especially at night. Proper training, socialization, and clear boundaries help keep barking reasonable rather than excessive.

## Conclusion

The Rafeiro do Alentejo is a calm, loyal, and powerful guardian best suited to owners who can provide space, leadership, and consistent socialization. In return, it offers steadfast devotion and a watchful, protective presence that has served Portuguese shepherds for generations.
