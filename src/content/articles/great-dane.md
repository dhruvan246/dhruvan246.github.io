Towering yet tender, the Great Dane is the quintessential "gentle giant." One of the tallest dog breeds in the world, the Dane is surprisingly calm, affectionate and great with families — a sweet soul in an enormous body.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Giant (45–90 kg) |
| Lifespan | 7–10 years |
| Coat | Short, smooth |
| Energy | Low to moderate |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Great Danes are immense, elegant dogs with a short coat in several colours. Despite their size, they are gentle, friendly and patient — often described as big lap dogs who long to be near their people.

## Diet & nutrition

- A quality **large/giant-breed diet** is important, especially for puppies, to support slow, healthy bone growth.
- Feed measured meals; **feed from a raised position and avoid exercise right after eating** to reduce bloat risk.

## Exercise

- Moderate daily walks; avoid high-impact exercise in puppies to protect developing joints.
- They're calmer indoors than you'd expect — but need space simply to move around.

## Grooming & care

- Easy — weekly brushing.
- Provide large, supportive bedding to protect their joints and elbows.

## Health & lifespan

Giant breeds have shorter lifespans and specific risks: **bloat (GDV)** — a life-threatening emergency — plus heart conditions (DCM), hip dysplasia and joint issues. Learn the signs of bloat, feed carefully, and choose health-tested lines.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Great Danes need a big house?**
They need room to move, but they're calm indoors. Space and sturdy furniture help.

**Are they good with children?**
Yes — gentle and patient, though their size means supervision with small kids.

## Conclusion

The Great Dane is a loving, gentle companion that bonds closely with its family. Feed carefully to prevent bloat, protect those joints, and cherish every moment with these wonderful giants.
