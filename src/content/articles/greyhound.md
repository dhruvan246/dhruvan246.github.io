The fastest dog breed on earth, the Greyhound can hit 45 mph — yet at home it's one of the calmest, gentlest "couch potatoes" you'll ever meet. Sweet and quiet, Greyhounds (including retired racers) make surprisingly easy companions.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Large (27–40 kg) |
| Lifespan | 10–14 years |
| Coat | Short, fine |
| Energy | Moderate (sprinter, not stamina) |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Greyhounds are tall, slender and aerodynamic with a short coat. They are gentle, calm and affectionate — content to lounge for hours, then enjoy a short burst of running. Many retired racing Greyhounds make wonderful, grateful pets.

## Exercise & care

- A couple of walks plus a chance to **sprint in a safe, enclosed area** — they're sprinters, not endurance runners.
- **Strong prey drive** — keep them leashed or securely fenced; recall is unreliable.
- They feel the cold (thin coat, low fat) — provide coats and soft, padded bedding.

## Grooming & care

- Very easy — a quick weekly brush.

## Health & lifespan

Generally healthy. Be cautious with anaesthesia (sighthound sensitivity — use a knowledgeable vet), watch for bloat, dental disease, and their thin skin (prone to cuts). Otherwise a robust breed.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Greyhounds hyperactive?**
No — they're famously lazy at home, happy to sleep most of the day.

**Are retired racing Greyhounds good pets?**
Yes — many adapt beautifully to home life and make gentle, loving companions.

## Conclusion

The Greyhound is a gentle, calm and affectionate companion that combines astonishing speed with a love of lounging. Give them a daily run, warmth and a soft bed, and you'll have a serene, devoted friend.
