One of the oldest known breeds, the Abyssinian is a lithe, athletic and endlessly curious cat. With a warm ticked coat and boundless energy, the "Aby" is an active, people-loving explorer.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (3–5 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–16 years |
| Coat | Short, fine, ticked |
| Energy | Very high |
| Good with kids | Yes — playful and sociable |

## Appearance & temperament

Abyssinians are slender and graceful with a distinctive "ticked" coat (each hair banded with colour). They are intelligent, inquisitive and highly active — climbing, investigating and staying involved in everything you do.

## Enrichment & exercise

- They **need lots of stimulation** — tall cat trees, puzzle toys and daily interactive play.
- Bright and busy, they don't like being idle or alone for long.

## Diet & nutrition

- A quality, high-protein diet supports their active lifestyle.

## Grooming & care

- Easy — the short coat needs only occasional brushing.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for dental disease (gingivitis), patellar luxation, certain eye conditions (PRA), and a kidney condition (renal amyloidosis) in some lines. Choose a reputable breeder and keep up vet care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Abyssinians cuddly?**
They're affectionate but active — more "involved companion" than lap cat.

**Do they get bored?**
Easily — they need plenty of play and enrichment to stay happy.

## Conclusion

The Abyssinian is a lively, intelligent and engaging companion for an owner who enjoys an interactive cat. Provide plenty of climbing, play and attention, and this curious explorer will thrive.
