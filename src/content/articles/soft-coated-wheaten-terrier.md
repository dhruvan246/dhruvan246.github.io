The Soft Coated Wheaten Terrier is a friendly, energetic Irish farm dog known for its silky, wheaten-colored coat and famously happy "Wheaten greetin'." Bred as an all-purpose working terrier, it remains spirited, affectionate, and devoted to its family.

## Overview

| Trait | Details |
| --- | --- |

| Lifespan | 12–14 years |

## Appearance & temperament

Wheatens are square, sturdy terriers standing roughly 17–19 inches at the shoulder and weighing about 30–40 pounds, with males slightly larger than females. Their hallmark is a soft, single coat that ranges from pale beige to shimmering gold and falls in loose waves. Unlike many terriers, they have no harsh outer coat and shed very little.

Temperamentally, Wheatens are upbeat, confident, and people-oriented. They tend to be less scrappy than some terriers, greeting strangers and other dogs with enthusiasm. They are intelligent and playful but retain a typical terrier streak of independence, so early socialization helps channel their exuberance.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, high-quality diet formulated for the dog's life stage and activity level. Most adult Wheatens do well on measured meals twice a day rather than free-feeding, which helps maintain a trim, athletic build. Puppies need a growth-appropriate formula fed in smaller, frequent portions.

Because the breed is predisposed to certain protein-losing conditions, watch for any changes in appetite, weight, or digestion and consult your veterinarian about appropriate diets. Provide constant access to fresh water and keep treats to about ten percent of daily calories.

## Exercise & training

Wheatens are active dogs that need 30–60 minutes of daily exercise, including brisk walks, play sessions, and chances to run safely. A securely fenced yard suits their love of dashing about, but they also adapt to active apartment life if their needs are met. Mental stimulation through games and puzzle toys is just as important as physical activity.

Training should be consistent, positive, and reward-based. Wheatens are smart but can be stubborn, so short, upbeat sessions work best. Early socialization and basic obedience prevent jumping up on people, a common quirk of this enthusiastic greeter.

## Grooming & care

The Wheaten's beautiful coat requires real commitment. Brush and comb thoroughly several times a week, ideally daily, to prevent mats and tangles that form easily in the soft hair. Many owners schedule professional grooming every six to eight weeks to keep the coat trimmed and tidy.

Routine care also includes regular nail trims, ear cleaning, and dental hygiene. Check the hair around the eyes, paws, and rear, trimming as needed to keep these areas clean and comfortable.

## Health & lifespan

With good care, Wheatens typically live 12–14 years. The breed is generally healthy but has some recognized concerns, notably protein-losing nephropathy and protein-losing enteropathy, as well as renal dysplasia, Addison's disease, and inflammatory bowel conditions. Responsible breeders screen their stock, and regular veterinary checkups support early detection.

Maintain a healthy weight, keep vaccinations and parasite prevention current, and watch for unexplained weight loss, vomiting, or swelling, which warrant prompt veterinary attention.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Soft Coated Wheaten Terriers shed a lot?**
No. They have a single coat and shed minimally, which some allergy sufferers tolerate better, though no dog is truly hypoallergenic. The trade-off is frequent brushing to prevent matting.

**Are Wheatens good family dogs?**
Yes. They are affectionate, playful, and generally good with children and other pets, making them well-suited to active families that can meet their exercise and grooming needs.

## Conclusion

The Soft Coated Wheaten Terrier rewards committed owners with loyalty, joy, and endless energy. If you can keep up with daily brushing and plenty of activity, this cheerful Irish terrier makes a delightful, devoted companion.
