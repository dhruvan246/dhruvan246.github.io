For years the most popular dog breed in many countries, the Labrador Retriever is friendly, smart and wonderfully adaptable. Whether as a family pet, guide dog or hunting companion, the Lab does it all with a wagging tail.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Large (25–36 kg) |
| Lifespan | 11–13 years |
| Coat | Short, dense, water-resistant |
| Energy | High |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Labs are sturdy, athletic dogs with a short weatherproof coat in yellow, black or chocolate, and a famous "otter" tail. They are outgoing, gentle and eager to please — one of the best all-round family dogs.

## Diet & nutrition

- Labs **love food** and gain weight easily — measure every meal.
- Many carry a gene linked to increased appetite, so portion control is vital.
- Keep treats modest and the dog lean to protect joints and health.

## Exercise & training

- Need **at least an hour or two** of daily exercise — walks, fetch, swimming (they love water).
- Highly trainable and food-motivated; excellent for positive-reinforcement training.
- Mental games prevent boredom and chewing, especially in adolescence.

## Grooming & care

- Short coat needs weekly brushing, more during heavy seasonal shedding.
- Occasional baths, regular nail trims and dental care.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for hip and elbow dysplasia, obesity, ear infections, and eye conditions. Bloat is a risk in deep-chested dogs — feed smaller meals. Buy from health-tested lines and keep them at a healthy weight.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Labradors easy to train?**
Yes — intelligent and food-motivated, they're one of the easiest breeds to train.

**Do Labs shed?**
Yes, quite a bit, with heavier seasonal sheds.

## Conclusion

The Labrador Retriever earns its popularity: friendly, trainable and brilliant with families. Keep them active, watch their weight closely, and you'll have a joyful, devoted companion for life.
