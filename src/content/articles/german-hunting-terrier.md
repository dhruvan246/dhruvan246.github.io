The German Hunting Terrier, known in its homeland as the Deutscher Jagdterrier, is a compact, intensely driven working dog developed in Germany in the early 20th century. Bred purely for function rather than looks, it remains one of the most determined and versatile hunting terriers in the world.

## Appearance & temperament

The German Hunting Terrier is a small, sturdy, rectangular dog standing roughly 13-16 inches (33-40 cm) at the shoulder. Its coat is typically black, black-and-tan, or dark brown, usually with lighter tan markings on the muzzle, eyebrows, chest, and legs. The coat may be smooth or harsh and broken, and both types are weather-resistant.

In temperament, this breed is bold, energetic, and exceptionally focused. Bred to flush and pursue quarry both above and below ground, it has a strong prey drive, high courage, and remarkable stamina. Jagdterriers are loyal and affectionate with their families but can be reserved with strangers and assertive with other dogs. They thrive with active, experienced owners and are not well suited to a sedentary household.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a high-quality complete dog food formulated for small, active or working breeds. Because of their intense energy and muscular build, German Hunting Terriers benefit from food with adequate protein and fat to fuel their activity. Divide the daily ration into two meals to support steady energy and reduce the risk of digestive upset.

Portion sizes depend on age, weight, and activity level, so follow the feeding guide on the packaging as a starting point and adjust to keep your dog lean. Keep treats modest, especially as training rewards, and always provide fresh water. Monitor body condition regularly, as this small breed can gain weight quickly if exercise drops.

## Exercise & training

This is an extremely high-energy breed that needs far more activity than its small size suggests. Plan for at least one to two hours of vigorous exercise daily, including walks, running, fetch, scent games, and problem-solving tasks. Without enough physical and mental outlets, a Jagdterrier can become destructive, vocal, or restless.

Training should be consistent, firm, and reward-based, ideally started early. These dogs are intelligent and quick learners but also independent and strong-willed. A securely fenced yard is essential, as their prey drive and digging instinct make them prone to chasing small animals and escaping. Early socialization helps temper their natural wariness and intensity.

## Grooming & care

Grooming needs are low. The short coat requires only weekly brushing, with the harsh-coated variety occasionally benefiting from hand-stripping. Bathe only when necessary to preserve natural coat oils. Check and clean the ears regularly, especially after outdoor work, trim nails as needed, and maintain a routine dental-care schedule to support long-term health.

## Health & lifespan

The German Hunting Terrier is generally a hardy, robust breed with a typical lifespan of around 12 to 15 years. As a working dog bred for function, it has relatively few widespread inherited problems. As with many breeds, possible concerns can include eye conditions, joint issues, and primary lens luxation. Responsible breeders screen their stock to reduce the risk of hereditary disease.

Routine veterinary checkups, vaccinations, parasite prevention, and a healthy weight all help support a long life. Because these dogs are so active and fearless, watch for minor injuries after rough play or outdoor work.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the German Hunting Terrier a good family pet?**
It can be, for active families with older children and experience handling driven working dogs. It is loyal and affectionate but needs significant exercise, training, and structure to thrive.

**Do German Hunting Terriers get along with other pets?**
They have a strong prey drive and may chase cats, rodents, or other small animals. Early socialization helps, but they often do best as the only small-prey pet in the home.

## Conclusion

The German Hunting Terrier is a small dog with enormous drive, courage, and stamina. For an active, experienced owner who can meet its considerable exercise and training needs, the Jagdterrier is a loyal, hardy, and deeply rewarding companion.
