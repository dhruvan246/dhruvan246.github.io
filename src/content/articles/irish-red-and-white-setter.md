The Irish Red and White Setter is an athletic, good-natured gundog from Ireland, distinguished from its solid-red cousin by a clearly patterned white coat broken with rich red markings. Strong, friendly and biddable, it makes a devoted companion for active homes.

## Overview

## Appearance & temperament

This breed is powerfully built yet balanced, standing roughly 57–66 cm at the shoulder. Its defining feature is the base white coat patterned with solid red patches, along with feathering on the ears, chest, legs and tail. Temperamentally, Irish Red and White Setters are affectionate, lively and people-oriented. They tend to be slightly steadier and more focused than the Irish (Red) Setter, but they remain high-spirited and playful well into adulthood. They thrive on companionship and do not enjoy long periods alone.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, good-quality diet appropriate to your dog's age, size and activity level. As an active large breed, working or very busy dogs may need more energy-dense food than couch companions, so adjust portions to maintain a lean, well-muscled body condition. Because deep-chested breeds can be prone to bloat, many owners split the daily ration into two meals and avoid heavy exercise immediately before and after eating. Always provide fresh water, and consult your vet before major diet changes.

## Exercise & training

Bred to range and hunt, this setter has genuine stamina and needs substantial daily exercise — brisk walks, off-lead runs in safe areas, fetch and canine sports such as scent work or agility all suit it well. A bored, under-exercised setter may become restless or mischievous. The breed is intelligent and eager to please, responding best to patient, consistent, reward-based training started early. Good socialisation and reliable recall practice are especially valuable given its hunting instincts.

## Grooming & care

The silky coat is fairly easy to maintain but does feather, so brush it several times a week to prevent mats and tangles, paying attention to the ears, legs and tail. Bathe occasionally as needed. Because the ears are pendulous and feathered, check and gently clean them regularly to reduce the risk of infection. Keep nails trimmed and maintain routine dental care.

## Health & lifespan

The Irish Red and White Setter is generally a hardy breed with a typical lifespan of around 11–15 years. As with many large, deep-chested dogs, responsible owners stay alert to hip dysplasia, gastric bloat (gastric dilatation-volvulus), and inherited eye conditions. The breed is also associated with canine leukocyte adhesion deficiency (CLAD), for which reputable breeders test their stock. Buying from health-screened lines, keeping your dog at a healthy weight and maintaining regular veterinary check-ups all support a long, comfortable life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Irish Red and White Setter the same as the Irish Setter?**
No. They are separate breeds. The Irish Red and White Setter has a patterned white-and-red coat and is often a touch steadier, while the Irish Setter is solid mahogany red.

**Are they good family dogs?**
Yes, for active families. They are affectionate, sociable and generally good with children, but they need plenty of exercise and company to be at their best.

## Conclusion

The Irish Red and White Setter is a handsome, friendly and energetic gundog that rewards an active, engaged owner. Give it ample exercise, early positive training, regular coat and ear care, and sensible health-conscious management, and it will be a loyal, joyful companion for many years.
