The Old German Shepherd Dog, often called the Altdeutscher Schäferhund, refers to the older, longer-coated working lines of herding dogs from Germany. Rather than a separate standardized breed, it describes traditional shepherd-type dogs prized for their steady temperament, working ability, and natural look. Loyal and intelligent, these dogs make devoted companions and capable working partners.

## Appearance & temperament

The Old German Shepherd Dog is a strong, well-proportioned large dog standing roughly 22-26 inches at the shoulder. Compared with the modern show German Shepherd, it typically has a straighter back, a more natural working build, and often a longer, fuller coat. Coat colors include black and tan, sable, solid black, and various shades of gray or brown.

Temperamentally, these dogs are loyal, confident, and alert. Bred for herding and guarding, they form close bonds with their families and tend to be protective. They are intelligent and eager to work, which makes them responsive to training but also means they need a job or regular mental engagement to stay content.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a high-quality complete dog food formulated for large, active breeds. Portion according to age, weight, and activity level, and split daily food into two meals to support steady energy and reduce the risk of bloat. Because this is a deep-chested large breed, avoid heavy exercise right before or after meals.

Keep fresh water available at all times, and monitor body condition rather than relying on package amounts alone. Treats are useful for training but should stay within a small fraction of daily calories to prevent weight gain, which strains the joints.

## Exercise & training

This is an active, working-bred dog that needs substantial daily exercise, generally at least one to two hours. Walks, jogging, fetch, herding-style games, and structured activities such as obedience, tracking, or agility all suit the breed well. Without enough physical and mental outlets, these dogs can become bored and develop unwanted behaviors.

Early, consistent training and thorough socialization are essential. They are quick learners that respond best to positive, reward-based methods and clear, fair leadership. Exposing them to varied people, animals, and environments while young helps channel their protective instincts appropriately.

## Grooming & care

The dense double coat sheds year-round and more heavily during seasonal changes. Brush several times a week, and daily during heavy shedding periods, to remove loose hair and prevent matting, especially in longer-coated individuals. Bathe only as needed to preserve natural coat oils.

Routine care includes regular nail trims, ear checks to catch moisture or debris, and dental care such as toothbrushing. Checking the coat for burrs and debris after outdoor work keeps the skin healthy.

## Health & lifespan

With a typical lifespan of about 10-13 years, the Old German Shepherd Dog is generally robust, though shepherd-type dogs can be prone to hip and elbow dysplasia. Responsible breeders screen breeding stock for joint health. Other concerns to watch for include bloat (gastric torsion), degenerative myelopathy, and general joint wear in older dogs.

Maintaining a healthy weight, providing regular exercise, and scheduling routine veterinary checkups all support long-term health. Discuss any persistent changes in mobility, appetite, or behavior with a veterinarian.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Old German Shepherd Dog the same as a regular German Shepherd?**
Not exactly. It refers to older, traditional working lines that tend to have a straighter back and a longer coat than the modern show-bred German Shepherd, rather than being a distinct, separately standardized breed.

**Are they good family dogs?**
Yes. They are loyal and affectionate with their families and can do well with children when properly socialized and supervised, though their high energy and protective nature suit active, attentive homes best.

## Conclusion

The Old German Shepherd Dog is a loyal, intelligent, and hardworking large breed that thrives with active owners who can meet its exercise and training needs. With consistent guidance, regular grooming, and attentive health care, these natural-looking shepherds make devoted, capable companions for years to come.
