Bred for centuries as a royal companion, the Shih Tzu lives for one thing: being with you. Friendly, sturdy and irresistibly cute, this little lap dog is a favourite for families and seniors alike.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Small (4–7.5 kg) |
| Lifespan | 10–16 years |
| Coat | Long, flowing double coat |
| Energy | Low to moderate |
| Good with kids | Yes — affectionate and tolerant |

## Appearance & temperament

The Shih Tzu has a sturdy little body, a short muzzle and a long, luxurious coat. They are outgoing, affectionate and happy little dogs that get along with almost everyone.

## Diet & nutrition

- Small-breed food in measured portions twice a day.
- Their short face can make some kibble shapes hard to eat — choose suitable sizes.
- Avoid overfeeding; keep treats modest.

## Grooming — a daily commitment

That gorgeous coat mats easily:

- Brush daily, or keep them in a short "puppy cut" for easier care.
- Clean around the eyes daily and tie up the topknot to keep hair from irritating them.
- Regular baths, ear cleaning and nail trims.

## Exercise & training

A short daily walk and indoor play are enough. Shih Tzus are people-pleasers but can be a little stubborn with house-training — patience and consistency win.

## Health & lifespan

As a flat-faced breed, watch for breathing issues, eye problems, dental disease and overheating. Keep them cool in summer and schedule regular vet visits.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Shih Tzus shed?**
Very little — they're often considered a good choice for shedding-sensitive homes, but they need lots of grooming.

**Are they good apartment dogs?**
Yes — calm, small and content indoors.

## Conclusion

The Shih Tzu is a loving, cheerful companion that asks for little more than your company and a daily brush. Commit to grooming and watch their flat-faced health needs, and you'll have a devoted friend.
