Conures are a popular group of small-to-medium parrots native to Central and South America. Affectionate, curious, and full of personality, they make rewarding companions for owners who can offer plenty of daily attention. Popular types include the green-cheeked, sun, and jenday conure.

## Overview

| Attribute | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Roughly 10-20 inches (25-50 cm), including tail |
| Lifespan | Commonly 15-30 years with good care |
| Noise level | Moderate to high; some species are very loud |
| Talking ability | Limited; a few words and many sounds |
| Care level | Intermediate |

## Appearance & temperament

Conures come in a wide range of vivid colors, from the fiery orange and yellow of the sun conure to the subtler greens of the green-cheeked. They have slender bodies, long tapering tails, and strong, hooked beaks. Sizes vary considerably between species.

Temperamentally, conures are playful, bold, and highly social. They form deep bonds with their caregivers and enjoy cuddling, exploring, and clowning around. Many love to lie on their backs in a hand or burrow into clothing. Without daily interaction they can become bored, frustrated, or prone to screaming.

## Housing & cage setup

Provide the largest cage you can, with horizontal bars for climbing and bar spacing narrow enough that the bird cannot get its head stuck (typically about 1/2 to 3/4 inch). The cage should comfortably allow full wing stretching and flight between perches.

Include perches of varying diameters and natural materials to support foot health, plus a mix of chewable, foraging, and puzzle toys. Place the cage in a busy family area away from drafts, kitchen fumes, and direct sun. Allow several hours of supervised out-of-cage time daily in a bird-safe room.

## Diet & nutrition

A balanced diet is built around a high-quality formulated pellet, which should make up the bulk of daily intake. Supplement with a variety of fresh vegetables and leafy greens, plus smaller amounts of fruit. An all-seed diet is a common cause of malnutrition and should be avoided.

Always provide clean, fresh water. Avoid avocado, chocolate, caffeine, alcohol, onion, garlic, and overly salty or sugary foods, all of which can be toxic or harmful to birds. Offer treats sparingly and use them for training.

## Enrichment & handling

Conures are intelligent and need consistent mental and physical stimulation. Rotate toys regularly, offer foraging opportunities, and teach simple tricks or step-up commands using positive reinforcement. Daily one-on-one time strengthens your bond and reduces problem behaviors.

Handle your conure gently and read its body language. Most enjoy head scratches and close contact once they trust you. Supervise interactions with children and other pets, and never force handling, as this erodes trust and can lead to biting.

## Health & lifespan

With proper care, conures can live for two to three decades, so they are a long-term commitment. Watch for warning signs such as fluffed feathers, lethargy, appetite loss, labored breathing, or changes in droppings, and seek an avian veterinarian promptly if they appear.

Common concerns include feather-destructive behavior (often linked to boredom or stress), respiratory issues, and obesity from poor diet. Routine wellness exams with an avian vet, a clean environment, and a balanced diet support a long, healthy life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are conures good pets for beginners?**
They suit dedicated first-time bird owners who can commit to daily interaction, but their noise and need for attention make them better described as intermediate-level pets.

**Do conures need a permit to keep?**
Legality and permit requirements vary by region and species, so check local regulations. Like all parrots, conures benefit from specialist avian veterinary care.

## Conclusion

Conures reward attentive owners with affection, humor, and years of companionship. If you can provide a spacious cage, a balanced diet, daily enrichment, and patient handling, a conure can be a vibrant, devoted member of your household for decades.
