The Yellow-Bellied Slider (*Trachemys scripta scripta*) is a hardy aquatic turtle native to the southeastern United States. A close relative of the red-eared slider, it is popular among reptile keepers but is a long-lived, water-loving animal that needs a large, well-equipped enclosure rather than a small tabletop tank.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Adult size | Females ~8-13 in; males ~5-9 in (shell) |
| Lifespan | Commonly 20-30+ years in captivity |
| Enclosure size | 75-125+ gallon aquatic setup |
| Temperament | Active, alert, generally not fond of handling |
| Care level | Intermediate |

## Appearance & temperament

Yellow-Bellied Sliders are named for the bright yellow plastron (underside) and the vertical yellow blotches on each side of the head. The carapace is dark olive to brown with yellow markings that fade with age, and older males may darken considerably. Females grow notably larger than males.

These turtles are active, curious swimmers that spend much of the day cruising the water or basking. They are intelligent and often learn to recognize the person who feeds them, but they are display animals more than handling pets. Most dislike being picked up and may withdraw or scratch when restrained.

## Habitat & enclosure

Provide a large aquatic enclosure with deep, open water for swimming. A rough guide is roughly 10 gallons of water per inch of shell length, so adults typically need a 75-125+ gallon tank or a stock tank/pond setup. Water should be deep enough for the turtle to swim and right itself easily.

A dry, easy-to-climb basking platform is essential so the turtle can leave the water completely and dry off. Sliders are heavy waste producers, so a powerful canister or external filter rated above the water volume is important, along with regular partial water changes to keep ammonia and nitrate low.

## Heating, lighting & humidity

Maintain water temperature around 75-80°F (24-27°C) using a guarded aquarium heater; hatchlings prefer the warmer end. Above the basking dock, provide a focused heat lamp creating a basking spot of about 88-95°F (31-35°C).

Crucially, sliders require UVB lighting over the basking area to synthesize vitamin D3 and absorb calcium; without it they develop metabolic bone disease. Replace UVB bulbs every 6-12 months as output fades. As an open aquatic setup, ambient humidity is not a concern, but a proper basking gradient is.

## Diet & feeding

Yellow-Bellied Sliders are omnivores whose diet shifts with age. Juveniles are more carnivorous and need more protein for growth, while adults eat more plant matter. Offer a quality commercial aquatic turtle pellet as a base, supplemented with leafy greens and aquatic plants such as romaine, dandelion greens, duckweed, and water lettuce.

Protein items like earthworms, krill, and occasional feeder fish can be offered, and a cuttlebone provides supplemental calcium. Feed juveniles daily and adults every other day, removing uneaten food to protect water quality. Avoid overfeeding, which causes obesity and shell deformities.

## Handling & health

Handle sliders only when necessary, supporting the body and washing your hands afterward, as turtles can carry *Salmonella*. Watch for signs of trouble: soft or pyramiding shell, swollen eyes, wheezing or bubbling (respiratory infection), and refusal to eat. Most health problems trace back to poor water quality, incorrect temperatures, or missing UVB. A reptile-experienced veterinarian should handle illness and routine checkups.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Yellow-Bellied Sliders good pets for children?** They can be rewarding to watch but are long-lived, demand a large filtered tank, and are not handling pets, so they suit committed keepers more than young children.

**Do they need to hibernate?** Healthy indoor pets kept warm year-round do not need to brumate; brumation is risky and best left to experienced keepers with appropriate conditions.

## Conclusion

The Yellow-Bellied Slider is a striking, active turtle that can thrive for decades when given a spacious aquatic home, strong filtration, correct heating, and reliable UVB. Local laws on keeping or releasing sliders vary by region, so check regulations and be ready for a multi-decade commitment before bringing one home.
