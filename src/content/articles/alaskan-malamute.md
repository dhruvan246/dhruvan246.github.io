Bigger and stronger than its Husky cousin, the Alaskan Malamute was bred to haul heavy freight across the Arctic. Powerful, friendly and independent, the Malamute is a magnificent dog for an active, experienced owner.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Large (34–43 kg) |
| Lifespan | 10–14 years |
| Coat | Thick double coat |
| Energy | High |
| Good with kids | Yes, with supervision |

## Appearance & temperament

Malamutes are large and substantial with a dense coat and a plumed tail. They are affectionate, friendly and pack-oriented — but also strong-willed, independent and powerful. They are not guard dogs; they tend to love everyone.

## Exercise & training

- Need **plenty of vigorous exercise** — hiking, pulling, running.
- Strong prey drive and digging instinct; secure, high fencing is essential.
- Independent and stubborn; consistent training and early socialisation are important.

## Grooming

- Brush several times a week; expect dramatic seasonal sheds ("blowing coat").

## Health & lifespan

Watch for hip dysplasia, eye conditions, hypothyroidism, and a polyneuropathy in some lines. They tolerate cold far better than heat — keep them cool in summer.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Malamutes good guard dogs?**
No — they're typically friendly to everyone.

**Do they shed a lot?**
Enormously, especially seasonally — regular grooming is essential.

## Conclusion

The Alaskan Malamute is a powerful, friendly and striking companion for an active, experienced owner with secure space. Meet their exercise needs, keep up grooming, and protect them from heat for a devoted Arctic friend.
