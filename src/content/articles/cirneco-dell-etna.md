An ancient hunting hound from the slopes of Mount Etna in Sicily, the Cirneco dell'Etna is a lean, athletic dog with large upright ears and a tan coat. Hardy and self-reliant, it blends sighthound speed with a scenthound's nose.

## Appearance & temperament

The Cirneco is a slender, elegant dog standing roughly 42–50 cm at the shoulder, with a wedge-shaped head and distinctive tall, erect, pointed ears. The short coat ranges from light tan to chestnut, often with white markings. Temperament is alert, independent and affectionate with family, though it can be reserved with strangers. Bred to hunt over rough volcanic terrain, it remains curious, energetic and quietly determined.

## Diet & nutrition

- Feed a complete, balanced dog food appropriate for the dog's age, size and activity level.
- This is a naturally lean breed; visible ribs covered by a thin layer is normal, so avoid overfeeding.
- Divide food into measured daily portions and adjust based on body condition and exercise.
- Provide constant access to fresh water, especially after exercise in warm weather.

## Exercise & training

- A high-energy hound that needs daily vigorous activity: brisk walks plus chances to run and explore.
- Strong prey drive means recall can be unreliable; keep it leashed or in securely fenced areas.
- Intelligent but independent, so use short, positive, reward-based sessions and keep training engaging.
- Early socialisation helps this sometimes-aloof breed feel comfortable with new people and situations.

## Grooming & care

- The short coat is very low-maintenance; a weekly brush and occasional wipe-down keep it clean.
- Bathe only when needed, as the breed is naturally tidy and largely free of doggy odour.
- Check and clean the large ears regularly and keep nails trimmed.
- Brush teeth often to support dental health, and watch out in cold weather since the thin coat offers little insulation.

## Health & lifespan

The Cirneco dell'Etna is generally considered a hardy, robust breed with relatively few inherited problems, and typically lives around 12–14 years. As with any dog, responsible breeding and routine veterinary care matter. Owners should keep up with vaccinations, parasite prevention and dental care, and watch for general issues such as ear problems or muscle strains in such an active dog. Always consult a veterinarian for individual health concerns rather than relying on general guidance.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Cirneco dell'Etna a good apartment dog?**
It can adapt to smaller homes if given plenty of daily exercise, but its energy and need to run mean access to safe, secure outdoor space is ideal.

**Can a Cirneco dell'Etna be let off-leash?**
Because of its strong hunting instinct and prey drive, off-leash freedom should be limited to securely enclosed areas where it cannot chase and run off.

## Conclusion

The Cirneco dell'Etna is an ancient, athletic and low-maintenance hound that rewards active owners with affection and companionship. Give it ample exercise, secure space to run, gentle positive training and routine care, and this hardy Sicilian breed makes a devoted, healthy companion.
