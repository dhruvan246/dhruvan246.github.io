The Berger Picard (also called the Picardy Shepherd) is a rustic French herding breed prized for its lively, observant nature and unmistakable shaggy looks. With its naturally erect ears, expressive eyes and crinkled weatherproof coat, this old farm dog combines hardiness with a playful, sometimes mischievous personality that has earned it a devoted following.

## Appearance & temperament

The Berger Picard is a well-muscled, square-built dog standing roughly 21–26 inches at the shoulder and weighing about 50–70 pounds. Its hallmark is the rough, slightly tousled coat of about two inches in length, paired with naturally upright ears, a slight beard and tousled eyebrows that give it a friendly, attentive expression. Coat colors are typically fawn or brindle in various shades.

Temperamentally, Picards are energetic, intelligent and devoted to their families. They tend to be lively and observant, with a streak of independence inherited from their herding background. Many are reserved with strangers but bond closely with their people. Early, ongoing socialization helps them grow into confident, well-mannered companions.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced diet formulated for an active large-breed dog, choosing a formula appropriate to your dog's life stage. As an athletic working breed, the Picard does well on quality protein to support muscle and stamina. Divide the daily ration into two meals rather than one large feeding.

Monitor body condition closely, as excess weight strains the joints. Measure portions, keep treats to a modest share of daily calories and provide constant access to fresh water. Consult your veterinarian for guidance tailored to your individual dog's age, weight and activity level.

## Exercise & training

This is a high-energy breed that thrives on vigorous daily activity. Plan for at least an hour or more of exercise each day through brisk walks, running, hiking or play, plus mental challenges such as puzzle toys, scent games or dog sports like agility, herding or obedience. A bored or under-exercised Picard can become restless or develop nuisance behaviors.

Picards are clever but can be strong-willed, so training works best with patience, consistency and positive, reward-based methods. They respond poorly to harsh handling. Keep sessions varied and engaging to hold their attention, and start training early.

## Grooming & care

The crinkled double coat is fairly low-maintenance and should look natural rather than groomed for show. Brush roughly once a week to remove loose hair and prevent matting, paying attention to the beard, eyebrows and legs. Hand-stripping rather than heavy clipping helps preserve the coat's texture. The breed sheds moderately and is not considered hypoallergenic.

Bathe only as needed, since the weatherproof coat resists dirt. Round out care with regular nail trims, routine tooth brushing and weekly ear checks to keep the upright ears clean and dry.

## Health & lifespan

The Berger Picard is generally a hardy, healthy breed with a typical lifespan of around 12–13 years. As with many breeds, responsible breeders screen for conditions such as hip dysplasia and certain eye disorders, including progressive retinal atrophy. The breed's prominent ears benefit from routine cleaning to avoid infection.

Maintaining a healthy weight, providing regular exercise and keeping up with routine veterinary checkups all support a long, active life. Choosing a breeder who performs recommended health testing helps reduce the risk of inherited problems.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Berger Picards good family dogs?**
Yes. They are loyal, playful and affectionate with their families and generally good with children when properly socialized. Their high energy makes them best suited to active households that can meet their exercise and companionship needs.

**Do Berger Picards shed a lot?**
They shed moderately rather than heavily. Their harsh, crinkled coat needs only weekly brushing, but they are not hypoallergenic, so some loose hair around the home is normal.

## Conclusion

The Berger Picard is a spirited, rugged companion that rewards an active owner with loyalty, intelligence and a touch of comic charm. Given plenty of exercise, mental stimulation, early socialization and consistent positive training, this distinctive French shepherd makes a devoted and engaging family member for years to come.
