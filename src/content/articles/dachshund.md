Famous for their long bodies, short legs and fearless attitude, Dachshunds (or "sausage dogs") were bred to hunt badgers underground. That bold, tenacious spirit lives on in this hugely popular little hound.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Small (4–15 kg, standard or miniature) |
| Lifespan | 12–16 years |
| Coat | Smooth, long-haired or wire-haired |
| Energy | Moderate |
| Good with kids | Yes, with gentle handling |

## Appearance & temperament

Dachshunds come in three coat types and two sizes. They are clever, curious and brave — sometimes stubborn — with a loud bark that makes them surprisingly good watchdogs.

## Diet & nutrition

- Keep them lean — **extra weight is dangerous for their long spine.**
- Measured meals and limited treats are essential.

## Exercise & training

- Moderate daily walks and play keep them fit.
- **Protect the back:** discourage jumping on/off furniture and use ramps; support their body when lifting.
- Training takes patience — they're smart but independent.

## Grooming

Depends on coat: smooth needs weekly brushing; long-haired needs several times a week; wire-haired needs occasional hand-stripping.

## Health & lifespan

The big concern is **Intervertebral Disc Disease (IVDD)** — back injuries from their long spine. Keep them slim, avoid jumping, and see a vet immediately for any back pain or wobbliness. Also watch dental health.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Dachshunds good apartment dogs?**
Yes, but their barking needs managing.

**Do they have back problems?**
They're prone to them — weight control and careful handling greatly reduce the risk.

## Conclusion

The Dachshund is a charming, spirited companion with a giant personality. Keep them lean, protect that famous long back, and you'll enjoy many happy years together.
