The California Kingsnake (*Lampropeltis californiae*) is one of the most popular pet snakes in the hobby, prized for its hardiness, manageable size, and striking color patterns. Native to the western United States and northern Mexico, this non-venomous constrictor adapts well to captivity and is widely recommended for first-time snake keepers.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Adult size | 3–4 ft (rarely up to 5 ft) |
| Lifespan | 15–20+ years in captivity |
| Enclosure size | 40-gallon / 36" x 18" for an adult |
| Temperament | Hardy, active, generally docile when handled |
| Care level | Beginner |

## Appearance & temperament

California Kingsnakes are famous for their bold banded patterns, classically black or brown with white or cream bands, though striped morphs and many selectively bred color variations exist. They have a slender, muscular body and a glossy appearance. While they can be nippy or musky as nervous hatchlings, regular gentle handling produces calm, tractable adults. They are powerful constrictors and known for eating other snakes in the wild, so they must always be housed alone.

## Habitat & enclosure

A secure, escape-proof enclosure is essential, as kingsnakes are notorious escape artists that will push at lids and exploit small gaps. Hatchlings can start in a smaller tub or 10-gallon enclosure, while adults need at least a 36-inch (40-gallon equivalent) terrarium. Provide a few inches of substrate such as aspen shavings or cypress mulch for burrowing, at least two snug hides (one on the warm side, one on the cool side), and sturdy branches or clutter for enrichment.

## Heating, lighting & humidity

Set up a thermal gradient using an under-tank heater or overhead bulb controlled by a thermostat. Aim for a warm basking area of about 85–90°F (29–32°C) and a cool side around 75–80°F (24–27°C), with a slight nighttime drop. Ambient humidity of 40–60% suits this species, which comes from relatively dry habitats. Although kingsnakes do not strictly require UVB, low-level UVB lighting on a day-night cycle can support overall wellbeing.

## Diet & feeding

In captivity, California Kingsnakes thrive on appropriately sized rodents—roughly the width of the snake's body. Hatchlings eat pinky mice every 5–7 days, while adults take adult mice or small rats every 7–14 days. Frozen-thawed prey is safest and most convenient, warmed to room temperature before offering. Always provide a sturdy water bowl large enough for drinking and occasional soaking, and keep it clean.

## Handling & health

Wait until a newly acquired snake is feeding reliably before handling, then keep early sessions short and calm. Support the body fully and avoid handling for a day or two after feeding. Common health issues include retained shed (often linked to low humidity), mites, respiratory infections, and obesity from overfeeding. Provide a humid hide during shedding and watch for incomplete sheds, wheezing, or refusal to eat. Consult a reptile-experienced veterinarian if problems arise.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are California Kingsnakes good for beginners?** Yes. Their hardiness, modest size, simple feeding, and generally calm disposition make them one of the most beginner-friendly pet snakes available.

**Is it legal to keep a California Kingsnake?** They are widely kept and legal in most areas, but regulations vary by region and country—always confirm local laws before acquiring one.

## Conclusion

With a secure enclosure, a proper temperature gradient, and a simple rodent diet, the California Kingsnake makes a rewarding, long-lived companion. Their resilience and handleable nature explain their enduring popularity, but always house them alone and verify local regulations before bringing one home.
