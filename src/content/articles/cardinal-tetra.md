The Cardinal Tetra (*Paracheirodon axelrodi*) is one of the most popular freshwater aquarium fish, prized for its brilliant red-and-blue stripe that runs the full length of its body. Native to the soft, acidic blackwater rivers of the upper Orinoco and Negro basins in South America, it is a peaceful schooling fish that brings vivid colour to planted community tanks.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Adult size | Around 1.5–2 in (4–5 cm) |
| Lifespan | Typically 4–5 years with good care |
| Minimum tank size | 15–20 gallons for a small school |
| Water type | Freshwater, soft and slightly acidic |
| Care level | Easy to moderate |

## Appearance & behaviour

Cardinal Tetras are slim, torpedo-shaped fish instantly recognisable by the iridescent blue stripe and the bright red band that extends the entire length of the body. (In the similar Neon Tetra, the red covers only the rear half.) Both sexes are colourful, though females are often slightly rounder.

They are gentle, active shoaling fish that feel secure only in groups. Keep at least six, and ideally ten or more, to see natural schooling behaviour and reduce stress. In larger groups they are bolder, more colourful, and spend more time in open water.

## Tank setup & water parameters

This species thrives in conditions that mimic its blackwater home. Aim for a temperature of about 73–81°F (23–27°C), a pH of roughly 5.5–7.0, and soft water (low GH). A mature, well-cycled tank is essential, as Cardinals are sensitive to ammonia and nitrite spikes.

Use a gentle filter, dim or subdued lighting, and a dark substrate to bring out their colours. Live plants such as Java fern, Amazon sword, and floating plants provide cover, while driftwood and dried leaf litter (Indian almond leaves) release tannins that recreate natural blackwater conditions.

## Diet & feeding

Cardinal Tetras are omnivores with a slight preference for small, meaty foods. Offer a varied diet built around high-quality micro pellets or flakes, supplemented with frozen or live brine shrimp, daphnia, micro worms, and bloodworms. Variety keeps colours vivid and supports overall health.

Feed small amounts once or twice a day, only what they can eat in a couple of minutes. Their mouths are small, so choose appropriately sized foods and remove uneaten leftovers to protect water quality.

## Tank mates & compatibility

Their peaceful temperament makes them excellent community fish. Good companions include other small, calm species such as Corydoras catfish, Otocinclus, dwarf cichlids like Apistogramma, rasboras, and small peaceful gouramis. They also pair beautifully with other tetras of similar size.

Avoid large or aggressive fish, fin-nippers, and anything big enough to view a Cardinal as food. Because they are small and slender, even some mid-sized fish may treat them as prey.

## Health & common issues

With clean, stable water Cardinal Tetras are fairly hardy, but newly imported fish can be delicate during acclimation. Common concerns include ich (white spot), fin rot, and bacterial infections, most often triggered by poor water quality, chilling, or stress.

Prevention is the best approach: maintain stable parameters, perform regular partial water changes, and quarantine new arrivals before adding them. If illness appears, isolate affected fish and consult a knowledgeable aquatics specialist or aquatic vet rather than guessing at treatments.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Cardinal Tetras hard to keep?** They are easy to moderate. The main challenge is providing soft, slightly acidic, stable water in a mature tank; once settled, they are robust and long-lived.

**How many Cardinal Tetras should I keep together?** A minimum of six, but groups of ten or more are far better. Larger schools display brighter colours, calmer behaviour, and more natural movement.

## Conclusion

The Cardinal Tetra rewards attentive aquarists with one of the most striking displays in the freshwater hobby. Give it a mature, soft-water planted tank, a varied diet, peaceful tank mates, and the company of a generous school, and this jewel-toned little fish will thrive for years. Availability and any local regulations can vary by region, so source captive-bred or responsibly collected stock and provide the specialist conditions it needs.
