Descended from working cats that kept ships and farms free of rodents, the American Shorthair is a hardy, good-natured and adaptable breed. Friendly without being demanding, they make wonderful, easy family cats.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium–large (4–7 kg) |
| Lifespan | 15–20 years |
| Coat | Short, dense |
| Energy | Moderate |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Sturdy and well-balanced, American Shorthairs come in many colours, with the silver tabby especially iconic. They are friendly, placid and sociable — happy with children, other pets and a relaxed household.

## Diet & nutrition

- Quality cat food in measured portions.
- They can gain weight as indoor cats, so watch the calories and encourage play.

## Grooming & care

- Easy — a weekly brush keeps the coat healthy.
- Provide scratching posts, climbing spots and interactive toys.

## Health & lifespan

A famously healthy, long-lived breed. Watch for obesity and, as with many cats, **hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM)**. Regular vet checks and weight control keep them thriving.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are American Shorthairs good family cats?**
Excellent — easy-going, friendly and tolerant of children and other pets.

**Are they high maintenance?**
No — one of the most low-maintenance, adaptable breeds.

## Conclusion

The American Shorthair is a hardy, friendly and low-fuss companion that fits almost any home. Keep them active and lean, and you'll enjoy a healthy, affectionate cat for many years.
