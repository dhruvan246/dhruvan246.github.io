Famous for being (nearly) hairless, the Sphynx is one of the most affectionate and attention-loving of all cat breeds. Warm, wrinkled and endlessly people-focused, they have unique care needs that set them apart.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium (3–5 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–15 years |
| Coat | Hairless (fine peach-fuzz) |
| Energy | High |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Sphynx cats have soft, warm, wrinkled skin with little to no coat. They are extroverted, playful and extremely affectionate — true "velcro cats" that crave company and warmth, and will follow you everywhere.

## Skin & warmth care — the big difference

- **No coat means special care:** their skin produces oils that would normally be absorbed by fur, so they need **regular bathing** (often weekly) to prevent greasy build-up.
- They get cold easily — provide warm spots, soft blankets and sometimes sweaters.
- Protect them from sunburn; keep them indoors.
- Clean ears regularly, as wax builds up faster.

## Diet & nutrition

- A quality diet; their high metabolism (keeping warm) means a good appetite — feed appropriately.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for **hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM)**, skin conditions, and dental issues. Choose breeders who screen for HCM and keep up regular vet care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Sphynx cats need baths?**
Yes — regular bathing is essential to manage skin oils.

**Are they hypoallergenic?**
No — they still produce allergens via skin and saliva, despite lacking fur.

## Conclusion

The Sphynx is a warm, affectionate and utterly people-loving companion with unique care needs. Keep them warm, bathe regularly, watch their heart health, and you'll have one of the most loving cats around.
