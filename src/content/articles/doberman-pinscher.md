Sleek, powerful and highly intelligent, the Doberman Pinscher is one of the finest guardian and working breeds. Far from the aggressive stereotype, a well-raised Doberman is loyal, affectionate and deeply bonded to its family.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Large (32–45 kg) |
| Lifespan | 10–13 years |
| Coat | Short, smooth |
| Energy | High |
| Good with kids | Yes, with training and socialisation |

## Appearance & temperament

Dobermans are athletic and elegant, with a short coat usually in black-and-tan. They are alert, fearless and intelligent — devoted "velcro dogs" that stick close to their people, and natural protectors when properly raised.

## Exercise & training

- Need **substantial daily exercise** plus mental work — they're athletes and thinkers.
- Extremely trainable and eager to please; thrive on obedience, sports and having a job.
- **Early socialisation and consistent training are essential** to raise a stable, confident adult.

## Diet & nutrition

- A quality, active-dog diet portioned to keep them lean and muscular.

## Grooming & care

- Very easy — weekly brushing.
- Their short coat offers little insulation; they feel the cold and benefit from a coat in winter.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for **dilated cardiomyopathy (DCM)** — a serious heart condition common in the breed — plus von Willebrand's disease (a clotting disorder), hip dysplasia and bloat. Choose health-tested lines and have the heart monitored.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Dobermans good family dogs?**
Yes — loyal and affectionate, but they need training, socialisation and exercise.

**Are they aggressive?**
No more than other guardian breeds when well-bred and raised; they're devoted companions.

## Conclusion

The Doberman Pinscher is an intelligent, loyal and capable companion for a committed, active owner. Provide training, socialisation, exercise and good heart-health care, and you'll have a devoted protector and friend.
