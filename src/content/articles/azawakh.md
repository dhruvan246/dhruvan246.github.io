The Azawakh is a tall, lean sighthound originating from the Sahelian regions of West Africa, including Mali, Niger, and Burkina Faso. Bred by nomadic peoples as a hunting companion and a loyal guardian, this breed combines striking elegance with a deeply devoted and independent nature.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |

| Good with kids | Yes, with gentle, respectful children |

## Appearance & temperament

The Azawakh is instantly recognizable for its very lean, almost angular frame, with long legs, a deep chest, and visible bone and muscle structure. The short, fine coat comes in a range of colors. Temperamentally, the breed is affectionate and intensely loyal to its own family but is typically aloof and reserved with strangers. Azawakhs form strong bonds and often attach closely to one or two people while remaining naturally watchful and protective.

## Diet & nutrition

- Feed a high-quality complete diet appropriate to the dog's age, weight, and activity level.
- A naturally lean physique is normal for the breed; ribs and hip bones may be slightly visible, and dogs should not be overfed to "fill out."
- Divide food into measured meals rather than free-feeding, and provide constant access to fresh water.
- Consult your veterinarian for guidance tailored to your individual dog.

## Exercise & training

- The Azawakh is athletic and enjoys regular opportunities to sprint at full speed in a safe, securely fenced area.
- Daily walks combined with occasional free running help satisfy its needs; like other sighthounds, it favors short bursts of speed over endurance.
- A strong prey drive means recall can be unreliable, so keep the dog leashed near roads or wildlife.
- Training works best with patient, positive, reward-based methods, as this independent breed responds poorly to harsh handling. Early, gentle socialization helps temper its natural wariness.

## Grooming & care

- The short, fine coat is very low-maintenance and needs only occasional brushing to remove loose hair.
- With little body fat and a thin coat, Azawakhs are sensitive to cold and benefit from a dog coat in chilly weather.
- Provide soft, padded bedding to cushion their bony frame.
- Routine nail trimming, dental care, and ear checks round out basic care.

## Health & lifespan

The Azawakh is generally a hardy, healthy breed with a typical lifespan of around 12 to 15 years. As with other sighthounds, individuals may show sensitivity to anesthesia, so it is wise to work with a veterinarian familiar with the breed. Responsible breeders screen for hereditary concerns. Routine veterinary checkups, parasite prevention, and a healthy weight support a long, comfortable life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Azawakhs good apartment dogs?**
They can adapt to smaller homes if given daily exercise and chances to run, but they need an outlet for their athletic energy.

**Are Azawakhs aloof with strangers?**
Yes. They are devoted to their family but naturally reserved and watchful around unfamiliar people, which makes early socialization important.

## Conclusion

The Azawakh is an elegant, loyal, and independent sighthound best suited to owners who appreciate its reserved nature and athletic needs. With gentle training, room to run, and protection from the cold, this distinctive West African breed makes a devoted lifelong companion.
