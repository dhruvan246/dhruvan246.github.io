The Uromastyx, often called the spiny-tailed lizard or dabb lizard, is a genus of desert-dwelling reptiles native to arid regions of North Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia. Known for their stocky bodies, club-like spiny tails, and surprisingly vivid colors, these herbivorous lizards have become popular among keepers who can provide the intense heat and dry conditions they require.

## Appearance & temperament

Uromastyx are robust, flattened lizards with broad heads and a distinctive tail ringed with rows of hard spines used for defense. Depending on the species, such as *U. ornata*, *U. geyri*, or *U. aegyptia*, adults may display brilliant yellows, oranges, reds, greens, or blues, with males typically more colorful than females. They are diurnal and most active when fully warm. While not naturally cuddly, captive-bred Uromastyx are usually placid and can grow accustomed to gentle interaction over time, though sudden movements may prompt them to flee into a burrow.

## Habitat & enclosure

A single adult needs a minimum footprint of about 4 by 2 feet, with larger species benefiting from more space. These lizards are burrowers and climbers, so provide a deep substrate, sturdy rocks, and hides. A loose, dry substrate such as a sand-and-soil mix or millet allows natural digging, though some keepers prefer non-particulate options to reduce impaction risk. Include a flat basking rock beneath the heat source and at least one cool hide on the opposite end to support thermoregulation.

## Heating, lighting & humidity

Uromastyx come from some of the hottest habitats on Earth and need a basking surface temperature of roughly 120–130°F (49–54°C), with a cooler end around 80–85°F (27–29°C). Use a strong overhead heat source and measure surface temperatures directly. A nighttime drop to the 70s°F is fine. High-output UVB lighting is essential for vitamin D3 synthesis and calcium metabolism. Humidity should remain low, generally under 30–40%, as damp conditions can cause respiratory and skin problems. Good ventilation is important.

## Diet & feeding

Uromastyx are primarily herbivorous and thrive on a varied plant-based diet. Offer leafy greens such as collard, dandelion, mustard greens, and endive, supplemented with a small amount of seeds, lentils, and dried legumes. Many keepers feed bird-seed mixes in moderation. Avoid feeding insects regularly, as the adult diet is largely vegetarian; occasional insects may be tolerated but are not necessary. Dust greens lightly with a calcium supplement. These desert reptiles obtain most of their water from food, so a standing water dish is often unnecessary and can raise humidity.

## Handling & health

Handle gradually and support the body fully, allowing the lizard to associate you with calm experiences. Common health concerns include metabolic bone disease from inadequate UVB or calcium, impaction from loose substrate, respiratory infections from excess humidity, and obesity from overfeeding seeds. A healthy Uromastyx is alert, active, and basks readily. Any lethargy, swelling, or appetite loss warrants a visit to a reptile-experienced veterinarian.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Uromastyx need to drink water from a bowl?**
Not usually. They are adapted to dry climates and absorb moisture from their food, so most keepers skip a standing water dish to keep humidity low, offering occasional misting of greens instead.

**Are Uromastyx legal to keep as pets?**
Legality and import rules vary by region, and some species face collection restrictions. Always check local and national regulations, and choose captive-bred animals from reputable sources.

## Conclusion

With their bold colors, hardy nature, and long lifespans, Uromastyx make rewarding companions for keepers who can meet their demand for extreme heat, strong UVB, low humidity, and a plant-based diet. Provide the right desert environment and these spiny-tailed lizards can thrive for two decades or more.
