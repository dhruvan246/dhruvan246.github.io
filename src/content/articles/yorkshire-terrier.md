Don't let the glamorous coat fool you — the Yorkshire Terrier is a true terrier at heart: brave, lively and full of confidence. The "Yorkie" packs enormous personality into a tiny, elegant package.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Tiny (2–3.5 kg) |
| Lifespan | 13–16 years |
| Coat | Long, fine, silky (low-shedding) |
| Energy | Moderate |
| Good with kids | Better with older, gentle children |

## Appearance & temperament

Yorkies have a glossy steel-blue and tan coat that grows long and fine. They are spirited, affectionate and devoted to their owners — bold little dogs that often think they're much bigger than they are.

## Diet & nutrition

- Small portions of high-quality small-breed food.
- Watch for low blood sugar in tiny puppies — feed frequently.
- Their fine coat reflects good nutrition.

## Grooming

- Brush daily if keeping the coat long; many owners choose a short "puppy cut" for ease.
- Regular baths, and tie up or trim the hair around the eyes.
- **Dental care is critical** — small breeds are very prone to dental disease.

## Exercise & training

A short daily walk plus indoor play is plenty. Yorkies are smart but can be stubborn and bark a lot — early training and socialisation help.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for dental disease, luxating patella, collapsing trachea (use a harness, never a collar), and low blood sugar in puppies. With good care, Yorkies live long lives.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Yorkies shed?**
Very little — their hair is more like human hair, which suits shedding-sensitive homes.

**Are they yappy?**
They can be vocal; training reduces nuisance barking.

## Conclusion

The Yorkshire Terrier is a tiny, devoted bundle of energy and elegance. Stay on top of grooming and dental care, use a harness, and you'll have a charming companion for many years.
