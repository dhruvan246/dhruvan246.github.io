The Epagneul Picard, or Picardy Spaniel, is a versatile French gundog prized for its calm, affectionate nature and willingness to work in tough cover and wet terrain. Bred in the Picardy region of northern France, it is a capable pointer and retriever that today also makes a devoted, even-tempered family companion for active homes.

## Appearance & temperament

The Epagneul Picard is a balanced, solidly built spaniel of medium size. Its coat is moderately long, dense and slightly wavy, with feathering on the ears, legs, chest and tail. The colouring is typically a speckled grey-brown (often described as roan) frequently marked with brown patches and sometimes tan points over the eyes and on the legs. The head is broad with long, low-set ears framing a soft, gentle expression.

In temperament the breed is affectionate, calm and friendly. These dogs bond closely with their families, generally get on well with children and other dogs, and tend to be sociable rather than aggressive. They are intelligent and eager to please, though their gundog heritage means they thrive when given a job and plenty of company.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, good-quality dog food appropriate to the dog's age, size and activity level. As an athletic working breed, an active Epagneul Picard may need a diet that supports sustained energy, while less active or older dogs need fewer calories to avoid weight gain. Divide the daily ration into two meals, provide constant access to fresh water, and keep treats modest. Because feeding needs vary by individual, ask your vet for guidance on portions and any breed- or age-specific considerations.

## Exercise & training

This is a high-energy breed that needs substantial daily exercise. Long walks, free running in safe areas, swimming and retrieving games all suit its athletic build, and scent-based activities or gundog work give it a satisfying mental outlet. Aim for well over an hour of varied activity each day. The Epagneul Picard is intelligent and naturally cooperative, responding best to consistent, reward-based training. Early socialisation and recall practice are especially valuable given its strong hunting instincts.

## Grooming & care

The medium-length, feathered coat needs regular brushing, ideally a few times a week, to prevent tangles and mats, particularly behind the ears and on the legs. Check and clean the long, drop ears routinely, as this conformation can trap moisture and debris. Trim nails as needed, maintain dental hygiene and bathe occasionally when the dog is dirty. Drying the coat thoroughly after swimming or wet outings helps keep the skin healthy.

## Health & lifespan

The Epagneul Picard is generally regarded as a hardy, healthy breed with a typical lifespan of around 12 to 14 years. As with many spaniels and active medium breeds, owners should be attentive to ear infections linked to the pendulous ears, and to joint conditions such as hip dysplasia. Routine veterinary check-ups, parasite prevention, a healthy weight and a sensible exercise routine all support a long, comfortable life. Always consult your vet about any health concerns.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Epagneul Picard a good family dog?**
Yes. It is known for being gentle, affectionate and sociable, generally doing well with children and other pets when properly socialised and given enough exercise.

**How much exercise does an Epagneul Picard need?**
A lot. As a working gundog it needs well over an hour of vigorous daily activity, plus mental stimulation, to stay happy and well-behaved.

## Conclusion

The Epagneul Picard is a friendly, hard-working French spaniel that rewards active owners with loyalty and an easygoing temperament. With a sound diet, plenty of exercise, routine ear and coat care, and regular veterinary attention, this versatile gundog can be a healthy, devoted companion for many years.
