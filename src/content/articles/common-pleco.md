The Common Pleco (often *Pterygoplichthys* or *Hypostomus* species) is a large suckermouth armoured catfish from South America, frequently sold as a cheap "algae eater." While hardy and long-lived, it grows far bigger than most beginners expect, making it a serious commitment rather than a simple cleanup crew.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Adult size | 12-24 inches (30-60 cm) |
| Lifespan | 10-15+ years |
| Minimum tank size | 100+ gallons for an adult |
| Water type | Freshwater, tropical |
| Care level | Intermediate |

## Appearance & behaviour

The Common Pleco has a flattened, armour-plated body covered in bony scutes, a wide sucker-like mouth, and rows of teeth adapted for rasping surfaces. Colouration is typically mottled brown, grey, or black, providing camouflage against riverbeds and driftwood. The tall, sail-like dorsal fin is a distinctive feature.

These catfish are mostly nocturnal, hiding during the day and becoming active at dusk. They are generally peaceful toward other species but can grow territorial with their own kind, and large adults may bully smaller tank mates or even rasp at the slime coat of flat-bodied, slow fish.

## Tank setup & water parameters

Because of their adult size, juveniles quickly outgrow small tanks. Provide robust mechanical and biological filtration, as plecos are heavy waste producers. Include caves, driftwood, and rockwork for daytime shelter; real driftwood is especially valued, as plecos rasp at it.

Aim for the following parameters:

- Temperature: 72-82 degrees F (22-28 degrees C)
- pH: 6.5-7.5
- Hardness: soft to moderately hard
- Ammonia and nitrite: 0 ppm; nitrate kept low with regular water changes

A tight-fitting lid is recommended, as plecos can be surprisingly strong and occasionally climb.

## Diet & feeding

Despite the "algae eater" label, adult Common Plecos do not survive on algae alone and become largely omnivorous to detritivorous. Offer sinking algae wafers, spirulina-based foods, and a variety of blanched vegetables such as zucchini, cucumber, sweet potato, and shelled peas. Driftwood supplies dietary fibre many plecos require.

Occasional protein, like sinking pellets or the odd bloodworm, rounds out the diet, but excess protein should be avoided. Feed after lights-out so this nocturnal fish gets its share.

## Tank mates & compatibility

Common Plecos pair well with similarly sized, robust community fish that occupy upper and mid water levels, such as larger tetras, barbs, gouramis, rainbowfish, and many cichlids. Avoid housing them with very small or delicate fish that could be outcompeted, and avoid slow flat-sided fish like discus or fancy goldfish, which plecos may rasp at.

Keeping multiple adult plecos together requires a very large tank and ample hiding spots to reduce territorial disputes.

## Health & common issues

Plecos are hardy but vulnerable to poor water quality given how much waste they produce; ammonia spikes and chronically high nitrates are the most common problems. Watch for ich (white spot), fin damage from aggression, and malnutrition in fish fed an algae-only diet.

A varied diet, stable warm water, and consistent water changes prevent most issues. Handle with care, as the sharp dorsal and pectoral spines can lock and injure both the fish and the keeper. If a fish becomes lethargic, stops eating, or shows clamped fins, test the water first.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Common Plecos really keep a tank clean?**
Only partly. Juveniles eat some algae, but as they grow they produce far more waste than they remove, so they add to your cleaning workload rather than reducing it.

**How big do Common Plecos actually get?**
Most reach 12-24 inches as adults, which is why a single specimen ultimately needs a 100-gallon-plus tank. They are not suited to small community aquariums long term.

## Conclusion

The Common Pleco is an impressive, long-lived catfish, but its eventual size and heavy waste output make it unsuitable for small tanks. Given a spacious aquarium, strong filtration, driftwood, and a varied diet, it becomes a hardy, characterful resident that can thrive for well over a decade.
