The molly (genus *Poecilia*) is one of the most popular freshwater aquarium fish, prized for its easygoing nature, bright colours and willingness to breed in the home tank. Mollies are livebearers, meaning females give birth to free-swimming young rather than laying eggs. They come in many forms, including the black molly, sailfin, lyretail, dalmatian and balloon molly. While often recommended for beginners, they do best with stable, slightly hard, alkaline water.

## Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|---|---|
| Adult size | Roughly 7-12 cm (3-5 in), depending on variety |
| Lifespan | Around 3-5 years with good care |
| Minimum tank size | About 75 litres (20 gallons) |
| Water type | Freshwater (tolerates slightly brackish) |
| Care level | Easy to moderate |

## Appearance & behaviour

Mollies have a stocky, torpedo-shaped body and a wide range of colours and patterns thanks to decades of selective breeding. Males are typically slimmer with a modified anal fin called a gonopodium, while females are rounder and often larger. Sailfin and lyretail varieties have showy, extended fins.

They are active, sociable swimmers that spend much of their time in the middle and upper levels of the tank. Mollies are generally peaceful but males can become pushy toward females, so keeping more females than males reduces stress.

## Tank setup & water parameters

Mollies appreciate space and a planted aquarium with open swimming areas. Aim for a temperature of around 24-28°C (75-82°F), a pH of about 7.5-8.5 and moderately hard water. They are sensitive to poor water quality, so a reliable filter and regular partial water changes are essential.

Many keepers add a small amount of aquarium salt or keep mollies in slightly brackish conditions, as this can improve health, though it is not strictly required in well-maintained fresh water. Always cycle the tank before adding fish and keep ammonia and nitrite at zero.

## Diet & feeding

Mollies are omnivores with a strong appetite for plant matter and algae. Offer a quality flake or pellet food as a staple, supplemented with blanched vegetables such as spinach, zucchini or peas, plus occasional protein treats like brine shrimp or bloodworms. A varied diet keeps colours vivid and supports healthy digestion. Feed small amounts once or twice daily and avoid overfeeding, which fouls the water.

## Tank mates & compatibility

Mollies are community-friendly and do well in groups of their own kind. Good companions include other peaceful livebearers such as platies and guppies, as well as tetras, danios, corydoras catfish and similarly sized non-aggressive species that enjoy comparable water conditions. Avoid known fin-nippers and large or aggressive fish. Because mollies breed readily, expect a steady supply of fry if both sexes are present.

## Health & common issues

With clean, stable water mollies are hardy, but they are prone to stress when parameters swing. Common problems include ich (white spot), fin rot and the so-called "shimmies," where a fish wobbles in place, often linked to poor water quality or insufficient mineral hardness. Prevention is largely about consistent maintenance, good filtration and a varied diet. If illness appears, quarantine affected fish and consult species-appropriate guidance or an aquatic vet rather than guessing at treatments.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do mollies need salt in their water?** Not necessarily. Mollies thrive in hard, alkaline fresh water. Some keepers add a little aquarium salt or use brackish conditions, but stable, well-maintained fresh water is usually enough.

**How many mollies should I keep together?** Keep at least three or four, and ideally more females than males to reduce harassment. Provide enough tank volume so the group is not crowded.

## Conclusion

Mollies are colourful, lively and rewarding fish that suit beginners willing to maintain stable, slightly hard water. Give them a spacious tank, a varied omnivorous diet and peaceful companions, and they will reward you with vibrant activity and, very likely, a new generation of fry.
