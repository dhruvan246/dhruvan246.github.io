The Australian Stumpy Tail Cattle Dog is a tough, naturally short-tailed herding breed developed in the Australian outback to move cattle across vast, rugged country. Often confused with its close relative the Australian Cattle Dog, the "Stumpy" is a distinct breed with a square build, a naturally bobbed or absent tail, and an independent, hard-working character.

## Appearance & temperament

The Stumpy Tail is a moderately sized, square-framed dog that appears slightly taller at the shoulder than long in the body, giving it an upstanding, alert outline. Adults typically stand 17-20 inches tall and weigh roughly 35-50 pounds (16-23 kg). The breed comes in two recognized colors: blue (often with or without black markings) and red speckle. Its hallmark is the naturally short or absent tail, a trait that occurs from birth rather than docking.

In temperament, the Stumpy is bold, loyal, and intensely devoted to its handler. Bred for endurance and independent decision-making, it is alert, watchful, and naturally protective, which makes it a capable watchdog. It tends to bond closely with its family while remaining reserved with strangers.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, high-quality diet formulated for active, medium-sized dogs. Because the Stumpy is built for stamina, portion food according to its real activity level, dividing the daily amount into two meals to support steady energy. Working dogs may need more calories than companions living a quieter life. Always provide fresh water, especially after exercise in warm climates, and monitor body condition to keep this athletic breed lean and well-muscled rather than overweight.

## Exercise & training

This is a high-drive working breed that needs substantial daily exercise, well beyond a short walk. Long walks, runs, fetch, herding activities, and dog sports such as agility or flyball all help channel its energy. Without enough physical and mental work, a bored Stumpy can become restless or develop nuisance behaviors.

Training should be consistent, positive, and started early. The breed is intelligent and quick to learn but also independent, so reward-based methods and clear boundaries work best. Early socialization with people, other dogs, and varied environments helps temper its natural wariness and herding instinct to nip at moving feet.

## Grooming & care

Grooming needs are minimal. The short, weather-resistant double coat sheds moderately year-round with heavier seasonal sheds, during which more frequent brushing helps remove loose hair. A weekly brushing is usually sufficient the rest of the time. Bathe only as needed, since the coat naturally repels dirt. Routine care includes regular nail trimming, ear checks, and dental hygiene to keep the dog comfortable and healthy.

## Health & lifespan

The Australian Stumpy Tail Cattle Dog is generally a robust, long-lived breed, often reaching 12-15 years. Like many herding dogs, it can be prone to certain inherited conditions, including hip dysplasia and progressive retinal atrophy, an eye disorder. Responsible breeders screen breeding stock for hip and eye health. Routine veterinary checkups, dental care, parasite prevention, and maintaining a healthy weight all support a long, active life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Australian Stumpy Tail Cattle Dog the same as the Australian Cattle Dog?**
No. Although they share ancestry and a similar working purpose, they are separate breeds. The Stumpy is squarer in build and is born with a naturally short or absent tail, whereas the Australian Cattle Dog has a full-length tail.

**Are Stumpies good apartment dogs?**
They can adapt to smaller homes only if given plenty of daily exercise and mental stimulation, but they are far happier with space to move and a job to do. Active households with room to roam suit them best.

## Conclusion

The Australian Stumpy Tail Cattle Dog is a hardy, loyal, and highly capable herder that thrives when given work, exercise, and close partnership with its people. For active owners ready to meet its energy and training needs, this distinctive bobtailed Australian breed makes a devoted and resilient companion.
