The Mini Rex is a popular dwarf rabbit breed famous for its uniquely soft, plush coat that feels like velvet. Compact and good-natured, it has become a favourite companion animal and a fixture at rabbit shows. With proper care, a Mini Rex makes a calm, affectionate, and rewarding pet.

## Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Size | Small (typically 1.4–2 kg / 3–4.5 lb) |
| Lifespan | Around 7–10 years with good care |
| Housing | Spacious enclosure or rabbit-proofed room |
| Temperament | Gentle, curious, generally docile |
| Care level | Beginner-friendly with daily commitment |

## Appearance & temperament

The Mini Rex's defining feature is its short, dense "rex" coat, in which the guard hairs are the same length as the undercoat, giving an exceptionally smooth, plush texture. It comes in many colours, including black, blue, white, chocolate, lilac, and broken patterns. Its body is compact and rounded with upright ears.

Temperamentally, Mini Rexes are usually friendly, calm, and inquisitive. Many enjoy human interaction once they learn to trust their owner. Like all rabbits, individuals vary, and some are more reserved, so patient, gentle socialisation from a young age helps build confidence.

## Housing & environment

Provide the largest enclosure you can, allowing the rabbit to make several full hops and stand upright. A hutch or pen should be supplemented with daily exercise in a safe, rabbit-proofed area. Use a solid floor rather than wire mesh, since the Mini Rex's thinner foot fur makes it prone to sore hocks on hard or wire surfaces.

Line the space with a thick layer of soft, dust-free bedding and provide a hiding box for security. Include a litter tray, as rabbits can be trained to use one. Keep the enclosure out of direct sun and draughts, ideally in moderate indoor temperatures, since rabbits tolerate heat poorly.

## Diet & nutrition

The foundation of a Mini Rex's diet is unlimited fresh grass hay, such as timothy or meadow hay, which supports digestion and keeps continuously growing teeth worn down. Offer a daily portion of washed leafy greens and a small, measured amount of quality rabbit pellets.

Always provide clean fresh water. Introduce new foods gradually and avoid sugary treats, most fruit, and starchy foods. Never feed iceberg lettuce, and avoid anything mouldy or toxic. A consistent, hay-rich diet helps prevent obesity and digestive problems.

## Handling & enrichment

Support a Mini Rex's chest and hindquarters whenever you lift it, and never pick a rabbit up by the ears or scruff. Handle gently and keep sessions calm so it associates contact with positive experiences. Most prefer interaction at floor level.

Rabbits are intelligent and need enrichment to avoid boredom. Offer chew toys, cardboard tunnels, digging boxes, and safe items to forage. Daily exercise and gentle company, whether human or a bonded rabbit companion, keep a Mini Rex active and content.

## Health & lifespan

With attentive care, Mini Rexes commonly live around 7–10 years. Watch for common rabbit health concerns such as dental overgrowth, digestive slowdown (gut stasis), overgrown nails, and sore hocks. Any drop in appetite or droppings warrants prompt veterinary attention, as rabbits can deteriorate quickly.

Find a vet experienced with rabbits, discuss recommended vaccinations and parasite control for your region, and consider neutering, which can benefit health and behaviour. Daily observation is the best early-warning tool.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Mini Rex good for first-time rabbit owners?** Yes. Its calm, friendly nature and manageable size make it a good choice, provided owners commit to daily feeding, cleaning, exercise, and gentle handling.

**Does the velvety coat need special grooming?** Their short coat is low-maintenance and needs only occasional light brushing. Avoid over-brushing, which can damage the dense fur; an occasional gentle smooth-over is usually enough.

## Conclusion

The Mini Rex combines a small, manageable size with a famously soft coat and an even, sociable temperament. Give it a spacious solid-floored home, a hay-based diet, gentle handling, and plenty of enrichment, and this charming breed can be a delightful, long-lived companion.
