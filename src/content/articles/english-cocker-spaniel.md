The English Cocker Spaniel is a compact, affectionate gundog known for its merry disposition and ever-wagging tail. Originally bred to flush and retrieve game birds, it remains an energetic, people-oriented companion that thrives on activity and close family contact.

## Overview

| Trait | Typical range |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium; roughly 15–17 in (38–43 cm) tall, 26–34 lb (12–15 kg) |
| Lifespan | About 12–15 years |
| Coat | Medium-length, silky, with feathering on ears, legs and belly |
| Energy | High; needs daily exercise and mental stimulation |
| Good with kids | Yes; generally gentle and sociable with children |

## Appearance & temperament

The English Cocker Spaniel is well balanced and slightly longer than tall, with a refined head, long low-set ears and a soft, intelligent expression. The coat is flat to slightly wavy and comes in many colours, including solid black, liver and red, as well as parti-colours and roan patterns. Temperamentally, the breed is famously cheerful, affectionate and eager to please. Cockers tend to form strong bonds with their people and can be sensitive, responding best to gentle, encouraging handling. They are typically friendly toward strangers and other pets, making them poor guard dogs but excellent companions.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food appropriate to your dog's age, size and activity level. Because Cockers are food-motivated and can gain weight easily, measure meals rather than free-feeding, and account for training treats in the daily total. Splitting the daily ration into two meals suits most adults. Always provide fresh water, and check that body condition stays trim, with a visible waist and easily felt ribs. Your veterinarian can help you choose a suitable diet and portion size for your individual dog.

## Exercise & training

As an active sporting breed, the English Cocker Spaniel needs at least an hour of varied exercise daily, such as walks, off-lead runs in safe areas, fetch and scent games. They enjoy having a "job" and often excel at activities like rally, agility, and gundog work. Mental enrichment matters as much as physical activity; bored Cockers may bark or become destructive. Training should be positive, consistent and reward-based, as this sensitive breed shrinks from harsh corrections. Early socialisation helps them grow into confident, well-mannered adults.

## Grooming & care

The silky coat and feathering need regular attention. Brush several times a week to prevent mats, paying close attention to the ears, armpits and leg feathering. Many owners have the coat trimmed or clipped every couple of months. The long, hairy ears trap moisture and debris, so check and gently clean them routinely to reduce the risk of infection. Keep nails trimmed, teeth brushed and eyes clear of debris as part of normal care.

## Health & lifespan

English Cocker Spaniels are generally hardy, often living about 12–15 years. Like many breeds, they have some predispositions worth knowing about. Their pendulous ears make them prone to ear infections. Breed-linked considerations can include certain inherited eye conditions and hip dysplasia, and responsible breeders screen for these. Routine veterinary checkups, dental care, parasite prevention and weight management support a long, healthy life. Discuss any specific concerns or symptoms with your veterinarian.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are English Cocker Spaniels good family dogs?**
Yes. They are typically gentle, sociable and affectionate, getting along well with children and other pets when properly socialised and given enough exercise.

**Do English Cocker Spaniels need a lot of grooming?**
Yes. Their silky, feathered coat requires brushing several times a week and periodic trimming, plus regular ear checks to help prevent infections.

## Conclusion

The English Cocker Spaniel is a joyful, devoted companion that rewards active owners willing to meet its exercise, grooming and companionship needs. With consistent positive training, routine care and regular veterinary attention, this merry spaniel can be a loving member of the family for many years.
