Cheerful, scruffy and endlessly curious, the Petit Basset Griffon Vendeen (often shortened to PBGV) is a small French scent hound bred to hunt rabbit and hare over the rough terrain of the Vendee region. With a shaggy double coat, low-slung body and a famously merry disposition, this hardy hound has won fans well beyond the field as a sturdy, fun-loving companion.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Size | Small to medium; roughly 13–15 in (33–38 cm) at the shoulder, around 25–40 lb (11–18 kg) |
| Lifespan | Typically 12–15 years |
| Coat | Rough, harsh double coat with a tousled, weatherproof texture |
| Energy | High; needs daily activity |
| Good with kids | Yes, generally friendly and playful with children |

## Appearance & temperament

The PBGV is longer than it is tall, built low to the ground with an alert, characterful expression framed by bushy eyebrows, a beard and a mustache. The rough outer coat is never silky or woolly; it lies over a thick undercoat and commonly appears in white with combinations of lemon, orange, black, sable or tricolor markings.

Temperamentally, this is an upbeat, sociable hound. PBGVs are happy, confident and outgoing, and they tend to get along well with people and other dogs. As a pack-bred scent hound, the breed enjoys company and can be vocal, with a deep bark used to signal interesting smells. Independent and occasionally stubborn, they think for themselves but rarely turn aggressive.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced dog food appropriate to your PBGV's age, size and activity level. Because this is an active, food-motivated hound that can be prone to weight gain, measure meals rather than free-feeding, and account for training treats within the daily total. Splitting the daily ration into two meals suits most adults. Keep fresh water available at all times, and check that your dog stays at a healthy weight with an easily felt waistline. Ask your veterinarian for guidance tailored to your individual dog.

## Exercise & training

Bred to work tirelessly in the field, the PBGV has genuine energy and stamina. Plan on at least an hour of activity each day through walks, play and chances to explore and sniff. A securely fenced yard is ideal, as a strong nose can lead this hound to wander off on a scent. Always keep them leashed in unfenced areas.

Training works best with short, upbeat, reward-based sessions. PBGVs are smart but independent, so patience and consistency matter more than repetition. Early socialization and engaging activities such as scent games or tracking channel their instincts productively.

## Grooming & care

The harsh coat is fairly low-maintenance but does need regular attention to prevent matting. Brush thoroughly once or twice a week, paying attention to the beard, legs and feathering. The coat is typically hand-stripped or tidied rather than clipped to keep its weatherproof texture. Check and clean the long, hanging ears regularly, as drop ears can be prone to trapping moisture and debris. Routine nail trims and dental care round out basic upkeep.

## Health & lifespan

The PBGV is generally a hardy, healthy breed with a typical lifespan of about 12 to 15 years. As with many breeds, certain conditions are seen more often, including eye issues, ear infections related to the long drop ears, and joint or hip concerns. Choosing a responsible breeder who screens breeding dogs, along with regular veterinary checkups, supports a long, healthy life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Petit Basset Griffon Vendeens good family dogs?**
Yes. Their merry, sociable nature makes them affectionate, playful companions that usually do well with children and other pets when properly socialized.

**Do PBGVs bark a lot?**
They can be fairly vocal. As scent hounds, they have a strong, carrying bark and may alert to scents and sounds, so early training helps manage excessive barking.

## Conclusion

The Petit Basset Griffon Vendeen is a hardy, happy hound that pairs working-dog stamina with an irresistibly cheerful personality. Owners who can meet its needs for exercise, mental stimulation and regular coat care will find a sturdy, devoted and entertaining companion for many years.
