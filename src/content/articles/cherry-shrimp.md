The Cherry Shrimp (*Neocaridina davidi*) is one of the most popular freshwater invertebrates in the aquarium hobby. Hardy, peaceful, and brightly colored, these small dwarf shrimp are an excellent choice for beginners and a rewarding addition to planted nano tanks.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Size | 2.5-4 cm (1-1.5 in) |
| Lifespan | 1-2 years |
| Housing | Planted freshwater tank, 19 L (5 gal) or larger |
| Temperament | Peaceful, social, non-aggressive |
| Care level | Beginner |

## Appearance & behaviour

Cherry Shrimp are best known for their vivid red coloration, though selective breeding has produced grades ranging from pale, translucent pink to deep, opaque "Painted Fire Red." Females are typically larger and more intensely colored than males, and a mature female often shows a yellow or greenish "saddle" of developing eggs behind her head.

These shrimp are active grazers, constantly picking at surfaces with their tiny claws. They are highly social and feel most secure in groups of ten or more, where you will see more natural foraging behaviour and bolder colors.

## Housing & environment

A stable, established tank is the single most important factor in shrimp keeping. House Cherry Shrimp in a cycled aquarium of at least 19 litres (5 gallons) with a sponge filter, which prevents tiny shrimp and shrimplets from being sucked in.

Live plants such as Java moss, Java fern, and Anubias provide grazing surfaces, hiding spots, and help maintain water quality. Aim for these parameters:

- Temperature: 20-26 C (68-78 F)
- pH: 6.5-7.5
- General hardness (GH): 4-8 dGH
- Ammonia and nitrite: 0 ppm

Avoid sudden swings in temperature or chemistry, and never add copper-based medications, which are toxic to invertebrates.

## Diet & feeding

Cherry Shrimp are omnivorous scavengers that graze continuously on biofilm, algae, and decaying plant matter. In a mature tank they find much of their own food, so supplementary feeding should be modest. Offer a small amount of high-quality shrimp pellets, blanched vegetables (zucchini, spinach, or carrot), or algae wafers a few times a week.

Feed only what they can finish within a couple of hours and remove leftovers, since overfeeding fouls the water and encourages harmful bacteria. Occasional protein from frozen foods supports molting and growth.

## Handling & care

Cherry Shrimp should not be handled directly; they are delicate and best enjoyed visually. The main hands-on tasks are gentle, consistent maintenance. Perform small weekly water changes of around 10-20 percent using dechlorinated water that is matched in temperature and parameters to the tank.

When acclimating new shrimp, drip-acclimate them slowly over 1-2 hours, as they are sensitive to abrupt changes. Keep them only with peaceful tankmates, or in a species-only tank, since many fish view shrimp and shrimplets as food.

## Health

Healthy Cherry Shrimp are active, well-colored, and molt regularly. Successful molting depends on stable water and adequate minerals, particularly calcium, so a GH within range is essential. A failed molt, where a shrimp gets stuck in its old shell, is often a sign of poor water quality or rapid parameter changes.

Watch for bacterial or fungal infections, which may appear as white or discolored patches, and for internal parasites. Most problems trace back to water quality, so consistent testing and gentle maintenance prevent the majority of issues. As tropical aquarium animals, Cherry Shrimp are legal to keep in most regions, but always release nothing into local waterways.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Cherry Shrimp breed easily?** Yes. In a stable, well-fed tank with no predators, they breed readily without intervention. Females carry eggs under their tails for about three to four weeks before releasing fully formed miniature shrimplets.

**Can Cherry Shrimp live with fish?** They can coexist with very small, peaceful fish, but most fish will eat baby shrimp. For maximum survival and breeding, a dedicated shrimp-only tank with dense plants is best.

## Conclusion

Cherry Shrimp reward attentive, low-effort care with vibrant color, fascinating grazing behaviour, and easy breeding. Focus on a stable, cycled, planted tank with clean water and gentle maintenance, and these resilient little invertebrates will thrive and multiply for generations.
