The Plott Hound is a powerful American scenthound originally developed for tracking and baying big game such as bear and boar. Tenacious, courageous and intensely loyal, the breed is the state dog of North Carolina and remains a working hunter as well as a devoted family companion for active homes.

## Appearance & temperament

The Plott Hound is a muscular, athletic dog of medium-to-large build with a short, glossy coat that most often appears in brindle, though black and other patterns occur. Ears hang moderately and the expression is alert and confident. Bred for stamina and grit on the trail, Plotts are bold, determined and vocal, with a loud, ringing bay. At home they are affectionate, eager to please and devoted to their people, while remaining alert and somewhat reserved with strangers. They have a strong prey drive and a tendency to follow their nose.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, balanced diet appropriate to the dog's age, weight and activity level. As an athletic working breed, active Plotts may need ample calories, but pet dogs can gain weight easily if overfed, so portion to maintain a lean, muscular body condition. Divide food into two meals a day rather than one large bowl, and provide constant access to fresh water, especially after exercise. Treats are useful for training but should stay within sensible daily limits.

## Exercise & training

Plott Hounds are high-energy dogs that need substantial daily exercise—long walks, jogging, hiking or structured play—to stay physically and mentally satisfied. A bored or under-exercised Plott may bark, howl or become destructive. Because of their strong scenting instinct and prey drive, they should be exercised on a secure leash or within a fenced area; a reliable off-leash recall is difficult once a scent is found. They are intelligent but independent and determined, so training works best with patient, consistent, reward-based methods and early socialization.

## Grooming & care

Grooming needs are modest. The short, smooth coat requires only weekly brushing to remove loose hair and distribute natural oils, with occasional baths as needed. Check and clean the hanging ears regularly, since drop ears can trap moisture and debris and are prone to infection. Trim nails routinely and maintain regular dental care through tooth brushing and veterinary checks.

## Health & lifespan

The Plott Hound is generally a hardy, healthy breed with a typical lifespan of around 12 to 14 years. As with many deep-chested dogs, bloat (gastric dilatation-volvulus) is a concern owners should learn to recognize as an emergency. Hip dysplasia and ear infections in the pendulous ears are also worth monitoring. Routine veterinary care, vaccinations, parasite prevention and weight management support a long, healthy life. Always consult a veterinarian for individualized health advice.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Plott Hounds good family dogs?**
Yes. Plotts are loyal and affectionate with their families and can do well with children when properly socialized and supervised, though their energy and size suit active households best.

**Do Plott Hounds bark a lot?**
They are vocal hounds with a distinctive, far-carrying bay, especially when excited, bored or tracking a scent. Adequate exercise and training help manage excessive vocalizing.

## Conclusion

The Plott Hound is a brave, energetic and devoted scenthound that rewards an active, committed owner. Give it plenty of exercise, secure containment, consistent positive training and routine care, and this loyal companion will thrive for many years.
