Built to survive harsh Scandinavian winters, the Norwegian Forest Cat ("Wegie") is a large, hardy and strikingly beautiful breed. Behind the dense, weatherproof coat is a friendly, easy-going companion.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Large (4–9 kg) |
| Lifespan | 13–16 years |
| Coat | Long, dense, double, water-resistant |
| Energy | Moderate |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Wegies are large and powerful with a glorious double coat, full ruff and bushy tail. They are friendly, calm and intelligent — affectionate without being clingy, and natural climbers who love heights.

## Diet & nutrition

- A quality diet portioned for their large size; they mature slowly.
- Watch weight as they age.

## Grooming & care

- Brush 2–3 times a week, and daily during the heavy seasonal shed.
- Provide tall, sturdy climbing structures — they love to perch up high.

## Health & lifespan

Generally hardy. Watch for **HCM (heart disease)**, hip dysplasia, and glycogen storage disease (a rare genetic condition — reputable breeders test for it). Keep up regular vet care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Norwegian Forest Cats friendly?**
Yes — affectionate and sociable, but independent enough not to be needy.

**Do they need lots of grooming?**
Regular brushing, with extra attention during seasonal sheds.

## Conclusion

The Norwegian Forest Cat is a stunning, hardy and friendly companion that combines beauty with an easy-going nature. Keep up the grooming, give them room to climb, and you'll have a magnificent feline friend.
