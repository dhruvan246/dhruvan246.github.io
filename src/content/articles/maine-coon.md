One of the largest domestic cat breeds, the Maine Coon is a friendly, dog-like "gentle giant." With a shaggy coat, tufted ears and a sociable, playful nature, they're among the most beloved cats in the world.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Large (4.5–11 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–15 years |
| Coat | Long, shaggy, water-resistant |
| Energy | Moderate |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Maine Coons are big and muscular with a long flowing coat, bushy tail and lynx-like ear tufts. They are friendly, gentle and intelligent — often described as dog-like for their love of company, games and even fetch. Many "chirp" and trill rather than meow.

## Diet & nutrition

- A quality diet portioned for their large size; growing kittens need ample nutrition.
- Watch weight as they mature.

## Grooming & care

- **Brush 2–3 times a week** to prevent mats and tangles in the long coat.
- Provide sturdy, large cat trees and scratching posts to suit their size.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for **hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM)**, hip dysplasia, and spinal muscular atrophy (SMA) — choose breeders who screen for these. Keep them lean and maintain regular vet care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Maine Coons friendly?**
Very — sociable, gentle and great with children and other pets.

**Do they need lots of grooming?**
Regular brushing (2–3 times weekly) keeps their long coat healthy and mat-free.

## Conclusion

The Maine Coon is a magnificent, affectionate and sociable companion that brings dog-like devotion in a feline package. Keep up the grooming, watch their heart health, and enjoy this wonderful gentle giant.
