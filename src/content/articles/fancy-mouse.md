The Fancy Mouse is a domesticated form of the house mouse (*Mus musculus*), selectively bred over generations for friendly temperament and a remarkable variety of coat colours and patterns. Small, curious, and active, they make engaging pets for owners who appreciate watching natural behaviour rather than frequent cuddling.

## Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|---|---|
| Size | 7-10 cm body, plus a similar-length tail |
| Lifespan | 1.5-3 years |
| Housing | Secure, well-ventilated cage or glass tank with deep bedding |
| Temperament | Active, curious, social, generally gentle |
| Care level | Beginner-friendly |

## Appearance & temperament

Fancy Mice weigh roughly 25-40 grams, with does (females) usually smaller than bucks (males). Decades of selective breeding have produced dozens of colours and patterns, from self (solid) shades to broken-marked, banded, and satin coats. They have large rounded ears, bright eyes, and long sensitive whiskers.

These are intelligent, busy little animals that explore constantly. Females are highly social and live well in same-sex groups. Bucks often fight when housed together, so they are typically kept alone or with a neutered companion. Bucks also produce a stronger, muskier scent than does.

## Housing & environment

Provide as much floor space as possible; a minimum of around 60 x 30 cm of floor area suits two to three mice, with more being better. Bar spacing must be very narrow, as mice squeeze through tiny gaps, so glass tanks with mesh lids or specialist cages work well. Avoid wire floors, which injure their delicate feet.

Add several centimetres of paper-based or aspen bedding for burrowing, plus hideouts, tunnels, climbing branches, and a solid-surface running wheel. Avoid cedar and pine shavings, whose aromatic oils can irritate the respiratory system. Keep the enclosure out of direct sunlight and draughts at a steady 18-24 C. Spot-clean daily and do a full clean weekly, since ammonia from urine builds up quickly in small spaces.

## Diet & nutrition

A quality commercial mouse or rodent pellet provides a balanced foundation. Supplement with small amounts of fresh vegetables such as broccoli, peas, and leafy greens, plus occasional treats like a few seeds or a sliver of fruit. A small mixed-grain ration can be scattered to encourage natural foraging.

Avoid sugary, fatty, or salty human foods, as mice are prone to obesity. Fresh, clean water should always be available, ideally from a gravity bottle with a metal spout. Remove uneaten fresh food daily to prevent spoilage.

## Handling & enrichment

Approach mice calmly and let them sniff your hand before scooping them up; never grab or lift them by the tail. Frequent gentle, brief handling from a young age builds trust, though some mice always prefer to stay busy in their home rather than be held.

Mice thrive on mental stimulation. Rotate cardboard tubes, chew toys, foraging puzzles, and digging boxes to keep them engaged. Their constantly growing incisors mean safe wooden gnaw items are essential for dental wear.

## Health & lifespan

Most Fancy Mice live around 1.5 to 3 years. Common concerns include respiratory infections (often signalled by sneezing or laboured breathing), overgrown teeth, mites, and tumours, which are relatively frequent in older mice. A clean, low-ammonia environment greatly reduces respiratory problems.

Watch for warning signs such as weight loss, lethargy, a rough or hunched posture, discharge from the eyes or nose, or any lumps. Because mice are small and hide illness well, prompt attention from a vet experienced with small rodents is important when something seems wrong.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can Fancy Mice be kept alone?** Females are social and happiest in same-sex groups. Males usually fight, so they are often kept singly; a lone mouse needs extra interaction and enrichment.

**Do Fancy Mice smell?** They are fairly clean, but bucks have a noticeably stronger scent than does. Regular spot-cleaning and full weekly cleans keep odour to a minimum.

## Conclusion

Fancy Mice are charming, low-cost pets that reward attentive owners with endless activity and personality. With a spacious enclosure, a balanced diet, appropriate company, and gentle handling, these tiny companions can live out their short lives in comfort and good health.
