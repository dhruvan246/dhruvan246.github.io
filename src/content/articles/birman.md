Steeped in legend as the "Sacred Cat of Burma," the Birman is a gentle, affectionate breed known for its silky colour-pointed coat, deep blue eyes and signature white "gloves" on each paw.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Medium–large (4–6 kg) |
| Lifespan | 12–16 years |
| Coat | Semi-long, silky (low-matting) |
| Energy | Moderate |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Birmans have a pale body with darker points and pure white paws, plus striking sapphire eyes. They are gentle, affectionate and sociable — calmer than a Siamese but very people-oriented, following you around and enjoying gentle company.

## Diet & nutrition

- Quality cat food in measured portions; watch weight as they're not highly active.

## Grooming & care

- The silky single coat resists matting — brush a couple of times a week.
- Provide companionship, perches and gentle play; they dislike being alone for long.

## Health & lifespan

Generally healthy. Watch for **HCM (heart disease)** and some breed-linked kidney concerns. Choose a reputable breeder and maintain regular vet care.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Birmans affectionate?**
Very — gentle, devoted and sociable, but less demanding than some pointed breeds.

**Do they get on with other pets?**
Yes — their easy-going nature suits multi-pet homes.

## Conclusion

The Birman is a beautiful, gentle and affectionate companion that brings calm devotion to the home. With modest grooming, good company and routine vet care, this sacred beauty makes a wonderful family cat.
