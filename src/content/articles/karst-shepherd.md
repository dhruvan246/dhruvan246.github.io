The Karst Shepherd (Kraševec) is an ancient livestock guardian breed from the Karst plateau region of Slovenia. Hardy, devoted, and protective, it was bred to watch over flocks of sheep against predators in harsh mountain terrain. Today it remains a calm but vigilant companion that bonds deeply with its family.

## Appearance & temperament

The Karst Shepherd is a robust, well-proportioned dog standing roughly 21-25 inches (54-63 cm) at the shoulder and weighing about 55-90 pounds (25-42 kg), with males larger than females. Its hallmark is a thick iron-grey coat, often with darker shading on the head and limbs, that protects it from cold and wet weather. The breed has a strong build, a broad head, and dark, intelligent eyes.

In temperament the Karst Shepherd is courageous, loyal, and deeply attached to its people. As a guardian breed it is naturally wary of strangers and can be territorial, so it requires confident, consistent handling. With its own family it is affectionate and gentle, but its protective instincts mean early socialization is essential.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a high-quality diet formulated for large breeds, with balanced protein and controlled fat to maintain a lean, muscular frame. Most adults do well on two measured meals per day rather than free-feeding, which helps prevent excess weight that strains the joints. Puppies should eat a large-breed puppy formula to support steady, controlled growth. Always provide fresh water, and adjust portions to activity level and body condition. Consult your veterinarian about appropriate amounts.

## Exercise & training

Bred for endurance rather than speed, the Karst Shepherd has moderate energy and appreciates daily walks, secure off-leash exploration, and a job to do. A securely fenced yard suits the breed well, as it likes to patrol and watch over its territory. Aim for at least an hour of activity each day.

Training should start early and rely on patience, firmness, and positive reinforcement. This is an independent, thinking breed that will not respond well to harsh methods. Early and ongoing socialization with people and other animals is crucial to channel its guarding instincts appropriately.

## Grooming & care

The dense double coat needs brushing two or three times a week, increasing to daily during seasonal shedding when the undercoat blows. Regular brushing keeps the coat healthy and reduces loose hair around the home. Bathe only as needed. Check and clean the ears, trim the nails regularly, and maintain dental hygiene. Because the breed tolerates cold well but can overheat, provide shade and water in warm weather.

## Health & lifespan

The Karst Shepherd is generally a hardy, healthy breed with a typical lifespan of around 11 to 12 years. As with most large dogs, hip dysplasia is a concern, so responsible breeders screen breeding stock. Owners should also watch for ear infections in the dense coat and signs of bloat, a serious condition in deep-chested breeds. Routine veterinary checkups, vaccinations, parasite prevention, and a healthy weight all support a long life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Karst Shepherd a good family dog?**
Yes, for the right home. It is loyal and gentle with its family but protective by nature, so it suits experienced owners who can provide leadership, early socialization, and supervised interaction with children.

**How much does a Karst Shepherd shed?**
It is a moderate to heavy shedder with a dense double coat. Expect year-round shedding and heavier seasonal coat blows, manageable with regular brushing.

## Conclusion

The Karst Shepherd is a steadfast, hardy guardian that rewards committed owners with unwavering loyalty. With proper socialization, consistent training, and routine care, this Slovenian breed makes a devoted protector and a calm, dignified companion.
