The Diamond Dove (*Geopelia cuneata*) is one of the smallest doves kept as a companion bird. Native to the dry interior of Australia, it has become a popular aviary and indoor pet thanks to its calm nature, soft cooing, and modest space requirements. It suits first-time bird keepers who prefer a quiet, low-maintenance companion over a demanding parrot.

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Size | Small (roughly 7.5–9 inches / 19–23 cm, including tail) |
| Lifespan | About 8–12 years, sometimes longer with good care |
| Noise level | Low; soft, gentle cooing |
| Talking ability | None; doves do not mimic speech |
| Care level | Easy, beginner-friendly |

## Appearance & temperament

Diamond Doves are slender, delicate birds with a long tail. The wild type is bluish-grey with white spots edged in black across the wings, giving the "diamond" look, plus a distinctive orange-red eye ring. Selective breeding has produced colour variations such as silver, fawn, and white. They are generally shy and peaceful, preferring to observe rather than interact closely. While they can become tame with gentle, patient handling, most remain hands-off pets that are enjoyed for their soothing presence and quiet cooing rather than physical interaction.

## Housing & cage setup

Because they fly horizontally, Diamond Doves benefit from a cage that is wider than it is tall. A single bird should have a cage of at least roughly 24 inches wide, and bigger is always better, especially for a pair. Bar spacing should be narrow (about half an inch) to prevent escape or injury. Provide several perches of varying thickness, placed so the long tail does not drag or get soiled. Doves are ground-foragers, so keep food and water dishes accessible. Locate the cage in a draft-free spot away from direct sun and kitchen fumes, and offer a shallow dish for occasional bathing.

## Diet & nutrition

A quality finch or dove/pigeon seed mix forms the base of the diet, ideally supplemented with a small-bird pelleted food for balanced nutrition. Offer fresh greens such as chopped leafy vegetables and small amounts of millet spray as a treat. A cuttlebone or mineral block and a source of fine grit support digestion and provide calcium, which is especially important for breeding hens. Provide clean, fresh water daily. Avoid avocado, chocolate, caffeine, and salty or sugary human foods, all of which are harmful to birds.

## Enrichment & handling

Diamond Doves are social and do best with at least one companion, though housing a compatible pair may lead to frequent breeding. They appreciate gentle enrichment such as natural branches, safe foraging opportunities, and a quiet, predictable environment. Most are not enthusiastic about being handled, so let any bird set the pace; never grab or chase them, as stress can cause panic flight. Supervised, dove-proofed time outside the cage in a safe room can be beneficial once the bird is settled and comfortable.

## Health & lifespan

With proper care, Diamond Doves commonly live around 8–12 years. Watch for signs of illness such as fluffed feathers, lethargy, laboured breathing, discharge, or changes in droppings or appetite, and consult an avian veterinarian promptly if these appear. Common concerns include respiratory issues, parasites, and egg-binding in hens. A clean cage, balanced diet, adequate calcium, and a stable temperature go a long way toward prevention. Quarantine new birds before introductions.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Diamond Doves noisy?** No. They are among the quieter pet birds, producing only soft, repetitive cooing, which makes them well suited to apartments.

**Can Diamond Doves live alone?** A single bird can thrive with regular human company, but doves are social and many owners keep a pair so the birds have constant companionship.

## Conclusion

Diamond Doves are gentle, quiet, and undemanding companions ideal for keepers who want a peaceful bird without the noise and intensity of parrots. Provide a wide cage, a seed-based diet with calcium and greens, and a calm environment, and these little doves can offer years of soft cooing and graceful charm. Availability and any keeping requirements can vary by region, so check local regulations, and seek an avian vet for specialist care.
