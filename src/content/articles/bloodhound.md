Possessing the most powerful nose in the dog world, the Bloodhound is a gentle, dignified scent hound used for tracking and search-and-rescue. Behind that famous nose and droopy face is a sweet, affectionate and famously stubborn companion.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Large (36–50 kg) |
| Lifespan | 10–12 years |
| Coat | Short, loose |
| Energy | Moderate |
| Good with kids | Excellent |

## Appearance & temperament

Bloodhounds are large and powerful with loose, wrinkled skin, long ears and a soulful expression. They are gentle, patient and affectionate — but ruled entirely by their nose, and famously single-minded once on a scent.

## Exercise & training

- Need plenty of daily exercise; they have stamina for trailing.
- **Always keep them leashed or securely fenced** — a scent will lead them for miles, and recall is poor.
- Notoriously stubborn; patient, consistent, positive training and early socialisation are essential.

## Grooming & care

- Short coat needs weekly brushing.
- **Clean those long ears and facial wrinkles regularly** to prevent infection — and expect plenty of drool.

## Health & lifespan

Watch for bloat (a serious risk — feed smaller meals), hip and elbow dysplasia, ear infections, and eye conditions. Choose health-tested lines.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Bloodhounds drool a lot?**
Yes — a great deal. Keep cloths handy.

**Can they be let off-lead?**
Rarely safe — their nose overrides recall; use secure areas only.

## Conclusion

The Bloodhound is a gentle, affectionate and remarkable scent hound for an owner ready for drool, stubbornness and serious nose power. Secure your space, keep up ear care, and you'll have a sweet, devoted tracking companion.
