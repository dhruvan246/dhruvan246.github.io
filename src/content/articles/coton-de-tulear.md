The Coton de Tulear is a small companion breed named for the port city of Tulear (Toliara) in Madagascar, where it developed as a cheerful lapdog. Its name comes from the French word for cotton, a nod to its uniquely soft, fluffy coat. Today the "Coton" is prized as an affectionate, sociable family dog.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Size | Small (about 9-11 in / 23-28 cm; 8-15 lb / 3.5-7 kg) |
| Lifespan | 14-16 years |
| Coat | Long, soft, cotton-like; low shedding |
| Energy | Moderate |
| Good with kids | Yes, very good |

## Appearance & temperament

The Coton de Tulear is a sturdy little dog with a rounded build and a plumed tail carried over the back. Its hallmark is the long, dry, cotton-textured coat, most often white, sometimes with light tan or gray markings on the ears. The expression is bright and friendly, with dark round eyes.

Temperamentally, Cotons are famously merry and people-oriented. They bond closely with their families, often following their owners from room to room, and tend to get along well with children and other pets. They are alert without being yappy and adapt happily to apartments or houses. Because they crave companionship, they dislike being left alone for long stretches.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, high-quality dog food formulated for small breeds and appropriate to your dog's life stage. Because of their small size, Cotons need only modest portions, and it is easy to overfeed them, so measure meals and limit treats. Dividing the daily ration into two meals helps maintain steady energy. Provide constant access to fresh water, and consult your veterinarian about portion sizes and any weight concerns.

## Exercise & training

Cotons have moderate exercise needs that are easily met with a couple of short daily walks plus indoor play. They enjoy fetch, gentle games, and learning tricks. Their athletic, agile nature also makes them well suited to activities like rally or agility at a fun, low-key level.

This breed is intelligent and eager to please, which makes training enjoyable. Positive, reward-based methods work best; harsh corrections are unnecessary and counterproductive. Early socialization helps them grow into confident, well-mannered adults, and gentle housetraining routines pay off with consistency.

## Grooming & care

The Coton's coat is its biggest care commitment. Brush it several times a week, ideally daily, to prevent the soft hair from matting, paying special attention to the areas behind the ears and under the legs. Many owners keep pet Cotons in a shorter trim for easier upkeep. Bathe as needed, keeping the coat clean and dry. Routine care also includes nail trimming, ear checks, and regular tooth brushing, as small breeds are prone to dental issues.

## Health & lifespan

The Coton de Tulear is generally a healthy, long-lived breed, often reaching 14-16 years. As with many small dogs, conditions to be aware of include luxating patella (slipping kneecaps), hip and eye disorders, and dental disease. Responsible breeders screen their dogs to reduce inherited problems. Routine veterinary checkups, dental care, parasite prevention, and a healthy weight all support a long, comfortable life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Coton de Tulears shed a lot?**
No. The Coton is a low-shedding breed, which many owners appreciate. However, its soft coat still requires frequent brushing to prevent tangles and mats.

**Are Coton de Tulears good family dogs?**
Yes. They are gentle, playful, and affectionate, generally doing well with children and other pets, which makes them excellent companions for families and individuals alike.

## Conclusion

The Coton de Tulear is a delightful small companion that combines a charming cottony coat with a sunny, devoted personality. With regular grooming, modest exercise, and plenty of togetherness, this Madagascar native rewards its owners with many happy, affectionate years.
