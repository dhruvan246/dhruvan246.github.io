Named for its large, butterfly-like ears (*papillon* is French for butterfly), this dainty toy breed is far hardier and smarter than it looks. Lively and trainable, the Papillon is one of the most intelligent of the toy dogs.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Tiny (2–4.5 kg) |
| Lifespan | 14–16 years |
| Coat | Long, fine, silky (single coat) |
| Energy | Moderate to high |
| Good with kids | Yes, with gentle handling |

## Appearance & temperament

Papillons have a fine silky coat and their trademark fringed, upright ears. They are alert, friendly and exceptionally clever — confident little dogs that love activity and learning.

## Diet & nutrition

- Small portions of quality small-breed food; watch for dental-friendly options.

## Grooming

- The single coat doesn't mat easily — brush a couple of times a week.
- Keep up dental care, as small breeds are prone to dental disease.

## Exercise & training

- Surprisingly active — enjoy walks, play and games.
- Among the smartest toy breeds; they excel at agility and trick training. Keep them mentally engaged.

## Health & lifespan

Long-lived and generally robust. Watch for luxating patella, dental disease, and a soft spot (in some lines). Regular vet and dental care keep them thriving.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Papillons easy to train?**
Very — they're highly intelligent and excel at canine sports.

**Are they good apartment dogs?**
Yes, though they're active and need play and mental stimulation.

## Conclusion

The Papillon is a bright, lively and affectionate companion that combines toy-dog charm with real intelligence. Keep them engaged, mind their teeth, and you'll have a clever little friend for many years.
