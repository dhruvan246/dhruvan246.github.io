The Morkie is a small, affectionate designer crossbreed created by mating a Maltese with a Yorkshire Terrier. Lively, devoted and brimming with personality, these tiny companions thrive on human attention and have become a popular choice for apartment dwellers and families seeking a loving lap dog.

## Overview

| Trait | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Size | Toy; roughly 4–8 lb (2–4 kg), 6–8 in tall |
| Lifespan | About 10–15 years |
| Coat | Long, soft, single-layered, low-shedding |
| Energy | Moderate; bursts of playfulness |
| Good with kids | Yes, best with gentle, older children |

## Appearance & temperament

Because the Morkie is a crossbreed, appearance varies even within a single litter. Most are tiny, weighing under 8 pounds, with a soft, silky coat that can be straight or slightly wavy and comes in shades of black, brown, tan, white or a mix. Many inherit the Yorkshire Terrier's expressive dark eyes and the Maltese's rounded head.

Temperamentally, Morkies are bold, lively and intensely bonded to their people. They are confident for their size and can be vocal, often alerting the household to visitors. Affectionate and playful, they dislike being left alone and may develop separation anxiety without companionship and gradual training to cope with solo time.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a high-quality food formulated for small or toy breeds, which offers the calorie density and small kibble size these tiny dogs need. Portions are modest, so measure meals carefully and divide them into two or three small servings to support stable blood sugar, as very small puppies can be prone to low blood sugar (hypoglycemia).

Because obesity strains a small frame, limit treats to a small fraction of daily calories. Provide fresh water at all times, and consult your veterinarian about appropriate portion sizes as your Morkie grows and ages.

## Exercise & training

Morkies have moderate energy and meet most of their exercise needs through indoor play and short daily walks. Two brief outings plus interactive games usually keep them content, and their small size makes them well suited to apartment living. Avoid over-exercising puppies, whose joints are still developing.

Training works best with patience, consistency and positive reinforcement. Morkies are smart but can be stubborn, and housetraining sometimes takes extra time. Early socialization and gentle correction of excessive barking help these spirited dogs grow into well-mannered companions.

## Grooming & care

The Morkie's long, low-shedding coat needs regular care. Brush several times a week, ideally daily, to prevent mats and tangles, and schedule professional grooming or trims every few months. Many owners keep a shorter "puppy cut" for easier maintenance.

Routine care also includes wiping around the eyes to reduce tear staining, weekly ear checks, regular nail trims and, importantly, frequent tooth brushing. Small breeds are prone to dental disease, so daily brushing and veterinary cleanings are valuable.

## Health & lifespan

Morkies typically live around 10 to 15 years. As a crossbreed, they can inherit conditions from either parent. Watch for dental problems, luxating patella (slipping kneecaps), collapsing trachea, low blood sugar in young puppies and eye issues. Their delicate size also means they can be injured by rough handling or falls.

Regular veterinary checkups, dental care, weight management and a safe home environment support a long, healthy life. Buying from a responsible breeder who screens parent dogs can reduce the risk of inherited problems.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do Morkies shed a lot?**
No. Morkies have a low-shedding, single coat, which some allergy sufferers tolerate better. However, no dog is truly hypoallergenic, and their coat still requires frequent brushing and grooming.

**Are Morkies good for first-time owners?**
They can be, thanks to their small size and affectionate nature, but their tendency to bark, stubborn streak and dislike of being alone require patient, consistent training and plenty of companionship.

## Conclusion

The Morkie packs enormous personality into a tiny body, offering devoted companionship to owners who can meet its needs for attention, grooming and gentle training. With proper care, dental attention and a watchful eye on its fragile frame, a Morkie can be a cheerful, loving partner for many years.
