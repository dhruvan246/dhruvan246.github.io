The Pont-Audemer Spaniel is one of France's rarest gundogs, a curly-coated water specialist developed in the marshes of Normandy. Affectionate, hardy and endlessly willing, this medium-sized retriever-spaniel is prized by the few who know it for its calm home manner and tireless drive in the field.

## Overview

| Trait | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Size | Medium (around 20-23 in / 51-58 cm at the shoulder) |

| Energy | High |
| Good with kids | Yes, gentle and patient |

## Appearance & temperament

The Pont-Audemer Spaniel is a sturdy, balanced dog of moderate build, typically weighing in the region of 40-60 pounds. Its most distinctive feature is the coat: tight curls or waves cover the body, while the head carries a soft, curly topknot of longer hair that gives the breed a charming, slightly tousled look. The coat is usually solid brown or brown roan and is naturally oily and water-resistant, a legacy of work in cold wetlands.

In temperament the breed is gentle, friendly and notably devoted to its family. It tends to be affectionate and even-tempered indoors, getting along well with children and other dogs. Bred to work closely with hunters, it is biddable and people-oriented rather than independent or aloof.

## Diet & nutrition

Feed a complete, good-quality diet formulated for an active medium-sized dog. Because this is a working breed with high energy, portions should match the individual dog's exercise level, and intake may need to rise during heavy activity and ease back in quieter periods. Divide the daily ration into two meals to support steady energy. Always provide fresh water, especially after swimming or strenuous exercise. Monitor body condition and adjust amounts to keep the dog lean. Consult your veterinarian for guidance tailored to your dog's age, weight and activity.

## Exercise & training

This is an athletic, high-energy breed that needs substantial daily exercise: long walks, free running, retrieving games and, ideally, swimming, which it loves. A bored or under-exercised Pont-Audemer Spaniel may become restless, so plan for both physical activity and mental stimulation such as scent games or training drills.

Training is generally straightforward thanks to the breed's eagerness to please and natural retrieving instinct. Positive, reward-based methods work best; harsh handling can dampen this sensitive dog's enthusiasm. Early socialisation helps it grow into a confident, well-mannered companion.

## Grooming & care

The curly coat needs regular attention to prevent mats and tangles. Brush or comb thoroughly once or twice a week, paying special attention to the topknot, ears and feathering. Check and dry the ears regularly, as drop ears combined with frequent swimming can trap moisture. Bathe only as needed, since the coat's natural oils help repel water. Routine nail trimming and dental care round out basic maintenance.

## Health & lifespan

The Pont-Audemer Spaniel is generally a hardy, healthy breed, with a typical lifespan of around 12-14 years. Because it is so rare, the small gene pool makes responsible breeding important. As with many drop-eared, water-loving dogs, ear infections are a sensible thing to watch for, and like many breeds it can be prone to hip issues. Routine veterinary check-ups and prompt attention to any concerns are the best ways to keep your dog well.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Pont-Audemer Spaniels good family pets?** Yes. They are gentle, affectionate and patient with children, and they bond closely with their people, making them excellent companions for active households.

**Do they need a lot of exercise?** Yes. As a working water dog they have high energy and need vigorous daily activity, including chances to run and, where possible, swim.

## Conclusion

The Pont-Audemer Spaniel is a rare gem: a hardy, loving and capable French water dog that pairs an enthusiastic working spirit with a calm, devoted home presence. For an active owner able to meet its exercise and grooming needs, this uncommon spaniel makes a loyal and rewarding companion.
