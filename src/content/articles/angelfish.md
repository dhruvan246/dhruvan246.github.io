The freshwater angelfish (*Pterophyllum scalare*) is one of the most recognisable aquarium fish, prized for its tall, triangular body and graceful, flowing fins. A member of the cichlid family native to the slow-moving waters of the Amazon basin, it brings elegance to home aquariums while remaining manageable for hobbyists who understand its needs.

## Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|---|---|
| Adult size | Around 12-15 cm tall, 8-10 cm long |
| Lifespan | Typically 8-10 years with good care |
| Minimum tank size | About 110 litres (30 gallons) |
| Water type | Warm freshwater |
| Care level | Beginner to intermediate |

## Appearance & behaviour

Angelfish have a distinctive laterally compressed, disc-shaped body with long dorsal and anal fins and trailing pelvic fins. Selective breeding has produced many colour and pattern varieties, including silver, marble, koi, gold, zebra and veiltail forms. They are generally peaceful but, as cichlids, can be territorial—especially when pairing off or guarding eggs. They appreciate a sense of vertical space and tend to swim calmly in the mid to upper levels of the tank.

## Tank setup & water parameters

Because of their height, angelfish need a tall aquarium rather than a long, shallow one. Aim for a temperature of roughly 24-28°C (75-82°F), a pH around 6.5-7.5, and soft to moderately hard water. Provide gentle filtration, as they dislike strong currents. Tall plants, driftwood and broad leaves mimic their natural habitat and offer spawning surfaces and hiding spots. Keep the tank covered and well cycled, and perform regular partial water changes to maintain stable, clean conditions.

## Diet & feeding

Angelfish are omnivores with a slight preference for protein. Offer a staple of high-quality flakes or pellets, supplemented with frozen or live foods such as bloodworms, brine shrimp and daphnia. Occasional blanched vegetables add variety. Feed small amounts once or twice a day, only what they can consume in a couple of minutes, to avoid overfeeding and fouling the water.

## Tank mates & compatibility

Calm, similarly sized companions work best. Good options include peaceful tetras (large enough not to be eaten), corydoras catfish, mollies, gouramis and rasboras. Avoid fin-nipping species like tiger barbs, very small fish that may become prey, and aggressive cichlids. Angelfish can be kept singly, in a bonded pair, or in a small group with enough space to reduce squabbling.

## Health & common issues

Like many aquarium fish, angelfish are susceptible to ich (white spot), fin rot and fungal infections, usually linked to poor water quality or stress. They can also be sensitive to internal parasites. The best prevention is stable water parameters, a clean tank and a varied diet. Quarantine new fish before adding them, watch for changes in appetite or behaviour, and consult an aquatics specialist or vet if illness is suspected rather than guessing at treatments.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do angelfish need to be kept in groups?** They can live alone, but many thrive in pairs or small groups. In groups, provide ample space and structure so individuals can establish territory peacefully.

**Are angelfish good for beginners?** Yes, with preparation. They are hardier than many tropical fish but need a tall, well-maintained tank and stable warm water, making them a good step up for newer keepers.

## Conclusion

Angelfish reward attentive care with years of graceful presence in a home aquarium. Provide a tall tank, warm and clean water, a varied diet and compatible companions, and these elegant cichlids will remain a striking centrepiece. With consistent maintenance and observation, they are a manageable and beautiful choice for many freshwater hobbyists.
