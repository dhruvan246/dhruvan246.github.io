The Carpet Python (*Morelia spilota*) is a non-venomous Australian and New Guinean snake popular with intermediate keepers for its striking patterns, manageable size, and reputation as a hardy, long-lived captive.

## Overview

| Attribute | Typical Range |
|---|---|
| Adult size | 5–9 ft (varies by subspecies) |
| Lifespan | 15–25+ years |
| Enclosure size | 4x2x2 ft minimum for adults |
| Temperament | Alert; can be defensive when young, calmer with age |
| Care level | Intermediate |

## Appearance & temperament

Carpet Pythons are slender, muscular constrictors named for their intricate, carpet-like markings of black, brown, cream, gold, and olive. Several subspecies exist, including the jungle, coastal, and diamond carpets, each differing in size and coloration. Hatchlings and juveniles are often nippy and may strike defensively, but most individuals become noticeably more tolerant of handling as they mature. They are semi-arboreal and appreciate climbing opportunities, frequently resting on elevated branches.

## Habitat & enclosure

Provide a secure, escape-proof enclosure, as these strong snakes are skilled at pushing lids and finding gaps. A single adult does well in an enclosure of at least 4x2x2 ft, with larger coastal carpets benefiting from more space. Because they are semi-arboreal, include sturdy branches and elevated perches alongside ground cover. Use an absorbent substrate such as cypress mulch or aspen, and offer at least two hides so the snake can shelter on both the warm and cool sides. Always provide a water bowl large enough for soaking.

## Heating, lighting & humidity

Establish a thermal gradient with a basking spot of around 88–92°F (31–33°C) and a cooler end near 75–80°F (24–27°C), allowing a modest drop at night. Use a thermostat-controlled heat source and verify temperatures with reliable thermometers. While Carpet Pythons can thrive without UVB, low-level UVB lighting on a day-night cycle is increasingly recommended to support natural behavior and well-being. Maintain humidity around 50–60%, raising it slightly during shedding to ensure clean, complete sheds.

## Diet & feeding

Carpet Pythons are carnivores fed appropriately sized rodents—mice for juveniles, graduating to rats for adults. Choose prey roughly the width of the snake's widest body point. Frozen-thawed prey is safest, avoiding the bite risks of live feeding. Hatchlings typically eat every 5–7 days, while adults need feeding only every 10–14 days or longer. Overfeeding leads to obesity, a common captive problem, so monitor body condition and adjust portions. Always offer fresh water and allow the snake to digest undisturbed after meals.

## Handling & health

Support the snake's body fully and avoid handling for 24–48 hours after feeding or during a shed cycle, when they are more defensive. Frequent, calm sessions help juveniles settle. Watch for common health issues such as respiratory infections (wheezing, open-mouth breathing), retained shed, mites, and scale rot from overly damp conditions. Good hygiene, correct temperatures, and proper humidity prevent most problems. Consult a reptile-experienced veterinarian for any persistent concerns rather than attempting home treatment.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Carpet Pythons good for beginners?** They are best suited to keepers with some experience. Juveniles can be defensive and their adult size requires a large, secure enclosure, though they are otherwise hardy and rewarding.

**Do I need a permit to own one?** Legality varies by region. In parts of Australia a license is required, and import or ownership rules differ elsewhere, so always check your local and national regulations first.

## Conclusion

With a properly heated, secure enclosure, a sensible feeding routine, and patient handling, a Carpet Python can be a long-lived and visually stunning companion. Their hardiness and decades-long lifespan make them a rewarding choice for keepers ready to commit to attentive, consistent care.
